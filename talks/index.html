Talks & Workshops

I give talks and run hands-on workshops on AI, surveillance, deepfakes, and creative technology — for festivals, universities, museums, and conferences. I also take on bespoke commissions.

Available Talks & Workshops

These can be adapted to your audience, from beginner to advanced. Previous hosts include IMPAKT Festival, University of Arts London, HKU, ArtEZ, Piet Zwart Institute, and iii: Instrument Inventors Initiative.

🧰

Deepfakes: Technology, Culture & Ethics

An exploration of synthetic media — from face-swap technology and voice cloning to AI-generated video. Covers how deepfakes work under the hood (GANs, diffusion models, encoder-decoder architectures), their cultural impact on identity and trust, the governance vacuum around non-consensual synthetic media, and emerging detection methods. Includes a live deepfake demonstration where participants see their own face transformed in real time.

TALK WORKSHOP ETHICS AI
Previously at: IMPAKT Festival (NL), Peckham Digital (UK), University of Arts London (UK)

AI for Artists

A practical introduction to artificial intelligence for creative practitioners. Participants learn to work with text-to-image generation, GANs, and large language models — not as black boxes, but as creative tools they can shape. The workshop addresses the politics of training data, questions of authorship and ownership, the environmental cost of large models, and how artists can critically engage with AI rather than passively consume it. No coding experience required.

WORKSHOP CREATIVE TECH AI
Previously at: HKU (NL), ArtEZ University of the Arts (NL), Piet Zwart Institute (NL), iii: Instrument Inventors Initiative (NL)

Generative AI & Creative Coding

Hands-on workshop where participants build generative artworks using AI and code. Covers text-to-image, text-to-video, text-to-audio, and image-to-image generation, prompt engineering as a creative practice, fine-tuning models on custom datasets, and algorithmic art with Processing. Explores how generative tools challenge traditional notions of artistic intent, the cultural implications of models trained on scraped data, and the governance questions around who controls these systems and who profits from them.

WORKSHOP CREATIVE TECH AI
Previously at: HKU (NL), iii: Instrument Inventors Initiative (NL)

Computer-Vision Surveillance for Artists

How do surveillance cameras see? This workshop dissects computer vision systems — facial recognition, object detection, gait analysis, and emotion recognition — from the inside out. Participants learn how these technologies are deployed in public spaces, the racial and gender biases embedded in training datasets, the governance frameworks (or lack thereof) that regulate their use, and how artists and activists are subverting surveillance through adversarial fashion, CV dazzle, and counter-forensics.

WORKSHOP SURVEILLANCE GOVERNANCE
Previously at: HKU (NL)

Algorithmic Violence & Predictive Policing

A talk examining how automated decision-making systems produce and reproduce structural violence — from predictive policing algorithms that reinforce racial profiling, to risk assessment tools in criminal justice, welfare, and immigration. Drawing on my work with Future Wake (Mozilla Creative Media Award 2021), this talk unpacks the data pipelines, feedback loops, and institutional incentives that make algorithmic harm systemic rather than accidental. Covers current EU AI Act governance, accountability gaps, and what meaningful oversight could look like.

TALK ETHICS GOVERNANCE
Previously at: Cross Pollination Podcast, Het Hem (NL), Fast Company, Coda Story

How to Build Your Digital Clone

What would it take to build a digital version of yourself? In this workshop, participants create their own digital clone — combining face-swap technology, voice cloning, and AI-generated text to produce a synthetic version of themselves that can look, sound, and respond like them. Along the way, we unpack the technical pipeline behind digital doubles, the cultural shift toward synthetic personas in media and marketing, the ethics of consent and likeness rights, and the governance questions emerging around digital identity ownership. Participants leave with their own working clone prototype and a deeper understanding of what it means when anyone can be copied.

WORKSHOP AI ETHICS IDENTITY
Related project: Do You Want To Be Me?

Necromemetics: Can Memes Dilute History?

An exploration of how the dead become memes — from Charlie Kirk to Harambe to MLK. This talk examines the lifecycle of posthumous memeification: how figures are stripped of context, flattened into punchlines, and circulated until their historical significance is overwritten by irony. With generative AI now able to produce endless synthetic content featuring the deceased — fake quotes, deepfake videos, AI-generated "interviews" — the talk asks what happens to collective memory when history becomes remix material. Covers the cultural mechanics of meme propagation, the ethics of using the dead as content, and the governance vacuum around posthumous digital likeness.

TALK WORKSHOP ETHICS CULTURE
Previously at: HKU (NL)

AI, Accessibility & Inclusive Design

Drawing on my work with Screen-to-Soundscape (supported by Stimuleringsfonds, the Processing Foundation, and Constant), this talk explores how AI can make — or break — accessibility. Covers the use of computer vision for generating rich alt-text, spatial audio as a navigation tool for blind users, and the governance questions around who gets to define "accessible." Challenges the tech industry's tendency to treat accessibility as an afterthought and argues for centering disabled perspectives in AI design from the start.

TALK ACCESSIBILITY AI
Supported by: Stimuleringsfonds, Processing Foundation, Constant Foundation
