Talks & Workshops

I give talks and run hands-on workshops on AI, surveillance, deepfakes, and creative technology for festivals, universities, museums, and conferences. I also take on bespoke commissions.

All talks and workshops can be adapted to your audience, from beginner to advanced. Previous hosts include IMPAKT Festival, University of Arts London, HKU, ArtEZ, Piet Zwart Institute, and iii: Instrument Inventors Initiative.

For Artists

Introduction to AI for Artists

  • Practical introduction to AI for creative practitioners, no coding experience required
  • Working with text-to-image generation, GANs, and large language models as creative tools
  • The politics of training data: where it comes from, who profits, and who is harmed
  • Questions of authorship, ownership, and copyright in AI-generated work
  • Environmental cost of large models
  • How artists can critically engage with AI rather than passively consume it
WORKSHOP CREATIVE TECH AI
Previously at: HKU (NL), ArtEZ University of the Arts (NL), Piet Zwart Institute (NL), iii: Instrument Inventors Initiative (NL)

Generative AI & Creative Coding for Artists

  • Hands-on workshop building generative artworks using AI and code
  • Text-to-image, text-to-video, text-to-audio, and image-to-image generation
  • Prompt engineering as a creative practice
  • Fine-tuning models on custom datasets and algorithmic art with Processing
  • How generative tools challenge traditional notions of artistic intent
  • Cultural implications of models trained on scraped data
  • Governance questions around who controls these systems and who profits from them
WORKSHOP CREATIVE TECH AI
Previously at: HKU (NL), iii: Instrument Inventors Initiative (NL)

Introduction to Computer-Vision Surveillance for Artists

  • How surveillance cameras see: facial recognition, object detection, gait analysis, emotion recognition
  • How these technologies are deployed in public spaces
  • Racial and gender biases embedded in training datasets
  • Governance frameworks (or lack thereof) that regulate their use
  • How artists and activists are subverting surveillance through adversarial fashion, CV dazzle, and counter-forensics
WORKSHOP SURVEILLANCE GOVERNANCE
Previously at: HKU (NL)

Deepfakes

Deepfakes: Technology, Culture & Ethics

  • How deepfakes work under the hood: GANs, diffusion models, encoder-decoder architectures
  • Face-swap technology, voice cloning, and AI-generated video
  • Cultural impact on identity, trust, and media literacy
  • The governance vacuum around non-consensual synthetic media
  • Emerging detection methods and their limitations
  • Live deepfake demonstration where participants see their own face transformed in real time
TALK WORKSHOP ETHICS AI
Previously at: IMPAKT Festival (NL), Peckham Digital (UK), University of Arts London (UK)

How to Build Your Digital Clone

  • Participants create their own digital clone combining face-swap, voice cloning, and AI-generated text
  • The technical pipeline behind digital doubles
  • Cultural shift toward synthetic personas in media and marketing
  • Ethics of consent and likeness rights
  • Governance questions around digital identity ownership
  • Participants leave with their own working clone prototype
WORKSHOP AI ETHICS IDENTITY
Related project: Do You Want To Be Me?

Necromemetics: Can Memes Dilute History?

  • How the dead become memes: from Charlie Kirk to Harambe to MLK
  • The lifecycle of posthumous memeification: stripped of context, flattened into punchlines
  • How generative AI accelerates this process with fake quotes, deepfake videos, and AI-generated "interviews"
  • What happens to collective memory when history becomes remix material
  • Cultural mechanics of meme propagation
  • Ethics of using the dead as content and the governance vacuum around posthumous digital likeness
TALK WORKSHOP ETHICS CULTURE
Previously at: HKU (NL)

Ethics & Design

Algorithmic Violence & Predictive Policing

  • How automated decision-making systems produce and reproduce structural violence
  • Predictive policing algorithms that reinforce racial profiling
  • Risk assessment tools in criminal justice, welfare, and immigration
  • Data pipelines, feedback loops, and institutional incentives that make algorithmic harm systemic
  • Drawing on my work with Future Wake (Mozilla Creative Media Award 2021)
  • EU AI Act governance, accountability gaps, and what meaningful oversight could look like
TALK ETHICS GOVERNANCE
Previously at: Cross Pollination Podcast, Het Hem (NL), Fast Company, Coda Story

AI, Accessibility & Inclusive Design

  • Drawing on my work with Screen-to-Soundscape (supported by Stimuleringsfonds, the Processing Foundation, and Constant)
  • How AI can make or break accessibility
  • Computer vision for generating rich alt-text
  • Spatial audio as a navigation tool for blind users
  • Governance questions around who gets to define "accessible"
  • Centering disabled perspectives in AI design from the start
