Cross-Disciplinary and Social Impact Grants (Worldwide)

Currently 5 active cross-disciplinary and social impact grants, fellowships and residencies open to applicants in Worldwide. Hand-curated and updated weekly. Every entry is funded, no exposure-only calls. Browse the list below, or use the interactive desk for filtering and shortlisting.

Open calls

  1. 2027 Elevate Prize

    Elevate Prize Foundation · Worldwide (US 501(c)(3) or international equivalent) · Deadline: 08 May 2026 · Award: $300,000 unrestricted ($250,000 to organisation + $50,000 to the individual)

    Nomination-based prize for nonprofit leaders driving meaningful change. Each winner receives $300,000 in unrestricted funding plus a two-year tailored programme with visibility, scaling support and a global community. Eligibility: 18+, serving as Executive Director, CEO, Founder or equivalent of a registered nonprofit (US 501(c)(3) or international equivalent) able to receive philanthropic funds. Ideally for organisations with annual budgets between $2M and $10M (others may be considered based on readiness and impact opportunity).

  2. Imagination Practice Fund: Year 4

    Collective Imagination Practice Community (Fund Circle, supported by Joseph Rowntree Foundation) · Worldwide · Deadline: 10 May 2026 · Award: Up to £3,000 per project; approximately 16 collective imagination projects funded

    Final-year practice fund supporting projects that use collective imagination to change the world by disrupting extractive or divisive narratives and/or nurturing equitable and regenerative imaginaries. Looking for work that pushes the boundaries of collective imagination practice, realises material benefits from it, creates change inside or outside the system, and contributes to the wider Collective Imagination Practice Community via the CIPC Seed Library. Two-stage application: (1) brief initial application by 23:59 BST Sunday 10 May 2026; (2) Community Shortlisting events Tuesday 12 May (10:00 to 12:00 BST) and Thursday 14 May (17:00 to 19:00 BST); (3) shortlist announced Monday 18 May; (4) full application from shortlisted applicants by 23:59 BST Sunday 7 June 2026; (5) successful projects announced Monday 22 June 2026. This year decision-making is shared with the community via the shortlisting events. Apply via the initial application form linked from the Medium post; questions to hello@imaginationpractice.info.

  3. Next Generation Foresight Practitioners (NGFP) Fellowship 2027

    Next Generation Foresight Practitioners (NGFP) · Global (online community plus regional hubs) · Deadline: 26 Jun 2026 · Award: $1,000 kick-starter grant + mentorship + global networking + opportunity to win $10,000 grand prize at year-end

    Fellowship for emerging changemakers aged 18 to 35 using futures thinking and foresight to drive social and environmental impact. Fellows receive a $1,000 kick-starter grant, mentorship from expert foresight practitioners, capability-building training, access to a 900+ strong global community, regional hubs, and the chance to win a $10,000 grand prize. Supports projects on climate transitions, democracy, emerging technologies, health and other systemic challenges.

  4. The Pollination Project: Daily Seed Grants

    The Pollination Project · Anywhere (worldwide) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Up to $500

    Daily seed grants for early-stage volunteer-driven projects with social and environmental impact. Open to grassroots changemakers worldwide: individuals, informal groups and small nonprofits. Project budget under $10,000 and organisational budget under $50,000; no paid staff. Applications reviewed monthly; submit before month-end for that month's review.

  5. The Awesome Foundation $1,000 Grant

    The Awesome Foundation (network of local chapters) · Worldwide (chapters across many cities) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: $1,000 (no strings, no equity)

    Monthly $1,000 micro-grants for awesome ideas. Decentralised network of local chapters around the world; each chapter awards one grant per month. Apply via your nearest chapter on the site. Your idea stays yours, no equity taken.
