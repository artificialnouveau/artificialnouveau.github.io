  1. Longview Philanthropy: Digital Minds Grants for Applied Work 2026

    Longview Philanthropy · Worldwide / remote (grant-eligible countries listed in application form) · Deadline: 10 Jul 2026 · Award: Typical grants $50,000–$2,000,000 per project; proposals outside this range considered when the work justifies it. May include some operational support for new organisations. No application fee.

    Longview Philanthropy invites proposals for applied work on the potential consciousness, sentience, moral status, or experiences of AI systems. Typical grants are $50,000–$2,000,000 per project, with proposals outside that range considered when justified. New organisations may also receive some operational support. ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES: seed funding for new organisations (think tanks, research bodies, initiatives); mentorship programmes for researchers entering the digital minds field; internships, new hires, or focused programmes on digital minds at existing AI organisations, academic labs, or policy institutions; convenings and field-building (workshops, conferences, summits, working groups addressing technical and governance questions). AREAS OF INTEREST: measuring AI welfare; consciousness and sentience research; governance and legal/social science frameworks for potentially sentient AI; communications work for thoughtful public discourse; research beyond the US; support organisations and operations professionals for independent technical researchers. SELECTION CRITERIA: potential impact, track record, demonstrated interest in digital minds and its relationship to AI risk, cost-effectiveness, legal compliance. Deadline: 10 July 2026. Questions: digitalminds-rfp@longview.org. Apply at https://www.longview.org/request-for-proposals-research-and-applied-work-on-digital-minds/.

  2. Longview Philanthropy: Digital Minds Research Fellowships 2026

    Longview Philanthropy · Worldwide / remote or institutional (grant-eligible countries listed in application form) · Deadline: 10 Jul 2026 · Award: $55,000–$120,000/year through a university, or $80,000–$150,000/year direct stipend; plus research budget (10% of salary), conference travel, and compute resources where applicable. No application fee.

    Longview Philanthropy Research Fellowships support scholars pursuing research on the potential consciousness, sentience, moral status, and legal position of AI systems. AMOUNT: $55,000–$120,000/year (through a university, with 10% research budget and standard fringe benefits), or $80,000–$150,000/year (direct stipend, with suggested 5–10% allocation for research and travel). Compute resources available for machine learning research. Invitation and funding to attend the annual Center for Mind, Ethics, and Policy Summit at NYU. ELIGIBILITY: must have or be expected to receive a doctoral degree in computer science, neuroscience, a related technical discipline, law, medicine, or applied social science by September 2027 or fellowship start date (exceptions for equivalent research experience); fellows arrange their own institutional affiliation; fellowship may be conditional on institutional affiliation or attendance at events. SELECTION CRITERIA: demonstrated interest in digital minds and its relation to AI risk; evidence of research promise (publications, academic achievements, references). DURATION: 1–2 years; second year conditional on ongoing programme requirements. AREAS OF INTEREST: introspection, welfare measurement, interpretability, model character, correlates of consciousness, valence research, governance frameworks, legal and political responses to potentially sentient AI. Deadline: 10 July 2026. Required application materials: CV, description of possible research directions (~1,000 words), intended outcomes, what you would do without this fellowship (~150–400 words), career goals (~300 words). Questions: digitalminds-rfp@longview.org. Apply at https://www.longview.org/request-for-proposals-research-and-applied-work-on-digital-minds/.

  3. Revitalizing Rituals 2026 Open Call

    Homo Faber Alapítvány (Homo Faber Foundation) · Bhar Lazreg, Tunisia and Somogy County, Hungary; multi-stage in-person residency across both sites. · Deadline: 19 Jul 2026 · Award: EUR 6,000 gross artist fee and production budget; international travel and per diems covered; on-site accommodation provided. No application fee.

    Revitalizing Rituals invites artists from all disciplines -- sound, movement, visual arts, installation, performance, and transdisciplinary practices -- to join a research-creation journey across two landscapes: Bhar Lazreg in Tunisia and the wetlands of Somogy County in Hungary. The project explores rituals as living forms of belonging: reconnecting communities, land, memory, food, ecological reciprocity, and the more-than-human world. Selected artists work with local communities as co-participants in a shared inquiry into moving from extraction toward reciprocity. Organised by Homo Faber Alapítvány (Hungary). AMOUNT: EUR 6,000 gross artist fee and production budget; international travel and per diems covered; on-site accommodation provided. ELIGIBILITY: minimum 5 years of active artistic practice; portfolio reflecting interest in situated or relational work; no formal degree required; working language is English (local language navigation valued); must be available for the full residency cycle including all international travel; must be able to legally receive the fee and invoice the organising partners from country of residence. Strongly encourages applications from historically marginalised or underrepresented voices. KEY DATES: Application Deadline 19 July 2026 (midnight) -- note the open call PDF also references a 5 July date; the officially promoted deadline is 19 July; Online Q&A 29 June 2026, 14:00 Tunisia / 15:00 CET (register at https://forms.gle/brnZvXvFe4JB8zL26); Online Conversations with shortlisted artists: mid July to mid August 2026; Selection Results: 31 August 2026; Placemaking Camp: 1 week, end of November 2026, Hungary; Storytellers Camp: 1 week, March 2027, Tunisia; Residency Period: minimum 1 month (splittable), April to July 2027; Final Presentation: August 2027; Closing Event: 3 days, winter/early spring 2028, Hungary. Apply via the open call form linked at https://homofaber.hu/wp-content/uploads/2026/06/Artist-Open-Call-FINAL-in-the-RR-form.pdf.

  4. Kometa Fellows 2026: Field Journalism, Photography, and Writing

    Kometa (French literary and journalism magazine) · Remote / field-based (international) · Deadline: 31 Jul 2026 · Award: €10,000 per Fellow, six Fellows selected. No application fee.

    Kometa, a French literary and journalism magazine, awards fellowships to journalists, writers, and photographers documenting reality in contexts of war, censorship, or severe social and political tensions. Projects must be rooted in direct field experience and focused on contemporary rather than historical events. Projects already under a contractual agreement are ineligible. AMOUNT: €10,000 per Fellow, six Fellows selected. WHAT FELLOWS RECEIVE: one year of funding; publication of the completed project (partial or full) in Kometa; opportunity to contribute to the monthly newsletter; mentorship through editorial collaboration. 2026 FOCUS AREAS: disinformation and narrative wars; political and social changes in the Gulf. DEADLINE: 31 July 2026. APPLY via the Tally form (https://tally.so/r/J9XxMK), in French or English (about 30 to 45 minutes). Contact: fellows@kometarevue.com.

  5. Jamverse Jam (Protocolized worldbuilding contest)

    Protocolized (Summer of Protocols) · Online / Worldwide · Deadline: 31 Jul 2026 · Award: One USD 1,000 grand prize plus at least 10 additional USD 200 prizes; winning artifacts are published on the Jamverse website. No entry fee.

    Protocolized's fourth contest invites artists and writers (storytellers, poets, comic artists) to build, extend, and connect the Jamverse, a shared near-future science fiction universe of four intersecting story cycles (The Stockton Chronicles, Legends and Ledgers, UET-Trainverse, and Zoothesia). ELIGIBILITY: open to anyone; you may submit up to three in-universe worldbuilding artifacts, and the same creator can win multiple prizes. REQUIREMENTS: each artifact must be under 1,000 words (200-700 is the sweet spot) or a visual equivalent (comic panel, poster, advertisement, etc.) and must include at least one element drawn from one of the four sub-world 'toy boxes', a surprising 'traffic jam' (an emergent, ah-ha consequence of a technology or protocol), and a relation to the theme of Stigmergy (indirect coordination through modifying a shared environment). Artifact types can include comic panels, job postings, poems, forum threads, historic documents, or encyclopedia entries. APPLY: submit each artifact as a public Google Doc via the contest form, including the artifact, a one-paragraph explanation of the worlds/toy-box components used and how the piece relates to stigmergy and explores a traffic jam, and, if AI was used, documentation of your AI/LLM protocol. PRIZES: one USD 1,000 grand prize and at least 10 USD 200 prizes, all published on the Jamverse website. DEADLINE: 31 July 2026. Contest rules, the current Jamverse stories, and starter materials are on the Jamverse website.

  6. Connections Through Culture Grants 2026 (British Council)

    British Council · International collaboration grant. Each application must involve at least one UK-based partner and at least one partner based in an eligible participating country; each partner must be legally based in the country they apply from throughout the project. · Deadline: 12 Aug 2026 · Award: Grants of between GBP 5,000 and GBP 15,000. Free to apply.

    Connections Through Culture (CTC) grants are designed to enable meaningful partnerships, cross-cultural exchange and long-term collaboration between the UK and participating countries. In 2026, CTC incorporates the grants programmes formerly known as Biennials Connect (Visual Arts) and Architecture, Design and Fashion Biennials and Festivals Grants, and serves as the British Council's major Arts grants programme for 2026 (in place of International Collaboration Grants). It offers grants of between GBP 5,000 and GBP 15,000 to support creative practitioners, cultural professionals, artists, organisations, festivals, biennials, hubs and collectives to develop and deliver collaborative projects across a wide range of themes and art forms, including applied arts, architecture, curating, dance, design, digital, drawing, education, fashion, film, installation, interdisciplinary work, painting, performance, photography, printmaking, public art, research, sculpture, social practice, sound art, textiles, video, visual arts and writing. ELIGIBILITY: applications must involve collaboration between at least one UK-based partner and at least one partner based in an eligible participating country; each partner must be legally based in the country they are applying from at the time of application and throughout the project activity period. PARTICIPATING COUNTRIES: Albania, Argentina, Australia, Bangladesh, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Colombia, Mainland China, Egypt, Indonesia, Kazakhstan, Kenya, Kosovo, Lebanon, Malaysia, Mexico, Montenegro, New Zealand, Nigeria, North Macedonia, Pakistan, Palestine, Peru, Philippines, Saudi Arabia, Serbia, South Africa, Tanzania, Thailand, Tunisia, Turkey, United Arab Emirates, UK, Ukraine, Uzbekistan, Venezuela, and Vietnam. Eligible applicants include artists and creative practitioners, arts and cultural organisations, festivals and biennials, cultural professionals, creative hubs/networks/collectives, and organisations working across arts, culture and the creative sectors. Note: applications involving projects organised by or taking place as part of Visual Arts, Architecture, Design, Fashion and Craft festivals should be made by the festival or biennial organisation rather than by individual artists, designers or practitioners. No application fee. Deadline 12 August 2026.

  8. Social Shifters Global Innovation Challenge 2026

    Social Shifters · Global; open to youth-led ventures from anywhere in the world. · Deadline: 31 Aug 2026 · Award: Multiple awards of up to USD 15,000 each (reported grants from USD 3,000 to USD 15,000), plus pitch coaching, a finalist showcase, free founder support from registration, and ongoing access to fellowships and paid work through the Shifters 100 alumni network that corporate partners recruit from. Free to enter.

    Social Shifters, a Scottish-registered charity backed by corporate partners, runs its Global Innovation Challenge as a funnel into a longer founder-development pipeline rather than a one-off prize. ELIGIBILITY: founders aged 18 to 30 at submission, leading a fully youth-led venture that is already live and producing measurable social or environmental impact, aligned to at least one Sustainable Development Goal; concept-stage ideas and student projects do not qualify. Multiple winners receive up to USD 15,000 each, plus pitch coaching, a finalist showcase, and ongoing access to fellowships and paid work through the Shifters 100 alumni network. Free coaching is available from the moment you register, so the payoff to registering early is high. Submissions close 31 August 2026 (5pm UTC), with finalists announced in October and winners in December 2026. Apply at https://www.socialshifters.co/global-innovation-challenge/.

  9. IIAS: Asia in the World Fellowship Programme 2027-2028

    International Institute for Asian Studies (IIAS), Leiden University · Two phases: a partner institution in Asia, Africa, Latin America, or Europe (Phase 1), then Leiden, Netherlands (Phase 2). Open internationally. · Deadline: 10 Sep 2026 · Award: Leiden phase: EUR 1,250 per month stipend plus EUR 1,200 per month housing allowance, plus a travel grant; the Phase 1 stipend at the co-host institution is set by that institution. No application fee.

    The Asia in the World Fellowship Programme, run by the International Institute for Asian Studies (IIAS) at Leiden University, is a nine-month, two-phase fellowship pairing a residency at a partner institution in Asia, Africa, Latin America, or Europe (Phase 1) with six months in residence at IIAS in Leiden, the Netherlands (Phase 2). The programme supports interdisciplinary, humanities- and social-science-based research engaging Asia and its connections with the wider world. SUPPORT during the Leiden phase: EUR 1,250 per month stipend, EUR 1,200 per month housing allowance, and a travel grant; the Phase 1 stipend is provided by the co-host institution. ELIGIBILITY: scholars and researchers worldwide; open internationally. APPLICATIONS open 1 July 2026; deadline 10 September 2026. Apply via the IIAS fellowships page. NOTE: dates confirmed via secondary sources at time of entry (the funder page was not directly reachable); verify before applying.

  10. Democracy Stories Lab: Open Call for Visual Storytellers

    People Powered and Democracy Narratives Alliance · Worldwide; remote. Global screening event for winners. · Deadline: 14 Sep 2026 · Award: USD $1,000 for each of 4 winners; USD $500 for each of 5 honorable mentions. Plus amplification across People Powered and Democracy Narratives Alliance networks and feature in a global screening event. No application fee.

    The Democracy Stories Lab, an initiative of People Powered (a global hub for participatory democracy) in partnership with the Democracy Narratives Alliance, invites creators worldwide to submit visual content -- short videos, illustrations, or graphics -- that tells a compelling story about democracy, voice, fairness, belonging, and community power. Content can be original or already published, as long as it is the applicant's own work and engages with narrative frameworks from the Democracy Narratives Alliance's research on 'How to Talk About Democracy.' Tone is open; serious and comedic approaches are both welcome. AWARDS: 4 winning entries receive USD $1,000 each; 5 honorable mentions receive USD $500 each; all winners receive amplification across People Powered and Democracy Narratives Alliance communications networks and are featured in a global screening event. WHO CAN APPLY: individual creators and small teams anywhere in the world; especially welcomes creators who reach audiences outside usual democracy circles, who come from communities traditionally with less of a say in public discourse, and who work in civic tech, advocacy, community organising, comedy, art, illustration, games, or lifestyle content. Applications open 14 July 2026 via the Democracy Stories Lab page at https://www.peoplepowered.org/news-content/announcing-the-democracy-stories-lab; subscribe to the People Powered newsletter to be notified when the form opens. Submission deadline: 14 September 2026. Winners announced October-November 2026. Note: the initiative was itself voted into existence through People Powered's participatory budgeting process (PP Decides).

Applications are open 15 April 2026 to 30 September 2026; results announced mid-December 2026.

  13. Emergent Ventures (Mercatus Center): Grants for Individuals

    Mercatus Center, George Mason University · Remote; projects and applicants worldwide · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Grant size is set per project and is not published (ranges from small fast grants to larger awards). No application fee.

    Emergent Ventures funds entrepreneurs and thinkers worldwide with highly scalable, zero-to-one ideas for meaningfully improving society. It is aimed at individuals (applicants must be 13 or older) rather than institutions, and international and non-US applicants are eligible; grants are awarded to people around the world. Dedicated support is available for projects focused on India, Africa, the Caribbean or Ukraine. Grant amounts are not disclosed and are set per project. Applications are accepted on a rolling, continuous basis through an online form. The programme launched in 2018 and is administered by Tyler Cowen at the Mercatus Center.

  16. Research Trip to Switzerland

    Pro Helvetia (Swiss Arts Council) · Two- to four-week research trip to one or more regions in Switzerland. Open to applicants based in Pro Helvetia's liaison-office regions (Arab region, East Asia, South America, South Asia and Vietnam, and West, Central, East and Southern Africa). · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Accommodation, travel costs, and daily allowances covered, plus a local coach (a cultural professional from the Swiss artistic field) to facilitate networking and exchange. Applicants with childcare responsibilities can request up to CHF 1,000 per month toward childcare or children's travel expenses. No application fee.

    Pro Helvetia offers a two- to four-week research trip to one or more regions in Switzerland for cultural practitioners from the regions of its liaison offices: the Arab region, East Asia, South America, South Asia and Vietnam, and West, Central, East and Southern Africa. Applicants active in any of the disciplines supported by Pro Helvetia are welcome, and applications from both individuals and artist duos are accepted. SELECTION CRITERIA: permanent residence in one of the liaison-office regions and active participation in the respective cultural scene; a comprehensive portfolio and proven track record of professional activity in the applicant's field; strong professional interest in the selected destination connected to the applicant's artistic practice and to networking with the local cultural field; a concrete research topic that can deepen exchange between the cultural scenes of Switzerland and the applicant's country of residence; and knowledge of English or one of the Swiss languages. For environmental reasons, applicants are asked to consider a travel duration of at least two weeks. WHAT IS OFFERED: accommodation, travel costs, and daily allowances, plus a coach (a cultural professional from the local artistic field) to facilitate networking and exchange; artists with childcare responsibilities can apply for up to CHF 1,000 per month toward childcare or children's travel expenses (a budget must be presented in advance). EXCLUSIONS: applicants from countries outside the Pro Helvetia liaison offices' area of responsibility may not apply, and Pro Helvetia cannot consider requests from applicants enrolled in a school, university curriculum, basic training, or education programme. APPLICATION: submit a title and brief description of the proposal; a motivation explaining the professional interest and artistic research in the selected destination; the desired length of stay and destination; and a CV and portfolio. All applications must be submitted in English. KEY DATES: submissions accepted year-round; notification of decision eight weeks after submitting; earliest project start three months after application. Pro Helvetia assumes no responsibility for the approval of visa applications. Apply via the Pro Helvetia application portal.
