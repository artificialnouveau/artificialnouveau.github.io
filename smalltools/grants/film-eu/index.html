    Mezipatra · Prague (5-12 November) and Brno (13-20 November), Czech Republic. Open internationally. · Deadline: 20 Jul 2026 · Award: Competition screening at Central Europe's main queer film festival. A small late-phase submission fee applies (free early bird ended 31 May).

  3. RIFF: Reykjavik International Film Festival 2026 Submissions

    Reykjavik International Film Festival (RIFF) · Reykjavik, Iceland (festival 24 September to 4 October 2026). Open to international entries; minimum Iceland premiere. · Deadline: 24 Jul 2026 · Award: Screening at RIFF's 23rd edition. Submission fee 7,600 ISK (approx. EUR 60).

  4. Fondazione Prada Film Fund 2026

    Fondazione Prada · Milan, Italy-based fund; open worldwide to independent producers and production companies. · Deadline: 06 Aug 2026 · Award: EUR 1.5 million total pool supporting 10 to 12 feature films. Development grants EUR 15,000 to 80,000; production grants EUR 30,000 to 250,000; post-production grants EUR 40,000 to 100,000. No application fee.

    The Fondazione Prada Film Fund is an annual EUR 1.5 million fund backing 10 to 12 feature-length films (fiction, documentary, and animation, 65 minutes or longer) across development, production, and post-production. ELIGIBILITY: independent producers and production companies with a track record (approximately two or more feature films premiered at major international festivals) and partial financing already secured; this is aimed at established independent producers, NOT individual emerging artists. DEADLINES: 6 August 2026 for projects in production; 10 September 2026 for development and post-production projects (this card uses the earliest deadline). Apply via the Fondazione Prada Film Fund page. NOTE: the funder's primary page was not directly reachable at time of entry; dates corroborated via industry trade press (Screen, Cineuropa). Verify the exact track deadline before applying.

  7. Democracy at Stake in Europe: Short Documentary Fund

    Guardian Documentaries and arte.tv · Europe. · Deadline: 14 Sep 2026 · Award: GBP 10,000 in production support for one short documentary project, plus editorial and creative input from the partners and distribution through Guardian and arte.tv platforms.

    Guardian Documentaries and arte.tv have opened a short documentary film fund on the theme 'Democracy at Stake in Europe', supporting the development and production of a character-led documentary short exploring threats, pressures and challenges facing democratic societies across Europe. The aim is to amplify personal stories that help audiences understand democracy in Europe today and to stimulate public debate; suggested areas of focus include press freedom, civic trust and democratic participation. AWARD: one project receives GBP 10,000 in production support; alongside the financial award, the selected filmmaker receives editorial and creative input from the partners, with distribution through Guardian and arte.tv platforms. The funding can be used for work linked to the film's development and production, such as research, filming and post-production, subject to the applicant's proposed budget and plan. ELIGIBILITY: open to European filmmakers aged 18 or over; the call is focused on a single short documentary and is intended for projects where individual experience and perspective are central to telling a wider story about democratic life in Europe. Deadline 14 September 2026.

  8. We Are Human Festival 2026: Call for Films (Paris)

    We Are Human Foundation · Paris, France (Forum des images screening 24 November 2026) · Deadline: 30 Sep 2026 · Award: Grand Prix EUR 5,000; Best Screenplay EUR 3,000; Ethics Award EUR 2,000. No submission fee.

    Call for short films (1-10 minutes) involving human/AI hybrid creation, from the We Are Human Foundation in Paris. Three cash awards: Grand Prix EUR 5,000, Best Screenplay EUR 3,000, and an Ethics Award EUR 2,000. ELIGIBILITY: international, applicants 18+; films must have been completed after 1 June 2025 and involve human/AI hybrid creation; a mandatory Ethics Notebook accompanies each submission. No submission fee. Festival screening at Forum des images, Paris, on 24 November 2026. Apply via FilmFreeway; deadline 30 September 2026.

  9. Basel House of Film Filmmakers Residency

    Verein Filmhaus Basel und Umgebung (Basel House of Film) · Basel, Switzerland · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Housing provided plus a stipend to cover travel and living costs; no fee to participate

    The Basel House of Film, run by the Verein Filmhaus Basel und Umgebung, offers a three month residency for emerging artists working in film and television. Residents receive housing at the Filmhaus in the Basel old town plus a stipend to cover travel and living costs. There is no obligation to produce work, and residents can use the open schedule to write a script, prepare a film, draw storyboards, or edit. The facility includes a clubhouse, meeting room, office space, a small cinema with VR equipment, and an editing and grading suite. The program targets up and coming filmmakers, and both national and international applicants are welcome. Applications are accepted on a rolling basis through the online form, followed by a CV, motivation letter, and portfolio sent to the residency email.
