Open calls

  1. CIRCLE Expanded Orbit 1st Edition

    CIRCLE Training Initiative (with Onassis ONX) · Athens, Greece · Deadline: 02 Jul 2026 · Award: No cash stipend. Provides individual mentorship from world-leading experts, group sessions, co-creation workshops, structured feedback, and access to an international professional network across film, art, design, and technology. Participation fee of EUR 2,000 per person (EUR 4,000 per team of two), with scholarships covering up to 50 percent available for those with financial need. EUR 2,000 participation fee per person (EUR 4,000 per team of two); up to 50 percent scholarships available

    CIRCLE Expanded Orbit is the first edition of an international training programme dedicated to immersive storytelling and XR-based practices, run by the CIRCLE Training Initiative in partnership with Onassis ONX. It supports women and gender-expansive filmmakers, artists, XR creators, and multidisciplinary storytellers working across XR, MR, VR, AR, AI, and game-based narratives, with no prior XR experience required. Selected individuals or teams of up to two receive individual mentorship, group sessions, co-creation workshops, and structured feedback, plus access to an international network of professionals. The in-person training takes place from November 21 to 26, 2026, in Athens, Greece. Applicants should be developing a project with a strong artistic vision and clear interest in immersive formats, ideally with a producer attached and partial budget secured. A participation fee of EUR 2,000 per person applies, with scholarships of up to 50 percent available based on financial need.

  2. CIRCLE Fiction Orbit 3rd Edition

    CIRCLE Training Initiative · Herceg Novi, Montenegro · Deadline: 10 Jul 2026 · Award: Accommodation and meals provided; in-depth script consultations, legal and production strategy guidance, career development mentorship, and psychological support. Travel not covered. Participation fee of EUR 2,000 per person applies. At least one 50 percent scholarship available annually for participants from lower-income countries, those without national funding, or with demonstrated financial need. EUR 2,000 participation fee (accommodation and meals included; travel not covered); 50 percent scholarship available for demonstrated need

    CIRCLE Fiction Orbit is a one-week retreat in Herceg Novi, Montenegro for up to six fiction feature film projects led by teams of women and gender-expansive filmmakers developing their second, third, or fourth narrative feature. Participants work with leading experts on script development, legal and production strategy, career positioning, and receive psychological support throughout the creative process. Applicants must identify as a woman or gender-expansive filmmaker, work as a screenwriter, director, or producer, have completed at least one feature, and submit a project with a producer attached. Accommodation and meals are provided, but travel is not covered, and a participation fee of EUR 2,000 per person applies. At least one 50 percent scholarship is available annually for those with demonstrated financial need. The third edition runs October 25 to November 1, 2026, with applications due July 10, 2026.

  3. We Are Human Festival 2026: Call for Films (Paris)

    We Are Human Foundation · Paris, France (Forum des images screening 24 November 2026) · Deadline: 30 Sep 2026 · Award: Grand Prix EUR 5,000; Best Screenplay EUR 3,000; Ethics Award EUR 2,000. No submission fee.

    Call for short films (1-10 minutes) involving human/AI hybrid creation, from the We Are Human Foundation in Paris. Three cash awards: Grand Prix EUR 5,000, Best Screenplay EUR 3,000, and an Ethics Award EUR 2,000. ELIGIBILITY: international, applicants 18+; films must have been completed after 1 June 2025 and involve human/AI hybrid creation; a mandatory Ethics Notebook accompanies each submission. No submission fee. Festival screening at Forum des images, Paris, on 24 November 2026. Apply via FilmFreeway; deadline 30 September 2026.

  4. Basel House of Film Filmmakers Residency

    Verein Filmhaus Basel und Umgebung (Basel House of Film) · Basel, Switzerland · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Housing provided plus a stipend to cover travel and living costs; no fee to participate

    The Basel House of Film, run by the Verein Filmhaus Basel und Umgebung, offers a three month residency for emerging artists working in film and television. Residents receive housing at the Filmhaus in the Basel old town plus a stipend to cover travel and living costs. There is no obligation to produce work, and residents can use the open schedule to write a script, prepare a film, draw storyboards, or edit. The facility includes a clubhouse, meeting room, office space, a small cinema with VR equipment, and an editing and grading suite. The program targets up and coming filmmakers, and both national and international applicants are welcome. Applications are accepted on a rolling basis through the online form, followed by a CV, motivation letter, and portfolio sent to the residency email.
