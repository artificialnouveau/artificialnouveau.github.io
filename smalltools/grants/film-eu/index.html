Film and Video Grants in the EU

Currently 11 active film and video grants, fellowships and residencies open to applicants in the EU. Hand-curated and updated weekly. Almost every entry is funded; a few notable unpaid open calls and festival submissions are included as clearly flagged exceptions. Open calls

  1. RIFF: Reykjavik International Film Festival 2026 Submissions

    Reykjavik International Film Festival (RIFF) · Reykjavik, Iceland (festival 24 September to 4 October 2026). Open to international entries; minimum Iceland premiere. · Deadline: 24 Jul 2026 · Award: Screening at RIFF's 23rd edition. Submission fee 7,600 ISK (approx. EUR 60).

    The Reykjavik International Film Festival accepts shorts (under 60 minutes) and features across all genres including documentary and hybrid work, completed after 1 January 2025, requiring at minimum an Iceland premiere. Late deadline 24 July 2026; submission fee 7,600 ISK (approx. EUR 60) via FilmFreeway.

  2. Fondazione Prada Film Fund 2026

    Fondazione Prada · Milan, Italy-based fund; open worldwide to independent producers and production companies. · Deadline: 06 Aug 2026 · Award: EUR 1.5 million annual endowment supporting up to 14 projects. Individual grant amounts are set by the Fondazione Prada selection committee (no fixed per-stage bands published). No application fee.

    The Fondazione Prada Film Fund is an annual initiative supporting independent cinema, with an annual endowment of EUR 1.5 million backing up to 14 feature film projects across development, production, and post-production. The Fund imposes NO restrictions on geography, theme, genre, or language and does not participate as a co-producer, guaranteeing complete artistic freedom to filmmakers. It supports both established authors and new talents, as well as research projects. SELECTION is based exclusively on quality, originality, vision, and the strength and reliability of the applicant structure; projects are evaluated by independent readers and external consultants, with final selection and grant amounts determined by a committee appointed by Fondazione Prada. ELIGIBILITY: applications may be submitted only by a production company involved in the project (the applicant company remains solely responsible for meeting all requirements); open worldwide. DEADLINES (2026 call opened 30 June 2026): projects in production 6 August 2026; projects in development and post-production 10 September 2026 (this card uses the earliest deadline). Post-production projects may submit a rough cut until 20 November 2026. Selected projects announced February 2027. Application documents are on the Fondazione Prada Film Fund page; questions to filmfund@fondazioneprada.org.

  3. QueerCineMad 2026 (31st edition, formerly LesGaiCineMad)

    Fundacion Triangulo · Madrid, Spain (festival November 2026). Open internationally. · Deadline: 15 Aug 2026 · Award: Screening plus prizes up to EUR 2,500 at a Goya- and Feroz-qualifying festival. Platform submission fees apply (Festhome/FilmFreeway).

    QueerCineMad (formerly LesGaiCineMad), organized by Fundacion Triangulo in Madrid, is a Goya- and Feroz-qualifying LGBTI+ film festival offering prizes up to EUR 2,500. International submissions close 15 August 2026 via Festhome or FilmFreeway (platform fees vary).

  4. Cheries-Cheris 2026: 32nd Paris International LGBTQIA+ Film Festival

    Cheries-Cheris · Paris, France (festival 14-24 November 2026, MK2 cinemas). Open internationally. · Deadline: 31 Aug 2026 · Award: Competition screening at France's largest queer film festival. Fees USD 8 shorts / USD 20 features (USD 12/25 in the late window).

    Cheries-Cheris, France's largest LGBTQIA+ film festival, screens at MK2 cinemas across Paris. Films must have an LGBTQIA+ theme or perspective. Regular deadline 31 August 2026 (USD 8 shorts / USD 20 features); late deadline 20 September 2026 at higher fees.

  5. Berlinale Talents 2027 (Berlin International Film Festival)

    Berlinale / Berlin International Film Festival · Berlin, Germany (during the Berlinale, February 2027); open internationally. · Deadline: 02 Sep 2026 · Award: Free participation in the week-long professional program (Summit workshops, Talents Lab, Talent Project Market, industry networking). NO stipend; travel and accommodation are self-covered (national film institutes often fund this; Netherlands-based filmmakers can approach the Netherlands Film Fund). EUR 15 application service fee (EUR 10 early bird before 19 August 2026).

    Berlinale Talents is the Berlin International Film Festival's talent-development program: a week of workshops, the Talents Lab and the Talent Project Market during the February 2027 Berlinale, plus access to the festival's industry network. ELIGIBILITY: international working film professionals with at least 1 feature, 2 shorts, or a widely screened or streamed series, across 15+ disciplines including directing, sound design, editing, production, cinematography and screenwriting. Participation itself is free; there is a EUR 15 application service fee (EUR 10 early bird before 19 August 2026) and travel and accommodation are not covered. Deadline 2 September 2026, 12:00 CEST. Apply via the Berlinale Talents portal.

  6. Democracy at Stake in Europe: Short Documentary Fund

    Guardian Documentaries and arte.tv · Europe. · Deadline: 14 Sep 2026 · Award: GBP 10,000 in production support for one short documentary project, plus editorial and creative input from the partners and distribution through Guardian and arte.tv platforms.

    Guardian Documentaries and arte.tv have opened a short documentary film fund on the theme 'Democracy at Stake in Europe', supporting the development and production of a character-led documentary short exploring threats, pressures and challenges facing democratic societies across Europe. The aim is to amplify personal stories that help audiences understand democracy in Europe today and to stimulate public debate; suggested areas of focus include press freedom, civic trust and democratic participation. AWARD: one project receives GBP 10,000 in production support; alongside the financial award, the selected filmmaker receives editorial and creative input from the partners, with distribution through Guardian and arte.tv platforms. The funding can be used for work linked to the film's development and production, such as research, filming and post-production, subject to the applicant's proposed budget and plan. ELIGIBILITY: open to European filmmakers aged 18 or over; the call is focused on a single short documentary and is intended for projects where individual experience and perspective are central to telling a wider story about democratic life in Europe. Deadline 14 September 2026.

  7. We Are Human Festival 2026: Call for Films (Paris)

    We Are Human Foundation · Paris, France (Forum des images screening 24 November 2026) · Deadline: 30 Sep 2026 · Award: Grand Prix EUR 5,000; Best Screenplay EUR 3,000; Ethics Award EUR 2,000. No submission fee.

    Call for short films (1-10 minutes) involving human/AI hybrid creation, from the We Are Human Foundation in Paris. Three cash awards: Grand Prix EUR 5,000, Best Screenplay EUR 3,000, and an Ethics Award EUR 2,000. ELIGIBILITY: international, applicants 18+; films must have been completed after 1 June 2025 and involve human/AI hybrid creation; a mandatory Ethics Notebook accompanies each submission. No submission fee. Festival screening at Forum des images, Paris, on 24 November 2026. Apply via FilmFreeway; deadline 30 September 2026.

  8. Berlin Senate: Arbeitsstipendien Film/Video 2027, Kuenstlerinnenfoerderung (Berlin-based women artists)

    Berlin Senate Department for Culture and Social Cohesion · Berlin, Germany. Women film and video artists based in Berlin only. · Deadline: 01 Oct 2026 · Award: Work stipend for women film and video artists; the amount for this round is not yet confirmed (sibling Senate work stipends pay EUR 2,000 per month). No application fee.

    Work stipends for women film and video artists under the Berlin Senate's Kuenstlerinnenfoerderung (women artists' funding program). ELIGIBILITY: women film and video artists based in Berlin. Deadline 1 October 2026, 11:00 CEST, per the Senate's official deadline calendar. NOTE: research-sourced from the Senate deadline calendar; the stipend amount was not published there, so confirm details on the Senate funding pages before applying.

  9. Medienboard Berlin-Brandenburg: Innovative Audiovisual Content / Immersive (2027 Round 1)

    Medienboard Berlin-Brandenburg GmbH · Berlin-Brandenburg, Germany. A Berlin-Brandenburg regional effect applies. · Deadline: 27 Oct 2026 · Award: FLAG, UNVERIFIED: amounts are not stated on the public scheme page. Financial terms, grant-versus-conditionally-repayable-loan status, own-contribution percentage and the Berlin-Brandenburg regional spend requirement are set out only in the downloadable guideline PDF (Merkblatt Innovative Audiovisuelle Inhalte). No application fee indicated. Confirm figures and whether support is a grant or a repayable loan before applying.

    Medienboard's Immersive strand funds the development and production of immersive and innovative audiovisual content: virtual, augmented and mixed-reality experiences plus multi-platform and cross-media projects, and it also supports festival presentation of XR and innovative audiovisual work. This is the most directly creative-technology-facing German regional fund open to organisations. The applicant must be a developer or independent producer with a registered company seat in Berlin-Brandenburg or elsewhere in Germany, which rules out individual artists without a company; international partners can participate as co-producers but cannot lead. THE PROCESS IS GATED: applicants submit a one-page project summary roughly six weeks before the deadline, then must hold an application meeting with a funding executive about two weeks before the deadline, and only after that meeting is portal access granted. Plan backwards from the deadline accordingly. The 2026 rounds closed on 3 February, 28 April and 21 July 2026; the next live deadline is 27 October 2026, feeding the first committee of 2027 with decisions announced in week 5 of 2027, a decision lag of about three months. The DIGI.TALE sub-strand is restricted to students of Film University Babelsberg KONRAD WOLF and DFFB.

  10. Basel House of Film Filmmakers Residency

    Verein Filmhaus Basel und Umgebung (Basel House of Film) · Basel, Switzerland · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Housing provided plus a stipend to cover travel and living costs; no fee to participate

    The Basel House of Film, run by the Verein Filmhaus Basel und Umgebung, offers a three month residency for emerging artists working in film and television. Residents receive housing at the Filmhaus in the Basel old town plus a stipend to cover travel and living costs. There is no obligation to produce work, and residents can use the open schedule to write a script, prepare a film, draw storyboards, or edit. The facility includes a clubhouse, meeting room, office space, a small cinema with VR equipment, and an editing and grading suite. The program targets up and coming filmmakers, and both national and international applicants are welcome. Applications are accepted on a rolling basis through the online form, followed by a CV, motivation letter, and portfolio sent to the residency email.

    Genres d'a cote · Brussels, Belgium (festival 29 October to 8 November 2026). Open internationally. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Screening at Brussels' queer film festival. Free via the festival's own submission form (FilmFreeway route may charge).

    Pink Screens, Brussels' queer film festival run by Genres d'a cote, accepts international films completed 2024 or later that were not previously submitted. Submissions are free via the festival's own form on pinkscreens.org (the FilmFreeway route may charge). No exact deadline is published, but the call is in its late phase, so submit soon.
