  1. Stichting Stokroos: Maker Initiatives Grant 2026

    Stichting Stokroos · Kingdom of the Netherlands (including its Caribbean parts) or Suriname; must have lived and worked there at least one year. Applicants outside the Randstad, or plans taking place there, are particularly encouraged. · Deadline: 31 Jul 2026 · Award: Fixed grant of EUR 25,000 or EUR 40,000 (chosen depending on need). Maximum participant fee EUR 2,200/month (40-hour week); third-party hire capped at EUR 85/hour excl. VAT. Co-funding not required. No application fee.

    Stichting Stokroos Maker Initiatives supports established visual artists, designers and craft practitioners (overlap possible), including duos and collectives, in developing and realising a multivocal artistic project or programme centred on a common concern, in collaboration with other makers or communities. There is one application round per year and Stokroos can support 4 to 6 proposals; a maximum of 40 eligible applications are considered per period. Eligibility: you completed your first subject-specific (vocational or higher) programme in 2020 or earlier, or have an equivalent established track record, or started without formal training (first presentation marks the start of your practice); at least 21 years old; living and working in the Kingdom of the Netherlands (including its Caribbean parts) or Suriname for at least one year; not currently enrolled in a study or post-academic programme. You must apply together with an artistic or creative partner (from professional makers to communities); duos and collectives must also have an external partner. You may submit only one application per year across all Stokroos grants, and must not have received a Seed Grant rejection in the previous calendar year. The grant is a fixed contribution of EUR 25,000 or EUR 40,000 depending on need. Plans can take place anywhere, start in the next calendar year and last a maximum of two years. Applications are submitted via the online form in Dutch or English (portfolio PDF up to 20 pages/person, optional video up to 10 minutes total, CV up to 2 pages/person, budget up to 3 pages, work/plan/collaboration descriptions up to 2,500 characters each). Stokroos aims to respond within 10 weeks; no appeal is possible. Application window: 20 to 31 July 2026. Questions: mail@stokroos.nl.

  2. TaDA Textile and Design Alliance: Artist in Residence Programme 2027 (Switzerland)

    TaDA Textile and Design Alliance · Arbon, Eastern Switzerland (Lake Constance). Open to Swiss and international practitioners. · Deadline: 12 Aug 2026 · Award: Funded residency: accommodation in Arbon a few minutes' walk from the workplace and the Bodensee, travel expenses, and a contribution to living costs, plus workspace at the Kreativzentrum ZIK (a former textile factory) and technology, knowledge and infrastructure support from 13 partner textile companies and research institutions. No application fee.

    TaDA (Textile and Design Alliance) invites artists, designers, architects, writers, performers and interdisciplinary practitioners to a three-month residency in Arbon, Eastern Switzerland, developing new work through direct collaboration with regional textile companies and research institutions. Applicants do not need to identify as textile artists; the programme welcomes practitioners from multiple disciplines who want to work with textile technology, materials and know-how. Six residents are selected annually by jury for one of two 2027 periods (April-June or September-November). Residents develop a new project in cooperation with local textile companies, present their work publicly and organise workshops. SUPPORT: accommodation, travel expenses, contribution to living costs, workspace at the Kreativzentrum ZIK, and access to the expertise and infrastructure of 13 partner companies. Applicants should identify specific TaDA partners whose expertise contributes directly to the proposal. Apply via the online form. DEADLINE: 12 August 2026, 23:59 CEST; selection results announced early November 2026.

  16. AHRC: Design Generators Round Two (2026)

    Arts and Humanities Research Council (AHRC), UK Research and Innovation · United Kingdom. Lead applicant must be employed by, or hold a formal written arrangement with, a UK research organisation eligible for AHRC funding. Project activities must take place in the UK. · Deadline: 29 Sep 2026 · Award: GBP 150,000 to 200,000 per project at 100 percent full economic cost; AHRC funds 80 percent and the host research organisation must cover the remaining 20 percent. Total fund GBP 1,000,000, approximately 5 awards. Covers research staff time, design prototyping, partner costs and public engagement activity. Subcontractors and PhD studentships are not fundable. No application fee.

    Design Generators round two funds design-led research projects combining design interventions with arts and humanities methodologies to contribute to the green transition. Eligible activity includes design research prototyping, circular business model exploration, strengthening third-sector resilience, skills development, policy and strategy work, and public engagement programmes, so exhibition-style and public-facing outputs are in scope where they sit inside a research project. Awards are GBP 150,000 to 200,000 at 100 percent full economic cost with AHRC paying 80 percent, meaning the host organisation carries a 20 percent institutional contribution. The lead must be a postdoctoral-standing researcher at an AHRC-eligible UK research organisation, and every project must pair that lead with at least one non-academic co-lead from business, the public sector, the third sector or a community organisation, which is the practical entry point for an arts organisation. Freelancers and independent consultants cannot lead. The opportunity opened 21 July 2026 and closes 29 September 2026 at 4:00pm UK time. Decision lag is roughly three months from the deadline. IMPORTANT: this round explicitly excludes international stakeholders and international activity, so non-UK partners cannot be involved.

  17. Stichting Cultuur Eindhoven: Project Subsidy (Projecten, 2027 round 1)

    Stichting Cultuur Eindhoven · Eindhoven. The applicant must be a legal entity based in Eindhoven and the activity must serve the Eindhoven cultural offering. City-restricted at the applicant level. · Deadline: 30 Sep 2026 · Award: Maximum EUR 35,000 per application and maximum 80 percent of eligible costs, so at least 20 percent co-financing is required. No application fee.

    The mid-size project strand of Stichting Cultuur Eindhoven, the arm's-length body that distributes the city's culture budget, and the main municipal route for an Eindhoven organisation to fund a festival, exhibition or public programme. Awards go up to EUR 35,000 and cover a maximum of 80 percent of eligible costs, so at least a fifth of the budget must come from elsewhere. Unlike the Snelgeld strand this route is closed to individuals: only private-law legal entities established in Eindhoven may apply. Rounds run three times a year in fixed windows; the next opens 1 September and closes at 17:00 on 30 September 2026, which is round 1 of the 2027 cycle. TIMING: the decision lag is long by municipal standards at up to 13 weeks after the submission window closes, so a September 2026 application is decided around the end of December 2026 and activity should be planned for 2027. Given Eindhoven's design and technology profile, media-art, design and creative-technology programming fits the remit particularly well. The parallel Snelgeld strand offers up to EUR 5,000 on rolling intake, reopening 1 September 2026 and running until 31 December 2026 or until the tranche budget is exhausted, whichever comes first.
