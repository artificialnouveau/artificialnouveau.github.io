Cross-Disciplinary and Social Impact Grants in the EU

Currently 10 active cross-disciplinary and social impact grants, fellowships and residencies open to applicants in the EU. Hand-curated and updated weekly. Almost every entry is funded; a few notable unpaid open calls and festival submissions are included as clearly flagged exceptions.   1. (Perma)culture Open Call 2026 (Ceske Budejovice 2028 European Capital of Culture)

    Ceske Budejovice - European Capital of Culture 2028 · Projects implemented in Ceske Budejovice / South Bohemian Region, Czech Republic · Deadline: 10 Jul 2026 · Award: Large projects: support up to CZK 4,000,000 (approx. EUR 160,000), min budget CZK 2,200,000, max 50% of total costs. Small projects: support up to CZK 1,500,000 (approx. EUR 60,000), min budget CZK 750,000, max 70% of total costs. Each project must include at least one international partner and at least one South Bohemian regional partner. Budget covers two years of preparation and implementation (2027 + 2028). No application fee.

    Second open call from Ceske Budejovice - European Capital of Culture 2028, expanding the ECoC 2028 programme. It funds cultural projects with an artistic or cultural output for the public in 2028, implemented in Ceske Budejovice or the South Bohemian Region, built on regional, national and international cooperation. The 2026 call theme is (Perma)culture as a content framework (care for the Earth, people and the future) reflected through a project's process, content and/or impact, rather than as a literal agriculture/ecology theme. All cultural and artistic fields are eligible, with extra evaluation points for: contemporary and conceptual fine art (site-specific/public-space installations, participatory and intermedia projects); audiovisual, multimedia and digital arts, immersive content, video game development and film (immersive AV installations, interactive digital projects, videomapping, VR/AR/XR, art/educational games, digital art working with data, AI or game principles); architecture; contemporary design and fashion; contemporary music and sound art; and photography. ELIGIBILITY: the 2026 call is open to legal entities (organisations) with no territorial restriction (South Bohemia, Czech Republic, EU and worldwide). Individuals cannot apply but can join project teams. APPLICATION: submit the application and mandatory attachments by email to opencall@budejovice2028.cz (subject '(Perma)culture Open Call 2026 + [Applicant Name]') and via the registration form, plus submission via data box per the call conditions. Deadline 10 July 2026.

  2. Faber Andorra Residency 2027

    Faberllull · La Massana, Andorra · Deadline: 13 Jul 2026 · Award: EUR 60 daily meal allowance plus fully equipped apartment (free) and 30 sqm visual/performing arts workshop. Travel and medical insurance NOT covered. No application fee.

    Individual interdisciplinary residency in La Massana, Andorra, in a natural mountain setting. ELIGIBILITY: open worldwide to any professional from the arts, sciences and humanities with a project requiring focused working time; visual and audiovisual arts, crafts, photography, dramaturgy, performing arts, cinema, history, curating, criticism, philosophy, sociology, anthropology, journalism, science, research, biology, ecology, economics, law, writing and translation are all welcome. WHAT IS FUNDED: fully equipped apartment with work area, optional 30 sqm visual and performing arts workshop, and a daily allowance of EUR 60 to cover meals. Travel costs to/from Andorra and mandatory medical insurance are NOT covered. DURATION: 2 to 6 weeks; stays scheduled between February and November 2027. RESIDENCY ACTIVITIES: residents must propose at least two activities to carry out during the stay (one aimed at school and/or university students); at least one activity must be executed. SELECTION: priority to projects related to Andorra and to activities most attractive to the Andorran community; diversity of backgrounds and areas of work valued; previous Faberllull Olot/Faber Andorra residents may reapply but new candidates are preferred. APPLY: fill out the Faberllull form with a 1-page cover letter and a 2-page proposal of two possible activities; documentation accepted in Catalan, Spanish, English or French. TIMELINE: deadline 13 July 2026.

  3. RAMDAM, un centre d'art: Extended Possibilities Residency 2027

    RAMDAM, un centre d'art · Sainte-Foy-les-Lyon (near Lyon), France; open to artists legally resident in Culture Moves Europe eligible countries (EU member states, outermost regions, overseas countries and territories, and some non-EU countries); artists from mainland (hexagonal) France cannot apply · Deadline: 14 Jul 2026 · Award: EUR 1,350 per resident allowance for subsistence and other costs, plus travel allowance of EUR 400 (under 5,000 km) or EUR 800 (from 5,000 km). Top-ups: green mobility EUR 400 per resident (600 km or more by non-plane transport), visa EUR 120, outermost regions/OCT EUR 175, family EUR 200 per child under 18. On-site accommodation and technical facilities provided. Supported by Culture Moves Europe.

    Extended Possibilities gives artists the opportunity to work on site at RAMDAM, un centre d'art for 45 days, a long period allowing them to benefit from technical support and to present ongoing work to the public. Artists receive on-site accommodation (a collective room for the whole 45 days), access to a well-maintained, up-to-date technical facility, a grant to support their work, and a lump-sum allowance for travel and visas. RAMDAM is an activist space supporting artists in their artistic research; residencies are open to artists or teams engaged in creation, research, or experimentation, with no obligation to produce a finished work: dance, theatre, performance, painting, sculpture, drawing, film, video, literary writing, screenwriting, digital art, and hybrid forms are all welcome. The call focuses on multidisciplinary projects and research that break down barriers between disciplines and challenge their own field of practice, and considerable attention is given to how the work fits into a social and/or political context and resonates with our times. THE CALL SUPPORTS: 1 or 2 artistic teams (RAMDAM can host at most 5 artists); performing artists of all disciplines; projects at early stages of research-creation and/or more advanced (residencies can be writing residencies and/or stage residencies). All artists must be available for the full 45 days from 1 February to 15 March 2027 and must be from eligible countries (EU countries, outermost regions, overseas countries and territories, and some non-EU countries); artists from mainland (hexagonal) France cannot apply. The working and living spaces are wheelchair accessible, though reaching RAMDAM by public transport can be difficult for disabled people; accessibility support is available via Culture Moves Europe for residents with disabilities. ALLOWANCES: EUR 1,350 per resident for subsistence; travel allowance EUR 400 under 5,000 km or EUR 800 from 5,000 km (travel under 600 km must be by green means of transport unless disability or island residence requires otherwise); green mobility top-up EUR 400 per resident for non-plane travel over 600 km; visa top-up EUR 120; outermost regions/OCT top-up EUR 175; family top-up EUR 200 per child under 18 (housing capacity is limited). TO APPLY: applications in English or French; download the residency project and administrative form documents and email the complete application to contact@ramdamcda.org with subject line 'extended possibilities - 2027' (no WeTransfer, no important information in the email body; reduce file sizes; a third document may be added to help understand the work). Confirmation within 7 working days; shortlisted applicants have video calls with the residency committee in mid-August. Deadline: Tuesday 14 July 2026, 11:59 p.m. NOTE: the call is arbitrarily limited to the first 40 applications received (still open as of 1 July 2026), so apply early. Made possible by the European programme Culture Moves Europe.

  4. Reflection (All Disciplines)

    Pro Helvetia (Swiss Arts Council) · Collectives of at least two cultural practitioners; for international collaborations at least 50 percent must be from Switzerland. · Deadline: 01 Sep 2026 · Award: Maximum CHF 25,000 (cannot exceed 50 percent of total budget). No application fee.

    Reflection is a Pro Helvetia grant supporting collective mediation projects that use innovative formats to foster critical reflection on artistic practice and cultural transformation, across all supported disciplines (design, literature, music, performing arts, visual arts, and innovation and society). Open to collectives comprising at least two cultural practitioners (artists, creatives, cultural practitioners, or art educators); solo projects are excluded. ELIGIBILITY: requires a connection to Switzerland (Swiss citizenship, or permanent residency with regular artistic activity in Switzerland); not open to practitioners without a Swiss connection. For international collaborations, at least 50 percent of practitioners must be from Switzerland. AMOUNT: maximum CHF 25,000, and no more than 50 percent of the total budget. Deadlines twice a year: 1 March (23:59 CET) and 1 September (23:59 CEST). Apply via the Pro Helvetia portal.

  5. National Gallery of Iceland: CUSP Artist Residency (Reykjavik 2027)

    National Gallery of Iceland (Listasafn Islands), CUSP project · Reykjavik, Iceland. Residency hosted by the National Gallery of Iceland. · Deadline: 03 Sep 2026 · Award: Accommodation and working space for the residency; travel and subsistence stipend; an artist fee for collaborating in the open-lab/masterclass; and, depending on the artist's request and museum means, hardware and software for the working space. No application fee.

    The National Gallery of Iceland announces an artist residency open call under its CUSP program, a European cooperation project funded through Creative Europe's Culture grant. Artists working in complex media or emerging technologies (e.g. generative AI, custom sensors, sound, software-based, blockchain, physical and digital hybrid) are invited to apply. This is the first of four residency stays offered to different artists through the program, one in each of the participating countries (Iceland, Norway, Czechia, and Ukraine), followed by two micro residencies in the Netherlands and Iceland. The programme brings together artists working in time-based media with museum professionals, conservators, and technology specialists developing preservation and conservation methodologies to ensure the long-term endurance of artworks. The selected artist will meet with a preservation specialist or conservator specializing in time-based media art for an in-depth artist interview addressing the preservation needs of their medium, and will present their body of work and working methods publicly during an open-lab masterclass designed in collaboration with the museum. RESIDENCY: up to four weeks in early 2027 in Reykjavik, exact dates arranged with the selected artist. WHAT IS PROVIDED: accommodation and working space; a travel and subsistence stipend; an artist fee for the open lab/masterclass; and possibly hardware and software depending on the artist's request and museum means. ELIGIBILITY: international artists based outside Iceland; applicants must be residents of any of the 27 EU member states or of non-EU countries with association agreements (EFTA/EEA members, acceding countries, candidate countries, and European Neighbourhood Policy countries). APPLICATION: complete the application form with full name, country of residence, gender identity, contact information, an artistic statement (max 2500 characters), a motivation letter (max 2500 characters), a draft idea of the public workshop or masterclass (max 1000 characters), links to social media/professional websites, and a CV and portfolio upload (max 10MB each). DEADLINE: 3 September 2026 at 23:59 GMT. ABOUT CUSP: Creative Understanding, Saving and Preserving Time-Based Media Art is a transnational cooperation project co-funded by the EU's Creative Europe Culture grant, led by the National Gallery of Iceland with Nasjonalmuseet (Norway), LI-MA (Netherlands), Vasulka Kitchen (Czechia), and Mystetskyi Art Arsenal (Ukraine), focused on preserving and presenting time-based media art. Inquiries: cusp.residency.is [at] gmail.com.

  6. Híbrides: Small Embodied Data - Call for Contributions (2nd Edition)

    Axolot.cat collective and DARTS research group, Universitat Oberta de Catalunya (UOC) · Barcelona, Spain; UOC Poblenou campus and Convent de Sant Agustí. In-person attendance required. · Deadline: 07 Sep 2026 · Award: Oral presentations: €100 honorarium (invoice required). Performances: €400 honorarium (invoice required). No submission fee.

    Híbrides is a biennial of artistic research co-organised by the Axolot.cat collective and the DARTS (Design, Art, Technology and Society) research group at the UOC in Barcelona. The second edition, "Small Embodied Data," centres on the concept of classification -- approached through machine learning, data science, algorithmic systems, and questions of who designs classifiers, under what ethical codes, and to what end. The biennial brings classification into dialogue with its previous themes of Small Data (community-defined, intentional data collection and analysis) and Embodiment (bodily experience as a foundation of situated, sensory knowledge). Both academic and non-academic contributions are welcome. FORMATS ACCEPTED: Oral Presentations (20 minutes; artistic practice, artistic research, or academic research projects; may be video-recorded for a public archive and developed into short papers); Performances (20-30 minutes; must engage with the physical body, stage, and technology; complete works, including previously presented work; takes place in a multipurpose space with technical support). SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS: online form with abstract (max 1,000 characters), professional biography (max 1,000 characters), and link to representative materials (3-5 images; video and technical rider required for performances). HONORARIA: oral presentations €100; performances €400; invoice required in both cases. Participants must follow the event's code of conduct. Event runs 26-28 November 2026 at UOC Poblenou campus and Convent de Sant Agustí, Barcelona. Deadline: 7 September 2026. Submit at https://hibrides.axolot.cat/opencall.html.

  7. Canon Foundation in Europe Research Fellowships 2026 (Europe–Japan Exchange)

    Canon Foundation in Europe · Europe or Japan (bilateral exchange; fellows arrange own host institution) · Deadline: 15 Sep 2026 · Award: Up to EUR 30,000 per year (pro-rated for shorter stays). No application fee.

    Canon Foundation in Europe Research Fellowships support bilateral research exchange between Europe and Japan, awarding up to 15 fellowships annually. AMOUNT: up to EUR 30,000 per year (pro-rated for shorter stays), flexible — covers living costs, travel, insurance, research expenses, and books; fellows determine their own allocation. ELIGIBILITY: European nationals (including UK, Israel, Switzerland, Turkey, Balkan and Baltic countries) conducting research in Japan; or Japanese nationals conducting research in Europe. Applicants must have completed at least a Master's or PhD degree within the last 10 years (older qualifications may apply with supporting documentation). No restrictions on research field. Priority given to first-time applicants to Europe/Japan. Fellows arrange their own host institution and advisor. Up to 15 fellowships awarded annually. Deadline: 15 September 2026, 23:59 CET (for fellowships starting January-December 2027). Apply at https://portal.canonfoundation.org/. Contact: canon.foundation@canon-europe.com.

  9. Ettijahat: Zad Mobility for Arab Artists in Europe (16 October 2026 Cut-Off)

    Ettijahat - Independent Culture · Mobility within Europe (current country of residence and across Europe) for artists from the Arab region now residing in Europe · Deadline: 16 Oct 2026 · Award: Financial contribution covering: travel and accommodation (transportation, internal transport, daily expenses, travel insurance including special-needs/health-related costs); travel costs for children and partners plus childcare costs if parents cannot travel without them; transportation and shipment of artworks and production materials; translation of artworks into multiple languages and simultaneous translation during live audience meetings. No application fee.

    Ettijahat's Zad programme is a support framework promoting mobility and communication for artists from the Arab region residing in Europe, designed to develop their artistic and professional path in collaboration with peers and across artistic spaces and platforms, while helping them reach diverse audiences and contribute to public life in their cities/countries of residence. SUPPORTED ACTIVITIES (examples): presenting theatrical works and live artistic performances; concerts at music festivals; streaming films in cities/places where they have not previously been shown; organising or joining literary readings; organising and relocating art exhibitions; sharing the outcomes of artist residencies. DISCIPLINES: all artistic and literary specialisations; open to artists of all ages. ELIGIBILITY: individual or group artists from the Arab region regardless of ethnic background who have relocated to Europe since 2015. PRIORITY for artists who moved from Syria, Palestine, Lebanon, Iraq, Jordan, Egypt, Yemen, Sudan, and Libya. The programme encourages environmental and economic solutions when planning travel. DEADLINE: this card is for the 16 October 2026 cut-off (other cut-offs each have their own card). APPLY via the Ettijahat Zad page.

  10. EFFEA Call #5: European Festivals Fund for Emerging Artists

    European Festivals Association (EFA) · Across eligible European countries; residencies built around partner festivals registered on FestivalFinder.eu · Deadline: 03 Nov 2026 · Award: Per-residency grants: EUR 8,000 (Discovery format) or EUR 15,000 (Springboard format)

    The European Festivals Fund for Emerging Artists (EFFEA), an initiative of the European Festivals Association co-funded by the European Union, will open its fifth call from 1 September to 3 November 2026, with full guidelines and application details published on 1 September 2026. Call #5 will again support around 50 cross-border co-production residencies taking place in 2027, each bringing together at least three festivals from different eligible countries to accompany an emerging artist or collective. EFFEA is a co-production residency scheme: festivals join forces across borders to support an emerging artist through a process of creation and presentation, providing dedicated time, space, and tailored mentoring, exchange, and collaboration, moving beyond a single invitation toward a shared artistic development journey. Each supported project is built around one leading festival working with at least two partner festivals based in different eligible countries; one emerging artist or collective at the centre; a combination of artistic development, mentoring, exchange, and audience engagement based on the artist's needs; and presentations or outcomes shared across the partner festivals. Two residency formats are offered. DISCOVERY (EUR 8,000 grant): the proposal must include a workshop (live or online) hosted by the leading festival with at least one programmer from each partner festival, and a work-in-progress presentation at the leading festival. SPRINGBOARD (EUR 15,000 grant): the proposal must include final presentations or production results shown within each festival (minimum the leading festival plus two partners) and a live masterclass led by the artist(s) at each festival (minimum the leading festival plus two partners). WHO CAN APPLY: festivals that have existed for at least one edition and are registered on FestivalFinder.eu; the festivals apply on behalf of the residency project. Residency projects must fall within these artistic fields: architecture, ballet, contemporary dance, circus, design/applied arts, digital arts, film, folklore and folk arts, heritage, interdisciplinary, literature, music, opera, street art, theatre, photography/video, puppetry, sculpture, painting. A series of EFFEA info, networking, and Q&A sessions runs on Tuesdays 11:00-12:00 CEST (15 September, 29 September, 13 October, and 20 October 2026) to help applicants navigate the process and find partners. Across Calls #1 to #4, EFFEA has supported 619 festivals and 200 artists/collectives across Europe and beyond.
