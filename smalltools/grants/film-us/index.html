Film and Video Grants in the US

Currently 9 active film and video grants, fellowships and residencies open to applicants in the US. Hand-curated and updated weekly. Almost every entry is funded; a few notable unpaid open calls and festival submissions are included as clearly flagged exceptions. Open calls

  1. Film Independent: Screenwriting Lab 2027

    Film Independent · Los Angeles, US · Deadline: 31 Aug 2026 · Award: Lab fellowship + access to bundled $10K fellowships (Cayton-Goldrich, MPAC, Sony Music Vision) and Climate Entertainment Development Grant ($25K for climate-focused fiction features)

    Competitive screenwriting lab for emerging feature screenwriters. Selected fellows are eligible for the Climate Entertainment Development Grant ($25K, climate-focused fiction features) and the same bundled $10K fellowship pool available across Artist Development programs (Cayton-Goldrich, MPAC Hollywood Bureau, Sony Music Vision). International fellows are also eligible for the Dolby Institute Fellowship ($50K post-production grant utilising Dolby Vision and Atmos). Lab alums become eligible to apply to the Amplifier Fellowship for Black filmmakers ($30K unrestricted plus year-long support; six fellows annually). Applications open 29 June 2026; non-member deadline 31 August 2026; Film Independent member extension to 14 September 2026.

  2. 100 Voices of Florida Film Grant

    100 Voices of Florida · Florida, United States · Deadline: 20 Sep 2026 · Award: Up to USD 5,000 (unrestricted) USD 25 application fee with a Florida connection, USD 35 without

    100 Voices of Florida is a grant for filmmakers and screenwriters that awards up to USD 5,000 in unrestricted funds, which winners can use for finishing funds, festival submission fees, cast and crew reimbursements, production, or anything else. Applicants submit either a short film (under twenty minutes) or a script (twenty pages or less); films in other languages must include accurate English subtitles, and scripts must be in English. The grant is open to writers, directors, and producers who are at least 18 years old, and creative teams may submit, though only one entrant receives the award. A panel of judges with professional filmmaking experience evaluates entries, and the winner also receives a meeting with an industry professional. The deadline for the current cycle is September 20, 2026.

  3. Film Independent: Episodic Directing Intensive 2027

    Film Independent · Los Angeles, US · Deadline: 28 Sep 2026 · Award: Lab fellowship + access to bundled $10K fellowships (Cayton-Goldrich, MPAC, Sony Music Vision) for selected fellows

    Intensive program for emerging episodic (TV/series) directors, with mentorship, set shadowing opportunities and industry access. Selected fellows are eligible for the same bundled $10K fellowship pool (Cayton-Goldrich, MPAC Hollywood Bureau, Sony Music Vision) available across Artist Development programs. Applications open 27 July 2026; non-member deadline 28 September 2026; Film Independent member extension to 12 October 2026.

  4. Pandora Short Film Grant 2026

    Pandora Short Film Grant · United States (early-career US filmmakers) · Deadline: 01 Oct 2026 · Award: $12,000 production grant to the winner, plus a Panavision camera package (worth about $15,000), an optional $1,000 Kodak Film Grant toward film stock and lab expenses, mentorship from an award-winning filmmaker, and fiscal sponsorship. Three finalists receive mentorship, fiscal sponsorship, consideration for the Panavision New Filmmaker Program, and $500 off Athos production insurance. ENTRY FEE: tiered, $30 regular ($27 Gold members) and $40 late ($36); Pandora states fee waivers are available on request.

    The Pandora Short Film Grant is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit that awards an annual $12,000 grant to an early-career US filmmaker for the production of a narrative short film. Projects at script stage or already filmed and seeking finishing funds are both eligible. The winner also receives a Panavision camera package (about $15,000), an optional $1,000 Kodak Film Grant, mentorship from an award-winning filmmaker (2026 mentors include Alma Har'el, Lila Neugebauer, Lauren Wolkstein, Sarah Elizabeth Mintz and Chris Merola), and fiscal sponsorship; three finalists receive mentorship, fiscal sponsorship, Panavision New Filmmaker Program consideration and discounted production insurance. ELIGIBILITY: open to all early-career US filmmakers; applicants are asked NOT to apply if they have directed a feature film with a budget over $250K or directed or produced television. Selection is blind, based on script and pitch materials, weighting artistic vision over industry resume. TIMELINE: submissions open 30 May 2026, close 1 October 2026, notifications 15 November 2026.

  5. NRDC Climate Storytelling Fellowship 2027 (via The Black List)

    Natural Resources Defense Council (NRDC) in partnership with The Black List · United States (verify international eligibility on Black List program page) · Deadline: 04 Dec 2026 · Award: $20,000 to each of three writers ($60,000 total) to support revision of a feature screenplay or pilot.

    Annual screenwriting fellowship from the Natural Resources Defense Council (NRDC) administered through The Black List, awarding $20,000 each to three writers to revise a feature screenplay or pilot that engages with climate change in a compelling way. Submission and selection happen on The Black List platform; applicants should review program eligibility and any associated hosting/submission fees on blcklst.com before applying. Strong fit for narrative writers using fiction to dramatise climate, ecology, energy, or environmental-justice themes (rather than documentary).

  6. Film Independent: Documentary Story Lab 2027

    Film Independent · Los Angeles, USA (week-long spring lab); international projects eligible if English-subtitled · Deadline: 14 Dec 2026 · Award: No cash grant or published stipend. The award is a week-long lab providing a dedicated editing advisor, mentorship and industry access. FEES: free for Filmmaker Pro Members (one waived fee per year), USD 45 for Film Independent Members, USD 65 for non-members. Accepted participants must join Film Independent at the USD 105 annual rate.

    One of Film Independent's two nonfiction Artist Development labs, running as a week-long spring intensive in Los Angeles for six documentary directors or directing teams. Unlike the Documentary Producing Lab, which serves producers, the Story Lab is aimed at directors whose films have finished or nearly finished shooting and are entering the edit. Each fellow is paired with an editing advisor to interrogate and restructure their story, and the lab supplements that one-on-one work with sessions featuring established documentary directors, funders, commissioning editors and distributors. There is no cash award attached to the lab itself, though Film Independent fellows across programmes become eligible for the organisation's separately administered fellowship pool. ELIGIBILITY: documentary directors or directing teams only. Projects must be in late production or early post-production; films still in research, or with a locked picture edit, are explicitly excluded. International projects are welcome provided materials are English-subtitled. Six projects are selected. TIMING: applications open 5 October 2026. Non-member deadline 14 December 2026; Film Independent member extended deadline 4 January 2027. FEES: free for Filmmaker Pro Members, USD 45 for members, USD 65 for non-members, plus a mandatory USD 105 membership on acceptance.

  7. CFA Institute x IMGN Grant 2026

    CFA Institute (in partnership with IMGN) · United States (50 states + DC; applicants must be 18+) · Deadline: 31 Dec 2026 · Award: $4,500 to one filmmaker. If the project is already completed before funds are disbursed, the $4,500 is paid as a reimbursement upon submission of a final cut.

    One-off film grant awarding a single filmmaker $4,500 to support an independent narrative project in 2026. Application is free to submit. Centerpiece of the application is a production book built on the IMGN platform: script breakdown, schedule, coverage, and pre-visualization for the project (tutorials are provided inside the application). Eligibility: US-based filmmakers in the 50 states plus DC, aged 18 and older. If the project is already completed before funds are disbursed, the grant is paid as a reimbursement upon submission of a final cut of the film. Winner announced and funds disbursed by end of January 2027.

  8. Panavision New Filmmaker Program: Camera Equipment Grant

    Panavision International L.P. · United States and Canada. For US awards the project must be recorded and produced entirely in the US; Canada-domiciled applicants may use the award in the US or Canada. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: In-kind only, no cash. Use of a Panavision film or digital camera package including lenses and accessories at little to no cost, subject to availability; Panavision does not publish a dollar value. REAL COSTS APPLY: a USD 1,000 refundable security deposit before the rental, plus a service fee whose amount is determined per project, Panavision-compliant production insurance delivered at least a week before prep, shipping and expendables. No application fee.

    Panavision's long-running in-kind equipment grant, giving new filmmakers in the US and Canada use of Panavision film or digital camera packages at little to no cost, subject to availability. Applicants download a region-specific submission agreement (United States or Canada, both revised January 2026), sign it, and submit through the online equipment grant form; questions go to NFP@panavision.com. WHAT IS EXCLUDED: anamorphic lenses, large-format lenses, and large-format cameras other than the Panavision DXL2. The grant is personal and non-transferable, cannot be redeemed for cash or credit, and cannot be combined with other offers or discounts. COSTS TO BUDGET FOR: 'little to no cost' is not the same as free. Recipients pay a USD 1,000 refundable security deposit, a project-dependent service fee that Panavision leaves open-ended, production insurance meeting Panavision's requirements delivered at least one week before prep, shipping, expendables, and any taxes on the award. ELIGIBILITY: applicants must be at least 18. Recipients must provide an SSN or TIN for tax purposes and sign a promotion agreement granting Panavision a perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free licence to behind-the-scenes footage and photos for marketing. DEADLINE: none published on the official programme page, the application page or either submission agreement, so the programme runs on a rolling basis. Widely circulated third-party claims of a February 1 deadline, a 'apply two months before your finishing date' rule, or a restriction to student thesis films and PSAs do not appear on Panavision's own materials. Past supported films include Requiem for a Dream, Napoleon Dynamite, Difret, and Palm Trees and Power Lines. Panavision also offers free presentations, equipment demos and facility tours in Los Angeles, New York, Chicago and Atlanta.

  9. Field of Vision: Documentary Commissions (Rolling)

    Field of Vision · United States and international; filmmakers based anywhere. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Commissioning fees and production funding (many films are commissioned assignments); specific amounts are not published publicly. No submission fee.

    Field of Vision is a filmmaker-driven documentary unit that creates and commissions original short-form and feature nonfiction visual journalism. It funds and commissions filmmakers, often as assignments, and supports projects in development and production (it does not acquire finished films). SUPPORT: commissioning fees and production funding; specific amounts are not published publicly. ELIGIBILITY: filmmakers of all experience levels, within and outside the United States. APPLICATIONS are accepted on a rolling basis, with no fixed funding cycles or deadlines. Submit via the Field of Vision submissions page.
