  8. ARIA: Future Proofing Our Climate and Weather (Opportunity Seeds) 2026

    ARIA (Advanced Research and Invention Agency) · UK-based funder (ARIA); proposals welcome across scientific, engineering and social science research. · Deadline: 31 Jul 2026 · Award: Opportunity seeds of up to GBP 500,000 for early-stage research. No application fee.

    ARIA is seeking proposals for opportunity seeds, funding of up to GBP 500,000 for early-stage research that explores new pathways for climate adaptation and resilience. Decarbonisation is the only sustainable route out of the climate crisis, but environmental changes are outpacing current mitigation efforts, and if an abrupt alteration in a climate system were to unfold we would have no tools to mitigate the effects; scientific, engineering and social science research could provide practical and responsible intervention and adaptation options. WHAT THEY ARE LOOKING FOR: ideas ranging from early-stage, curiosity-driven research through to advanced translational science. Specific areas of interest include, but are not limited to: carbon dioxide removal (novel concepts to accelerate the drawdown of atmospheric carbon); extreme weather forecasting (breakthroughs in predicting extreme weather events to better prepare vulnerable systems); and new ideas related to solar geoengineering, strictly excluding proposals specifically designed to investigate altering the Earth's surface temperature by affecting planetary albedo, incoming solar radiation, or the atmosphere's emissivity (the core focus of the main Exploring Climate Cooling, ECC, programme). OUT OF SCOPE: ideas that squarely fit within the ECC programme; emissions reduction projects and improvements to the energy efficiency of buildings or vehicles; and commercial products or research likely to be funded by traditional venture capital or grants. CONSTRAINTS: any proposal for outdoor experiments must demonstrate conformity to ARIA's governance framework for outdoor experiments, and all successful applicants must sign ARIA's Intellectual Property Pledge to ensure findings are accessible for the public good.     Design Generators round two funds design-led research projects combining design interventions with arts and humanities methodologies to contribute to the green transition. Eligible activity includes design research prototyping, circular business model exploration, strengthening third-sector resilience, skills development, policy and strategy work, and public engagement programmes, so exhibition-style and public-facing outputs are in scope where they sit inside a research project. Awards are GBP 150,000 to 200,000 at 100 percent full economic cost with AHRC paying 80 percent, meaning the host organisation carries a 20 percent institutional contribution. The lead must be a postdoctoral-standing researcher at an AHRC-eligible UK research organisation, and every project must pair that lead with at least one non-academic co-lead from business, the public sector, the third sector or a community organisation, which is the practical entry point for an arts organisation. Freelancers and independent consultants cannot lead. The opportunity opened 21 July 2026 and closes 29 September 2026 at 4:00pm UK time. Decision lag is roughly three months from the deadline. IMPORTANT: this round explicitly excludes international stakeholders and international activity, so non-UK partners cannot be involved.

  22. Jerwood Foundation: Arts Grants (2027 round)

    Jerwood Foundation · United Kingdom. Applicants must be UK registered charities or UK limited companies. · Deadline: 17 Mar 2027 · Award: No published minimum or maximum. Previous awards have reached up to GBP 200,000, but most grants are considerably smaller. Funds project and activity costs. No formal match-funding requirement, but a fundraising strategy must be submitted. No application fee.

    The Jerwood Foundation makes grants to UK organisations for public-facing arts activity across theatre, music, dance, visual arts and literature, including artist development programmes, commissions, residencies and projects involving the Jerwood Collection. Awards have historically gone as high as GBP 200,000, though the Foundation states most grants are much smaller. Applications are single-stage: a written proposal of no more than 10 sides of A4 excluding financial statements, sent by email to info@jerwood.org or by post, and it must include a summary sheet giving location, amount requested, timeline and audience figures. Applications missing that summary sheet are rejected automatically. The March 2026 deadline has passed; the next round opens 3 February 2027 at 9am and closes 17 March 2027 at 2pm, with decisions on 28 April 2027 and all applicants notified by 11 May 2027. TIMING RULE: activity funded in that round must start or open to the public between 1 July 2027 and 30 June 2028, so there is a long lead time and retrospective funding is impossible. Grants to individuals are not made, so artists must be supported through a host organisation. Core costs and capital work are explicitly out of scope, which makes this a good fit for discrete festivals, exhibitions and public programmes.

  23. Creative Scotland: National Lottery Open Fund for Organisations (rolling)

    Creative Scotland · Activity must take place in Scotland and benefit people in Scotland. Applicant organisations must be constituted and based in Scotland; organisations elsewhere in the UK with a UK-wide remit may apply for activity in Scotland; organisations outside the UK must have a UK-based partner lead the application. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: GBP 1,000 to 100,000. Covers a one-off project or production, creative research and development, a longer-term programme of work, or business and organisational development. No stated match-funding requirement and no application fee. Applicants must hold a UK bank account in the same name as the application.

    The National Lottery Open Fund for Organisations is Creative Scotland's main open-access grant for constituted arts, creative and cultural organisations, and it is genuinely rolling with no deadlines. It supports a specific one-off project or production, creative research and development, or a longer-term programme of work, and can also fund business sustainability and organisational development costs. Eligible organisations can apply for GBP 1,000 to 100,000 for projects lasting up to 18 months. TIMING RULES: decision lag is banded by request size, with GBP 1,000 to 20,000 decided within 10 weeks and GBP 20,000 to 100,000 within 14 weeks. The start date must be after the decision date, and the guidance recommends allowing a further 2 to 4 weeks for funding agreements and first payment. Critically for festivals, applicants are barred from starting activity, including advertising events or issuing calls for collaborators, before funding is confirmed, so a public programme should be planned to start roughly 12 to 18 weeks after submission. Only one application may be in assessment at any time. Non-UK organisations can be involved but cannot lead. Current Regularly Funded and Multi-Year Funded Organisations are excluded.

  24. Esmee Fairbairn Foundation: Community-led Art and Creativity (rolling)

    Esmee Fairbairn Foundation · United Kingdom. Work must directly benefit communities in the UK. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: GBP 30,000 minimum, no maximum. Funds core costs or project costs including staff salaries and overheads; unrestricted funding available to charities. No co-financing requirement stated. No application fee.

    Community-led Art and Creativity sits within Esmee Fairbairn's Creative, Confident Communities aim and is one of the Foundation's two arts-related priorities. It funds work where local people and local artists lead the creative and cultural activity in a place, emphasising cross-sector collaboration that delivers social, environmental and economic benefit alongside artistic outcomes. Cited examples include the Migration Museum's People's Panel, B Arts in Stoke and WeCanMake's Festival of Making Together, which confirms festivals and public programmes are in scope where genuinely community-directed. Applications are rolling with no deadlines, starting with an eligibility quiz and an expression of interest. The minimum grant is GBP 30,000 with no upper limit, and most awards run three to five years covering core or project costs including salaries and overheads. Applicants need at least GBP 100,000 annual turnover in the latest accounts and a formal constitution, charitable or non-charitable so long as the work is charitable. This is highly competitive: in 2025 only around 5 percent of initial expressions of interest converted into funded grants, so long-term systems-change framing matters more than the individual event. Esmee's other arts priority, Arts and Creativity Making Change, is currently closed.

  25. Paul Hamlyn Foundation: Arts-based Learning Fund (rolling)

    Paul Hamlyn Foundation · United Kingdom. Activity must take place with UK state-funded schools, SEND specialist schools, FE colleges or alternative provision settings. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: GBP 30,000 to 300,000 total, capped at GBP 100,000 per year. Covers project costs and associated core and overhead costs of arts-education partnership work. No stated co-financing requirement. No application fee.

    The Arts-based Learning Fund supports sustained partnerships between arts and cultural organisations and state-funded educational settings so that arts-based approaches become embedded in how young people learn. Grants run GBP 30,000 to 300,000 over one to four years, with a ceiling of GBP 100,000 in any single year, and can cover both project delivery and a fair share of the applicant's core costs. Applicants must be arts and cultural organisations (charities, community organisations, social enterprises, not-for-profits) applying in partnership with at least one state-funded educational setting. Applications are accepted on a rolling basis with no deadlines; an optional enquiry call with a grants manager is encouraged before applying. The process is two-stage: an initial online application, then, if shortlisted, further information plus a phone or video conversation, with a final decision by a grants panel. TIMING RULE: applicants should expect a response within about four months, and successful applicants should allow roughly six months from application to receiving funds, so projects should not be planned to start sooner than that. The Foundation explicitly encourages applications from organisations led by people most impacted by racism, ableism, classism, sexism, xenophobia, homophobia and transphobia. IMPORTANT: this is the only Paul Hamlyn arts route open to new applicants in 2026, since the main Arts Fund is suspended to new applications until at least April 2027.

  26. BFI National Lottery Impact Feature Funding (Higher-Budget Features)

    British Film Institute (BFI Filmmaking Fund, National Lottery) · United Kingdom (applications must come from a limited company registered and centrally managed in the UK) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Up to GBP 1,250,000, the largest single award in the current BFI portfolio, though the BFI notes it does not expect to support many projects at the maximum level. Production budgets from GBP 1M to GBP 15M for experienced directors, GBP 3.5M to GBP 15M for debut directors. No application fee.

    Impact is the higher-budget production strand of the BFI Filmmaking Fund and carries the largest award available in the current BFI portfolio at up to GBP 1.25 million. It sits above the Discovery strand in scale, serving experienced directors with production budgets from GBP 1 million to GBP 15 million, and debut directors whose budgets exceed GBP 3.5 million. UNLIKE DISCOVERY, Impact accepts applications on a continuous rolling basis with no round structure and no published closing date, though applicants must submit at least 20 weeks before principal photography begins. SELECTION is explicitly criteria-driven: projects must address at least three of the BFI's stated aims, covering equity and inclusion, audience or cultural impact, talent development, creative risk, production activity across the UK nations and regions, and environmental sustainability. ELIGIBILITY: applications must come from a limited company registered and centrally managed in the UK, so individuals cannot apply. A dedicated producer is always required, though unlike Discovery the writer and director may be the same person. Directing credits must be evidenced. No explicit nationality or residency test for the director or writer is published. NOTE: the BFI cautions that maximum-level awards are rare, so realistic requests will typically fall well below the GBP 1.25 million ceiling.

  27. BFI National Lottery Development Funding (Feature Script and Package Development)

    British Film Institute (BFI Filmmaking Fund, National Lottery) · United Kingdom (producers must apply through a limited company registered at Companies House) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Award amounts are not published on the fund page. No application fee.

    Development Funding is the earliest-stage strand of the BFI Filmmaking Fund, supporting original live action and animated feature films through script and package development to the point where they are viable candidates for production finance. It is aimed at distinctive fiction projects from what the BFI calls 'adventurous storytellers', across a wide range of genres rather than a single house style. UNLIKE DISCOVERY, it does not operate in rounds: applications are accepted year-round, with the only fixed interruption being an annual closure through the month of April, plus occasional short pauses during periods of high demand or holidays. ELIGIBILITY: the application must be made by a producer through a limited company registered at Companies House, so unincorporated individuals are ineligible. Nationality and residency requirements are not published on the fund page. NOTE: award amounts are not published, so the ceiling should be treated as unknown rather than assumed. Successful applicants are required to publicly acknowledge National Lottery support in project credits and materials.

  28. BFI National Lottery Audience Projects Fund

    British Film Institute (BFI National Lottery) · United Kingdom (legally constituted organisations centrally managed in the UK, not more than 50% foreign-owned) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Pilot projects GBP 10,000 to GBP 20,000. Standard projects GBP 20,000 to GBP 500,000 per year. Immersive projects may include up to GBP 150,000 of production costs within the GBP 500,000 cap. Total fund GBP 19.7M over three years. No application fee.

    The Audience Projects Fund distributes GBP 19.7 million of National Lottery money over three years to widen UK audiences for film and immersive work, covering exhibition, distribution, festivals, touring programmes and sector-support initiatives. It reopened in November 2025 and runs continuously with no closing date, which makes it one of the more accessible large BFI pots for organisations working on the audience-facing rather than production side. AWARDS are tiered: small pilot projects receive GBP 10,000 to GBP 20,000, while full projects can receive GBP 20,000 to GBP 500,000 per year, with immersive projects permitted to include up to GBP 150,000 of production cost inside that envelope. ELIGIBILITY is strictly institutional. Applicants must be legally constituted organisations centrally managed in the UK and not more than 50% foreign-owned. Eligible types include limited companies (not classed as 'large' under the Companies Act 2006), community interest companies, LLPs, UK local or statutory authorities, and registered UK charities or trusts. Individual practitioners cannot apply. Applicants must evidence experience in marketing, festivals, distribution, immersive installations, cultural programming, touring or sector support. TIMING: there are no deadlines but lead times are enforced. Single-film distribution projects must apply at least 10 weeks before release; all other projects at least 16 weeks before the project starts.

  29. BFI National Lottery Screen Heritage Fund: Individual Skills Funding

    British Film Institute (BFI National Lottery Screen Heritage Fund) · United Kingdom (applicants must hold a UK bank account and be eligible to work in the UK) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Award amounts are not published on the fund page. No application fee.

    The rare BFI stream that individuals can apply to directly. Individual Skills funding supports training and professional development for people working, or intending to work, in the UK screen heritage sector, the field concerned with preserving, digitising and widening public access to screen archive collections. ELIGIBILITY: applicants must be 18 or over, working or intending to work in the UK screen heritage sector, hold a UK bank account, and be eligible to work in the UK. TIMING: applications are rolling with no deadline, but must be submitted at least eight weeks before the training starts. All funded activity must be completed by 31 March 2029, the end of the current National Lottery funding plan period. CONTEXT: the wider Screen Heritage Fund has three strands. Project funding, open to legally constituted not-for-profit organisations operating a screen archive or collection network, runs year-round with a 16-week lead time and prioritises collections that better reflect the diversity of the UK. Resilience funding for the 2026-29 cycle closed on 31 March 2026 and is not currently accepting applications. NOTE: award ranges are not published on the fund overview page.

  30. Creative Scotland Open Fund for Individuals

    Creative Scotland · Scotland, United Kingdom. Applicants must be living in Scotland and hold a UK bank account in their own name. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Between GBP 500 and GBP 50,000. Overall annual budget for the fund is GBP 5 million.

    The Open Fund for Individuals is one of Creative Scotland's key funding programmes, supporting the wide range of activity initiated by artists, writers, producers and other creative practitioners in Scotland. The overall annual budget for this fund is GBP 5 million. WHAT IT SUPPORTS: periods of research, development and/or delivery of creative activity, such as specific projects and productions, for up to 24 months; it can support an individual's time where this is related to specific creative outcomes. Applicants are asked to give a start and end date for the activity and to describe the outcomes, benefits and impacts they wish to achieve. ELIGIBILITY: freelance and self-employed artists and creative practitioners living in Scotland who are at least 18 years old; applicants must have a UK bank account in their own name. Those who are not self-employed individuals or sole traders may instead be eligible for the National Lottery Open Fund for Organisations. FUNDING AMOUNTS: between GBP 500 and GBP 50,000. TIMESCALES: requests between GBP 500 and GBP 20,000 receive an outcome in about 10 weeks; requests between GBP 20,001 and GBP 50,000 in about 14 weeks. This is a rolling open fund with no fixed deadline. Download the guidance for applicants and apply via the online portal.

    Abbey Road Red (Abbey Road Studios / Universal Music Group) · London, UK. Global applicants; selected startups are based in London during the 6-month programme. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: In-kind incubator: access to Abbey Road and UMG studios, experts, facilities and the global label and publisher network, plus mentorship and some finance.     Abbey Road Red is the music-technology incubator run by Abbey Road Studios (part of Universal Music Group). It is primarily an in-kind and mentorship programme rather than a cash grant: selected early-stage music-tech startups (building tools to create, capture or connect music) get access to Abbey Road and UMG studios, experts and facilities, and the global label and publisher network, plus mentorship and some finance, over a 6-month programme. In exchange, Universal Music Group takes a 2 percent equity stake plus a participation right in future financing rounds. Open to startups worldwide; participants are based in London during the programme. Applications are rolling via the open form on the Abbey Road Red site. No application fee. Note: this is an equity-based incubator, not a grant.
