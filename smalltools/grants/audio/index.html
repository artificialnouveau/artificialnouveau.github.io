  6. Statens kunstnerstipend: Norwegian State Artist Stipends 2026

    Statens kunstnerstipend, administered by Kulturdirektoratet (Arts and Culture Norway) · Norway. For professionally active artists (yrkesaktive kunstnarar) across all artist groups. Applications are assessed by 19 discipline-specific stipend committees, which submit recommendations to a five-member selection board; a sound or interdisciplinary artist should check which committee fits their practice before applying, as committee choice materially affects the reading. · Deadline: 01 Sep 2026 · Award: NOK.     Norway's flagship state artist stipend and the largest individual-artist commitment in the Nordic region, with 1,069 artists funded in the most recent round. It is unrestricted support that buys working time rather than funding a defined deliverable, which suits research-heavy practices where the output is a system, an instrument or a performance language rather than a release. All artist groups are covered, including music and interdisciplinary practice. Note the deadline change: the application form opens 1 August 2026 at 13:00, and the funder has moved the closing date forward to 1 September, stating on its own site that 'Soknadsfristen for Statens kunstnerstipend flyttes frem til 1. september. Applications go through a formal application form on the Kulturdirektoratet site.

    Kulturdirektoratet / Arts and Culture Norway (Kulturradet) · Norway. Open to private individuals as well as private and public institutions, organisations and other businesses.   14. Koda Kultur: Koncertproduktion og Formidling (Autumn 2026)

    Koda Kultur, with Dansk Komponistforening · Denmark. Open to music professionals working in art music genres. Koda membership is not stated as a requirement on this scheme page.   16. Statens Kunstfond: Bestillingsopgaver Musik, Commission Fees (Autumn 2026)

    Statens Kunstfond / Danish Arts Foundation, Projektstotteudvalget for Musik · Denmark. No explicit nationality or residency restriction in the guidelines, but submission requires MitID authentication via Tilskudsportalen, which in practice means a Danish CPR or CVR number is the real eligibility gate. · Deadline: 01 Oct 2026 · Award: DKK, no fixed ceiling stated; awards vary by project and available funds.     Funds the composition fee for a new work when a creative artist has already received a commission from a third party.   17. GEMA-Stiftung: Projektfoerderung, Project Funding (October 2026 Deadline)

    GEMA-Stiftung · Germany. No nationality or residency requirement is published, but the foundation states that 'Eine Voraussetzung fuer Foerdermassnahmen ist in der Regel die Mitgliedschaft der beteiligten Musikautoren und -verleger in der GEMA', so as a rule the participating composers and publishers need GEMA membership.     The GEMA Foundation funds projects that stage works by several living music authors, which fits a curated multi-artist night, a contemporary or electronic music concert series or a festival programme far better than a solo project. Two deadlines run each year, 15 May and 15 October. Applications go through Koda's online system. Two deadlines each year, 1 May and 1 November, both 23:59. Run with SPOR festival, Dansk Komponistforening and Koda Kultur, funded by Koda Kultur and Region Midtjylland's Culture Development Pool.

  21. Konstnarsnamnden: Arbets- och Langtidsstipendium, Musik (2026/27)

    Konstnarsnamnden / Swedish Arts Grants Committee · Sweden. Resident in Sweden or practising your art predominantly in Sweden; professionally active and earning wholly or partly from artistic work; not enrolled in studies over 50 percent; no debts referred to Kronofogden; no bankruptcy. · Deadline: 09 Dec 2026 · Award: SEK, amount set by the committee and published when the application window opens in November 2026. Work stipends (arbetsstipendium) run one to several years; long-term stipends (langtidsstipendium) run considerably longer. No application fee, no co-financing requirement.

    Konstnarsnamnden's core unrestricted working stipend for music. It buys time rather than funding a defined deliverable, which makes it well suited to research-heavy practice where the output is a system, an instrument or a body of experimental work rather than a release or a concert. This is the Swedish functional equivalent of the Danish Arbejdslegater and the Norwegian Statens kunstnerstipend, and an artist resident in Sweden should treat it as the primary annual target, with the committee's international travel grant as a supplement. The application window is 9 November to 9 December 2026, with decisions announced in March 2027, so budget roughly a three-month decision lag. Applications go through the committee's e-service and must arrive by 14:00 on the final day. Note that the specific amount and the detailed music-discipline criteria are not published in advance; the individual scheme page goes live when the window opens, so confirm figures in early November 2026.

  22. Statens Kunstfond: Arbejdslegater for Musik (2027 Round)

    Statens Kunstfond / Danish Arts Foundation, Projektstotteudvalget for Musik · Denmark. Open to creative artists in music: composers, songwriters, sound artists, arrangers and producers. Applications are filed through Tilskudsportalen using MitID, which in practice requires a Danish CPR or CVR number, so Danish registration is the real gate rather than a stated nationality rule. · Deadline: 04 Feb 2027 · Award: DKK. The committee sets the amount; applicants do not request a sum. Both one-year and up to three-year stipends are awarded. No application fee, no co-financing requirement. Past award levels are published at kunst.dk/tildelinger.

    Denmark's principal work stipend for creative artists in music, across all genres and explicitly including sound artists and producers as well as notated composers, which puts electronic and computer-based practice inside the scheme on its face. The committee assesses 'kunstnerisk intention' (originality and a clear artistic vision) and 'handvaerk' (command of one's musical tools), and states it values music that is challenging, thought-provoking, innovative or artistically relevant, so the application should lead with the conceptual proposition rather than with output volume. Applications go through Tilskudsportalen with MitID; mobile submission is not supported, desktop only. Required attachments are the completed application template as PDF, one to two MP3 audio examples, and optionally up to ten pages of supplementary documentation. Groups may apply but each member must submit individually and document their own share of the creative process. Decision within 12 weeks of the deadline, notified by email and in Tilskudsportalen. Closes 14:00 Danish time.

  23. Abbey Road Red: Music-Tech Incubator (rolling, London)

    Abbey Road Red (Abbey Road Studios / Universal Music Group) · London, UK. Global applicants; selected startups are based in London during the 6-month programme. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: In-kind incubator: access to Abbey Road and UMG studios, experts, facilities and the global label and publisher network, plus mentorship and some finance. Universal Music Group takes a 2 percent equity stake plus a participation right in future financing rounds. This is NOT a grant. No application fee.

    Abbey Road Red is the music-technology incubator run by Abbey Road Studios (part of Universal Music Group). It is primarily an in-kind and mentorship programme rather than a cash grant: selected early-stage music-tech startups (building tools to create, capture or connect music) get access to Abbey Road and UMG studios, experts and facilities, and the global label and publisher network, plus mentorship and some finance, over a 6-month programme. In exchange, Universal Music Group takes a 2 percent equity stake plus a participation right in future financing rounds. Open to startups worldwide; participants are based in London during the programme. Applications are rolling via the open form on the Abbey Road Red site. No application fee. Note: this is an equity-based incubator, not a grant.

  24. Yamaha Music Innovations Fund (rolling, equity investment)

    Yamaha Music Innovations, LLC · Silicon Valley, USA. Global reach; no stated geographic restriction. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: USD 50 million venture fund making early-stage equity investments. This is an equity INVESTMENT, not a grant. No application fee.

    The Yamaha Music Innovations Fund is a USD 50 million venture fund (Yamaha Music Innovations Fund I, LP, managed by Yamaha Music Innovations, LLC) that makes early-stage equity investments in startups across four themes: creative tools and infrastructure; innovative and interactive media; community and collaboration; and empowerment and accessibility (including AI for music and video production, instruments and audio gear, and creator streaming and monetisation). It is an equity investment vehicle, not a grant. Based in Silicon Valley with global reach and no stated geographic restriction. Startups pitch on a rolling basis via the fund's Pitch Us form. No application fee.
