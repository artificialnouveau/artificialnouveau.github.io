Open calls

  1. A.R.M. Holding / Art Dubai Projects: H Residence Sculpture Park Commission (Dubai)

    A.R.M. Holding / HUNA / Art Dubai Projects · H Residence Sculpture Park, Dubai, UAE. Open to UAE-based, regional and international artists. · Deadline: 25 Jul 2026 · Award: AED 1,000,000 commission envelope, inclusive of artist fee, design development, fabrication, materials, production, transport, installation, lighting and finishing. Proposal fees are available for shortlisted artists. No submission fee.

    A.R.M. Holding and HUNA, with the commission supported and managed by Art Dubai Projects, invite artists to propose a permanent public artwork for the H Residence Sculpture Park in Dubai, the anchor piece of the park's programme. Artists respond to the theme 'We Emerge Stronger than Before' through a permanent, site-specific outdoor sculpture; the strongest proposals will find what the phrase opens up (continuity, endurance, renewal, collective presence, the long view) through material, form, scale and the conditions of the site. Where language or text is incorporated, Arabic should lead. TECHNICAL: footprint minimum around 15 sqm, up to 3 m high; weight restriction 5.5 kN/m2; must be suitable for long-term outdoor display in Dubai (summer over 40 C, humidity to 90 percent, about six hours direct sun daily); presence by day and night. ELIGIBILITY: open to artists with or without gallery representation; UAE-based, regional and international artists welcome; prior experience with permanent or large-scale outdoor/public installation required. BUDGET: AED 1,000,000 all-inclusive; shortlisted artists receive full budget parameters and proposal fees at the proposal development stage. SUBMISSION: CV and portfolio (attention to outdoor, large-scale or permanent commissions), concept statement (max 300 words), a render or visualisation in context plus at least one sketch, technical specifications, and a site response. TIMELINE: submissions close 25 July 2026; shortlist notified from 10 August; commission awarded after September proposal development; work unveiled at H Residence on 2 December 2026. Local fabrication strongly preferred. Questions: Shahad@artdubai.ae.

  2. India Foundation for the Arts: Arts Research Programme (May 2026 Request for Proposals)

    India Foundation for the Arts (IFA) · India; project-based with no residency requirement. Open to Indian nationals only. · Deadline: 10 Aug 2026 · Award: Up to INR 500,000 (around USD 5,700 / EUR 4,900) per project. Honorarium is capped at 35 percent of the budget. Institutional overhead, building costs, infrastructure and equipment purchases are excluded. No application fee.

    IFA's Arts Research Programme funds research into forms of knowing and thinking rooted in creative, scholarly or cultural practice. It explicitly welcomes experimental and self-reflexive methodologies, practice-based inquiry, interdisciplinary dialogue, community engagement, translation studies and work on marginalised narratives, with a shareable public outcome expected. Open to artists, scholars, curators, translators, educators and practitioner-researchers. ELIGIBILITY IS RESTRICTED: Indian nationals only, and PIO and OCI card holders are explicitly excluded; any collaborators must also be Indian nationals. Applicants need a PAN card and valid ID. PROCESS NOTE: this is a two-stage email application, not a web form. Applicants were expected to discuss the project concept with the Programme Officer (harshita@indiaifa.org) by 11 July 2026, with the final proposal, CV and budget breakdown emailed by 10 August 2026 at 23:59 IST, in an email no larger than 25 MB. Proposals may be written in any Indian language, including English.

  3. Hong Kong Baptist University: International Writers' Workshop 2027 (Writers-in-Residence)

    International Writers' Workshop (IWW), Hong Kong Baptist University (HKBU) · HKBU campus, Hong Kong · Deadline: 14 Aug 2026 · Award: Round-trip economy class airfare; accommodation on HKBU's campus for 4 weeks; a per diem. No application fee.

    International Writers' Workshop (IWW) at Hong Kong Baptist University (HKBU), founded in 2004, has hosted over 150 writers from more than 60 countries. The 2027 Writers-in-Residence Programme runs 26 February to 25 March 2027 and culminates in the IWW Literary Festival on the theme 'Inheritance'. Writers are encouraged to share a piece of writing in conversation with the festival theme. ELIGIBILITY: have at least one published book; currently reside outside of Hong Kong; have a functional command of English or Chinese (Mandarin or Cantonese). WHAT IWW PROVIDES: round-trip economy class airfare; accommodation on HKBU's campus for 4 weeks; a per diem. DEADLINE: 14 August 2026. APPLY via the IWW HKBU call for application (PDF).

  4. Inlaks Shivdasani Foundation: Inlaks Fine Art Award 2027

    Inlaks Shivdasani Foundation · India. Open to Indian citizens currently living in India. · Deadline: 15 Aug 2026 · Award: INR 300,000 (around USD 3,400 / EUR 2,950) for one year, plus a fully covered four-week art residency at an Indian art institution. No application fee.

    A one-year award enabling an emerging Indian visual artist to pursue independent practice, with a stipulation to attend a recognised art residency programme in India for four weeks during the year. ELIGIBILITY IS RESTRICTED: Indian citizens currently living in India, born on or after 1 January 1996, who are either recent graduates, postgraduates or final-year students of a leading Indian visual arts institute, or artists without those formal credentials but with a record of exhibition participation. First-year MA students are not eligible. Awardees submit progress reports at six months and on completion, plus documentation of the residency. Applications close 15 August 2026 at 23:59 IST via Submittable. Shortlisting is expected in late October 2026 with interviews in early November.

  5. Inlaks Shivdasani Foundation: Inlaks Theatre Art Award 2027

    Inlaks Shivdasani Foundation · India. Open to Indian citizens. · Deadline: 15 Aug 2026 · Award: INR 15,000 per month for up to 12 months, around INR 180,000 total (roughly USD 2,050 / EUR 1,780). No application fee.

    A skill-enhancement award for emerging Indian theatre practitioners, supporting participation in recognised training workshops, residencies and similar programmes rather than the production of a specific work. ELIGIBILITY IS RESTRICTED: Indian citizens born after 1 January 1991, with preference given to holders of a theatre degree from a leading Indian institution. Recipients report at the six-month mark and on completion. Applications close 15 August 2026 at 23:59 IST via Submittable. The foundation runs several other awards on adjacent deadlines, including the Inlaks-Attakkalari Dance Studentship (30 August 2026) and a two-month Inlaks Residency at the International Center of Photography in the USA for emerging Indian photographic practitioners (31 August 2026).

  6. transmediale x ARKO Residency 2027

    transmediale (in cooperation with Arts Council Korea / ARKO) · Berlin, Germany (two-month residency around the transmediale festival); applicants must be of Korean nationality (based in Korea or elsewhere) · Deadline: 28 Aug 2026 · Award: Two-month residency with an artist fee of EUR 1,500 per month, additional production support, plus travel and accommodation support for the festival and residency. For duos or collectives the fee remains the same and is shared. No application fee.

    Third edition of transmediale's residency in collaboration with Arts Council Korea (ARKO), awarded to one person of Korean nationality (based in Korea or elsewhere) working in digital art and moving image practices, design, sound and media theory. Open to artists, researchers, practitioners, designers and sound artists across all formats. The two-month residency comes with an artist fee of EUR 1,500 per month, additional production support, and travel and accommodation support for the festival and residency; duos and collectives are welcome but share the same fee. Part of transmediale's 2027 programme (40th anniversary, themed 'RFC 40'), which seeks proposals offering alternatives to how we understand, respond to and shape our technological conditions, moving from critique to construction outside extractive logics. Deadline: 28 August 2026, 23:59 CEST.

  7. POM Fukuoka 2027: Politics of the Machines, Call for Abstracts

    POM (Politics of the Machines) Conference, hosted at Kyushu University Ohashi Campus · Fukuoka, Japan (Kyushu University Ohashi Campus, in person) · Deadline: 30 Aug 2026 · Award: Unpaid, and self-funded. POM explicitly cannot provide financial support for travel, accommodation, registration or other participation costs, and authors of accepted abstracts must register for the conference in order to present. No abstract submission fee.

    Call for abstracts for the POM (Politics of the Machines) conference in Fukuoka, hosted on Kyushu University's Ohashi Campus, organised around the theme of transduction. TRACKS: 01 The Landscape of Transduction: Infrastructural Entanglements of Energy, Data, and More-than-Human Life; 02 Parasitic Transductions Beyond the Human; 03 Real Machines: Computation, Subjectivity, and the Politics of Reality-Production; 04 Technogenetic Transductions across Fields of Experience: Affective Politics in/for Times of Crisis; 05 Transducing Disgust: Bodies, Technologies, and the Synthetic Uncanny; 06 Methoding Transduction: Sound, Data, Sonic Research; 07 Metabolic Worldviews and Hybrid Intelligences; 08 Fermentative Systems; 09 Unhearing Bodies: Life as Transduction; and 10, an open track for Speculations and Culturing Immature Ideas. FORMATS: academic papers, lecture-performances, artistic interventions and workshops, with proposals for alternative formats also encouraged; indicate the intended format in the submission. FACILITIES: beyond the Multi Purpose Building and Design Library, the campus offers a BioFoodLab (a Biolab and a Foodlab, each holding roughly 20 people) and an Acoustic Research Center (recording studio for up to 50, anechoic chamber for up to 15, echo chamber / reverberation room for up to 10, and semi-anechoic chamber for up to 20), which the organisers flag as relevant to tracks engaging sound, food, perception, signal and technological mediation. SUBMIT: an abstract of up to 500 words under one of the tracks; submissions without a track will be assigned to the most appropriate one. All submissions go through double-blind peer review, and authors of accepted abstracts are invited to present. The submission platform is described as opening soon. AFTER: accepted authors are invited to submit a full paper of up to 4,000 words including references, with a formal call for papers to follow. COSTS: note that this is a self-funded opportunity. Presenting requires paid conference registration, and POM cannot cover travel, accommodation, registration or other participation expenses. DEADLINE: 30 August 2026.

  8. Hayama Artist Residency

    Hayama Artist Residency · Hayama, Kanagawa Prefecture, Japan · Deadline: 30 Sep 2026 · Award: Fully funded: roundtrip airfare to Tokyo (Narita or Haneda), shared accommodation for 4 weeks, and USD 800 for meals and local transportation. Group exhibition at KOKI ARTS gallery in Tokyo. USD 95 application fee for first-time applicants

    The Hayama Artist Residency introduces visual artists from around the world to Japanese culture and offers them their first gallery exhibition in Japan. The four-week residency takes place in Hayama, a coastal town in Kanagawa Prefecture about 1.5 hours from Tokyo. Selected artists receive roundtrip airfare to Tokyo, shared accommodation with one other resident, and USD 800 for meals and local transportation. A core element is a group exhibition during the stay, hosted in partnership with KOKI ARTS, a contemporary gallery in the Bakurocho art district of Tokyo. The program is open to visual artists over 21 working in any medium, based anywhere in the world, and applications are reviewed by the director and an international selection committee. The June 2027 cycle has an application deadline of September 30, 2026, with notification by December 15, 2026.

  9. A4 Residency Art Center: International Residency Programme 2028 (Chengdu)

    A4 Residency Art Center (A4 Art Museum) · Chengdu, China · Deadline: 31 Dec 2026 · Award: Studio and accommodation provided; round-trip economy airfare or second-class train ticket within a set budget; production grant of RMB 10,000 per person/team for creative production and material costs (excluding living expenses). No application fee.

    Multidisciplinary international residency at the A4 Residency Art Center in Chengdu, China, a global exchange platform integrating creation, networking and resources, dedicated to fostering cross-cultural creative collisions and innovation through residency programmes while promoting deep integration of creative culture with commerce and communities. Residents initiate one to two participatory local activities to facilitate communication with the city and community; A4 also organises group activities, intimate sharing and exhibition opportunities to foster understanding among creators and connect them with local resources. AVAILABLE DATES: Spring 2028 (April - June) or Autumn 2028 (July - October). ELIGIBILITY: international applicants from any field; A4 welcomes applications from multidisciplinary artists (visual, performing, music, film, sound, architecture), curators, designers (product, fashion, spatial, interactive), and interdisciplinary creatives. PROGRAMME SUPPORT: studio and accommodation; round-trip economy airfare or 2nd-class train within a set budget; RMB 10,000 production grant per person/team (excluding living expenses). DEADLINE: 31 December 2026 (2028 cycles). APPLY via the A4 Residency Art Center latest-recruitment page.
