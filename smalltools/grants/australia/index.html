Grants, Fellowships and Residencies in Australia

Almost every entry is funded; a few notable unpaid open calls and festival submissions are included as clearly flagged exceptions. Open calls

  1. Create NSW: Stories Matter NSW Literary Fellowships 2026

    Create NSW (administered by Writing NSW) · New South Wales, Australia; applicants must be NSW-based. · Deadline: 27 Jul 2026 · Award: AUD 50,000 per fellowship, 10 fellowships awarded (4 emerging, 3 mid-career, 3 established). No application fee.

    Stories Matter is a new NSW literary fellowship program from Create NSW, administered by Writing NSW, awarding ten fellowships of AUD 50,000 each to support NSW-based writers to focus on a major new work over a 12-month period. The 2026 round funds four emerging, three mid-career, and three established writers working across prose fiction, poetry, illustration, graphic novels, and playwriting. ELIGIBILITY: writers must be based in New South Wales; open to writers at any career stage within the three tiers (not open to applicants outside NSW). No application fee. DEADLINE: 27 July 2026, 2:00pm AEST. Apply via the Create NSW grants portal.

  2. The Wheeler Centre: The Next Chapter 2027

    The Wheeler Centre · Melbourne, Australia; includes residency at Varuna, The National Writers' House, and an end-of-year publishing intensive at The Wheeler Centre. · Deadline: 31 Jul 2026 · Award: AUD $15,000 stipend per fellow. No application fee.

    The Next Chapter is The Wheeler Centre's flagship writing program, designed to elevate Australian stories that are underrepresented and to nurture a new wave of writers to tell them. Each fellow receives a AUD $15,000 stipend and is paired with a handpicked mentor for a twelve-month period to progress a major writing project. Fellows also receive a residency at Varuna, The National Writers' House; an end-of-year publishing intensive at The Wheeler Centre; and meaningful opportunities to connect with peers, agents, publishers, and readers. One fellowship place is dedicated specifically to a poet: The Malcolm Robertson Fellowship. 2027 judges: Melissa Lucashenko, Micaela Sahhar, Cher Tan, and Melanie Saward. ELIGIBILITY: Australian writers. No specific genre restrictions stated; the program focuses on stories that are not currently being told and voices that are underrepresented. TIMELINE: submissions open 2 June 2026; submissions close 31 July 2026 (5pm); 2027 Next Chapter Fellows announced 10 November 2026. Questions: thenextchapter@wheelercentre.com. Apply at https://www.wheelercentre.com/the-next-chapter.

  3. Melbourne Queer Film Festival 2026 Submissions

    Melbourne Queer Film Festival (MQFF) · Melbourne, Australia (festival 12-22 November 2026). Open internationally. · Deadline: 03 Aug 2026 · Award: Screening at Australia's largest queer film festival. Fees USD 30 shorts / USD 50 features (waivers for Australian and First Nations entrants only).

    MQFF, Australia's largest queer film festival, accepts international films of interest to LGBTQIA+ audiences. Deadline 3 August 2026; fees USD 30 for shorts and USD 50 for features, with fee waivers reserved for Australian and First Nations entrants.

  4. Copyright Agency Cultural Fund: Fellowship for Non-Fiction Writing 2026

    Copyright Agency (Cultural Fund) · Australia; Australian authors only. · Deadline: 17 Aug 2026 · Award: AUD 80,000 (one fellowship). No application fee.

    The Copyright Agency Cultural Fund's Fellowship for Non-Fiction Writing awards AUD 80,000 to one mid-career or established Australian author to research and write a substantial new work of literary non-fiction. Eligible forms include memoir, biography, history, criticism, arts and science writing, and environmental and political writing. ELIGIBILITY: mid-career or established Australian authors with a demonstrated publication record and publisher interest in the proposed work; Australian residents only. APPLICATIONS open 20 April 2026 and close 17 August 2026, 1:00pm AEST. Apply via the Cultural Fund fellowship page.

  5. Copyright Agency Cultural Fund: Author Fellowship 2026

    Copyright Agency (Cultural Fund) · Australia; Australian authors only. · Deadline: 17 Aug 2026 · Award: AUD 80,000 (one fellowship). No application fee.

    The Copyright Agency Cultural Fund's Author Fellowship awards AUD 80,000 to one established Australian author to create a significant new work for publication. ELIGIBILITY: established Australian authors with critical acclaim and confirmed publisher interest in the proposed work; Australian residents only. This is a separate fellowship from the Cultural Fund's Fellowship for Non-Fiction Writing, though administered on the same cycle. APPLICATIONS close 17 August 2026, 1:00pm AEST. Apply via the Cultural Fund fellowship page.

  6. Creative Australia: Arts Projects for Organisations (September 2026 round)

    Creative Australia · Activity may take place nationally within Australia, internationally, or online. · Deadline: 01 Sep 2026 · Award: AUD 20,000 to 100,000. No matching or co-financing requirement. No application fee. Organisational administrative costs should generally not exceed 10 percent of the total project budget or the application becomes less competitive.

    Creative Australia's principal open-round project grant for arts organisations and the most directly relevant Australian scheme for mounting festivals, exhibitions and public programmes. The guidelines explicitly list festivals, productions, exhibitions, performances, touring, publishing, recording, collaborations, research and community engagement as supported activity. Organisations may propose either a single project or an entire programme of projects, which can extend to a full artistic programme for a given calendar year or longer, so this can function as de facto annual programme funding. The category is artform-agnostic and routinely supports media arts, digital culture, experimental sound and art-and-science work; activity delivered online is explicitly permitted. NOTABLE: international organisations are eligible where the project demonstrably benefits practising Australian artists, their work, or Australian audiences, so a non-Australian festival can apply if it is programming Australian artists. The applicant must be a legally constituted entity: incorporated association, company limited by guarantee, or government statutory authority. Registration in the Application Management System must be completed at least one business day before the closing date. The round closes 1 September 2026 at 3pm AEST.

  7. Creative Australia: International Engagement Fund (October 2026 round)

    Creative Australia · International, with a confirmed overseas partner. · Deadline: 13 Oct 2026 · Award: AUD 5,000 to 30,000. No application fee. No formal matching requirement, but assessment explicitly weighs diversity and scale of income and co-funding, including earned income, grants, sponsorship and in-kind contributions.

    Supports reciprocal international exchange between Australian practitioners or organisations and confirmed overseas partners, which makes it the right instrument for co-hosted convenings, labs and cross-border programme partnerships rather than one-way touring. Supported activity types are creative collaboration and development, cultural exchange and knowledge sharing, and labs and practice-exchange models, mapping closely onto media arts residencies, art-and-science research exchanges and digital culture symposia. Eligible costs include artist fees, travel, accommodation, production expenses, marketing, access costs, and childcare or carer support. International organisations are eligible where the project benefits Australian artists or creative workers, and a confirmed international partner is mandatory and must confirm participation in writing. The reciprocity requirement is substantive: the strongest applications embed mutually beneficial exchange rather than simply presenting an Australian artist abroad. Creative Australia's published assessment feedback notes top-ranked applications foregrounded First Nations self-determined or diasporic-led activity and demonstrated sector-wide impact. It is competitive: 157 eligible applications in the March 2026 round yielded 33 grants, about a 21 percent success rate. Contemporary music practitioners are excluded and directed to Music Australia programmes. Closes 13 October 2026 at 3pm AEDT.

  8. Blackbird Foundation: Believers Fund III 2026

    Blackbird Foundation · Australia and/or New Zealand; individuals, groups or organisations established in AU/NZ, with projects taking place in AU/NZ. · Deadline: 30 Jun 2027 · Award: AUD 5,000 and AUD 10,000 grants. No application fee.

    Believers funds the people, projects and programs creating the conditions for young people's creativity to flourish at scale. Creativity is meant in the broadest sense: artists are creative, but so are scientists, debaters, community builders and dancers, and a project can be anything from a summer camp to a robotics competition to a co-working holiday house for artists. Blackbird looks for inventive, dynamic experiments that encourage young people to be curious, to explore, to be lifelong learners, and to build community and become high-agency people, backing projects that are 0-1 (just starting) or 1-10 (scaling or iterating) rather than established groups. Examples: a neighbourhood community science lab, a hacker house, a hardware garage makerspace, a travelling STEM roadshow, or an after-school arts or coding club. ELIGIBILITY (no flexibility): individuals, groups or organisations established in Australia and/or New Zealand; projects taking place in AU/NZ; projects completed within 12 months; projects whose core focus is primarily creative outcomes; and organisations with less than AUD 1.5M in revenue. WHEN TO APPLY: Believers is open year-round, with EOIs and applications reviewed and progressed on a rolling basis; the program closes when all funds are disbursed or at the end of the FY27 period (30 June 2027), whichever comes first. Expressions of interest for Believers Fund III open on 30 June 2026.

    Queer Screen · Sydney, Australia (festival February 2027). Open internationally. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Screening at one of the world's biggest queer film festivals, with audience awards. Modest FilmFreeway fees.

    Queer Screen's Mardi Gras Film Festival in Sydney is one of the largest queer film festivals in the world. Submissions reopened in July 2026 via FilmFreeway; the final deadline is historically mid-October (around 15 October 2026, unconfirmed). Audience awards; modest submission fees.
