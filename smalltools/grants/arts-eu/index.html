Visual and Media Arts Grants in the EU

Currently 25 active visual and media arts grants, fellowships and residencies open to applicants in the EU. Hand-curated and updated weekly. Almost every entry is funded; a few notable unpaid open calls and festival submissions are included as clearly flagged exceptions. Browse the list below, or use the interactive desk for filtering and shortlisting.

  1. Arts Council of Ireland: Visual Arts Project Award 2027

    Arts Council of Ireland / An Chomhairle Ealaion · Republic of Ireland. Applicants based outside Ireland may apply if the application clearly shows how outcomes benefit the arts in the Republic of Ireland. · Deadline: 23 Jul 2026 · Award: Three strands: up to EUR 12,000 (planning and pre-production), up to EUR 60,000 (production and engagement), up to EUR 150,000 (partnership production). No application fee.

    The Arts Council of Ireland's Visual Arts Project Award funds professional visual artists, curators, and producers to develop and deliver visual arts projects, in three strands: planning and pre-production (up to EUR 12,000), production and engagement (up to EUR 60,000), and partnership production (up to EUR 150,000). Primarily aimed at applicants based in the Republic of Ireland, but the Arts Council's standard eligibility clause allows applicants based elsewhere where the application explicitly demonstrates how the outcomes will benefit the arts in Ireland. The wider Project Award 2027 covers nine disciplines including film and music on a similar timeline. Deadline 23 July 2026, 17:30 Irish time.

  2. APOSTAXIS Artist Residency & Exhibition 2026 (Ukraine / Cyprus)

    ZMINA (Ukraine) with Cypriot partner organisation · Ternopil, Ukraine and Larnaca, Cyprus (hybrid participation possible) · Deadline: 26 Jul 2026 · Award: Fully funded: artist fee of EUR 1,800 (including tax; paid in equivalent UAH for Ukrainian artists), EUR 300 production budget, plus travel, accommodation, meals and curatorial support. No application fee.

    APOSTAXIS is an international residency and exhibition programme exploring displacement, memory, identity and cultural resilience. It selects four visual artists (three from Ukraine and one from Cyprus) for two simultaneous 20-day residencies in Ternopil, Ukraine and Larnaca, Cyprus (6 to 28 October 2026), followed by post-production, a digital exhibition, and a curated physical exhibition in Larnaca in January 2027. ELIGIBILITY: visual artists from Ukraine and Cyprus with lived experience or family heritage connected to displacement, working in community-engaged or research-based practices (installation, mixed media, participatory, social practice, documentary, performance, archive-based work). Artists unable to travel due to war-related restrictions or personal circumstances remain eligible via hybrid participation. Fully funded: EUR 1,800 artist fee (equivalent UAH for Ukrainian artists), EUR 300 production budget, travel, accommodation, meals and curatorial support; no application fee. Deadline: 26 July 2026.

  3. ZAMEK Artist Residency 2027 (ZAMEK Culture Centre, Poznan)

    ZAMEK Culture Centre (Centrum Kultury ZAMEK) · Poznan, Poland · Deadline: 27 Jul 2026 · Award: Accommodation in an apartment in the former imperial residence, studio access, a fee of PLN 6,000 gross per month (contract for specific work), a production budget of PLN 5,000 to 15,000 gross depending on length of stay and project, plus curatorial support and publicity. No application fee listed.

    Open call for ZAMEK Culture Centre's 2027 Artist-in-Residence programme in Poznan. Supports artists working across visual arts, performance, choreography, dance, music, sound art, architecture and filmmaking (literature is excluded). Two formats: a Conceptual Residency (creative stay, no requirement to complete or present a final work, with public meetings at least every two weeks) and an Operational Residency (concluding with an artistic event such as an exhibition, concert or screening, plus regular public meetings). Selected artists receive accommodation in the former imperial residence, a studio, a fee of PLN 6,000 gross per month, a production budget of PLN 5,000 to 15,000 gross, curatorial support and publicity. Residencies run 4 to 8 weeks in 2027. ZAMEK is particularly interested in projects that engage changing realities, the current moment and the future (climate and migration crises, the war in Ukraine, social and political issues) or the context, history and architecture of the Castle; project scale must fit the programme budget, and projects should not rely on external co-financing. Download the Terms & Conditions on the ZAMEK site to apply. Deadline: 27 July 2026.

  7. AVISTAVULCÃO: Azores Artist Residency 2027 (Faial, Portugal)

    AVISTAVULCÃO / Barca 13 · Faial Island, Azores, Portugal (near the Capelinhos Volcano). Open to artists from countries eligible for the Creative Europe programme. · Deadline: 01 Aug 2026 · Award: EUR 1,350 allowance per resident for the month, plus travel allowance (EUR 400 to 800), green mobility top-up (EUR 400), visa top-up (EUR 120/resident), outermost regions and overseas territories top-up (EUR 175/resident), family top-up (EUR 200 per child/resident), and accessibility support. No application fee.

    AVISTAVULCÃO opens a call for a one-month artist-in-residence programme focused on research and creation, taking place in March 2027 and intended for an artist or artistic duo. Located on the island of Faial near the Capelinhos Volcano (Azores, Portugal), the residency offers immersion in a territory shaped by geology, insularity, and the interplay between nature and human presence, with the Azores treated as living material for creation. Artists are invited to develop projects engaging with landscape, memory, local history, and the contemporary challenges facing peripheral regions; suggested starting points include geological time, transformation, resilience, isolation, mobility, and the relationships between communities and the environment. Context-sensitive proposals that prioritise observation, listening and a relationship with the place are valued. Residents meet local stakeholders and share moments with the community, culminating in a public presentation of the process or results. SUPPORT (in-person mobility): EUR 1,350 allowance per resident, travel allowance of EUR 400 to 800, green mobility top-up of EUR 400, visa top-up of EUR 120 per resident, outermost regions top-up of EUR 175 per resident, family top-up of EUR 200 per child, and accessibility support. ELIGIBILITY: individual artists and duos (cross-disciplinary welcome) from countries eligible for the Creative Europe programme. DEADLINE: 1 August 2026.

  9. Stiftung Künstler*dorf Schöppingen: Residency Fellowship 2027 Open Call

    Stiftung Künstler*dorf Schöppingen · Schöppingen, Germany (residency requires living and working on-site) · Deadline: 31 Aug 2026 · Award: Stipend of EUR 1,500 per month, minus roughly EUR 100-200 for rent and operating expenses. For collectives, EUR 1,000 per person up to a maximum of EUR 3,000 per month, minus rent and operating costs. Travel, insurance and material costs are covered only in exceptional cases, on request.

    Stiftung Künstler*dorf Schöppingen offers residency fellowships for professional visual artists, authors and composers; since 2023 applications from collectives are also accepted. The call is open to people of all nationalities and ages, and accommodation for partners, family members or assistance can be arranged upon agreement and availability (for physical, cognitive or sensory disabilities, contact the foundation to explain any barriers). Fellows reside and work at the Künstler*dorf for a period of their choosing between two and six months, using the premises and infrastructure on-site. The stipend is EUR 1,500 per month, minus roughly EUR 100-200 for rent and operating expenses; for collectives it is EUR 1,000 per person up to a maximum of EUR 3,000 per month, minus rent and operating costs. Travel, insurance or material costs are covered only in exceptional cases on request. The fellowship is not designed as a production residency and there is no obligation to present results, though exhibition and presentation facilities are available and the voluntary co-organization of events (lectures, readings, workshops or similar) is welcomed. A willingness to participate in events and to engage in exchange with other fellows is a prerequisite. Selection is made by expert juries on the quality of previous work, the quality and plausibility of the project for Schöppingen, and program contributions (interaction / community building). Applications for 2027 can be submitted from 27 June to 31 August 2026 (midnight CET) via the online application portal.

  21. Phenomenon Grant 2026 (Second Edition)

    Phenomenon Association, supported by Kerenidis Pepe · Greece (projects rooted in Greece) · Deadline: 30 Sep 2026 · Award: Grants ranging from EUR 5,000 to EUR 10,000, based on the application's actual funding needs. Each edition awards a minimum of EUR 20,000 to two to four grantees (in 2025, three grants of EUR 8,500 each were awarded).

    Created in 2025, the Phenomenon Grant is a yearly program that supports art projects in Greece with grants of EUR 5,000 to EUR 10,000 for individuals, collectives, and non-profit organizations engaged with queer, feminist, anti-colonial, and other emancipatory art practices. The grant aims to support critical phases of artistic development, enabling experimentation, research, collaboration, and the realization of new and ongoing projects. In a context marked by rising authoritarianism, genocidal forces and the dismantling of social justice advancements, the program seeks to support voices that critically and ethically engage with these issues, create spaces of resistance, reimagine futures beyond exclusion, persecution, and domination, and open pathways toward collective liberation. Particular attention is paid to collaborative endeavours rooted in Greece that are built on connections with the Global Majority, promoting networks of translocal solidarity and interconnected struggles. Organized and supported by Kerenidis Pepe, which has pledged a total budget of EUR 60,000 over 2025-2027, the amount awarded is based on the application's actual funding needs. In 2025 the program received 193 applications and awarded three grants of EUR 8,500 each to Athens Museum of Queer Arts (AMOQA), Forensic Architecture Initiative Athens (FAIA) and Theo Panagopoulos. The 2026 selection committee is composed of Athena Athanasiou, Dora Garcia, Christina Li, VASKOS, and chaired by Aggelika Mitsiou. The submission period runs from 1 September to 30 September 2026, with selected projects announced in November 2026.
