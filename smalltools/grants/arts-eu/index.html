Visual and Media Arts Grants in the EU

Currently 36 active visual and media arts grants, fellowships and residencies open to applicants in the EU. Hand-curated and updated weekly. Almost every entry is funded; a few notable unpaid open calls and festival submissions are included as clearly flagged exceptions.   4. Kulturstiftung des Bundes: General Project Funding (Allgemeine Projektfoerderung)

    Kulturstiftung des Bundes (German Federal Cultural Foundation) · Activity must be visible to audiences in Germany. Institutions headquartered in Germany or abroad may apply. · Deadline: 31 Jul 2026 · Award: No fixed ceiling, but total project costs must exceed EUR 50,000 to be eligible. The Foundation allocates up to EUR 10 million per year across this line. At least 20 percent of total project costs must be secured from own capital and/or third-party funding. No application fee.

    Germany's flagship open, non-thematic federal project line, and one of the largest European funders that will back a festival, exhibition programme or multi-partner convening run by an organisation rather than an individual artist. It funds large innovative projects across all artistic fields, explicitly including New Media alongside visual and performing arts, music, film, photography and architecture. There is no published maximum, but the EUR 50,000 minimum total project cost sets the scale, and at least 20 percent co-financing is mandatory. Deadlines are twice yearly at 12:00 CET: 31 July 2026 and 31 January 2027. TIMING RULE: decisions are made by an external jury meeting in May and November, and no project may start before its jury date, so plan four to six months of lead time. Non-German organisations are genuinely eligible, which is unusual at this scale, provided the project is visible in Germany and has an international dimension. Two practical barriers: the application form must be completed in German, and project communications must include a German-language version.
