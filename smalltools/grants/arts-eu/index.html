  1. AY-ROOP / Le Milieu: Reuse - (Re)making for Circus Residency 2026 (Rennes Métropole, France)

    AY-ROOP · Le Milieu, Saint-Jacques-de-la-Lande, Rennes Métropole, France. Applicants must legally reside in a Creative Europe country other than France and the French overseas territories (DROM-COM). · Deadline: 03 Jul 2026 · Award: Funded through Culture Moves Europe: EUR 900 mobility allowance per person plus flat-rate travel coverage; supplements for green mobility, visa fees, dependent children under 18, OCT/OR residents, and disability/accessibility needs (including possible full coverage of a sign-language interpreter). On-site single-room accommodation, lunches at Le Milieu canteen (Tuesday to Friday), shared kitchen, and technical support provided. No application fee.

    AY-ROOP is launching an open call to select three circus artists and/or circus set designers/builders for a 21-day Culture Moves Europe research residency at Le Milieu (Saint-Jacques-de-la-Lande, Rennes Métropole) from 28 November to 18 December 2026. The residency is a research laboratory on reuse in contemporary circus: how everyday, locally available materials (paper/cardboard, plastics, lightweight metal, textiles, reclaimed wood, bicycle parts, etc.) can be used to create scenographic elements, sets, props and small apparatuses for juggling, acrobatics and balancing. The core principle is that objects are recreated locally from resources available in each territory rather than transported. AY-ROOP provides an equipped construction workshop, a rehearsal stage and circus tent, a guide to local reuse-sector contacts, and a full residency environment (accommodation, shared kitchen, working facilities); two mentors (a reuse specialist and a performing-arts professional) support the artists. PROFILES: circus artists interested in construction/scenography/DIY making, and builders/set designers working in circus; individual or group applications (max 3 people per group); emerging or established, any background. Accessible to artists with disabilities and accessibility needs. Working language English. No aerial apparatus or rigging: all work is ground-based. ELIGIBILITY: aged 18+ (no upper limit); legally resident in a Creative Europe country other than France and DROM-COM; available for the full residency. APPLY with a single PDF (1-page bio/CV, 5-page portfolio or video links, 1-page statement of intent, proof of residence). DEADLINE: 3 July 2026. Results announced 13 July 2026. Check the linked call PDF for full details.

  3. Artist Residency Autumn 2026 (Centre for Multispecies Relations and Future Ecologies, Palermo)

    Centre for Multispecies Relations and Future Ecologies · Altarello di Baida district, Palermo, Sicily, Italy. On-site 62-day residency, 12 October - 12 December 2026. Open to all nationalities, but applicants must be officially residing in a Creative Europe country (excluding Italy). · Deadline: 05 Jul 2026 · Award: Total allowance of EUR 1,860 (before tax) per artist, plus a travel budget of EUR 400 per artist (for distances under 4,000 km) with a EUR 400 green-mobility top-up, plus a production budget. Also provided: on-site accommodation in a fully furnished independent house (private bedroom; shared kitchen, living room, two bathrooms; outdoor studio space 100m from the working site), access to recycled/salvaged materials through the Centre's partnerships, a bicycle per resident, and a final public event.

    The Centre for Multispecies Relations and Future Ecologies invites applications from two artists for a 62-day residency (12 October - 12 December 2026) at its site in the Altarello di Baida district of Palermo, Sicily. The residency is structured around a 1,500 sqm garden at the threshold between the city's urban expansion and the historic rural landscape of the surrounding hills; the site, degraded through previous use (soil compaction and significant biodiversity loss), functions as both studio and subject, and artists participate in an ongoing process of ecological transformation, producing interventions that emerge from extended observation and fieldwork. Applications are welcome across all mediums and disciplines (installation, sound, performance, video, food art, participatory practice, and beyond); research-based and interdisciplinary practices are especially encouraged. The Centre works at the intersection of art, science, and (agro)ecological practices, with a focus on multispecies coexistence and climate change in the Mediterranean, and has been based in Palermo since 2021. PROGRAMME: five phases (orientation with small trips and gatherings; observation with independent fieldwork and consultations with artists, curators, scientists, and anthropologists; proposal and ecological feasibility developed with mentors; creation; and public restitution with an open event, a workshop, and a public discussion). Each artist develops an independent site-specific intervention; the two residents share the site, living space, and process, with exchange encouraged but not prescribed. Artist duos and artists who want to collaborate are encouraged to apply (you can apply as an individual for one position, as a duo for both, or as two artists collaborating for two positions). Mentorship by co-founders/artistic directors Fabio Aranzulla and Luca Cinquemani (weekly site visits, studio discussions, work-in-progress presentations); logistics managed by Francesca Farris. WHO: artists whose work engages with ecology, multispecies relations, or the more-than-human, who can move between observation and making and are interested in dialogue with scientists, land workers, and local communities. Priority is given to artists from working-class and less-privileged backgrounds, those who self-identify as part of marginalised communities, and candidates reflecting diversity in gender, age, geographical background, and ability; green travel is prioritised as a condition of participation where feasible. Applicants should be open to living and working in a neighbourhood on the margins of Palermo's periphery. Funded by Culture Moves Europe (Creative Europe programme of the European Union). APPLICATION: first phase via the online form (opened 10 June); shortlisted artists contacted before the end of July; second phase is video calls with shortlisted candidates; applications accepted in English and Italian. Contact: contact.cmrfe@gmail.com. Deadline 5 July 2026.

  4. Feminist Gaming Fellowship: Open Call 2026

    Feminist Gaming Project · Remote across the Netherlands, Türkiye, Germany, and the UK · Deadline: 12 Jul 2026 · Award: EUR 1,000 honorarium per fellow (paid half at the start, half on completion)

    The Feminist Gaming Project is opening applications for its first Fellowship cohort: five fellows from different creative and research fields spending three months developing 3D assets, tools, and documentation for its Open Access Asset Library (OAL), a free, open repository of inclusive 3D content created by, with, and for intersectionally feminist and diverse communities. Where mainstream marketplaces default to hyper-athletic, idealized, or hyper-sexualized characters, the OAL offers average bodies, real postures, everyday objects, and the kinds of lives most games leave out (pregnancy and postpartum, care work, ageing, disability, domestic labor). This first cohort is dedicated to 3D content. Supported by the Creative Industries Fund NL and working across the Netherlands, Türkiye, Germany, and the UK, the project challenges exclusionary norms in how games are made. The fellowship offers a EUR 1,000 honorarium per fellow over three months (half at start, half on completion); mentorship from the core team and advisors in feminist design, game studies, 3D production, and digital culture; a peer community across NL, TR, DE, and the UK with biweekly meetings; visibility through a travelling exhibition program, publications, and public events; and authorship credited in the OAL under its license terms. Five roles are recruited (one fellow each): 3D Character Artist for inclusive base meshes; 3D Character Artist for pregnancy and postpartum assets; 3D Animator for neutral and everyday movement; 3D Modeller for domestic and care-labor environments; and Researcher/Writer for documentation and the representation framework; applicants may rank up to two. Open to 3D artists, animators, modellers, designers, researchers, and writers committed to inclusive and intersectional representation in games; prior game-industry experience is not required, and emerging creators, underrepresented practitioners, and researchers at the intersection of games, gender, and culture are especially encouraged, particularly those based in or connected to NL, TR, DE, or the UK. To apply, email feministgamingproject@gmail.com with a short statement (max ~500 words), a portfolio or work samples (a link is fine), and confirmation of availability for the three-month period; name the role(s) in the subject line or statement. The project explicitly asks applicants to write statements in their own voice rather than using AI-generated text. Indicative timeline: applications close 12 July 2026; selection and notification late July; fellowship period August to October 2026; showcase and publication November to December 2026.
