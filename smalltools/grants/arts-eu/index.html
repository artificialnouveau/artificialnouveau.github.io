Visual and Media Arts Grants in the EU

Currently 21 active visual and media arts grants, fellowships and residencies open to applicants in the EU. Hand-curated and updated weekly. Almost every entry is funded; a few notable unpaid open calls and festival submissions are included as clearly flagged exceptions.   1. Aerowaves Twenty27: Call for Emerging European Choreographers

    Aerowaves (Spring Forward festival hosted in Limerick, Ireland) · Festival in Limerick, Ireland (14-17 April 2027) plus partner venues across Europe; applicants must be resident in geographical Europe · Deadline: 15 Jul 2026 · Award: For presentation at Spring Forward: a fee plus expenses including travel within Europe, hotel and a per diem/hospitality for each team member. Around 100 partner performance opportunities are guaranteed each year and supported by Aerowaves. Selected applicants additionally receive a year of Aerowaves promotion (artist profile, images, video, press coverage). No application fee.

    Aerowaves selects 20 emerging choreographers based in geographical Europe each year (the Twenty27 cohort) to have their work presented at the Spring Forward festival (14-17 April 2027 in Limerick, Ireland) and at many of Aerowaves' partners around Europe; Aerowaves promotes the selected artists for a year, creating performance opportunities with partners. EVERY APPLICANT (selected or not) has the chance to be programmed by Aerowaves network partners; around 100 performance opportunities per year are supported by Aerowaves. ELIGIBILITY: applicants must be resident in geographical Europe; due to the Russian invasion of Ukraine, Aerowaves partners do NOT accept applications of works made in Russia or Belarus. ONE work per applicant per year. The work must have been produced in geographical Europe; must be 15-40 minutes; must be finished and have been presented to the public (a studio sharing counts; work-in-progress or rehearsal footage does NOT). The work should be easily included in a double or triple bill with simple technical requirements; works created for public/non-conventional spaces and outdoors are welcome. WHAT SELECTED ARTISTS GET: presentation fee at Spring Forward plus travel (within Europe), hotel and per diem/hospitality for each team member; a year of Aerowaves website promotion. DEADLINE: 15 July 2026. APPLY via the Aerowaves how-to-apply page.

  3. Arts Council of Ireland: Visual Arts Project Award 2027

    Arts Council of Ireland / An Chomhairle Ealaion · Republic of Ireland. Applicants based outside Ireland may apply if the application clearly shows how outcomes benefit the arts in the Republic of Ireland. · Deadline: 23 Jul 2026 · Award: Three strands: up to EUR 12,000 (planning and pre-production), up to EUR 60,000 (production and engagement), up to EUR 150,000 (partnership production). No application fee.

    The Arts Council of Ireland's Visual Arts Project Award funds professional visual artists, curators, and producers to develop and deliver visual arts projects, in three strands: planning and pre-production (up to EUR 12,000), production and engagement (up to EUR 60,000), and partnership production (up to EUR 150,000). Primarily aimed at applicants based in the Republic of Ireland, but the Arts Council's standard eligibility clause allows applicants based elsewhere where the application explicitly demonstrates how the outcomes will benefit the arts in Ireland. The wider Project Award 2027 covers nine disciplines including film and music on a similar timeline. Deadline 23 July 2026, 17:30 Irish time.

  4. Simone Veil Prize for European Jewish Cultural Heritage 2026

    European Commission, Directorate-General for Education, Youth, Sport and Culture · EU Member States and third countries associated to the Creative Europe programme · Deadline: 31 Jul 2026 · Award: €10,000 per Grand Prix winner (five winners, one per category); up to 25 finalists recognized. No application fee.

    The Simone Veil Prize, administered by the European Commission, recognises outstanding projects preserving and promoting European Jewish cultural heritage. It is awarded across five categories: Shared Histories, Heritage for Dialogue, Living Heritage, Cross-border Cooperation and Shared Narratives, and Next Generation/Youth Engagement and Education. AWARD: €10,000 per Grand Prix winner (one per category, five in total); up to 25 finalists receive recognition. ELIGIBILITY: legal or natural persons (individuals or organisations) registered in EU Member States or third countries associated to the Creative Europe programme; projects must have been completed between January 2023 and January 2026. DEADLINE: 31 July 2026 at 23:59 CET. APPLY by submitting the completed application form by email to applications@simoneveilprize.eu.

  6. Stiftung Künstler*dorf Schöppingen: Residency Fellowship 2027 Open Call

    Stiftung Künstler*dorf Schöppingen · Schöppingen, Germany (residency requires living and working on-site) · Deadline: 31 Aug 2026 · Award: Stipend of EUR 1,500 per month, minus roughly EUR 100-200 for rent and operating expenses. For collectives, EUR 1,000 per person up to a maximum of EUR 3,000 per month, minus rent and operating costs. Travel, insurance and material costs are covered only in exceptional cases, on request.

    Stiftung Künstler*dorf Schöppingen offers residency fellowships for professional visual artists, authors and composers; since 2023 applications from collectives are also accepted. The call is open to people of all nationalities and ages, and accommodation for partners, family members or assistance can be arranged upon agreement and availability (for physical, cognitive or sensory disabilities, contact the foundation to explain any barriers). Fellows reside and work at the Künstler*dorf for a period of their choosing between two and six months, using the premises and infrastructure on-site. The stipend is EUR 1,500 per month, minus roughly EUR 100-200 for rent and operating expenses; for collectives it is EUR 1,000 per person up to a maximum of EUR 3,000 per month, minus rent and operating costs. Travel, insurance or material costs are covered only in exceptional cases on request. The fellowship is not designed as a production residency and there is no obligation to present results, though exhibition and presentation facilities are available and the voluntary co-organization of events (lectures, readings, workshops or similar) is welcomed. A willingness to participate in events and to engage in exchange with other fellows is a prerequisite. Selection is made by expert juries on the quality of previous work, the quality and plausibility of the project for Schöppingen, and program contributions (interaction / community building). Applications for 2027 can be submitted from 27 June to 31 August 2026 (midnight CET) via the online application portal.

AMOUNT: CHF 25,000 per year (maximum CHF 5,000 for mentoring and CHF 10,000 for musician fees). Submission deadline 1 September (23:59 CEST). Apply via the Pro Helvetia portal.

  16. Phenomenon Grant 2026 (Second Edition)

    Phenomenon Association, supported by Kerenidis Pepe · Greece (projects rooted in Greece) · Deadline: 30 Sep 2026 · Award: Grants ranging from EUR 5,000 to EUR 10,000, based on the application's actual funding needs. Each edition awards a minimum of EUR 20,000 to two to four grantees (in 2025, three grants of EUR 8,500 each were awarded).

    Created in 2025, the Phenomenon Grant is a yearly program that supports art projects in Greece with grants of EUR 5,000 to EUR 10,000 for individuals, collectives, and non-profit organizations engaged with queer, feminist, anti-colonial, and other emancipatory art practices. The grant aims to support critical phases of artistic development, enabling experimentation, research, collaboration, and the realization of new and ongoing projects. In a context marked by rising authoritarianism, genocidal forces and the dismantling of social justice advancements, the program seeks to support voices that critically and ethically engage with these issues, create spaces of resistance, reimagine futures beyond exclusion, persecution, and domination, and open pathways toward collective liberation. Particular attention is paid to collaborative endeavours rooted in Greece that are built on connections with the Global Majority, promoting networks of translocal solidarity and interconnected struggles. Organized and supported by Kerenidis Pepe, which has pledged a total budget of EUR 60,000 over 2025-2027, the amount awarded is based on the application's actual funding needs. In 2025 the program received 193 applications and awarded three grants of EUR 8,500 each to Athens Museum of Queer Arts (AMOQA), Forensic Architecture Initiative Athens (FAIA) and Theo Panagopoulos. The 2026 selection committee is composed of Athena Athanasiou, Dora Garcia, Christina Li, VASKOS, and chaired by Aggelika Mitsiou. The submission period runs from 1 September to 30 September 2026, with selected projects announced in November 2026.

  17. EMAP / EMARE: European Media Art Platform Residencies 2027

    European Media Art Platform (EMAP / EMARE) · Two-month production residency at one of 15 host institutions across Austria, France, Slovakia, Portugal, Belgium, Netherlands, Slovenia, Croatia, Spain, Italy, Cyprus, Greece, Latvia, Germany and Poland · Deadline: 06 Nov 2026 · Award: EUR 4,000 grant for the applying artist (including subsistence); EUR 2,000 for a collaborating artist (EUR 6,000 total for a duo); EUR 4,000 project budget; accommodation provided by the host; travel covered per EU ceilings; access to technical facilities and media labs. No application fee.

    The European Media Art Platform (EMAP/EMARE) is a network of leading European media-art organisations offering fully funded two-month production residencies for artists working with digital media, including media art, bio art and robotic art. Each residency provides a EUR 4,000 grant to the applying artist (including subsistence), a EUR 4,000 project budget, host-provided accommodation, travel covered according to EU ceilings, and access to technical facilities and media labs; a collaborating artist receives an additional EUR 2,000 (EUR 6,000 total for a duo). Residencies take place at one of 15 host institutions across Austria, France, Slovakia, Portugal, Belgium, the Netherlands, Slovenia, Croatia, Spain, Italy, Cyprus, Greece, Latvia, Germany and Poland. ELIGIBILITY: applicants must be EU residents or taxpayers in an EU member state, or residents/taxpayers of one of the eligible non-EU countries (Netherlands-based artists qualify). Undergraduate and Master's students are not eligible; PhD candidates and emerging artists of any age and academic background may apply. IMPORTANT: applicants must apply as a duo or collective, or propose a collaborative project, rather than as a purely solo applicant. The 2027 open call runs from 3 September 2026 to 6 November 2026 at 14:00 CET. Apply online at call.emare.eu. No application fee.

  18. Visual Anthropology Center (VAC): Residency Programme (Belgrade)

    Visual Anthropology Center (VAC) · Belgrade, Serbia (accommodation and studio in central Belgrade; additional workspace at Community Center KROV) · Deadline: 31 Dec 2027 · Award: EUR 1,000–1,500 per month (stipend, varies by project requirements), plus accommodation and studio space provided. No application fee.

    Visual Anthropology Center (VAC) in Belgrade invites filmmakers, visual anthropologists, artists, and researchers to apply for its residency programme. The residency specialises in visual anthropology but is also open to individual and collaborative projects in experimental ethnography, creative documentary film, and other artistic mediums. Residents may apply with a project aimed at a public showing, or use the time and space to reflect, research, or write without a specific outcome. WHAT IS PROVIDED: accommodation and studio space in central Belgrade; additional workspace and community connection through Community Center KROV (an independent creative hub hosting lectures, events, and exhibitions); project guidance and mentorship from film professionals, ethnomusicologists, producers, artists, curators, and anthropologists; connections to local cultural and academic institutions, archives, galleries, and exhibition spaces; access to camera equipment, translators for fieldwork or editing, fixers, and project-specific services or materials; opportunity to exhibit a final project as an exhibition, installation, screening, workshop, or lecture. Eligible projects may be promoted through VAC's platforms and partner networks, including the Vizantrop Film Festival, for regional and international visibility. ELIGIBILITY: filmmakers, visual anthropologists, artists, and researchers; individual and collaborative projects welcome. Applications accepted on a rolling basis.
