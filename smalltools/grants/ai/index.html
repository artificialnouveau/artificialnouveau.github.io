    A joint philanthropic funding call by Schmidt Sciences, Google DeepMind, ARIA, the Cooperative AI Foundation and Google.org to catalyse the foundational scientific research needed to understand, evaluate and control risks emerging from large-scale ecosystems of interacting AI agents deployed by multiple actors (multi-principal, multi-agent settings). The call argues that focusing only on the safety and alignment of individual models is insufficient, and seeks system-level approaches. RESEARCH AGENDA (four clusters): (1) Sandboxes and Testbeds, realistic, reproducible multi-agent environments for studying populations of frontier-model agents; (2) The Science of Agent Networks, how collective capabilities emerge and scale, how networks fail, and how dangerous population-level properties can be detected; (3) Strengthening Agent Infrastructure, evaluating and stress-testing primitives such as identity, verifiability, reputation, communication and commitment; (4) Multi-Agent Oversight and Control, detection, attribution, security and intervention methods to keep deployed agent populations safe at scale. Proposals may target one cluster or span several, with priority on depth over breadth and on realistic sandboxes and testbeds. OUT OF SCOPE: single-agent safety, capability advancement without a clear safety motivation, toy systems, naive application of pre-existing solutions, commercial product development, and purely conceptual/policy work. ELIGIBILITY: individual researchers, research teams, research institutions and multi-institution collaborations across universities, national laboratories, institutes and nonprofit research organisations; open globally.   25. GovAI DC Winter Fellowship 2027

    Centre for the Governance of AI (GovAI) · Washington, DC, USA; in-person, full-time (part-time possible for DC-based think tank or government staff). · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: $21,000 stipend for the three-month term, plus travel support to DC and weekday lunch provided.     GovAI's DC Winter Fellowship is a three-month bipartisan in-person fellowship in Washington, DC, designed for individuals seeking to enter or advance in US AI governance and policy. The program emphasises bipartisan engagement, rigorous analysis, and practical policy relevance, with a focus on how AI policy is shaped within the US political system. Fellows conduct independent research on a topic of their choice with mentorship from a GovAI supervisor; the first two weeks explore the AI policy landscape before a research proposal is finalised. Outputs may include a report, white paper, journal article, op-ed, or blog post targeted at an audience relevant to AI policy. Fellows also attend expert Q&As, skills workshops, seminars, work-in-progress meetings, and social events, and are encouraged to discuss follow-on career opportunities. GovAI alumni have gone on to roles in US government bodies (NSC, OSTP, Senate, Department of Commerce, DHS); think tanks (CSIS, CSET, CNAS, RAND, Heritage Foundation, FAI); AI companies (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind); and universities (Yale, Stanford, MIT). AMOUNT: $21,000 stipend for three months, plus travel support to DC and weekday lunch. Part-time fellowship at a pro-rated salary available for DC-based think tank researchers, government staffers, or professionals doing AI governance work; pay accommodations possible. ELIGIBILITY: candidates must have the right to work in the US; no visa sponsorship. Open to a wide range of backgrounds (government, academia, industry, civil society); promising undergraduates welcome; no strict degree requirements. Relevant areas include public policy, political science, engineering, computer science, economics, biosecurity, cybersecurity, China studies, and risk management. SELECTION CRITERIA: quality and clarity of written work, relevant expertise, judgement, and team fit. APPLICATION PROCESS: (1) written application and automated abstract reasoning assessment; (2) paid remote work test (August 2026); (3) remote interview and reference checks (September 2026); decisions communicated October 2026. Application deadline not yet publicly stated; applications open June 2026 -- check programme page for the deadline.

  26. microagi Research Fellowship - Cohort 01

    microagi · Munich, Germany or Zurich, Switzerland; in-person, 6-month residence. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Up to $2,000,000 in compute resources; $10,000/month stipend. No application fee stated. $10,000 referral bonus if you refer an accepted fellow.

    The microagi Research Fellowship (Cohort 01) provides researchers with frontier-lab resources and the freedom to work on hard problems in robotics, embodied AI, and the systems around them. Fellows work in residence for six months at microagi's offices in Munich or Zurich. AMOUNT: up to $2,000,000 in compute; $10,000/month stipend. RESEARCH AREAS: (1) Long Horizon Dexterous Manipulation -- end-to-end models for complex multi-stage tasks with dexterous hands, natural language instruction following, memory across long task sequences, fine-grained physical control in unstructured environments; (2) Cross Embodiment Learning; (3) On Device Physical Intelligence; (4) Safe and Reliable Deployment. microagi's model is to deploy embodied intelligence into physical-world operations and turn what it learns there into compounding research. REFERRAL: $10,000 bonus for referring a candidate who is accepted into the cohort. No application deadline publicly stated as of June 2026 -- one cohort only, apply as soon as possible via https://www.microagi.ai/fellowship.
