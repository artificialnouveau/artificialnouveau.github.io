Open calls

  1. AGI Governance Fellowship

    Johns Hopkins University, School of Government and Policy (SOGP) · Hopkins Bloomberg Center, Washington, D.C. (in-person only) · Deadline: 15 Jul 2026 · Award: Fully funded: travel, accommodation, and meals covered.

    The AGI Governance Fellowship is the inaugural three-week intensive run by Johns Hopkins University's School of Government and Policy (SOGP) to develop the next generation of leaders in AI governance. COVERS: frontier AI legislation at state and federal levels; building societal resilience amid rapid capability growth; democratic practices in AGI alignment; and designing new governing institutions for the AGI era. WHO SHOULD APPLY: early-career researchers and practitioners (within roughly eight years of undergrad) with solid foundations in AI governance, including congressional staffers, AGI company employees, recent CS or policy PhDs, and nonprofit/civil society professionals; a postgraduate degree or equivalent experience is required. This is a capstone program for rising leaders, not an intro course. FUNDING: travel, accommodation, and meals are fully covered. DATES: 8-25 September 2026 at the Hopkins Bloomberg Center in Washington, D.C. (in-person only). DEADLINE: apply by 15 July 2026 for full consideration; rolling applications accepted. APPLY: https://sogp.jh.edu/agi-governance-fellowship/

  2. Claude Corps Fellowship

    Anthropic (funded); CodePath (employer of record); Social Finance (evaluation) · USA; in-person at host nonprofit organizations across America (placement location varies; relocation support available). · Deadline: 17 Jul 2026 · Award: $85,000 full-time annual salary plus benefits, expansive Claude token budget, and relocation support as needed. No application fee.

    Claude Corps is a national fellowship program from Anthropic placing early-career fellows full-time at nonprofits across America for one year, with the goal of extending AI's benefits to communities and building fellows' AI skills. Anthropic funds the program and provides Claude expertise; CodePath (America's largest collegiate CS education provider) is the fellows' official employer of record and leads programming; Social Finance leads measurement and evaluation. AMOUNT: $85,000 full-time salary plus benefits; expansive Claude token budget; relocation support as needed. FELLOW EXPERIENCE: intensive Claude training at the start; five hours of ongoing weekly training throughout; support from a CodePath mentor; Anthropic office hours for technical questions; and professional guidance from the host organization manager. SAMPLE HOST ORGANIZATIONS: Braven (Chicago), Code the Dream (Durham NC), Heartland Forward (Bentonville AR), Montgomery County Food Bank (Conroe TX), Reef Environmental Education Foundation (Key Largo FL), StriveTogether (Cincinnati), YMCA of Greater Charlotte, and more than 400 nonprofits total. ELIGIBILITY: anyone over 18 with under two years of full-time work experience, regardless of educational background; must be authorized to work in the US; must be comfortable working with Claude and willing to relocate if necessary. COHORTS: Cohort 1 (100 fellows, starts October 2026) -- applications close 17 July 2026; Cohort 2 (starts January 2027) and Cohort 3 (starts August 2027) -- rolling applications. Apply at https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-corps.

  3. Horizon Institute for Public Service: AI Rapid Response Fellowship

    Horizon Institute for Public Service · Washington, DC area, United States (in-person; full-time placements in U.S. executive branch offices). · Deadline: 22 Jul 2026 · Award: Fellow track: USD 170,000 to 200,000+, plus an additional stipend for benefits. Senior Fellow track: from USD 250,000+, plus a benefits stipend, commensurate with the seniority of the placement.

    The AI Rapid Response Fellowship is a focused effort by the Horizon Institute for Public Service to close the AI security talent gap in the U.S. federal government. Horizon is recruiting a specialized cohort of exceptional technical and policy talent to work full-time in executive branch offices on AI security-related issues, with placements beginning as soon as possible. TRACKS: the Fellow track is for candidates with technical or policy expertise relevant to AI security, typically 2-6+ years of full-time relevant experience; the Senior Fellow track is for candidates with substantial prior experience, typically 10-15+ years in relevant technical, policy, or leadership roles. FOCUS: AI governance, AI security, policy, and public service. COMPENSATION: Fellow track USD 170,000-200,000+ plus a benefits stipend; Senior Fellow track from USD 250,000+ plus a benefits stipend, commensurate with the seniority of the placement. DURATION: one year full-time, with the opportunity for renewal, based in the Washington, DC area. ELIGIBILITY: U.S. citizenship is required. An existing security clearance, or eligibility to obtain one, is helpful but not required. DEADLINE: applications are due July 22, 2026.

  4. Genesis Mission AI Fellowship 2026

    Horizon Institute for Public Service (with Renaissance Philanthropy, SeedAI, and Fulcrum Science) · United States. Fellows are forward deployed to US Department of Energy national labs (initial partners: Idaho National Laboratory, Brookhaven National Laboratory, Princeton Plasma Physics Laboratory). Remote or in-person; on-site travel at least once a quarter. Must be eligible to work in the US (no visa sponsorship; US green card holders eligible). · Deadline: 31 Jul 2026 · Award: Prorated total annual compensation of USD 200,000.

    The Horizon Institute for Public Service, Renaissance Philanthropy, SeedAI, and Fulcrum Science are partnering to launch a fellowship that embeds technical AI talent across the US Department of Energy national labs to advance the goals of the Genesis Mission, DOE's flagship science and technology initiative launched in late 2025. The Genesis Mission identifies 26 national science and technology challenges critical to US leadership and envisions a national platform connecting supercomputers, experimental facilities, AI systems, and unique datasets, with DOE's 17 national labs playing a key role. Genesis Mission AI Fellows are forward deployed to DOE national labs to bring frontier AI expertise to high-priority scientific challenges. As core members of the technical staff on a Genesis Mission project, fellows work alongside national lab scientists and partners and apply AI to national priorities, from building cutting-edge models of plasma dynamics in pursuit of controlled fusion to advancing state-of-the-art models for foundational materials discovery. Initial national lab partners include Idaho National Laboratory, Brookhaven National Laboratory, and the Princeton Plasma Physics Laboratory; additional partners may be announced later. COMPENSATION/DURATION: 9-12 months starting fall 2026, with a prorated total annual compensation of USD 200,000; fellows are expected to travel to their national lab site at least once a quarter but may work on-site if they prefer. ELIGIBILITY: competitive applicants should have at least 2 years of industry experience building AI models or agents (those with industry experience building AI applications or data infrastructure will also be considered); scientific domain expertise is not required, but any placement will involve some training and study in a specific scientific discipline or energy topic, supported by expert colleagues at the host institution. All fellows must be eligible to work in the United States for the duration of the placement; US employment visas cannot be sponsored, but US permanent residents (green card holders) are eligible. APPLICATION: initial form requires a CV or resume (max 2 pages) and short-answer questions on motivations and experiences (max 200 words); successful applicants complete interviews through partner organizations and national labs, and selected fellows are matched to partner labs by scientific domain and availability. The review uses evidence-based best practices including blind grading of application materials. TIMELINE: application deadline 31 July 2026; final fellow selection August-September 2026; expected start fall 2026.

  5. Scaling AI Safety for a Multi-Agent World (Joint Funding Call)

    Schmidt Sciences, Google DeepMind, the Advanced Research & Invention Agency (ARIA), the Cooperative AI Foundation, and Google.org · Global; open worldwide, with collaborations across geographic boundaries encouraged. · Deadline: 08 Aug 2026 · Award: Two funding tiers (applicants may apply to either or both), projects 1 to 2 years. Tier 1: up to USD 300,000 for exploratory research, pilot studies, or focused technical investigations. Tier 2: USD 300,000 to USD 1,000,000 for more ambitious or collaborative projects. For projects funded by Schmidt Sciences, indirect costs must be at or below 10%. No application fee.

    A joint philanthropic funding call by Schmidt Sciences, Google DeepMind, ARIA, the Cooperative AI Foundation and Google.org to catalyse the foundational scientific research needed to understand, evaluate and control risks emerging from large-scale ecosystems of interacting AI agents deployed by multiple actors (multi-principal, multi-agent settings). The call argues that focusing only on the safety and alignment of individual models is insufficient, and seeks system-level approaches. RESEARCH AGENDA (four clusters): (1) Sandboxes and Testbeds, realistic, reproducible multi-agent environments for studying populations of frontier-model agents; (2) The Science of Agent Networks, how collective capabilities emerge and scale, how networks fail, and how dangerous population-level properties can be detected; (3) Strengthening Agent Infrastructure, evaluating and stress-testing primitives such as identity, verifiability, reputation, communication and commitment; (4) Multi-Agent Oversight and Control, detection, attribution, security and intervention methods to keep deployed agent populations safe at scale. Proposals may target one cluster or span several, with priority on depth over breadth and on realistic sandboxes and testbeds. OUT OF SCOPE: single-agent safety, capability advancement without a clear safety motivation, toy systems, naive application of pre-existing solutions, commercial product development, and purely conceptual/policy work. ELIGIBILITY: individual researchers, research teams, research institutions and multi-institution collaborations across universities, national laboratories, institutes and nonprofit research organisations; open globally. Informational webinars on 30 June and 23 July 2026; notification of decisions in autumn 2026. Proposals due 8 August 2026 by 11:59pm Anywhere on Earth. Contact: multiagentsafety@schmidtsciences.org. Apply at https://schmidtsciences.smapply.io/prog/scaling_ai_safety_for_a_multi_agent_world/.

  6. AIxDESIGN: Grain, Open Call for Critical AI Literacy Recipes

    AIxDESIGN, co-produced with the Netherlands Film Academy · Remote/online; open to educators and practitioners based anywhere · Deadline: 31 Aug 2026 · Award: Need-based fee of EUR 150 per recipe for freelance and independent practitioners (email hello@aixdesign.co if the time would be hard to make without it). Your recipe is formatted and credited to you in the first edition of Grain, plus a print copy and an invite to the launch. Published under CC BY-SA 4.0; you keep authorship.

    Grain is a cookbook of critical and creative AI literacy exercises for educators across audiovisual and creative practice, co-produced by AIxDESIGN and the Netherlands Film Academy and led by Nadia Piet. The open call collects 'recipes': tried-and-tested group exercises for the classroom or workshop that get people thinking critically and making creatively with AI. Exercises can be hands-on or analog, 15 minutes or a full day, beginner or advanced; the one hard requirement is that someone else can read your recipe and run it themselves. Examples of what might fit: hands-on experiments with specific AI tools (image generators, chatbots, code assistants, video models); analog activities that reveal computational logic through embodied practice; critical readings, watchings, or listenings with structured discussion; provocations, games, or role-plays that get at how AI sees, thinks, or makes; and exercises that help people figure out how they want to relate to AI in their own practice. WHAT YOU GET: your recipe formatted and credited to you in the first edition of Grain, a print copy when it lands, and an invite to the launch. Everything is published under CC BY-SA 4.0 so the work can travel and you keep authorship. FEE: this round offers a need-based fee for freelance and independent practitioners of EUR 150 per recipe; if making the time would be hard without it, email hello@aixdesign.co with your proposal. After this round the call stays open but the fee will not. LIVE SESSION: an optional 30-minute live session on Wednesday 12 August 2026 at 16:00 CEST lets you bring a rough exercise and turn it into a recipe on the spot with the Grain Recipe Converter (online, sign up at luma.com/84w148in). Submissions are open until the end of August 2026; Grain publishes March 2027. Submit via the form; questions to hello@aixdesign.co.

  7. AIxDESIGN x KB: Open Call for Sessions, (Art)works and Performances

    AIxDESIGN, co-presented with the Koninklijke Bibliotheek (KB), National Library of the Netherlands · KB, The Hague, Netherlands (in-person exhibition and programme); pitches welcome from people based anywhere · Deadline: 31 Aug 2026 · Award: Fees by track: opening-night experience EUR 1,500 to 2,000; a session in the events programme EUR 2,000 to 2,500; an existing (art)work for the exhibition EUR 200 to 400 honorarium (including licensing and production). The fee stays the same wherever you are based.

    AIxDESIGN and the KB (Koninklijke Bibliotheek, the National Library of the Netherlands) are building an exhibition and public programme about AI, made from the national collection, at the KB in The Hague. Nadia Piet is Creative Lead with the AIxDESIGN community, co-presented with the KB. The premise: long before neural nets, libraries were the machines we built to organise human knowledge, and the show uses the KB's collection to look at what AI is, where it comes from, and how it connects to the older story of how we store, sort, and search what we know. Part of the show lives in the KB Atelier, with talks, workshops, and screenings alongside, intended as AI literacy you can wander into. WHAT THEY WANT: work that engages AI in some way (critical, creative, hands-on, or playful); bonus if it also speaks to archives, libraries, or how we store and sort knowledge, though you do not need to touch the KB's collection to take part. You may pitch to more than one track: (A) SOMETHING FOR THE OPENING NIGHT, a performance, talk, screening, tour, ritual, booth, game, or other experience for the November 2026 opening, fee EUR 1,500 to 2,000; (B) A SESSION IN THE EVENTS PROGRAMME, a workshop or other session once during the three-month run, around 2.5 to 4 hours, fee EUR 2,000 to 2,500; (C) AN (ART)WORK FOR THE EXHIBITION, existing work in any format (installation, sculpture, zine stand, a corner of your own) sturdy enough for a public area with daily traffic, honorarium EUR 200 to 400 including licensing and production; (D) SOMETHING ELSE ENTIRELY, pitch it anyway. WHO: artists, designers, researchers, technologists, performers, musicians, archivists, or educators working around AI, data, libraries, or cultural heritage; no finished idea or polished CV needed, just a clear, specific pitch. You can be based anywhere and they especially welcome people outside the Netherlands; the show is in-person in The Hague and the fee stays the same wherever you are coming from. HOW TO APPLY: send a short pitch through the form (which track, your idea in a paragraph or two, roughly what it would need, and a link to your work); takes about five minutes, no full application required. Deadline: end of August 2026. Questions to hello@aixdesign.co.

    Prototype Fund Switzerland (Opendata.ch) · Switzerland; applicants must be eligible to work in Switzerland. · Deadline: 07 Sep 2026 · Award: Up to CHF 50,000 per project, plus coaching, mentoring and workshops and access to a tech/policy/society network. No application fee.

    Since 2020 the Prototype Fund Switzerland has strengthened the common good with open-source, public-interest technology, supporting interdisciplinary teams to develop and test early-stage projects that show how open source, data and digital technologies such as AI can create real societal value. The 2026-2027 edition focuses on Responsible and Sustainable AI: building the path toward a more responsible and sustainable AI, tech and digitalization landscape, open source and beyond. THIS YEAR'S FOCUS: projects that reduce resource use, energy consumption or emissions; strengthen accountability, transparency and governance; and contribute to resilient and trustworthy digital systems, including sustainable digitalization and digital sufficiency, public-interest AI and data infrastructures, and tools for governance, oversight or digital sovereignty. PUBLIC INTEREST TECH areas span sustainability and climate, participation and civic tech, education and literacy, health and inclusion, and open knowledge and data. WHO THEY FUND: individuals and small teams open to open source, AI and iterative experimentation, eager to combine technical capability with societal and political awareness, willing to share learnings, motivated to work on pressing societal problems through technology, with an early-stage but well-thought-out concept, and eligible to work in Switzerland. SUPPORT: up to CHF 50,000 in funding, a 4-month prototyping phase, coaching, mentoring and workshops, and access to a strong network across tech, policy and society. TIMELINE: applications 21 July to 7 September 2026; rolling jury selection August-September 2026; prototyping mid-October 2026 to mid-February 2027; outputs and learnings November 2026 to July 2027. Questions: info@prototypefund.ch.

  12. CHAI: Research Fellowship (Center for Human-Compatible AI, UC Berkeley)

    Center for Human-Compatible AI (CHAI), UC Berkeley · Berkeley, California, USA (in-person) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Salary commensurate with qualifications and experience, on UC Berkeley salary scales (negotiated). Funding for two years, renewable up to three given satisfactory performance. Eligible for visa sponsorship, with CHAI also contributing to visa costs for fellows and their dependents. No application fee.

    The CHAI Research Fellowship trains highly qualified postdoctoral researchers to advance beneficial AI, working with CHAI faculty Stuart Russell, Pieter Abbeel and Anca Dragan and collaborating with affiliate faculty at Berkeley AI Research and beyond. Fellows have broad freedom to pursue novel research toward provably beneficial AI systems in areas such as reasoning, decision making, learning, multi-agent systems and philosophical foundations, using probability theory, game theory and control theory. Current topics of interest include theoretical foundations of intelligent agent architecture, expressive formal languages for probability models (including probabilistic programming), decision making over long time scales, cumulative lifelong learning, human-in-the-loop planning and reinforcement learning, human-robot interaction, the structure of human preferences, acting on behalf of multiple humans, robust cooperation in heterogeneous multi-agent systems, and mechanism design for human-machine systems. Fellows may also lead collaborations, advise junior researchers, and teach. QUALIFICATIONS: a PhD (or about to be obtained) in a relevant technical discipline (computer science, statistics, mathematics, or theoretical economics) and a record of high-quality published research; prior work on the AI control problem is not required. International applicants are eligible (visa sponsorship provided). TO APPLY: submit via the CHAI application form a resume, a one-page statement of interest describing the research you would like to undertake, and the names and emails of two academic referees. Start date is flexible and applications are reviewed on a rolling basis (no fixed deadline). General enquiries: chai-info@berkeley.edu.
