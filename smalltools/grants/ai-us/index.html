Open calls

  1. GovAI U.S. AI Policy Program

    Centre for the Governance of AI (GovAI) · Washington, D.C., USA (hybrid; remote participation available) · Deadline: 05 Jul 2026 · Award: Free to participate; in-person attendees provided dinner (value kept at or below USD 20 per session)

    The GovAI U.S. AI Policy Program is a bipartisan, part-time, 12-week program designed to accelerate or launch careers in U.S. AI policy. Open to U.S. policy professionals across government and think tanks, it builds a technically-informed understanding of the AI policy landscape through guided discussion sessions, with an emphasis on general-purpose AI. Topics include how general-purpose AI systems are developed; inputs to AI development such as energy, chips, and data centers; AI's effects on productivity, scientific discovery, and economic growth; national security and great power competition; corporate self-governance; AI regulation in other jurisdictions; and federal and state AI regulatory proposals in the U.S. Participants also have weekly opportunities to question world-leading experts; prior guest speakers have included White House OSTP staffers, former frontier-lab employees, and people who helped draft U.S. state- and federal-level AI policy proposals. The program runs early September to early December 2026 (break for Thanksgiving week) with a roughly 5-hour weekly commitment: two hours of synchronous evening sessions (EST), held in-person at GovAI's D.C. office near DuPont Circle or remotely with cameras on, plus three hours of independent reading and podcasts from a free syllabus. To list completion on a resume, participants must attend 9 of 12 sessions, write a short policy memo or op-ed, and pass an interview-based exam. Eligible participants include executive and legislative branch staff (fellows, interns, detailees, military personnel, national laboratory personnel) and think-tank policy researchers and affiliates, across a range of seniority levels, with preference for the D.C. metropolitan area; participants need not currently cover AI but should aspire to a career in U.S. tech or national security policy. Selection has two stages: an online application with six biographical questions, a resume, two verbal reasoning questions, and an approximately 7-minute video; followed by a 30-minute interview in July, with final decisions in early August.

  2. Technology Fellow: AI and Philanthropy

    Ford Foundation · New York, New York, USA · Deadline: 06 Jul 2026 · Award: USD 166,000 to 170,000 annual salary, plus comprehensive benefits and retirement contributions of up to 13%

    The Ford Foundation seeks a two-year Technology Fellow for AI and Philanthropy, an internally-facing role focused on cross-programmatic learning and advisory support. Reporting to the Director, Technology and Society, the AI Tech Fellow will support Foundation staff in building and adapting internal frameworks for evaluating AI use in ways that prioritize mission and strategy and address ethical, sociotechnical, and operational considerations. Responsibilities include designing and curating hands-on trainings, workshops, and learning sessions to help staff build practical familiarity with AI tools and critically assess their value and efficacy; creating accessible documentation and guides on mission-aligned AI experimentation; curating sociotechnical conversations on how philanthropy and the nonprofit sector are experimenting with AI in ways that respect privacy, safety, and equity; monitoring emerging global AI developments and providing timely briefings to program teams; conducting landscape research on AI initiatives in the philanthropy and social justice sectors; and building relationships with AI researchers, practitioners, grantees and partners working on mission-enabling AI. The Technology Fellows Program is administered by Ford's Technology and Society Program and is designed to increase sociotechnical capacity within the Foundation. Requirements include a minimum of 7 years working within public-interest, government, civil society, or commercial technology sectors; a baccalaureate degree or equivalent experience; demonstrated sociotechnical expertise in artificial intelligence and related technologies including machine learning systems and generative AI and their societal implications; and experience designing, implementing or supporting AI applications and tools in a complex global setting. Hybrid role based in New York (three days per week in office). Not eligible for visa sponsorship; candidates must be authorized to work in the United States. Submit both a resume and a cover letter to be considered.

  3. Frame Fellowship 2.0 (Residential Accelerator for AI Safety Content Creators) 2026

    Frame Fellowship · San Francisco, USA; residential (in person). · Deadline: 10 Jul 2026 · Award: USD 10,000 stipend for the 10 weeks, plus accommodation, meals and flights covered. Access to production-ready studios in San Francisco, a mentor and creator network, and connections to funding, sponsorship and partnership opportunities at organizations such as FLI, CAIS and Seismic. Top performers may be eligible for additional grant opportunities beyond the program. No application fee.

    Frame Fellowship is a 10-week residential accelerator in San Francisco for content creators educating the public on AI safety. Frame's premise is that content creators can solve a long-standing bottleneck of expanding reach and trust beyond the AI safety and effective altruism (EA) bubble; creators today hack attention, empower mass action and shape culture. Past Frame Fellows include award-winning filmmakers, journalists from leading newsrooms and ex-activists who transitioned to educating, empowering and preparing their communities for the impacts of AGI. Frame helps amplify their impact to millions of people, connects them to funding and sponsorship opportunities at organizations such as FLI, CAIS and Seismic, and helps establish their path as a leading content creator. This cohort runs 10 weeks with a stronger mentor and creator network, access to production-ready studios in San Francisco, housing, food and a stipend, plus connections to funding and partnership opportunities after the program. TWO TRACKS: (1) Independent Track, build your own channel and become a defining voice on a topic; (2) Amplifier Track, embed with an AI org/company and grow their reach. SUPPORT: Frame covers accommodation, meals, flights and a USD 10,000 stipend for the 10 weeks so fellows can go all in; top performers may be eligible for additional grant opportunities beyond the program. Early deadline: 10 July 2026. Apply at https://framefellowship.com/.

  4. AGI Governance Fellowship

    Johns Hopkins University, School of Government and Policy (SOGP) · Hopkins Bloomberg Center, Washington, D.C. (in-person only) · Deadline: 15 Jul 2026 · Award: Fully funded: travel, accommodation, and meals covered.

    The AGI Governance Fellowship is the inaugural three-week intensive run by Johns Hopkins University's School of Government and Policy (SOGP) to develop the next generation of leaders in AI governance. COVERS: frontier AI legislation at state and federal levels; building societal resilience amid rapid capability growth; democratic practices in AGI alignment; and designing new governing institutions for the AGI era. WHO SHOULD APPLY: early-career researchers and practitioners (within roughly eight years of undergrad) with solid foundations in AI governance, including congressional staffers, AGI company employees, recent CS or policy PhDs, and nonprofit/civil society professionals; a postgraduate degree or equivalent experience is required. This is a capstone program for rising leaders, not an intro course. FUNDING: travel, accommodation, and meals are fully covered. DATES: 8-25 September 2026 at the Hopkins Bloomberg Center in Washington, D.C. (in-person only). DEADLINE: apply by 15 July 2026 for full consideration; rolling applications accepted. APPLY: https://sogp.jh.edu/agi-governance-fellowship/

  5. Claude Corps Fellowship

    Anthropic (funded); CodePath (employer of record); Social Finance (evaluation) · USA; in-person at host nonprofit organizations across America (placement location varies; relocation support available). · Deadline: 17 Jul 2026 · Award: $85,000 full-time annual salary plus benefits, expansive Claude token budget, and relocation support as needed. No application fee.

    Claude Corps is a national fellowship program from Anthropic placing early-career fellows full-time at nonprofits across America for one year, with the goal of extending AI's benefits to communities and building fellows' AI skills. Anthropic funds the program and provides Claude expertise; CodePath (America's largest collegiate CS education provider) is the fellows' official employer of record and leads programming; Social Finance leads measurement and evaluation. AMOUNT: $85,000 full-time salary plus benefits; expansive Claude token budget; relocation support as needed. FELLOW EXPERIENCE: intensive Claude training at the start; five hours of ongoing weekly training throughout; support from a CodePath mentor; Anthropic office hours for technical questions; and professional guidance from the host organization manager. SAMPLE HOST ORGANIZATIONS: Braven (Chicago), Code the Dream (Durham NC), Heartland Forward (Bentonville AR), Montgomery County Food Bank (Conroe TX), Reef Environmental Education Foundation (Key Largo FL), StriveTogether (Cincinnati), YMCA of Greater Charlotte, and more than 400 nonprofits total. ELIGIBILITY: anyone over 18 with under two years of full-time work experience, regardless of educational background; must be authorized to work in the US; must be comfortable working with Claude and willing to relocate if necessary. COHORTS: Cohort 1 (100 fellows, starts October 2026) -- applications close 17 July 2026; Cohort 2 (starts January 2027) and Cohort 3 (starts August 2027) -- rolling applications. Apply at https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-corps.

  6. Genesis Mission AI Fellowship 2026

    Horizon Institute for Public Service (with Renaissance Philanthropy, SeedAI, and Fulcrum Science) · United States. Fellows are forward deployed to US Department of Energy national labs (initial partners: Idaho National Laboratory, Brookhaven National Laboratory, Princeton Plasma Physics Laboratory). Remote or in-person; on-site travel at least once a quarter. Must be eligible to work in the US (no visa sponsorship; US green card holders eligible). · Deadline: 31 Jul 2026 · Award: Prorated total annual compensation of USD 200,000.

    The Horizon Institute for Public Service, Renaissance Philanthropy, SeedAI, and Fulcrum Science are partnering to launch a fellowship that embeds technical AI talent across the US Department of Energy national labs to advance the goals of the Genesis Mission, DOE's flagship science and technology initiative launched in late 2025. The Genesis Mission identifies 26 national science and technology challenges critical to US leadership and envisions a national platform connecting supercomputers, experimental facilities, AI systems, and unique datasets, with DOE's 17 national labs playing a key role. Genesis Mission AI Fellows are forward deployed to DOE national labs to bring frontier AI expertise to high-priority scientific challenges. As core members of the technical staff on a Genesis Mission project, fellows work alongside national lab scientists and partners and apply AI to national priorities, from building cutting-edge models of plasma dynamics in pursuit of controlled fusion to advancing state-of-the-art models for foundational materials discovery. Initial national lab partners include Idaho National Laboratory, Brookhaven National Laboratory, and the Princeton Plasma Physics Laboratory; additional partners may be announced later. COMPENSATION/DURATION: 9-12 months starting fall 2026, with a prorated total annual compensation of USD 200,000; fellows are expected to travel to their national lab site at least once a quarter but may work on-site if they prefer. ELIGIBILITY: competitive applicants should have at least 2 years of industry experience building AI models or agents (those with industry experience building AI applications or data infrastructure will also be considered); scientific domain expertise is not required, but any placement will involve some training and study in a specific scientific discipline or energy topic, supported by expert colleagues at the host institution. All fellows must be eligible to work in the United States for the duration of the placement; US employment visas cannot be sponsored, but US permanent residents (green card holders) are eligible. APPLICATION: initial form requires a CV or resume (max 2 pages) and short-answer questions on motivations and experiences (max 200 words); successful applicants complete interviews through partner organizations and national labs, and selected fellows are matched to partner labs by scientific domain and availability. The review uses evidence-based best practices including blind grading of application materials. TIMELINE: application deadline 31 July 2026; final fellow selection August-September 2026; expected start fall 2026.

  7. ZwillGen Fellowship Program 2027-2028 (Technology Law)

    ZwillGen · Washington, D.C., USA (must work out of the DC office) · Deadline: 31 Aug 2026 · Award: Salary USD 115,000 to USD 125,000, with a reduced annual billable target (1,250 hours) to create space for writing, professional development, and policy engagement. No application fee.

    The ZwillGen Fellowship is a 12-month position for recent law-school graduates and early-career attorneys interested in working at the intersection of law, technology, and public policy. Fellows work alongside attorneys on matters involving privacy, cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, online platforms, surveillance, digital advertising, fintech, online safety, and technology regulation, contributing to client counseling, investigations, litigation, and emerging regulatory matters, as well as research, writing, and speaking. The program includes a reduced billable target (1,250 hours) to make room for thought leadership and engagement with the technology policy, privacy, and civil liberties community. ELIGIBILITY: must have graduated from law school before the program starts; must have taken or plan to take a bar exam near the start of the Fellowship; must be able to work out of the DC office and be eligible to apply for admission to the DC Bar. Candidates primarily interested in traditional IP practice may find it less aligned. A small number of applicants may also be considered for the Hannah Schaller Memorial Skylark Fellowship (privacy-law focus, with a reduced 1,100-hour billable target for additional community engagement). Following the Fellowship, fellows may be invited into a full-time attorney role or supported in pursuing other technology law and policy opportunities. APPLY with a resume, law-school transcript, writing sample, and cover letter by 31 August 2026.

  8. CHAI: Research Fellowship (Center for Human-Compatible AI, UC Berkeley)

    Center for Human-Compatible AI (CHAI), UC Berkeley · Berkeley, California, USA (in-person) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Salary commensurate with qualifications and experience, on UC Berkeley salary scales (negotiated). Funding for two years, renewable up to three given satisfactory performance. Eligible for visa sponsorship, with CHAI also contributing to visa costs for fellows and their dependents. No application fee.

    The CHAI Research Fellowship trains highly qualified postdoctoral researchers to advance beneficial AI, working with CHAI faculty Stuart Russell, Pieter Abbeel and Anca Dragan and collaborating with affiliate faculty at Berkeley AI Research and beyond. Fellows have broad freedom to pursue novel research toward provably beneficial AI systems in areas such as reasoning, decision making, learning, multi-agent systems and philosophical foundations, using probability theory, game theory and control theory. Current topics of interest include theoretical foundations of intelligent agent architecture, expressive formal languages for probability models (including probabilistic programming), decision making over long time scales, cumulative lifelong learning, human-in-the-loop planning and reinforcement learning, human-robot interaction, the structure of human preferences, acting on behalf of multiple humans, robust cooperation in heterogeneous multi-agent systems, and mechanism design for human-machine systems. Fellows may also lead collaborations, advise junior researchers, and teach. QUALIFICATIONS: a PhD (or about to be obtained) in a relevant technical discipline (computer science, statistics, mathematics, or theoretical economics) and a record of high-quality published research; prior work on the AI control problem is not required. International applicants are eligible (visa sponsorship provided). TO APPLY: submit via the CHAI application form a resume, a one-page statement of interest describing the research you would like to undertake, and the names and emails of two academic referees. Start date is flexible and applications are reviewed on a rolling basis (no fixed deadline). General enquiries: chai-info@berkeley.edu.

  9. Perplexity Research Residency (San Francisco / Palo Alto)

    Perplexity · San Francisco or Palo Alto, California, USA (in-person, on-site) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: $220,000 annualized base salary (approximately $55,000 over the three-month residency), plus comprehensive benefits (health, dental, vision) and immigration/visa sponsorship based on individual circumstances. No application fee.

    Research residency at Perplexity for exceptional researchers, engineers and analysts from any quantitative or analytical discipline (physics, cognitive science, quantitative finance, theoretical mathematics, etc.) to apply their expertise to AI research challenges including agentic systems and human-AI interaction. ELIGIBILITY: no PhD or deep-learning-framework experience required; instead, strong mathematical foundations, demonstrated technical depth in a rigorous quantitative field, curiosity-driven research practice and clear communication skills. FORMAT: in-person residency paired with senior researcher mentors, with access to compute, research tools and proprietary datasets; residents are encouraged to publish at top-tier conferences and contribute to open source. Rolling start within ~8 weeks of acceptance. Applications via the Perplexity portal: resume/CV or portfolio plus a cover letter describing your interest in AI research at Perplexity; an optional research statement of project ideas is welcomed.

  10. GovAI DC Winter Fellowship 2027

    Centre for the Governance of AI (GovAI) · Washington, DC, USA; in-person, full-time (part-time possible for DC-based think tank or government staff). · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: $21,000 stipend for the three-month term, plus travel support to DC and weekday lunch provided. No visa sponsorship. No application fee.

    GovAI's DC Winter Fellowship is a three-month bipartisan in-person fellowship in Washington, DC, designed for individuals seeking to enter or advance in US AI governance and policy. The program emphasises bipartisan engagement, rigorous analysis, and practical policy relevance, with a focus on how AI policy is shaped within the US political system. Fellows conduct independent research on a topic of their choice with mentorship from a GovAI supervisor; the first two weeks explore the AI policy landscape before a research proposal is finalised. Outputs may include a report, white paper, journal article, op-ed, or blog post targeted at an audience relevant to AI policy. Fellows also attend expert Q&As, skills workshops, seminars, work-in-progress meetings, and social events, and are encouraged to discuss follow-on career opportunities. GovAI alumni have gone on to roles in US government bodies (NSC, OSTP, Senate, Department of Commerce, DHS); think tanks (CSIS, CSET, CNAS, RAND, Heritage Foundation, FAI); AI companies (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind); and universities (Yale, Stanford, MIT). AMOUNT: $21,000 stipend for three months, plus travel support to DC and weekday lunch. Part-time fellowship at a pro-rated salary available for DC-based think tank researchers, government staffers, or professionals doing AI governance work; pay accommodations possible. ELIGIBILITY: candidates must have the right to work in the US; no visa sponsorship. Open to a wide range of backgrounds (government, academia, industry, civil society); promising undergraduates welcome; no strict degree requirements. Relevant areas include public policy, political science, engineering, computer science, economics, biosecurity, cybersecurity, China studies, and risk management. SELECTION CRITERIA: quality and clarity of written work, relevant expertise, judgement, and team fit. APPLICATION PROCESS: (1) written application and automated abstract reasoning assessment; (2) paid remote work test (August 2026); (3) remote interview and reference checks (September 2026); decisions communicated October 2026. Application deadline not yet publicly stated; applications open June 2026 -- check programme page for the deadline.
