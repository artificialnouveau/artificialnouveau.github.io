    Mountainfilm · Application online; Telluride, Colorado, United States (fellowship trip during the festival). Mountainfilm actively encourages diversity behind the camera, and a portion of funding is set aside for two focus areas: projects featuring an LGBTQ+ protagonist or a story about overcoming personal challenges, societal stigmas, and/or community obstacles with a meaningful connection to the outdoors or nature-based pursuits; and projects exploring immigration or migration, or created by immigrant or first-generation immigrant filmmakers. AWARDS: individual Commitment Grants range from USD 3,000 to 6,000. EMERGING FILMMAKER FELLOWSHIP: one early-career filmmaker is selected from the Commitment Grant applicant pool for a yearlong mentorship that includes edit and story consultation, networking opportunities, a cash award, and a funded trip to Telluride during the festival over Memorial Day weekend (travel, lodging, and a Mountainfilm pass, applicable to the year following the award). Prior Commitment Grant recipients should not re-apply for the same project; previously declined projects may re-apply only with substantial changes. REQUIREMENTS: a video sample or trailer of the current project no longer than 10 minutes (password-protected Vimeo links preferred); the screener link must stay active and cannot be changed after submission. The Emerging Filmmaker application additionally requires a director bio, headshot, and cover letter. Recipients are expected to provide progress updates 2-3 times a year, include Mountainfilm recognition in credits, and discuss premiere consideration with the programming team. TIMELINE: applications are open July 1 to July 16, 2026 (closing 11:59 PM MDT), with notifications in late August or early September. Questions: entries@mountainfilm.org.
