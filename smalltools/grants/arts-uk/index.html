Visual and Media Arts Grants in the UK

Currently 18 active visual and media arts grants, fellowships and residencies open to applicants in the UK. Hand-curated and updated weekly. Almost every entry is funded; a few notable unpaid open calls and festival submissions are included as clearly flagged exceptions. Browse the list below, or use the interactive desk for filtering and shortlisting.

  1. Modern Art Oxford: Collaborative Micro-commissions Open Call 2026

    Modern Art Oxford · Oxford, UK. Open to collectives, duos, partnerships and collaborative groups based in the Arts Council England South East area (Oxfordshire, Berkshire, Buckinghamshire, Hampshire and Isle of Wight, Surrey, Kent, East Sussex, West Sussex). · Deadline: 26 Jul 2026 · Award: GBP 1,000 artist fee per selected collective, plus up to GBP 500 production budget, curatorial mentorship from the Modern Art Oxford team, four months of public exhibition display, professional installation photography, promotion, technical and production support, artwork transport support within the South East, peer networking, and inclusion in a public preview event. No application fee.

    Modern Art Oxford marks its 60th anniversary with an open call for three collaborative micro-commissions responding to the question: how can collective practice/s help us to embrace uncertainty and complexity? Collectives, duos, partnerships and collaborative groups based across the South East of England are invited to propose new small-scale experimental works for a group exhibition in the Ground Floor Gallery, running alongside a major solo exhibition by Mike Nelson, who is also on the selection panel. Proposals may take the form of multidisciplinary research, site-responsive work, grassroots organising, performance, visual art, sound, poetry or other creative practices; ideas should respond to the theme of collective practice and uncertainty, explore collaborative approaches, experiment across disciplines, be achievable within the budget and timeline, and be original to this open call (building on existing research is fine). WHO: groups of two or more people, including established artist collectives, newly formed collaborations, interdisciplinary partnerships, informal or community-led groups, and family collaborations, based in the Arts Council England South East area (Oxfordshire, Berkshire, Buckinghamshire, Hampshire and Isle of Wight, Surrey, Kent, East Sussex, West Sussex). SUPPORT: each of the three selected collectives receives a GBP 1,000 artist fee, up to GBP 500 production budget, curatorial mentorship, four months of exhibition display, installation photography, promotion, technical and production support, and transport support within the South East. TIMELINE: online briefing and Q&A 15 June 2026 (email creativelearning@modernartoxford.org.uk for the link); selection confirmed 14 August 2026; production August to November 2026; preview party 4 December 2026; exhibition 5 December 2026 to April 2027. Apply via Google Forms; an information pack PDF and FAQ are on the call page. DEADLINE: Sunday 26 July 2026, 23:59.

  2. Arts Council of Wales: Festivals (2026 rounds)

    Arts Council of Wales · Festival must take place in Wales and demonstrate cultural value to the local community. Welsh language integration expected. · Deadline: 29 Jul 2026 · Award: GBP 500 to 100,000 across four tiers: Small up to GBP 10,000; Large up to GBP 35,000 (1+ year track record); Large up to GBP 60,000 (3+ years, regional significance, 2+ day programme); Large up to GBP 100,000 (5+ years, national significance). Co-financing mandatory: the Council funds a maximum of 75 percent of eligible costs, so at least 25 percent of project income must come from non-Arts Council and non-Lottery sources, of which no more than 10 percent may be in-kind. No application fee.

    Festivals is one of the new National Lottery programmes Arts Council of Wales launched on 13 April 2026, and it is the clearest organisation-facing route for funding a public festival in Wales. It supports focused, time-limited festivals delivering high-quality arts programmes to broad audiences, in a single artform or mixed artforms, with preference given to festivals that commission, create and present new artistic work. Grants run GBP 500 to 100,000 across four tiers, with the tier ceiling tied to how long the festival has been established. Match funding is mandatory: the Council covers at most 75 percent of eligible costs and in-kind support is capped at 10 percent. There are two deadlines in 2026, both at 1pm: Wednesday 29 July 2026 and Wednesday 16 December 2026. TIMING RULE: a minimum of twelve working weeks must fall between the deadline and the project start date, so activity applied for in the July round cannot begin before roughly late October 2026. Retrospective costs are ineligible. Individual artists cannot apply.

  3. Arts Council of Wales: Create and Engage (2026 rounds)

    Arts Council of Wales · Projects must take place mainly in Wales. Individual applicants must be Wales-based; organisations outside the UK may take part as partners provided a UK-based eligible organisation leads. · Deadline: 29 Jul 2026 · Award: Small awards up to GBP 10,000; Large awards GBP 10,001 to 60,000; exceptionally up to GBP 100,000 with prior approval from an Arts Council manager. At least 10 percent of project costs must come from non-Arts Council and non-Lottery sources. No application fee.

    Create and Engage is Arts Council of Wales's general-purpose National Lottery programme for making and presenting arts work, launched alongside Festivals in April 2026, and is the natural fit for exhibitions, public programmes, touring and participatory events that are not structured as a festival. It funds creation, presentation, programming, touring, community participation and, for organisations only, professional development. Small awards go up to GBP 10,000 and large awards run GBP 10,001 to 60,000, with up to GBP 100,000 possible in exceptional cases with advance manager approval. At least 10 percent of project costs must come from non-Council, non-Lottery sources, a notably lighter co-financing burden than the Festivals fund's 25 percent. TIMING RULES: Small Create and Engage is rolling with no set deadlines but requires a minimum of 10 working weeks between submission and project start; Large Create and Engage has fixed 1pm deadlines on 29 July 2026, 30 September 2026 and 16 December 2026, with a minimum of 12 working weeks between deadline and project start. Non-UK organisations can participate as partners but cannot lead. Applicants cannot have an application in to more than one Arts Council fund at the same time.

  4. Arts Council of Northern Ireland: Artists' International Development Fund for Organisations (2026)

    Arts Council of Northern Ireland · Applicant organisations must be based in Northern Ireland; the funded activity takes place internationally, with an overseas partner or host. · Deadline: 30 Jul 2026 · Award: GBP 1,000 to 7,000. Covers art materials, residency costs, travel (mileage at 25.7p per mile plus airfare), artistic and technical fees for production or post-production, venue hire, project equipment capped at 50 percent of the request value, and IT equipment up to GBP 1,200 for laptops or GBP 500 for tablets. No application fee.

    The Artists' International Development Fund (Organisations) is Arts Council of Northern Ireland's route for NI-based arts organisations to take a first substantive step into international working. It is the organisations counterpart to the individual-artist AIDF under the Support for the Individual Artist Programme, which is a separate scheme with its own amount band. Grants run GBP 1,000 to 7,000 and cover art materials, residency costs, travel including airfare and mileage, artistic and technical fees for production or post-production, venue hire, and equipment capped at 50 percent of the request value. The deadline is Thursday 30 July 2026 at 12:00 noon, and the online system closes precisely at noon. Applicants must be NI-based, must have received recognition for their work there, and must not already have extensive international experience. The application must evidence genuine interest from an overseas partner or host, so a confirmed international invitation, residency offer or presentation opportunity is effectively a precondition.

  5. Hugo Burge Foundation: Grants for Creative Individuals 2026

    Hugo Burge Foundation · United Kingdom · Deadline: 31 Jul 2026 · Award: Up to GBP 5,000 per individual (can fund up to 100% of a project under GBP 10,000). No application fee.

    Project grants from the Hugo Burge Foundation supporting individual creatives across all disciplines based in the UK, with a focus on craftsmanship, creative communities and education. The Creative Individuals stream awards up to GBP 5,000 and can cover up to 100% of a project budget under GBP 10,000. ELIGIBILITY: practitioners in all creative disciplines based across the UK. Applications open 1 June 2026 via Submittable and close 31 July 2026.

  6. CRASSH 25th Anniversary Exhibition: Knowledge in a Fractured World

    CRASSH, University of Cambridge · Cambridge, United Kingdom (Alison Richard Building). Open worldwide. · Deadline: 31 Jul 2026 · Award: Exhibition from 5 October to 11 December 2026; no commission taken on sales. Free to enter.

    The Centre for Research in the Arts, Social Sciences and Humanities (CRASSH) at the University of Cambridge marks its 25th anniversary with an open-call exhibition on the theme Knowledge in a Fractured World: how knowledge is produced, contested, and shared in a fragmented age. Accepts 2D, 3D, video, film, and sound work; a strong thematic fit for critical data and research-based practices. Open worldwide, free to enter, no commission on sales. Deadline 31 July 2026.

  7. Sarabande Foundation: Studio Residency 2026/27 (UK-based only)

    Sarabande Foundation · London, United Kingdom. Requires a UK address and right to work in the UK; no visa sponsorship. · Deadline: 01 Aug 2026 · Award: Heavily subsidised studio space (GBP 1 per sq ft per month including utilities), mentoring from industry experts, pro bono business support, and access to the Sarabande network. Free to apply.

    The Sarabande Foundation, founded by Lee Alexander McQueen, offers subsidised London studio residencies for creatives across disciplines (fine art, fashion, design, digital), with structured mentoring and pro bono expert support. ELIGIBILITY: applicants must have a UK address and the right to work in the UK; the foundation cannot sponsor visas. Deadline 1 August 2026.

  8. Henry Moore Foundation: Henry Moore Grants (Autumn 2026 round)

    Henry Moore Foundation · Anywhere. The Foundation welcomes applications from UK and international non-profit organisations. · Deadline: 01 Sep 2026 · Award: Up to GBP 20,000 for New Projects and Commissions, Acquisitions and Collections, and Long-Term Research. Conferences, Lectures and Publications up to GBP 5,000. Research and Travel (individuals) up to GBP 2,500. Roughly GBP 750,000 distributed a year. The Foundation does not normally fund whole project costs and expects other funding to have been applied for. No application fee.

    Henry Moore Grants fund exhibitions, exhibition catalogues, sculpture commissions, conferences, non-commercial publications, research, and the development of collections through acquisition, conservation, cataloguing and display. The programme distributes roughly GBP 750,000 a year across four assessment rounds, each with a one-month application window. Maximum awards are GBP 20,000 for New Projects and Commissions, GBP 5,000 for Conferences, Lectures and Publications, and GBP 2,500 for individual Research and Travel. Applicants must be non-profit organisations operating for at least 12 months, and both UK and international bodies may apply, which makes this unusually open among UK trusts. TIMING RULE: decisions arrive approximately six to eight weeks after each closing date, and projects funded in a given round cannot begin before a fixed date roughly four months after the deadline. Remaining 2026 windows are 1 August to 1 September 2026 (projects starting 1 January 2027 or later) and 1 November to 1 December 2026 (projects starting 1 April 2027 or later). The sculpture focus is a hard constraint, so media-arts or digital-culture projects qualify only where sculpture or expanded three-dimensional practice is genuinely central. Residencies and individual artists developing their own practice are excluded.

  9. Freelands Foundation: Learning Through Making Fund

    Freelands Foundation · UK (England, Northern Ireland, Scotland, Wales); activity must occur within the United Kingdom. · Deadline: 11 Sep 2026 · Award: Up to £25,000. No application fee.

    The Learning Through Making fund supports UK-based visual art organisations to bring opportunities for making and material exploration into their art education work. Freelands Foundation is looking for exploratory projects that embrace openness, collaboration, and iteration -- enabling organisations to work with materials in an expansive way, unpicking preconceptions about making and material literacy without predefined outcomes. ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES: providing materials, transport, or access to expertise and facilities; action-research trials such as a new series of workshops or events developing material literacy; adding a making-focused element to existing activity. Projects may range from a few days to several months, but must take place between 1 January and 31 December 2027. AMOUNT: up to £25,000; funding provided no earlier than December 2026. ELIGIBILITY: UK-based visual art organisations run with a charitable purpose and for public benefit; eligible types include charities registered in England and Wales, Scotland, or Northern Ireland; exempt charities; galleries, museums, or organisations within a Local Authority or larger charitable body (e.g. universities); and Community Interest Companies or Community Benefit Societies meeting specific criteria (charitable purposes, asset lock, no dividend distribution, ability to segregate restricted funds). Organisations with a prior Freelands Foundation grant relationship are eligible; you may not apply for activity already funded by Freelands Foundation. Individuals are not eligible. HOW TO APPLY: download the Application Form, Application Guidance, and optional Access Form from freelandsfoundation.co.uk; email completed form to grants@freelandsfoundation.co.uk with subject line 'LTM Fund - Application'; one additional page of images or diagrams may be included. APPLICATION SUPPORT SESSIONS (online): 9 July 2026, 10:30am-12pm; 12 August 2026, 10:30am-12pm. KEY DATES: applications open 17 June 2026; close 11 September 2026 at 12pm; outcomes shared 23 November 2026.

  10. Arts Council of Northern Ireland: National Lottery Rural Engagement Arts Programme (2026)

    Arts Council of Northern Ireland · Activity must target rural communities in Northern Ireland per the NISRA 2015 settlement classification. Organisations based outside Northern Ireland may apply if the project benefits rural NI residents. · Deadline: 24 Sep 2026 · Award: GBP 500 to 10,000 per project. Eligible costs include artist fees, transport, materials, venue hire, accessibility support, equipment (capped at 50 percent of the request value), volunteer expenses and evaluation. No application fee.

    The National Lottery Rural Engagement Arts Programme funds organisations to deliver arts activity in rural Northern Ireland, addressing isolation and social inclusion. It supports either a single one-off project or a multi-event public programme, making it usable for exhibitions, workshops, touring events and community-facing digital or media arts work. Grants range from GBP 500 to 10,000, covering artist fees, transport, materials, venue hire, accessibility support, volunteer expenses, evaluation, and equipment up to 50 percent of the request value. The deadline is Thursday 24 September 2026 at 12:00 noon, and the online system closes precisely at noon with no late or incomplete submissions accepted. TIMING RULE: funded activity must fall between 1 December 2026 and 30 November 2027, setting an effective earliest start date of roughly ten weeks after the deadline. Registered charities, formally constituted non-profit groups, local authorities and arts organisations are eligible, including bodies based outside Northern Ireland where the benefit accrues to rural NI residents. Consortium applications demonstrating partnership working are explicitly prioritised. The rural and community framing means creative-technology work qualifies only where framed for that audience.

  11. Art Fund and Jerwood Foundation: Jerwood Art Fund Commissions (2026 round)

    Art Fund (in partnership with Jerwood Foundation) · United Kingdom and the Channel Islands. · Deadline: 02 Oct 2026 · Award: GBP 70,000 to 100,000 per commission, two commissions awarded per year for three years. Intended to cover all delivery and presentation costs, so no co-financing requirement is stated. No application fee.

    Jerwood Art Fund Commissions is a partnership between Art Fund and the Jerwood Foundation supporting museums and galleries to commission early to mid-career artists to make work of exceptional innovation and quality. Two commissions of GBP 70,000 to 100,000 are awarded each year for three years, covering the full cost of delivering and presenting the work, which makes this one of the largest single commission awards available to UK public collections. All media are in scope, including textiles, sculpture, ceramics, film and photography. The resulting work is expected to enter a permanent public collection or be installed in the public realm. Applicants must be Accredited or provisionally Accredited public collections open at least half the week for six months of the year, and partnerships between institutions are welcomed. Applications for the 2026 round opened on 18 May 2026 and close on 2 October 2026, submitted as a completed Word form emailed to programmes@artfund.org. IMPORTANT: artists cannot apply directly and must be put forward by an eligible host institution. Unsuccessful applicants cannot reapply with the same project. The permanent-collection outcome requirement means this suits commissioned artworks rather than time-limited festivals.

  12. Jerwood Foundation: Arts Grants (2027 round)

    Jerwood Foundation · United Kingdom. Applicants must be UK registered charities or UK limited companies. · Deadline: 17 Mar 2027 · Award: No published minimum or maximum. Previous awards have reached up to GBP 200,000, but most grants are considerably smaller. Funds project and activity costs. No formal match-funding requirement, but a fundraising strategy must be submitted. No application fee.

    The Jerwood Foundation makes grants to UK organisations for public-facing arts activity across theatre, music, dance, visual arts and literature, including artist development programmes, commissions, residencies and projects involving the Jerwood Collection. Awards have historically gone as high as GBP 200,000, though the Foundation states most grants are much smaller. Applications are single-stage: a written proposal of no more than 10 sides of A4 excluding financial statements, sent by email to info@jerwood.org or by post, and it must include a summary sheet giving location, amount requested, timeline and audience figures. Applications missing that summary sheet are rejected automatically. The March 2026 deadline has passed; the next round opens 3 February 2027 at 9am and closes 17 March 2027 at 2pm, with decisions on 28 April 2027 and all applicants notified by 11 May 2027. TIMING RULE: activity funded in that round must start or open to the public between 1 July 2027 and 30 June 2028, so there is a long lead time and retrospective funding is impossible. Grants to individuals are not made, so artists must be supported through a host organisation. Core costs and capital work are explicitly out of scope, which makes this a good fit for discrete festivals, exhibitions and public programmes.

  13. Creative Scotland: National Lottery Open Fund for Organisations (rolling)

    Creative Scotland · Activity must take place in Scotland and benefit people in Scotland. Applicant organisations must be constituted and based in Scotland; organisations elsewhere in the UK with a UK-wide remit may apply for activity in Scotland; organisations outside the UK must have a UK-based partner lead the application. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: GBP 1,000 to 100,000. Covers a one-off project or production, creative research and development, a longer-term programme of work, or business and organisational development. No stated match-funding requirement and no application fee. Applicants must hold a UK bank account in the same name as the application.

    The National Lottery Open Fund for Organisations is Creative Scotland's main open-access grant for constituted arts, creative and cultural organisations, and it is genuinely rolling with no deadlines. It supports a specific one-off project or production, creative research and development, or a longer-term programme of work, and can also fund business sustainability and organisational development costs. Eligible organisations can apply for GBP 1,000 to 100,000 for projects lasting up to 18 months. TIMING RULES: decision lag is banded by request size, with GBP 1,000 to 20,000 decided within 10 weeks and GBP 20,000 to 100,000 within 14 weeks. The start date must be after the decision date, and the guidance recommends allowing a further 2 to 4 weeks for funding agreements and first payment. Critically for festivals, applicants are barred from starting activity, including advertising events or issuing calls for collaborators, before funding is confirmed, so a public programme should be planned to start roughly 12 to 18 weeks after submission. Only one application may be in assessment at any time. Non-UK organisations can be involved but cannot lead. Current Regularly Funded and Multi-Year Funded Organisations are excluded.

  14. Esmee Fairbairn Foundation: Community-led Art and Creativity (rolling)

    Esmee Fairbairn Foundation · United Kingdom. Work must directly benefit communities in the UK. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: GBP 30,000 minimum, no maximum. Funds core costs or project costs including staff salaries and overheads; unrestricted funding available to charities. No co-financing requirement stated. No application fee.

    Community-led Art and Creativity sits within Esmee Fairbairn's Creative, Confident Communities aim and is one of the Foundation's two arts-related priorities. It funds work where local people and local artists lead the creative and cultural activity in a place, emphasising cross-sector collaboration that delivers social, environmental and economic benefit alongside artistic outcomes. Cited examples include the Migration Museum's People's Panel, B Arts in Stoke and WeCanMake's Festival of Making Together, which confirms festivals and public programmes are in scope where genuinely community-directed. Applications are rolling with no deadlines, starting with an eligibility quiz and an expression of interest. The minimum grant is GBP 30,000 with no upper limit, and most awards run three to five years covering core or project costs including salaries and overheads. Applicants need at least GBP 100,000 annual turnover in the latest accounts and a formal constitution, charitable or non-charitable so long as the work is charitable. This is highly competitive: in 2025 only around 5 percent of initial expressions of interest converted into funded grants, so long-term systems-change framing matters more than the individual event. Esmee's other arts priority, Arts and Creativity Making Change, is currently closed.

  15. Paul Hamlyn Foundation: Arts-based Learning Fund (rolling)

    Paul Hamlyn Foundation · United Kingdom. Activity must take place with UK state-funded schools, SEND specialist schools, FE colleges or alternative provision settings. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: GBP 30,000 to 300,000 total, capped at GBP 100,000 per year. Covers project costs and associated core and overhead costs of arts-education partnership work. No stated co-financing requirement. No application fee.

    The Arts-based Learning Fund supports sustained partnerships between arts and cultural organisations and state-funded educational settings so that arts-based approaches become embedded in how young people learn. Grants run GBP 30,000 to 300,000 over one to four years, with a ceiling of GBP 100,000 in any single year, and can cover both project delivery and a fair share of the applicant's core costs. Applicants must be arts and cultural organisations (charities, community organisations, social enterprises, not-for-profits) applying in partnership with at least one state-funded educational setting. Applications are accepted on a rolling basis with no deadlines; an optional enquiry call with a grants manager is encouraged before applying. The process is two-stage: an initial online application, then, if shortlisted, further information plus a phone or video conversation, with a final decision by a grants panel. TIMING RULE: applicants should expect a response within about four months, and successful applicants should allow roughly six months from application to receiving funds, so projects should not be planned to start sooner than that. The Foundation explicitly encourages applications from organisations led by people most impacted by racism, ableism, classism, sexism, xenophobia, homophobia and transphobia. IMPORTANT: this is the only Paul Hamlyn arts route open to new applicants in 2026, since the main Arts Fund is suspended to new applications until at least April 2027.

  16. Art Fund: Commission Grants (rolling)

    Art Fund · United Kingdom and the Channel Islands. Applicant institutions must be based there. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Small grant of GBP 7,500 or less where the request is GBP 20,000 or under and total project cost is GBP 1,000,000 or under; main grant above GBP 7,500 for larger requests. Art Fund normally funds only part of the cost, with no fixed percentage stated. No application fee.

    Art Fund Commission Grants support accredited public collections to commission new artworks that will enter a permanent collection or archive, or be installed in the public realm. The scheme splits into two routes with different timing: small grants of GBP 7,500 or less are assessed on a rolling basis with no strict deadline and a decision in approximately six to eight weeks from submission, while main grants above GBP 7,500 go to trustees' meetings held in February, May, July, October and December, with a decision within one week after the relevant meeting. Art Fund normally funds only part of the cost, so applicants should expect to bring other income. Eligibility is tightly drawn: the applicant must be a public museum, gallery, historic house, library or archive that is open at least half the week for at least six months of the year and holds at least provisional Arts Council Accreditation. IMPORTANT: artists cannot apply directly and must be nominated by an eligible host institution. A key constraint for temporary programming is that the commissioned work must result in a permanent acquisition or archive of material and be publicly accessible for a minimum of three years, so this funds durable commissions rather than time-limited festival editions. Not to be confused with VIA Art Fund, a separate US funder.

  17. BFI Doc Society: Features Production Fund (UK, Documentary)

    BFI Doc Society · UK · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Up to £150,000 non-recoupable grant. No application fee.

    The BFI Doc Society Features Production Fund (delivered via BFI with Doc Society as delegate partner) supports UK filmmakers producing non-fiction feature films intended for theatrical release. Two strands: Directions (first- or second-time directors) and Momentum (established talent). AMOUNT: up to £150,000 non-recoupable grant (the total Doc Society contribution per project cannot exceed £150,000). ELIGIBILITY: UK-based filmmakers, both emerging and established directors; experienced and debut producers eligible; projects must meet at least three of six stated priorities: equity/diversity, audience impact, talent development, creative risk, UK-wide access, and environmental sustainability. PROCESS: two-stage — submit an Expression of Interest, then a Full Application if invited. Rolling intake year-round. Opened May 2026. APPLY via BFI Doc Society website. Contact: hellobfi@docsociety.org.

  18. Hypha Studios: Speculative Exhibition Proposals (rolling)

    Hypha Studios · London and across the United Kingdom (meanwhile-use spaces). · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Free gallery/exhibition space in vacant high-street properties when scheduling gaps open; proposals are kept on file. Free to apply.

    Hypha Studios matches artists with free meanwhile-use spaces across London and the UK. Speculative exhibition proposals are accepted on a rolling basis and kept on file until a suitable space opens. No geographic restriction is stated, but applicants practically need to be able to install and attend in the UK.
