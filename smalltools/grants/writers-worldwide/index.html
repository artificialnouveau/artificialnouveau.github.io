Open calls

  1. Jamverse Jam (Protocolized worldbuilding contest)

    Protocolized (Summer of Protocols) · Online / Worldwide · Deadline: 31 Jul 2026 · Award: One USD 1,000 grand prize plus at least 10 additional USD 200 prizes; winning artifacts are published on the Jamverse website. No entry fee.

    Protocolized's fourth contest invites artists and writers (storytellers, poets, comic artists) to build, extend, and connect the Jamverse, a shared near-future science fiction universe of four intersecting story cycles (The Stockton Chronicles, Legends and Ledgers, UET-Trainverse, and Zoothesia). ELIGIBILITY: open to anyone; you may submit up to three in-universe worldbuilding artifacts, and the same creator can win multiple prizes. REQUIREMENTS: each artifact must be under 1,000 words (200-700 is the sweet spot) or a visual equivalent (comic panel, poster, advertisement, etc.) and must include at least one element drawn from one of the four sub-world 'toy boxes', a surprising 'traffic jam' (an emergent, ah-ha consequence of a technology or protocol), and a relation to the theme of Stigmergy (indirect coordination through modifying a shared environment). Artifact types can include comic panels, job postings, poems, forum threads, historic documents, or encyclopedia entries. APPLY: submit each artifact as a public Google Doc via the contest form, including the artifact, a one-paragraph explanation of the worlds/toy-box components used and how the piece relates to stigmergy and explores a traffic jam, and, if AI was used, documentation of your AI/LLM protocol. PRIZES: one USD 1,000 grand prize and at least 10 USD 200 prizes, all published on the Jamverse website. DEADLINE: 31 July 2026. Contest rules, the current Jamverse stories, and starter materials are on the Jamverse website.

  2. Berggruen Prize Essay Competition 2026: A New Axial Age?

    Berggruen Institute · Remote/online; open to the public worldwide (English and Chinese submissions) · Deadline: 17 Aug 2026 · Award: USD 50,000 for the winning English essay and USD 50,000 for the winning Chinese essay. If there is more than one winner in a language category, the prize is split evenly. Winning essays are published through BI Press or Noema. No entry fee.

    Annual essay competition from the Berggruen Institute, a companion to the Berggruen Prize for Philosophy & Culture, seeking new thinking and cross-cultural perspectives on fundamental philosophical questions of significance for contemporary life and the future. It looks for essays in the tradition of Rousseau, Montaigne, and Emerson (models cited include Frankfurt, Nagel, Sontag, Fukuyama, and recent Noema pieces), not peer-reviewed academic work: novel ideas, clearly argued in compelling ways for intellectually serious readers. THEME for 2026: 'A New Axial Age?' Referencing Karl Jaspers's Axial Age (c. 800-200 BCE), the prompt asks whether civilisation is again at a 'hinge of history' undergoing a pivotal transformation of consciousness driven by rapid technological change and its social, economic, and political consequences. Suggested (non-exhaustive) angles: the foundational ideas and institutions underpinning present and future societies; the philosophical implications of the current era; the future of human evolution and life amid the ecological crisis; shared conceptual foundations for a common future; global transformation driven by technology and the rise of AI; the end of the global order and its ramifications; and the future of life amid biotechnology and synthetic life. Entrants may choose their own topic; for 2026 creative fiction is accepted. ELIGIBILITY: open to the public; not for academic papers and not governed by academic disciplinary standards or affiliations. Length up to 10,000 words in English or 15,000 characters in Chinese (excluding diagrams, tables, endnotes, bibliography, or authorship declaration). One submission per year per entrant, in either English or Chinese; co-authorship allowed. Submissions must be original and previously unpublished. AI USAGE: generative AI must not be used to create content or substitute original thought, and entrants must engage primary sources rather than AI summaries; AI is permitted only for proofreading/grammar, as quoted supporting material when AI itself is the subject of the research, or for translating an essay into English or Chinese, and all AI use (including AI-assisted translation) must be disclosed on the front page or the entry is disqualified. JUDGING: originality, clarity, and style. Essays are due by 23:59 PT on 17 August 2026; PDF only. Chinese-language submissions use a separate page. Inquiries to essay@berggruen.org.

  3. OIF: Support for Artist Mobility and Circulation of Cultural Goods 2026 (Francophonie)

    Organisation internationale de la Francophonie (OIF) · Francophone zone (OIF member states globally) · Deadline: 31 Aug 2026 · Award: Up to EUR 5,000 for artist mobility (travel, accommodation, visas, logistics). Up to EUR 2,500 for circulation of cultural goods (excess baggage, freight, customs). Both can be combined. No application fee.

    The OIF Support for Artist Mobility and Circulation of Cultural Goods programme provides grants of up to EUR 5,000 for mobility (travel, accommodation, visa fees, logistics) and up to EUR 2,500 for circulation of cultural goods (excess baggage, freight, customs) within the Francophone zone; both can be combined. ELIGIBILITY: professional artists and established artistic collectives with a demonstrated connection to the Francophone space (by nationality, working language, or artistic collaborations), residing in an OIF member state, with minimum 2 years of experience in the cultural or artistic sector; must directly manage the project (not act as intermediaries); one application per individual per call. ELIGIBLE DISCIPLINES: live performance (music, theatre, dance, comedy, storytelling), cinema/audiovisual, literature/publishing, and visual arts. PRIORITY given to projects promoting gender equality, women artists, creators under 35, and South-South mobility. Projects must take place between 25 March and 30 November 2026. Multiple selection sessions throughout the year; call open until 31 August 2026. Information webinars: 11 March, 13 May, and 8 July 2026 at 3 PM Paris time. Contact: culture.dlc@francophonie.org. Apply at https://appelsaprojets.francophonie.org/fr/applications/soutien-a-la-circulation-des-biens-et-mobilite-des-artistes.

  4. International Promotion: Institutions Outside Switzerland (Literature)

    Pro Helvetia (Swiss Arts Council) · Event organizers, publishing houses, professional associations, and digital platforms based outside Switzerland; projects must feature Swiss books/authors/translators and Swiss partners. · Deadline: 01 Sep 2026 · Award: Maximum CHF 25,000. No application fee.

    Pro Helvetia's international promotion grant (institutions outside Switzerland variant) supports organizations abroad promoting contemporary Swiss literary works and authors at book fairs, festivals, and events (analogue, digital, or hybrid). Open to event organizers, publishing houses, professional associations, or digital platforms based outside Switzerland; no Swiss residency or nationality is required, but projects must feature Swiss books, authors, or translators together with Swiss partner organizations. AMOUNT: maximum CHF 25,000. Deadlines twice a year: 1 March (23:59 CET) and 1 September (23:59 CEST). Apply via the Pro Helvetia portal.

  5. Alfred P. Sloan Foundation: Book Grants (Public Understanding of Science and Technology)

    Alfred P. Sloan Foundation · Remote / anywhere (US foundation; grants paid directly to authors or via a nonprofit host) · Deadline: 01 Sep 2026 · Award: Typically up to $60,000 per book grant

    The one Sloan program that funds individuals directly: grants for authors researching and writing nonfiction books that bring science and technology to a general audience. Over 200 authors supported since 1996. Favoured topics include elucidating confusing or controversial scientific subjects, profiles of figures in science and technology, science-technology-art connections, women and science, and the culture and philosophy of science. Start with a letter of inquiry to program director Doron Weber; full proposals are by invitation only. Reviewed three times a year: LOIs by 1 September (decision by 15 February), 1 January (by 31 May) and 1 May (by 31 October). No application fee.

  6. Printed Matter Publisher Work Grant 2026

    Printed Matter · New York, USA-based; open to publishers based anywhere in the world · Deadline: 04 Oct 2026 · Award: Three unrestricted grants of $10,000 USD each, plus a featured exhibitor presence (complimentary table) at a forthcoming Printed Matter NY or LA Art Book Fair. No application fee.

    Third annual cycle of Printed Matter's Publisher Work Grant, supported by Wagner Foundation. Three unrestricted grants of $10,000 go to independent artists' book publishers, acknowledging past work and providing capacity-building funds for future book projects and publisher sustainability. ELIGIBILITY: independent imprints with an 'independent spirit,' generally small in scale, with a publishing history of one or more years and one or more titles per year, currently active with future titles in the works; open to publishers based anywhere (international presses encouraged). NOT for individual artists seeking to publish their own work, nor for commercial presses that exclusively publish client work; more established, well-funded, or institutionally supported publishers may be deprioritised. Funds are unrestricted (production, artists' fees, equipment, studio costs, rent, etc.). Applications reviewed by Printed Matter and a guest jury based on the imprint's body of work, upcoming projects, and potential impact. Selected presses also receive a highlighted presence at a 2026 Printed Matter Art Book Fair (travel and accommodation are the grantee's responsibility). Deadline: Sunday 4 October 2026, 11:59PM EST.

  7. Extra Teeth: Open Submissions for Fiction and Nonfiction (paid)

    Extra Teeth · Scottish magazine with an international outlook. Open to writers anywhere; Scottish writers especially championed, and submissions from underrepresented groups especially welcome. · Deadline: 14 Dec 2026 · Award: GBP 200 per piece selected for the magazine, plus two copies of the issue. Four pieces are also selected for the With Bite Substack at GBP 150 each. Guest illustrator (one per issue) is paid GBP 943.90. Paid by bank transfer within 30 days of a signed publishing agreement. No submission fee.

    Extra Teeth is a Scottish literary magazine with an international outlook, paying for fiction and nonfiction. Submissions are currently open. No theme is set per issue; send your best work on any topic. Pieces should be 800 to 4,000 words, previously unpublished, submitted as a Word document (standard font, size 12, double line spacing) titled with 'F' for fiction or 'NF' for nonfiction followed by your name, plus a title sheet (name, address, word count, bio under 100 words); cover letters are not required. Send one piece only per submission period to submissions@extrateeth.co.uk with 'SHORT STORY: TITLE' or 'ESSAY: TITLE' in the subject line; secondary submissions are deleted. Copyright remains with the author. Work in translation is accepted with the necessary permissions. The magazine does not currently publish poetry, scripts or artwork (aside from its guest illustrator). PAYMENT: GBP 200 per piece for the magazine plus two contributor copies; four pieces are also chosen for the With Bite Substack at GBP 150 each. AI POLICY: no AI-written work is published or considered. ILLUSTRATORS: one guest illustrator is commissioned per issue (paid GBP 943.90); send work to hello@extrateeth.co.uk with 'ILLUSTRATOR' in the subject line; marginalised illustrators especially encouraged. Allow eight weeks after the submission period before querying. Check the submissions page for current open periods.

  8. CRAFT Literary: Creative Nonfiction (Year-Round Submissions)

    CRAFT Literary · Online (US-based, international writers accepted) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: $200 per original creative-nonfiction piece ($100 for flash). No submission fee.

    Online literary magazine paying for original creative nonfiction (essays and flash), open year-round with no submission fee. Useful, low-barrier way for an emerging writer to publish a standalone critical/creative-nonfiction piece and build a publication record toward a book. ELIGIBILITY: open to any emerging or established author; submissions accepted from international writers. Creative categories are open on a rolling basis year-round.

  9. C Magazine: Call for Pitches (Art Writing & Criticism)

    C Magazine · Toronto, Canada (open to writers internationally) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: 35-45 cents/word; print reviews flat CAD $430; online reviews CAD $210. No fee.

    Contemporary art magazine commissioning paid art writing and criticism, with pitches accepted on an ongoing basis (online reviews/pitches continuous; themed print issues have set deadlines, e.g. the Spring issue around 15 September 2026). A direct paid outlet for criticism on digital, new-media and AI art. ELIGIBILITY: writers at all experience levels are invited; not restricted to Canada. Mostly pitch-based, with short online reviews accepted unsolicited.
