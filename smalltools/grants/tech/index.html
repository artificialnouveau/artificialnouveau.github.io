Tech and Infrastructure Grants, Fellowships and Residencies

Currently 11 active paid tech and infrastructure grants, fellowships and residencies. Hand-curated and updated weekly. Every entry is funded, no exposure-only calls. Browse the list below, or use the interactive desk for filtering and shortlisting.

Open calls

  1. Internet Society Foundation: Research Grant Program 2026

    Internet Society Foundation · Worldwide · Deadline: 22 May 2026 · Award: $200,000 to $500,000 per project

    Research grants for innovative work deepening understanding of the Internet and its impact on society. Four focus areas: Inclusive Internet (structural barriers to digital participation), Greening the Internet (environmental impact and sustainability), Measuring Meaningful Connectivity (frameworks including AI, cloud, platform ecosystems), and A Trustworthy Internet (reliability, security, accountability). Open to individual researchers and organisations worldwide; underrepresented groups and Global Majority applicants strongly encouraged. Applications via Fluxx in English, French or Spanish. Deadline 22 May 2026, 21:00 UTC.

  2. NGI Fediversity (11th call)

    NLnet / NGI Fediversity (funded by EU Horizon Europe) · Worldwide · Deadline: 01 Jun 2026 · Award: €5,000 to €50,000 per project

    Small to medium-sized R&D grants for projects making federated and self-hosted internet services easier to deploy and operate at scale. Funds practical work on portable Fediverse-style infrastructure, hosted Mastodon-like or PeerTube-like services, secure email, private cloud and other tools that support decentralisation and digital self-determination. Open to individuals and organisations of any type; results released under free or open-source licences. Deadline 1 June 2026, 12:00 CEST.

  3. NGI Fediversity (11th call): Hosting Stack of the Future

    NLnet / NGI Fediversity (funded by EU Horizon Europe) · Worldwide · Deadline: 01 Jun 2026 · Award: €5,000 to €50,000 per project (€450,000 total budget for open calls)

    Small to medium-sized R&D grants for projects that strengthen Fediversity, an effort to bring easy-to-use hosted cloud services with service portability and personal freedom at their core to everyone. Built on NixOS for declarative, reproducible, reliable deployment of Fediverse services (PeerTube, Mastodon, Owncast, Lemmy), email, VPN, private cloud storage, wikis and more. Project results released under open source licences. Deadline 1 June 2026, 12:00 CEST.

  4. NGI TALER (13th call): Privacy-Preserving Digital Payments

    NLnet / NGI TALER (with GNU Taler community, funded by EU Horizon Europe and Swiss SERI) · Worldwide · Deadline: 01 Jun 2026 · Award: €5,000 to €50,000 per project (€676,000 total budget for open calls)

    Small to medium-sized R&D grants for free and open source efforts aligned with privacy-preserving digital payments. Build new GNU Taler capabilities, auxiliary tools, UX, FOSS-app and open-standard integrations (P2P micropayments in messengers, social media, video conferencing), or merchant-backend improvements. All results released under a free or open-source licence. Strong projects can scale up via NGI0 Core. Deadline 1 June 2026, 12:00 CEST.

  5. NGI Zero Commons Fund (13th call)

    NLnet / NGI Zero (funded by EU Horizon Europe and Swiss SERI) · Worldwide · Deadline: 01 Jun 2026 · Award: €5,000 to €50,000 per project (scalable for proven potential)

    Small to medium-sized R&D grants to help reclaim the public nature of the internet across the full technology spectrum: libre silicon, middleware, P2P infrastructure, end-user applications, open standards, open data and AI, open science, creative commons and open educational resources. Project results released under free or open-source licences. Aimed at troubleshooters tackling hard, important internet-commons challenges. Total programme: €21.6m through 2027. Deadline 1 June 2026, 12:00 CEST.

  6. Wikimedia Foundation Rapid Fund

    Wikimedia Foundation · Worldwide (subject to legal eligibility by country) · Deadline: 01 Jul 2026 · Award: $500 to $5,000 USD per grant (distributed in local-currency equivalent at time of application). Per-applicant cap $10,000/fiscal year; individuals may not hold multiple open Rapid Funds, organisations/groups limited to 2 open grants at a time.

    Quick-turnaround grants for individuals, Wikimedia community members, groups and affiliates running short-term, low-cost Wikimedia-focused projects: editathons, workshops, community meetups, education projects, cultural heritage initiatives, gender/diversity programmes, small-scale software development and content campaigns. Five cycles per year; upcoming deadlines: 1 July 2026, 1 September 2026, 1 November 2026, 1 February 2027, 1 April 2027. Approximately 2 months processing time per cycle. Standard track and Technical Projects track (Grants:Project/Rapid/Tech) share deadlines and amounts. Apply via the Wikimedia Foundation Grantee Portal (Fluxx) at https://wmf.fluxx.io/. Transfers run slower in June and December. CEE region applications routed via the CEE Hub from 1 April 2026. Ineligible: General Support Fund grantees, applicants on SDN lists, those with recent UCoC violations.

  7. Wikimedia Conference Fund

    Wikimedia Foundation · Worldwide (subject to legal eligibility by country) · Deadline: 31 Aug 2026 · Award: From USD 10,000 per grant (no preset upper cap stated). No application fee.

    Wikimedia Foundation grants supporting the organisation of local, regional and thematic conferences that bring Wikimedians together for knowledge sharing, skill development and professional networking. Two rounds per year. Round 1 deadline: 31 August 2026. Round 2 deadline: 1 February 2027. ELIGIBILITY: groups, organisations and Wikimedia affiliates; individuals may apply via a fiscal sponsor. SUPPORT: from USD 10,000 per grant; processing time approximately 3 months from submission to decision, plus 2-3 weeks for finalisation before fund disbursement. No application fee. Apply via the page at meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Grants:Conference.

  8. Wikimedia Community Fund: General Support Fund (2026-27 cycles)

    Wikimedia Foundation · Worldwide; reviewed through regional committees (e.g., North America, CEE, MENA, Sub-Saharan Africa, etc.) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Minimum USD 10,000 per year; regional maximums vary (e.g., up to USD 500,000/year in North America). The 2024-25 average for first-time grantees was approximately USD 33,000 globally. No application fee.

    Wikimedia Foundation's largest community-side grant: multi-month or annual programming budgets supporting education, cultural heritage, gender and diversity, community engagement, free-knowledge advocacy and other Wikimedia-aligned work. ELIGIBILITY: individuals, groups, Wikimedia affiliates and organisations from the Wikimedia movement; grantees who currently hold a General Support Fund cannot simultaneously hold a Rapid Fund. AMOUNTS: minimum USD 10,000/year; regional maximums vary (e.g., up to USD 500,000/year for North America); the 2024-25 average for first-time grantees was about USD 33,000 globally. DURATION: minimum 6 months of programming; typically 12 months, with multi-year options. CYCLES: two rounds per year, processed by regional review committees. Round 2 (2025-26) deadlines passed in early March 2026. Round 1 (2026-27) deadlines typically fall in late August / early September 2026, with exact dates varying by region - confirm the regional deadline on the linked page before applying. No application fee.

  9. Wikimedia Hub Fund

    Wikimedia Foundation · Worldwide (groups working on Wikimedia hub initiatives) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: No preset minimum or maximum. Historical reference: research initiatives around USD 25,000; pilot-year hubs typically USD 50,000-110,000. Total pool USD 700,000 for the July 2025 - June 2026 fiscal cycle. No application fee.

    Wikimedia Foundation grants supporting hub initiatives - regional or thematic structures that coordinate Wikimedia work across multiple affiliates or contributors. Funds may cover staffing, programming, community support and operational expenses for hubs at pre-pilot, pilot or operational stages. ELIGIBILITY: groups only (not individuals). Applicants must be Wikimedia Movement groups composed of Wikimedia affiliates, unaffiliated organisations, organisers and/or project contributors working on a hub initiative. AMOUNTS: no preset cap; research initiatives historically around USD 25,000, pilot-year hubs typically USD 50,000-110,000; total pool USD 700,000 for the July 2025 - June 2026 fiscal cycle. CYCLES: four quarterly rounds per annual pool (Round 1: 1 August; Round 2: 1 November; Round 3: 1 February; Round 4: 1 April). The 2025-26 cycle's final round closed 1 April 2026; the next annual pool (2026-27) is expected to follow the same quarterly pattern, with Round 1 likely 1 August 2026 - confirm on the linked page. No application fee.

  10. Mozilla Festival (MozFest) 2026: Call for Proposals - Wilding

    Mozilla Foundation · Barcelona, Spain (Recinte Fabra i Coats; co-hosted with the City of Barcelona as part of Open Tech Week) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Limited travel support, not guaranteed: flights to Barcelona booked through Mozilla's platform; a local hotel room reserved by Mozilla; a 5-day Metro pass including airport transfer. No additional funds for food, other ground transport or travel insurance. Visa fee reimbursements handled through a separate process. NO artist fee, stipend or honorarium.

    MozFest 2026 is Mozilla Foundation's annual festival of debates, labs and workshops, forums, showcases, talks and installations - this year at Recinte Fabra i Coats in Barcelona (28-30 October 2026), co-hosted with the City of Barcelona during Open Tech Week. 2026 THEME 'WILDING': a provocation about loosening rigid systems and creating conditions for communities and technologies to evolve collectively, naturally and intuitively. Inspired by ecological rewilding, it explores community-owned mesh networks, federated platforms, open protocols, community archives, and tools built on transparency, trust and mutual flourishing. Mozilla is inviting proposals that exemplify Wilding and celebrate community-led alternatives - work that is proof of what is possible. FESTIVAL TRACKS: Wilding Advocacy; Wilding Knowledge; Wilding Systems; Wilding With Nature; Youth Wilding; Wilding Creativity; Founders Wilding; Developers Wilding. TRAVEL SUPPORT (limited; the festival cannot guarantee support for every applicant): flights to Barcelona booked through Mozilla's platform, a local hotel room reserved by Mozilla, and a 5-day Metro pass including the airport. Mozilla cannot provide additional funds for food, other ground transport or travel insurance; visa fee reimbursements are handled through a separate process. There is no artist fee, stipend or honorarium. PROCESS: submit a proposal via the MozFest CFP on the Mozilla Foundation festival site; travel support requests are reviewed after the CFP closes. The CFP closing date is not stated in the linked page - verify the deadline on the MozFest site before applying.

  11. OTF Internet Freedom Fund

    Open Technology Fund (OTF) · Worldwide · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: $10,000 to $900,000 (sweet spot $50,000 to $200,000)

    Rolling-deadline fund for technology-focused projects that promote human rights, internet freedom and open societies. Funds anti-censorship, anti-surveillance, privacy-preserving and circumvention tools, plus applied research. Two-stage process: submit a Concept Note via the OTF online application system; reviewed monthly with feedback in 6-8 weeks. Open internationally.
