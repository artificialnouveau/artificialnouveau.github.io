Projects led by a Coalition member organization; geography otherwise unrestricted. · Deadline: 29 Jul 2026 · Award: Micro-grants of USD 10,000 or less, a deliberate change from Round 1's larger awards so the Fund reaches more of the Coalition.     MEMBERS ONLY: this is a member-led collaboration fund, not an open public call. Projects must be led by a Project Liberty Coalition (Alliance) member organization. A STRONG APPLICATION SHOWS: work that builds the Coalition as an entity, such as shared tools, infrastructure or connections that make joint action easier; clear alignment with a Coalition workstream; value for multiple Coalition organizations; a strong case that the work would not happen, or would move more slowly, without the Fund; a concrete outcome such as a tool, resource, convening, campaign or research product; and readiness to begin substantive work within two weeks of approval. Background on the Co-Lab program is in the Coalition's overview document.

  3. Center on AI, Security, and Technology (CAST) Fellowship

    RAND Corporation, Center on AI, Security, and Technology (CAST) · Washington DC, San Francisco or Pittsburgh, USA, or remote within the United States; fellows may also be based in the United Kingdom · Deadline: 01 Aug 2026 · Award: US fellows: annual stipend from USD 40,000 to 200,000 for full-time fellows depending on experience, plus a monthly payment usable for health and dental insurance. UK fellows: compensation based on experience. No application fee.

    RAND's Center on AI, Security, and Technology (CAST) runs a rolling fellowship for researchers working on AI security and technology policy, with mentorship from RAND policy experts. Fellows can be based in Washington DC, San Francisco or Pittsburgh, work remotely within the United States, or be based in the United Kingdom. Terms are flexible: engagements typically start at six months and can be extended up to three years, full-time or part-time. US fellows receive an annual stipend ranging from USD 40,000 to 200,000 for full-time work depending on experience, plus a monthly payment that can be put toward health and dental insurance; UK fellows are compensated based on experience. The programme spans career stages from undergraduate through mid-career. ELIGIBILITY: applicants must be based in the US or UK; US applicants need to be eligible for a government security clearance, and UK applicants must have a current and ongoing right to work in the UK. Current RAND staff are not eligible. ROLLING DEADLINE: applications are submitted through an online form and reviewed quarterly, with published cutoffs of 1 February, 1 May, 1 August and 1 November, each answered by the first of the following month. The date recorded here is the next cutoff (1 August 2026); the call does not close after it. RAND notes that if one of its listed staff openings matches your interest you should apply to that instead, and suggests not applying to the fellowship if you have submitted another CAST application within the last six months.

  4. Innovate UK: Next Wave Breakthrough Wave 1, Createch (2026)

    Innovate UK, UK Research and Innovation · United Kingdom. Project work must be carried out in the UK, results exploited in the UK, and most funding spent in the UK. · Deadline: 11 Aug 2026 · Award: GBP 100,000 to 500,000 total project costs from a GBP 10 million fund; no organisation may receive more than GBP 3 million across the programme. Grant intervention rates for industrial research: up to 70 percent for micro and small enterprises, 60 percent for medium, 50 percent for large.     Next Wave Breakthrough Wave 1 is Innovate UK's current open Createch competition, investing up to GBP 10 million in early-stage innovation at the intersection of creative technology and the frontier industries named in the Industrial Strategy: video games, advertising and marketing, film and television, and music and the performing and visual arts. It funds industrial research to take innovations beyond proof of concept and de-risk future investment, with individual projects between GBP 100,000 and 500,000 in total costs. Grant rates depend on organisation size, from 70 percent for micro and small enterprises down to 50 percent for large organisations, so applicants must supply match funding for the remainder. The lead must be a UK-registered SME; non-profits and CICs are barred from leading, though they can participate as collaborators alongside a commercial lead. Non-funded partners may be based internationally. The competition opened 23 June 2026 and closes 11 August 2026 at 11:00am UK time. NOTE ON FIT: this is an R&D competition for creative-technology product development, not a fund for delivering festivals or exhibitions, so it suits an organisation whose work can be framed as industrial research with a commercial SME lead.

Applications close 30 August 2026 at 11:59pm Anywhere on Earth.

  19. WBSO: R&D Tax Credit (rolling, Netherlands)

    Rijksdienst voor Ondernemend Nederland (RVO) · Netherlands. Dutch companies and self-employed paying payroll or income tax that conduct qualifying R&D. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Payroll/income tax reduction for R&D, not a cash grant. 2026: 36 percent of the first EUR 380,000 of R&D wage costs (50 percent for startups), 16 percent above; fixed deduction for self-employed. National budget EUR 1.817 billion for 2026. No application fee.

    The WBSO (Wet Bevordering Speur- en Ontwikkelingswerk) is the Dutch R&D tax credit administered by RVO. It reduces payroll/wage tax for companies with R&D staff and provides an income-tax deduction for self-employed people doing R&D; it is a tax credit, not a cash grant. For 2026 the benefit is 36 percent of the first EUR 380,000 of R&D wage and other costs (50 percent for qualifying startups) and 16 percent above that; self-employed receive a fixed deduction (and need at least 500 R&D hours per year). Eligible applicants are Dutch companies and self-employed people paying payroll or income tax who carry out qualifying R&D in the EU. Applications are made in advance via Mijn RVO and cannot be retroactive; for coverage of the final three months of a year the deadline is 30 September. Time-keeping and project administration are required. No application fee.
