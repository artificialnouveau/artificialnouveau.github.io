Open calls

  1. Technology Fellow: Digital Safety and Security

    Ford Foundation · New York, New York, USA · Deadline: 06 Jul 2026 · Award: USD 166,000 to 170,000 annual salary, plus comprehensive benefits and retirement contributions of up to 13%

    The Ford Foundation seeks a two-year Technology Fellow for Digital Safety and Security to expand the foundation's ability to support grantee digital safety and security. Civil society organizations face targeted information security threats yet many lack the resources and training to maintain robust digital security. Reporting to the Director, Office of the Vice President for U.S. Programs, the Fellow will identify field-building opportunities in the public-interest digital safety and security field, work directly with program officers across US and global teams to integrate digital security capacity into Ford's technical assistance programs, provide strategic support to high-risk grantees, and assess strategic gaps in the ecosystem for safety and security providers. The Technology Fellows Program is administered by Ford's Technology and Society Program and is designed to increase sociotechnical capacity within the Foundation. Requirements include a minimum of 7 years' professional experience with media, technology, advocacy or policy organizations; a baccalaureate degree or equivalent experience; sophisticated knowledge of existing and emerging technologies with a focus on surveillance technologies and organizational digital safety and security; and experience designing, implementing or supporting digital safety and security solutions in a complex global setting. Hybrid role based in New York (three days per week in office) with approximately 10 to 20 percent domestic and international travel. Not eligible for visa sponsorship; candidates must be authorized to work in the United States. Submit both a resume and a cover letter to be considered.

  2. EIC Accelerator 2026 (European Innovation Council)

    European Innovation Council (European Commission) · EU Member States and Horizon Europe Associated Countries. Single-company applicants; third-country applicants must relocate before the full-application stage. · Deadline: 08 Jul 2026 · Award: Grant up to EUR 2.5 million (non-dilutive lump sum) for innovation activities, plus optional equity investment of EUR 1-10 million via the EIC Fund. Options: grant-only, blended finance, or investment-only. No application fee.

    The EIC Accelerator funds high-risk, high-impact deep-tech SMEs and start-ups to develop and scale breakthrough innovations. It offers a grant of up to EUR 2.5 million (a non-dilutive lump sum) for innovation activities, and can combine this with an equity investment of EUR 1-10 million through the EIC Fund (blended finance), or provide investment only. The Step 1 short application can be submitted at any time; full (Step 2) applications are evaluated at fixed cut-offs. The next 2026 cut-offs are 8 July, 2 September and 4 November 2026 (all 17:00 Brussels time). Eligible applicants are for-profit SMEs and start-ups (fewer than 250 employees, turnover up to EUR 50M or balance sheet up to EUR 43M) established in an EU Member State or Horizon Europe Associated Country; small mid-caps up to 499 employees are eligible for investment-only support. Apply via the EU Funding and Tenders portal (topic HORIZON-EIC-2026-ACCELERATOR-01).

  3. Circuit Breakers '26: Tech Worker Conference - Call for Proposals (New York City)

    Tech Workers Coalition · New York City, USA. In-person conference 17-18 October 2026. Open to workers across the tech sector, from software engineers and salespeople to warehouse workers, ride-share drivers and contractors. · Deadline: 15 Jul 2026 · Award: No funding. This is a free call for talk/session proposals at a volunteer-organised tech labour conference, not a grant. No submission fee.

    Circuit Breakers '26 is a tech worker conference organised by volunteers from across the tech labour movement, bringing together workers, organisers and activists in the tech industry to learn, build community and strategise. Tech Workers Coalition defines tech workers broadly, from software engineers and salespeople to warehouse workers and ride-share drivers. The conference centres rank-and-file organising, with workers sharing stories, running skills trainings and leading debates. The open call for proposals welcomes sessions on all aspects of labour organising in tech, with particular interest in three areas: Labor Organizing Against Imperialism; Tech Workers Taking on Technology (layoffs, surveillance, worker control over AI and automation); and Bold Strategy (solidarity, membership structures, return-to-office actions, cross-border organising). Sessions can be proposed via a short form (about 10 minutes). New York City, 17-18 October 2026. This is not a grant and carries no funding; it is a free call for conference talks. NOTE: the call-for-proposals deadline was not yet published at time of listing; confirm the submission deadline and form link on the Circuit Breakers page.

    Wikimedia Foundation grants supporting the organisation of local, regional and thematic conferences that bring Wikimedians together for knowledge sharing, skill development and professional networking.   5. Eurostars Call 11 2026 (Eureka / Eurostars-3)

    Eureka / Eurostars-3 (Dutch participation via RVO) · International consortia from at least two Eurostars countries, led by an innovative SME. Dutch funding administered by RVO. · Deadline: 10 Sep 2026 · Award: Co-funds collaborative international R&D; national funding varies by country. For the Dutch project share: up to EUR 500,000 per project at 50 percent reimbursement of eligible R&D costs. No application fee.

    Eurostars (part of the Eureka network, the Eurostars-3 programme co-funded by Horizon Europe) supports collaborative international R&D projects led by innovative SMEs. Each country funds its own participants, so amounts vary; for the Dutch share, RVO funds up to EUR 500,000 per project at a 50 percent reimbursement rate on eligible R&D costs. Projects require an international consortium of at least two independent organisations from at least two Eurostars countries, led by an innovative SME, with the SME budget at least 50 percent of the total and no single participant exceeding 70 percent. Projects must run no longer than 36 months and be civil (non-defence) in purpose. Call 11 opens 9 July 2026 and closes 10 September 2026, 14:00 CEST. No application fee. Submit via the Eureka project platform (myeurekaproject.org); Dutch national information at RVO.

  6. MIT R&D Collaboration Projects 2026 (MKB Innovatiestimulering Topsectoren)

    Rijksdienst voor Ondernemend Nederland (RVO) · Netherlands. SME consortia (minimum two collaborating SMEs). · Deadline: 15 Sep 2026 · Award: Grant covering 35 percent of eligible costs. Small projects EUR 50,000-200,000; large projects EUR 200,000-350,000 per project (EUR 25,000-100,000 or EUR 25,000-175,000 per participant). No application fee.

    The MIT R&D Collaboration Projects scheme (MIT R&D-samenwerkingsprojecten, part of MKB Innovatiestimulering Topsectoren) funds joint R&D projects by Dutch SMEs within the national Top Sectors and Mission-driven Innovation policy. The grant covers 35 percent of eligible costs: small projects of EUR 50,000-200,000 and large projects of EUR 200,000-350,000 (with per-participant ranges of EUR 25,000-100,000 for small and EUR 25,000-175,000 for large). Eligible applicants are consortia of at least two collaborating SMEs, with no single participant carrying more than 70 percent of costs. The national RVO round runs 9 June to 15 September 2026 (closes 17:00) and is a ranked tender, not first-come-first-served; some provinces run separate regional MIT windows. No application fee. Apply via RVO.

  8. Sony Innovation Fund (rolling, equity investment)

    Sony Ventures Corporation (Sony Innovation Fund) · Global; offices in the US (Bay Area, Los Angeles), London/EU, Tokyo, Bangalore and Israel. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Corporate venture capital; typical check roughly USD 100,000 to USD 5 million (reported AUM USD 250 million plus), plus mentorship and access to Sony's network. This is an equity INVESTMENT, not a grant. No application fee.

    The Sony Innovation Fund (now under Sony Ventures Corporation) is Sony's corporate venture capital arm, investing in early-stage (Seed, Series A and Series B) transformational-technology startups across business, entertainment and society, including entertainment, gaming, AI, robotics, fintech, climate and blockchain. Music, entertainment and AI startups fit within its thesis, though it is not a music-only fund. Typical checks are roughly USD 100,000 to USD 5 million, alongside mentorship and access to Sony's network and R&D. It is an equity investment, not a grant. Global, with offices in the US, London/EU, Tokyo, Bangalore and Israel. Startups engage on a rolling basis via the contact and inquiry form. No application fee.

  9. UNIIQ: Proof-of-Concept Fund (rolling, Zuid-Holland)

    UNIIQ · Zuid-Holland (South Holland), Netherlands only. Early-stage research-driven startups. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Around EUR 300,000 per company as a convertible loan (investment, not a grant). Total fund EUR 50 million. No application fee.

    UNIIQ is a proof-of-concept investment fund for the earliest, riskiest phase of company building in Zuid-Holland (South Holland). It invests around EUR 300,000 per company as a convertible loan (for example redeemed over about three years or converted at a follow-on valuation), drawn from a EUR 50 million fund. It is an investment, not a grant. Eligible companies are early-stage, research-driven startups based in Zuid-Holland, typically in life sciences, deep tech and high tech. Intake is continuous via an orientation meeting followed by a funding application. No application fee.

  10. ROM InWest: Startup and Scale-up Financing (rolling, Noord-Holland)

    ROM InWest (Regional Development Company for Noord-Holland) · Noord-Holland (North Holland), Netherlands. Startups, scale-ups and innovative SMEs. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Financing of EUR 300,000 to EUR 5,000,000 as loans, equity participations or a combination (not a gift); cannot exceed 49.9 percent of a financing round. This is investment, not a grant. No application fee.

    ROM InWest is the regional development company for Noord-Holland (North Holland). It finances startups, scale-ups and innovative SMEs working on food, energy, healthcare and circular-economy transitions, providing EUR 300,000 to EUR 5,000,000 as loans, equity participations or a combination (explicitly not a gift), and not exceeding 49.9 percent of a financing round. It is an investment, not a grant. Companies must be based in Noord-Holland. Intake is continuous, starting with a quick-scan tool. No application fee.

  11. WBSO: R&D Tax Credit (rolling, Netherlands)

    Rijksdienst voor Ondernemend Nederland (RVO) · Netherlands. Dutch companies and self-employed paying payroll or income tax that conduct qualifying R&D. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Payroll/income tax reduction for R&D, not a cash grant. 2026: 36 percent of the first EUR 380,000 of R&D wage costs (50 percent for startups), 16 percent above; fixed deduction for self-employed. National budget EUR 1.817 billion for 2026. No application fee.

    The WBSO (Wet Bevordering Speur- en Ontwikkelingswerk) is the Dutch R&D tax credit administered by RVO. It reduces payroll/wage tax for companies with R&D staff and provides an income-tax deduction for self-employed people doing R&D; it is a tax credit, not a cash grant. For 2026 the benefit is 36 percent of the first EUR 380,000 of R&D wage and other costs (50 percent for qualifying startups) and 16 percent above that; self-employed receive a fixed deduction (and need at least 500 R&D hours per year). Eligible applicants are Dutch companies and self-employed people paying payroll or income tax who carry out qualifying R&D in the EU. Applications are made in advance via Mijn RVO and cannot be retroactive; for coverage of the final three months of a year the deadline is 30 September. Time-keeping and project administration are required. No application fee.

    The Sovereign Tech Fund invests in the open digital infrastructure that underpins the modern internet, funding maintenance, security and development of critical open-source base technologies. ELIGIBILITY: individuals, companies and non-profits maintaining or developing critical open-source infrastructure; open worldwide.     FLOSS/fund, backed by Zerodha, provides financial support to free and open-source software projects and maintainers.   15. OTF Internet Freedom Fund

    Open Technology Fund (OTF) · Worldwide · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: $10,000 to $900,000 (sweet spot $50,000 to $200,000)

    Rolling-deadline fund for technology-focused projects that promote human rights, internet freedom and open societies.   16. Tech Matters: Tech for Good Project Fellow 2026

    Tech Matters · Remote (worldwide; US-based hires also receive health and related insurance) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Paid: $70,000-$80,000 for US-based employees; compensation adjusted for local circumstances outside the US. No application fee.

    Tech Matters, the nonprofit founded by Jim Fruchterman (MacArthur Fellow, Skoll Awardee), is hiring a Project Fellow to work directly with the CEO on ensuring technology better serves the 90% of humanity typically neglected by the commercial tech industry. Project work spans (1) expanding the Better Deal for Data, a practical data-governance standard for the nonprofit sector, by supporting nonprofits adopting and endorsing the standard; (2) prototyping methods for improving LLM and search-engine answers on critical social problems (initial focus: preventing sexual abuse of children); and (3) helping develop an open course based on Jim's recent book Technology for Good (CC-licensed, global release September 2026). REMOTE; open to candidates around the world (US-based hires get health and related insurance). WHAT THEY LOOK FOR: demonstrated interest and 5+ years' experience in tech-enabled organisations; Bachelor's/Master's in CS, data science, MLIS, MBA, or social sciences; familiarity with SaaS platforms; clear and kind writing; comfortable in an early-stage team. NICE TO HAVE: Wikipedia editing, data-governance expertise, additional languages. APPLY by emailing a resume and a real (not LLM-generated) cover letter to jobs@techmatters.org with subject 'Tech for Good Project Fellow - [Your Name]'. The cover letter should explain why this role and mission resonate with you. NOTE: this is a job/fellowship, not a grant; no specific application deadline listed (rolling).
