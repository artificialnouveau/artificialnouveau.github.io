Open calls

  1. Mountainfilm: Commitment Grants & Emerging Filmmaker Fellowship

    Mountainfilm · Application online; Telluride, Colorado, United States (fellowship trip during the festival). Commitment Grants are open to filmmakers from any country; the Emerging Filmmaker Fellowship requires current U.S. residency. · Deadline: 16 Jul 2026 · Award: Over USD 90,000 in total funding. Individual Commitment Grants range from USD 3,000 to 6,000. The Emerging Filmmaker Fellowship adds a yearlong mentorship, a cash award, and a funded trip to Telluride over Memorial Day weekend (travel, lodging, and a festival pass).

    Mountainfilm's Commitment Grants support documentary filmmakers with projects in post-production, with over USD 90,000 in funding this cycle for films that celebrate adventure, activism, social justice, culture, the environment, and indomitable spirit. Mountainfilm actively encourages diversity behind the camera, and a portion of funding is set aside for two focus areas: projects featuring an LGBTQ+ protagonist or a story about overcoming personal challenges, societal stigmas, and/or community obstacles with a meaningful connection to the outdoors or nature-based pursuits; and projects exploring immigration or migration, or created by immigrant or first-generation immigrant filmmakers. AWARDS: individual Commitment Grants range from USD 3,000 to 6,000. EMERGING FILMMAKER FELLOWSHIP: one early-career filmmaker is selected from the Commitment Grant applicant pool for a yearlong mentorship that includes edit and story consultation, networking opportunities, a cash award, and a funded trip to Telluride during the festival over Memorial Day weekend (travel, lodging, and a Mountainfilm pass, applicable to the year following the award). ELIGIBILITY: Commitment Grants are open to applicants from any country; the Emerging Filmmaker Fellowship accepts applicants from any country but requires current U.S. residency. Post-production projects only, completed, released, or festival-screened projects are not eligible, and impact campaigns are not funded. Prior Commitment Grant recipients should not re-apply for the same project; previously declined projects may re-apply only with substantial changes. REQUIREMENTS: a video sample or trailer of the current project no longer than 10 minutes (password-protected Vimeo links preferred); the screener link must stay active and cannot be changed after submission. The Emerging Filmmaker application additionally requires a director bio, headshot, and cover letter. Recipients are expected to provide progress updates 2-3 times a year, include Mountainfilm recognition in credits, and discuss premiere consideration with the programming team. TIMELINE: applications are open July 1 to July 16, 2026 (closing 11:59 PM MDT), with notifications in late August or early September. Questions: entries@mountainfilm.org.

  2. CASA PIP Residency for Filmmakers

    CASA PIP · Portugal; open internationally to filmmakers. · Deadline: 31 Jul 2026 · Award: Free residency (no cost to attend), offering time, space, and peace to work on a project or an early idea. A EUR 35 application fee applies.

    CASA PIP is a residency program in Portugal founded and run by filmmakers. It encourages residents to slow down and play, providing filmmakers with the time, peace, space, and general "rulelessness" to move meaningfully forward on their work, whether it is a whole project or just the budding idea for one. The residency is free to attend. ELIGIBILITY: open internationally to filmmakers. There is a EUR 35 application fee. Deadline: July 31, 2026.

    AXS Film Fund · Remote (worldwide; English-language application; non-English projects must have English subtitles) · Deadline: 31 Jul 2026 · Award: Up to $10,000 per grant; up to 5 creators awarded (aim to support 3-5 artists in this round). Application Assistance Stipends also available on a first-come, first-served basis to hire an application assistant. No application fee.

    Grants from the AXS Film Fund supporting documentary and nonfiction new media creators, particularly those who identify as living with a disability and especially from underserved communities, though applications are welcome from all creators regardless of background. PROJECT ELIGIBILITY: feature-length documentary films (45 minutes or longer; experimental nonfiction accepted) or nonfiction new media projects with a film/video component; projects in any stage of production; non-English projects must have English subtitles and the application must be completed in English. INELIGIBLE: fiction projects (including narrative fiction based on a true story); projects where applicants are hired, employed, or commissioned by another entity; incomplete applications or those missing supporting materials; late submissions. APPLICANT ELIGIBILITY: 18 or older; not currently enrolled in a degree-granting program; individual applicants (fiscal sponsors permitted); applicant must be the director or producer of the project. SELECTION CRITERIA: strength of proposal and artistic approach; feasibility; ethics and accountability; completeness; and meeting eligibility requirements. ACCESSIBILITY: alternative application methods available (email filmfund@axslab.org by 24 July 2026); an Application Assistance Stipend is offered for 2026 to allow applicants to hire an application assistant, available first-come, first-served (not guaranteed). TIMELINE: open call opens 1 June 2026 (12:00 AM EST); deadline 31 July 2026 (11:59 PM EST). Apply via SurveyMonkey Apply.

  6. Day Job: Short Film Fund 2026

    Day Job Films · Worldwide applicants; all production must take place in the UK · Deadline: 10 Aug 2026 · Award: Production budget of up to GBP 7,000, with full creative control and no producer or external interference. No application fee.

    Short film competition from Day Job Films created to give auteur directors full creative freedom, awarding a production budget of up to GBP 7,000 to make a short film with no producer interference or commercial agenda. Open to animation, live-action and documentary projects. Runs in three rounds: (1) a pitch deck outlining concept, story, key characters and visual style; (2) a full script for those who progress; and (3) an interview to discuss the vision and execution. A panel of industry professionals judges the pitch deck, script and interview, with at least one team member reading each pitch deck and script in full. The winner receives full creative control over the project. ELIGIBILITY: applicants can be from anywhere worldwide, but all production must take place in the UK; applicants must have a producer on board who is separate from the director/writer. Applicants may optionally include a plan for adapting the script into a feature-length film (this does not affect the outcome). Submissions open February 10, 2026 and close August 10, 2026. Enquiries: hello@dayjobfilms.com.

  16. IDA Emergency Assistance

    International Documentary Association · Remote (worldwide applicants) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: One-time emergency grant of USD 500 to USD 5,000

    The International Documentary Association runs an Emergency Assistance program that provides direct financial support to documentary filmmakers whose work puts them in imminent and extreme physical danger. Eligible filmmakers may request a one-time emergency grant between USD 500 and USD 5,000. IDA responds to all applications within 24 hours and handles them with strict confidentiality, with approved funds transferred in as few as five business days. Applicants need an existing risk assessment plan, a concrete safety plan, two references confirming the emergency, and a secure way to receive funds. The program launched as a pilot on March 9, 2026 and accepts applications on a rolling basis. For sensitive cases, applicants can contact emergency@documentary.org to share information over an end-to-end encrypted app such as Signal.
