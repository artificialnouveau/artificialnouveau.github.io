Open calls

  1. SIAP: Major Individual Artists Award 2026/27 (Arts Council NI)

    Arts Council of Northern Ireland · Northern Ireland, United Kingdom. Applicants must be based in Northern Ireland. · Deadline: 20 Jul 2026 · Award: Up to GBP 15,000 per award; four awards available in 2026/27. Funded through the National Lottery Support for Individual Artists Programme (SIAP).

    The Major Individual Artists award, part of Arts Council NI's National Lottery Support for Individual Artists Programme (SIAP), funds extended or ambitious work by established artists, covering specific projects, specialised research, personal artistic development, and materials/equipment. AMOUNT: four awards of up to GBP 15,000 each in 2026/27. ELIGIBILITY: established artists across all disciplines with national/international recognition who have not previously received a major award; academics and postgraduate artists are eligible if the project costs do not relate to their institutional work or studies. Applicants must contact the relevant artform officer before submission (by 10 July 2026); applications without this prior contact are ineligible. No application fee. The online application system closes precisely at the deadline and applicants must receive a confirmation email to verify successful submission. Deadline: Monday 20 July 2026, 12:00 noon.

  2. Creative Steps for Individuals 2026 (Arts Council of Wales)

    Arts Council of Wales · Wales, United Kingdom. For artists and creatives based in Wales. · Deadline: 29 Jul 2026 · Award: GBP 500 to GBP 7,500 (plus access costs). Applicants can apply for up to 90% of the developmental cost; the remaining 10% must come from a non-Arts Council of Wales / non-National Lottery source (in-kind, cash, or both).

    Creative Steps funds the professional, business and organisational development of artists who have experienced barriers to careers within the arts. Creative Steps for Individuals is open to individual artists and creatives who identify as being Ethnically and Culturally diverse, D/deaf, disabled or neurodivergent, groups that are underrepresented in Arts Council of Wales funding programmes due to barriers to entry. Applicants must have demonstrable experience as an artist or creative professional; those without may still be supported on the basis of artistic potential by submitting a portfolio, CV or examples of work, discussed with a Relationship Manager. WHAT IT SUPPORTS: career development, NOT creating new work or project delivery. Examples include peer-to-peer learning, training courses (not formal/vocational education), building networks, mentorship/coaching, business development/social media/marketing advice, website development/marketing materials/equipment or materials, and setting up a new organisation. Budgets may include the applicant's own time, freelancer costs (paid at standard industry rates), mentor/consultant costs, access costs, and childcare/caregiving costs. AMOUNT: GBP 500 to GBP 7,500 (plus access costs); up to 90% of the developmental cost, with the remaining 10% from a non-Arts Council of Wales / non-National Lottery source (in-kind, cash, or both, confirmed by letters of support). No application fee. APPLY: applicants must express interest at least 3 weeks before the deadline to receive support from an Application Coach or Access Support coordinator; an application form is provided by a Relationship Manager. The fund runs in rounds; the next round (Round 2 2026) has an Expression of Interest cut-off of 8 July 2026 and an application deadline of 29 July 2026. Later rounds: Round 3 (EOI 30 September, deadline 21 October 2026) and Round 4 (EOI 10 February, deadline 3 March 2027).

  3. SIAP: Project Funding for Individual Artists 2026 (Arts Council NI)

    Arts Council of Northern Ireland · Northern Ireland, United Kingdom. Applicants must be based in Northern Ireland. · Deadline: 30 Jul 2026 · Award: GBP 62 to GBP 15,000. Funded through the National Lottery Support for Individual Artists Programme (SIAP).

    Project Funding for Individuals, part of Arts Council NI's National Lottery Support for Individual Artists Programme (SIAP), funds project-based creative activities across all artistic disciplines. Eligible activities include creating new work, research and development, residencies, mentoring, professional development, and time dedicated to specific creative outcomes. ELIGIBLE COSTS: art materials, artist fees/time, residency costs, travel (mileage at GBP 0.257/mile), venue hire, technical equipment (max 50% of request value), and IT equipment (laptops/PCs up to GBP 1,200; tablets up to GBP 500). ELIGIBILITY: artists of all disciplines and career stages, including emerging artists (less than 5 years professional experience), and in some cases technical staff/administrators; students and academics at third-level institutions may apply with departmental approval showing the costs are not covered by their employer or academic work. AMOUNT: GBP 62 to GBP 15,000. No application fee. Deadline: Thursday 30 July 2026, 12:00 noon (online submission required).

  4. SIAP: Artists' International Development Fund 2026 (Arts Council NI)

    Arts Council of Northern Ireland · Northern Ireland, United Kingdom. Applicants must be based in Northern Ireland; international development takes place abroad with overseas partners/hosts. · Deadline: 30 Jul 2026 · Award: GBP 62 to GBP 15,000. Funded through the National Lottery Support for Individual Artists Programme (SIAP).

    The Artists' International Development Fund (AIDF), part of Arts Council NI's National Lottery Support for Individual Artists Programme (SIAP), funds early-stage international development opportunities for individual, freelance and self-employed artists to build networks abroad. ELIGIBLE COSTS: art materials, travel, residencies (up to 4 weeks), technical fees, venue hire, and equipment, with IT devices capped at GBP 1,200 for laptops/PCs and GBP 500 for tablets. ELIGIBILITY: artists across all disciplines, particularly emerging artists (under 5 years professional experience); academics and students at third-level institutions may apply if the project differs from their employment/studies. AIDF applicants must have received recognition for their work in Northern Ireland, lack extensive international experience, and demonstrate interest in their work from an overseas partner/host. AMOUNT: GBP 62 to GBP 15,000. No application fee. Deadline: Thursday 30 July 2026, 12:00 noon.

  5. Creative Scotland Open Fund for Individuals

    Creative Scotland · Scotland, United Kingdom. Applicants must be living in Scotland and hold a UK bank account in their own name. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Between GBP 500 and GBP 50,000. Overall annual budget for the fund is GBP 5 million.

    The Open Fund for Individuals is one of Creative Scotland's key funding programmes, supporting the wide range of activity initiated by artists, writers, producers and other creative practitioners in Scotland. The overall annual budget for this fund is GBP 5 million. WHAT IT SUPPORTS: periods of research, development and/or delivery of creative activity, such as specific projects and productions, for up to 24 months; it can support an individual's time where this is related to specific creative outcomes. Applicants are asked to give a start and end date for the activity and to describe the outcomes, benefits and impacts they wish to achieve. ELIGIBILITY: freelance and self-employed artists and creative practitioners living in Scotland who are at least 18 years old; applicants must have a UK bank account in their own name. Those who are not self-employed individuals or sole traders may instead be eligible for the National Lottery Open Fund for Organisations. FUNDING AMOUNTS: between GBP 500 and GBP 50,000. TIMESCALES: requests between GBP 500 and GBP 20,000 receive an outcome in about 10 weeks; requests between GBP 20,001 and GBP 50,000 in about 14 weeks. This is a rolling open fund with no fixed deadline. Download the guidance for applicants and apply via the online portal.

  6. Restless Egg: Batch 2, Luxury Technology (Accelerator for Artist-Founders) 2026

    Restless Egg · London-based incubator (UK); open to creative "artist-founders" internationally, with in-person gatherings (a first-batch salon was held at Sybil in Berlin). · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Equity-based investment (NOT a non-dilutive grant): USD 100,000 initial investment for 5% equity, plus up to USD 175,000 in follow-on investment when predefined performance metrics are met. Includes a high-touch accelerator program and access to a network of subject-matter experts, collaborators, operators and investors.

    Restless Egg is a London-based incubator for "artist-founders", an emerging genre of creative people whose work sits between art, technology and building commercial products. It invests in creative founders and runs a six-month, high-touch accelerator program to help them build commercially successful and scalable companies at the bleeding edge of culture and technology. BATCH 2 (LUXURY TECHNOLOGY) is open for applications, seeking founders building luxury technologies: commercial products that use AI and other emerging technologies to unlock richer, stranger, more meaningful forms of human experience, not just more efficiency. For this batch Restless Egg has doubled its initial investment to USD 100,000 for 5% equity, and increased its follow-on investment to up to USD 175,000, aiming to give founders more runway, more support and more room to build the most ambitious companies. The program supports rapid learning, sharper product and narrative development, and real investability through close engagement with Restless Egg and its network of subject-matter experts, collaborators, operators and investors; the follow-on is released when predefined performance metrics are met. NOTE: this is a dilutive equity investment (it takes a 5% stake), not a non-dilutive grant or fellowship. Applications are open (no fixed deadline stated). Apply at https://apply.restlessegg.com.

  7. Creative Debuts: Black Artists Grant (BAG)

    Creative Debuts · United Kingdom (UK-based artists) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: GBP 500 unrestricted, no-strings grant to one recipient per month, usable for work, materials, equipment, travel, research or living costs. No application fee.

    Creative Debuts' Black Artists Grant (BAG) is a small, fast, recurring and unconditional cash grant for UK-based Black artists, deliberately barrier-free with no outcomes or reporting required. ELIGIBILITY: UK-based artists of Black heritage who self-identify as Black, across all creative disciplines (visual art, music, film, jewellery, sculpture, choreography and more); no age limit. One recipient is selected each month; apply once and the application is retained for future months. Apply via the Creative Debuts BAG page.

  8. The White Pube Creatives Grant

    The White Pube · United Kingdom · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: £500 (no strings attached)

    Monthly £500 grant for a different working-class creative practitioner based in the UK. Open to anyone making stuff: art, writing, performance, sound, music, craft, comedy, games. Money can be used for time, materials, equipment, research, subscriptions, development, travel, or rent and bills. Apply by emailing funding@thewhitepube.com with a brief intro, contact and a work sample. Rolling, no deadlines, no reporting expected. Non-recipients stay in consideration for future months without re-applying.
