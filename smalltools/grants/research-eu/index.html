Open calls

  1. European Commission: Safeguarding Europe's Born-Digital Heritage (PPPA-2026-BORN-DIGITAL)

    European Commission (Pilot Projects and Preparatory Actions, Culture strand) · European Union. Consortium of at least 5 independent entities from 5 different EU member states required. · Deadline: 16 Jul 2026 · Award: Up to €1,985,000, covering up to 85% of eligible project costs (15% co-financing required). No application fee.

    The European Commission is offering a single grant of up to €1,985,000 (85% co-financing) for a project focused on safeguarding born-digital cultural heritage across EU member states. Scope includes: assessing the preservation state of born-digital art, web archives, video games, and digital content created outside cultural institutions; mapping EU and national legal frameworks; identifying non-legislative barriers (technical, financial, organisational, cultural); and providing guidance on creating preservation copies of licensed or legally accessible materials. This is a scoping and advocacy project, not a digitisation grant. ELIGIBILITY: the application must be led by a consortium of at least 5 independent entities from 5 different EU countries, which rules out solo applicants and single-country organisations. Grant reference: PPPA-2026-BORN-DIGITAL. Deadline: 16 July 2026. Verify the current deadline at ec.europa.eu before applying (one secondary source listed 1 June 2026; the primary call-fiche PDF and the Cyprus government funding portal both confirm 16 July 2026).

  2. CAIS Research Sabbaticals (Fellowships) 2027-28

    Center for Advanced Internet Studies (CAIS), Bochum, Germany · Bochum, Germany (residential at CAIS; rent-free, fully furnished apartment provided plus private office) · Deadline: 23 Jul 2026 · Award: Sabbatical leave on full salary: additional €600/month grant. No regular income: €2,000/month grant. Regular income below €1,400/month: top-up to the full grant. Alternative: reimbursement of salary or substitute costs within reasonable limits. €100/month extra per child under 18 for fellows on a full or compensatory grant. CAIS also covers one return trip to Bochum (or a daily commute if local), provides a rent-free fully furnished apartment and a private office. Fellows can request financial support for research expenses, invite a Visiting Fellow for up to 3 weeks of collaboration, and invite up to three European experts for half-day workshops (CAIS covers travel, accommodation and a daily allowance of up to €24).

    Residential research sabbaticals at CAIS, explicitly open to excellent scholars AND practitioners across all career stages and disciplines (not academia-only). Funds individual projects on the societal impact of digital transformation, including pure research and applied projects that develop new theories, methods or perspectives for practice. Project must be self-contained with specific milestones and produce an independent output suitable for short-timeframe publication: peer-reviewed paper or conference contribution, book chapter, policy paper, or prototype. Fellows join a vibrant interdisciplinary research community with regular joint activities (breakfast Tuesdays, colloquium and dinner Wednesdays, occasional workshops Thursdays) and an international network of alumni, working groups and affiliates. In Germany, full and compensatory grants are not subject to social security contributions and are usually tax-exempt; fellows resident abroad should verify their own tax position. Note: CAIS is currently reviewing the application format and selection process for the next call, so details may change. Next call publishes at the beginning of June 2026; deadline 23 July 2026 for fellowships in the period October 2027 to March 2028. Contact: Dr. Esther Laufer, esther.laufer@cais-research.de.

  3. Journalismfund Europe: European Cross-Border Grants

    Journalismfund Europe · Europe (cross-border projects) · Deadline: 30 Jul 2026 · Award: Variable per-team grants (typically a few thousand to ~EUR 20,000+). The July 2026 round has a total budget of around EUR 280,000.

    Supports independent investigative journalism across Europe, well-suited to surveillance, AI, platform governance and data-driven investigations. Cross-border team requirement: at least two professional journalists (freelance or staff) from at least two different countries; at least 80% of the budget must go to journalists/media from EU member states. The next round closes 30 July 2026 at 1 pm CET.

  4. Digital Democracy Centre x TrygFonden Fellowship Program 2027

    Digital Democracy Centre (University of Southern Denmark), with support from TrygFonden · Odense, Denmark (University of Southern Denmark). Flexible mix of in-office stays at the DDC in Odense and remote periods; stays planned to the Danish working calendar. · Deadline: 01 Sep 2026 · Award: Either a salary subsidy or a stipend to help with travel and/or housing; specific benefits (financial support, access to DDC research resources, flexible working spaces, speaker series, networks) vary by agreement between the fellow, DDC, and partnering organizations. Applicants describe the posts they seek support for (flights, housing, research costs, salary compensation, stipend, etc.).

    The Digital Democracy Centre (DDC) at the University of Southern Denmark, with support from TrygFonden, is calling for applications for its 2027 fellowship cohort. The program fosters collaboration between researchers, journalists, and policymakers exploring the impact of digital technology and AI on media, politics, and democracy. Fellows collaborate with a self-selected DDC mentor to generate new knowledge, contribute to public debate, develop networks, and produce tangible outreach outputs such as policy briefs, educational packages, and talks. THREE FELLOWSHIP TYPES: (1) academic fellows researching digital technologies and democracy; (2) journalist fellows covering digital technology and democracy; (3) policy fellows contributing to the regulatory debate. Target group: researchers, journalism graduates, and policymakers in research/educational institutions, media organizations, ministries, NGOs, etc. PRIORITIES: projects with clear relevance to Denmark or Danish society (including comparative/international projects yielding insights relevant to a Danish context); in 2027, projects related to DDC's Hybrid Warfare Initiative (societal resilience to new forms of international conflict, with a focus on digital technologies and AI) are also prioritized. EXPECTATIONS: applicants must secure confirmation of mentorship from their desired DDC mentor BEFORE applying (applications without a confirmed mentor are automatically rejected; PhD students and research assistants are generally not eligible as mentors, and Sten Schaumburg-Muller is unavailable as a mentor for the 2027 call); applicants must also coordinate support from their current supervisors/managers. Fellows engage in DDC activities, seminars/workshops, and public outreach, and submit a short report within two months of finishing. APPLICATION (in English): preliminary project title, fellowship type, brief project description (max 1000 chars), detailed description and timeline (max 2400 chars, including duration, confirmed mentor, and remote/in-office work plan), description of activities and collaboration with external practice partners (max 1000 chars), expected product/impact/output (max 500 chars), a CV (max 5 pages), and an overview of the posts for which financial support is sought. Submit the application form (PDF or Word) plus CV as PDFs to ddc@sdu.dk with subject line 'DDC / TrygFonden Fellowship application'. Deadline 1 September 2026; decisions communicated no later than 1 October 2026.

  5. Marie Skłodowska-Curie Postdoctoral Fellowships 2026 (MSCA-PF)

    European Commission (Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions, under Horizon Europe; managed by the European Research Executive Agency, REA) · Europe (host institution in an EU Member State or Horizon Europe Associated Country; also possible: Global Postdoctoral Fellowships hosted in non-associated third countries with a return phase in Europe) · Deadline: 09 Sep 2026 · Award: Standard MSCA unit costs covering researcher allowance (living, mobility, family), research/training/networking costs and management/indirect costs (annual values published in the call's Work Programme; typically a fully-funded postdoc package of roughly €5,000 to €8,000+ per researcher per month equivalent depending on host country correction coefficient)

    MSCA Postdoctoral Fellowships support researchers holding a PhD who wish to carry out research abroad, acquire new skills, develop their careers and have international mobility. Open to excellent researchers of any nationality. The 2026 call opened 9 April 2026 and closes 9 September 2026 at 17:00 CEST; notification of call results expected February 2027; grant agreement signature April 2027. Two strands: European Postdoctoral Fellowships (12 to 24 months in Europe) and Global Postdoctoral Fellowships (12 to 24 months outside Europe + 12-month return phase in Europe). The project must take place in a country different from where the researcher has worked or studied. Approximately 1,600 projects funded. Apply via the EU Funding & Tenders Opportunities Portal; submission is by the fellow plus host institution. Eligibility: researcher must have a PhD at the call deadline (or have submitted thesis with all requirements met), maximum 8 years full-time-equivalent research experience post-PhD, must comply with the mobility rule (no more than 12 months in the host country in the 36 months before the deadline).

  6. Royal Conservatoire Antwerp: Call for Artistic Research Proposals (2027 start)

    Royal Conservatoire Antwerp (AP University of the Arts) · Antwerp, Belgium. Salaried research position; international applicants must hold a valid Belgian work permit. · Deadline: 21 Sep 2026 · Award: Salaried research position (contractual appointment). Start projects: 20% employment for 12 months + EUR 1,000 operating resources. One-year projects: 55% employment for 12 months + EUR 1,000. Two-year projects: 55% employment for 24 months + EUR 2,000. No application fee.

    The Royal Conservatoire Antwerp (AP University of the Arts) invites applications for artistic research projects starting 20 September 2027, in three types: start projects, one-year projects, and two-year research projects. Applications are embedded in one or more of the Conservatoire's four research groups, supervised by a supervisor, and must align with the Conservatoire's mission and vision. FOCAL POINTS across disciplines: research into improvisation and intuitive creation and performance practices; new dramaturgical and scenographic approaches in cross-over projects; methodologies for interdisciplinary creation processes; sustainable relationships between artists and urban communities; the possibilities and limitations of inclusion in education and/or on stage; and well-being and resilience in music and performing arts. FUNDING (salaried research position): start projects, 20% employment for 12 months plus EUR 1,000 operating resources; one-year projects, 55% employment for 12 months plus EUR 1,000; two-year projects, 55% employment for 24 months plus EUR 2,000. PRECONDITIONS: the applicant holds a relevant master's degree and conducts research situated in or arising from their own artistic practice with the capacity for discursivity; the proposal must align with the Conservatoire's mission/vision; the applicant must be able to enter contractual employment with the Royal Conservatoire Antwerp/AP University of the Arts for the duration of the project (international applicants must hold a valid Belgian work permit); proposals may be submitted only in Dutch or English. PROCEDURE (two steps): STEP 1, submit an expression of interest by 21 September 2026, 12:00 noon (applicants informed by the week of 5 October whether they may develop a full proposal with a research group); STEP 2, submit the full project dossier by 1 December 2026, 12:00 noon. Evaluation by an independent reading committee aims to conclude in March 2027. Contact: Kevin Voets, Head of Research, kevin.voets@ap.be.

  7. Horizon Europe Cluster 2: Culture, Creativity and Inclusive Society (HORIZON-CL2-2026-01-HERITAGE) — incl. AI and Creative Content topic

    European Research Executive Agency (REA) / European Commission, Horizon Europe · European Union and associated countries. Consortium-based; typically requires partners from multiple EU/Horizon member states. · Deadline: 23 Sep 2026 · Award: Indicative total budget €85.5 million across 7 topics (2026 call). Individual project amounts vary by topic. Up to 100% funding rate for non-profit research organizations. No application fee.

    Horizon Europe Cluster 2 funds research on culture, creativity, and inclusive society. The 2026 call (HORIZON-CL2-2026-01-HERITAGE, launched May 12, 2026) covers 7 topics with an indicative budget of €85.5 million. Topic HERITAGE-04 is explicitly titled 'Towards a fair and transparent market for cultural and creative content in the era of generative AI,' covering: impacts of generative AI on creative industries employment; upskilling needs for creative workers; policy and regulatory frameworks; fair remuneration and transparency. Highly relevant for research into AI and cultural/creative industries, artists' rights in the AI era, and sociotechnical studies of AI and culture. ELIGIBILITY: consortium-based applications (minimum 3 organisations from 3 different EU/associated countries are typical for Horizon RIA grants; check call conditions on the Funding & Tenders portal for specific requirements). Deadline: 23 September 2026. Apply via the EU Funding & Tenders portal (ec.europa.eu/info/funding-tenders).

PROCEDURE (two steps): STEP 1, apply via the Expression of Interest form sent to research_royalacademyantwerp@ap.be before 3 November 2026, 12:00 noon (applicants informed no later than end November 2026 whether they may develop a full dossier); STEP 2, submit the full dossier via the two-year project application form before 1 February 2027, 12:00 noon. Evaluation by an independent reading committee aims to conclude in May 2027. Contact: research_royalacademyantwerp@ap.be.
