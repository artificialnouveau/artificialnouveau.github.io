Cross-Disciplinary and Social Impact Grants in the Netherlands

Currently 7 active cross-disciplinary and social impact grants, fellowships and residencies open to applicants in the Netherlands. Hand-curated and updated weekly. Almost every entry is funded; a few notable unpaid open calls and festival submissions are included as clearly flagged exceptions. Open calls

  1. Stimuleringsfonds: Digital Culture Grant Scheme 2026 (final round)

    Stimuleringsfonds Creatieve Industrie (Creative Industries Fund NL) · Netherlands. Requires Dutch Chamber of Commerce (KvK) registration. · Deadline: 19 Aug 2026 · Award: EUR 10,000-25,000 for solo projects; EUR 10,000-50,000 for collaborative projects with partners. At least 20 percent of total project costs must be covered by co-financing. No application fee.

    The Stimuleringsfonds Digital Culture Grant Scheme (Deelregeling Digitale cultuur) supports projects by makers, researchers, design studios and cultural institutions positioned professionally in digital culture. Solo projects can receive EUR 10,000-25,000; collaborative projects with partners can receive up to EUR 50,000. At least 20 percent of total project costs must be covered by co-financing. Applicants must be registered with the Dutch Chamber of Commerce (KvK). The 2026 application window for this round runs 12-19 August 2026 (opens 12 August 15:00, closes 19 August 16:00 CEST); in 2026 the scheme runs only two rounds and this is the last. No application fee. Apply via the official scheme page.

  2. Stimuleringsfonds + Film Fund: Immerse\Interact 2026 (second round)

    Stimuleringsfonds Creatieve Industrie and the Netherlands Film Fund · Netherlands. Requires Dutch Chamber of Commerce (KvK) registration. · Deadline: 25 Aug 2026 · Award: Development: EUR 15,000 for individual makers (plus up to EUR 5,000 coaching, max EUR 20,000) or EUR 25,000 for producers (plus up to EUR 5,000 coaching). Realisation: up to EUR 35,000 for individual makers or up to EUR 125,000 for producers (Fund covers max 85 percent). No application fee.

    Immerse\Interact is a joint grant scheme of the Stimuleringsfonds Creatieve Industrie and the Netherlands Film Fund for immersive and interactive media projects (VR, MR, AR and other interactive storytelling). It funds both development and realisation. Development support is EUR 15,000 for individual makers (plus up to EUR 5,000 coaching, capped at EUR 20,000) or EUR 25,000 for producers; realisation support is up to EUR 35,000 for individual makers or up to EUR 125,000 for producers (the Fund covers a maximum of 85 percent of costs). Open to professional makers, collectives and producers with a demonstrable track record in immersive or interactive media; applicants must be registered with the Dutch Chamber of Commerce (KvK). The second 2026 round runs 24 June to 25 August 2026 (opens 24 June 15:00, closes 25 August 16:00 CEST). No application fee. Questions: immerseinteract@stimuleringsfonds.nl.

  3. Stimuleringsfonds: Open Call Fresh Perspectives 2026

    Stimuleringsfonds Creatieve Industrie (Creative Industries Fund NL) · Netherlands (Dutch fund; cross-sector collaboration) · Deadline: 25 Aug 2026 · Award: Two-phase grant. Phase 1: ten grants of EUR 10,000 (max 5 months, startup 1 December 2026 to 30 April 2027) to prepare, explore and prototype the project. Phase 2: ten grants of EUR 50,000 (max 12 months, implementation 1 September 2027 to 31 August 2028) for projects selected and fully completed in Phase 1. No application fee.

    Open call from the Creative Industries Fund NL (Stimuleringsfonds) for cross-sector collaboration projects that offer new perspectives on current challenges such as climate, housing, polarisation, migration or inequality of opportunity. The procedure runs in two phases. In Phase 1, the applicant and partner use the period to prepare and kick off the project, further explore the theme, intended end product and collaboration, and investigate possibilities (including working on a prototype or proof of concept), concluding with a project plan, budget and collaboration agreement. Phase 2, open only to projects selected and fully completed in Phase 1, focuses on implementation and requires both a written application and a presentation to the advisory committee; projects may deliver various end products (an activity, intervention, event, publication, service, product or process) accompanied by a suitable form of knowledge sharing such as a presentation, symposium or publication. ELIGIBILITY: applicants working with at least one partner in cross-sector collaboration; see the fund's open-call page for full eligibility details. Deadline 25 August 2026, 16:00 CEST.

  4. Stimuleringsfonds: Festivals Creative Industries 2027

    Stimuleringsfonds Creatieve Industrie · Netherlands · Deadline: 23 Sep 2026 · Award: Per-festival grant; specific maximum per Festivals scheme rules (cash subsidy, not in-kind)

    Annual scheme funding festival organisations in the creative industries (design, architecture, digital culture, fashion, e-culture, etc.) presenting a 2027 edition. Application window opens 25 August 2026 at 15:00 CEST and closes 23 September 2026 at 16:00 CEST. Supports both content programming and the organisational/curatorial running of a festival; applicants must be organisations rather than individuals, but the scheme is the natural home for any artist-led collective or platform that runs a critical-AI / digital-culture / surveillance-focused festival event. Note the wider 2026 context: Stimuleringsfonds is restructuring its grant offering for 2027, and several individual schemes (Design, Digital Culture) have been reduced to two rounds in 2026. The Festivals scheme is a separate annual track and one of the cleaner ways to access SCI funding for an event-format project.

  5. Rijkscultuurfondsen: Interdisciplinary Grant

    Dutch national cultural funds (Rijkscultuurfondsen), via the Mondriaan Fund · Netherlands. For professional makers and organisations based in the Netherlands or the Caribbean part of the Kingdom whose practice spans multiple disciplines. · Deadline: 01 Oct 2026 · Award: EUR 12,500 to 50,000 per project. The individual-maker track is approximately EUR 24,900 for 12 months (income cap EUR 55,000 including the grant).

    The Interdisciplinary grant is a new joint scheme of the six Dutch national cultural funds (Mondriaan Fund, Performing Arts Fund NL, Fund for Cultural Participation, Netherlands Film Fund, Creative Industries Fund NL, and the Dutch Foundation for Literature), announced on 7 July 2026. It supports makers and organisations whose work genuinely crosses the boundaries of the individual funds' disciplines (visual art, design, film, literature, performing arts, and cultural participation) and cannot be contained within a single discipline. TWO TRACKS: an Individual track for professional makers with a demonstrable interdisciplinary practice (at least one year of experience; income cap EUR 55,000 including the grant; approximately EUR 24,900 for 12 months); and an Organisation track for organisations running multi-discipline projects. AMOUNT: EUR 12,500 to 50,000 per project. ELIGIBILITY: makers and organisations based in the Netherlands or the Caribbean part of the Kingdom. APPLYING: handled through the Mondriaan Fund's online application portal (info@mondriaanfonds.nl); an account is required in advance. DEADLINE: 1 October 2026, 16:00 Dutch time (single application round).

  6. International Vouchers

    Stimuleringsfonds Creatieve Industrie (Creative Industries Fund NL) · Netherlands (for international travel) · Deadline: 27 Oct 2026 · Award: Up to €1,500 within Europe (zone 1) / up to €2,500 outside Europe (zone 2)

    Travel voucher for Dutch-based professionals in design, architecture and/or digital culture who have been invited by a foreign party to give a presentation, lecture or workshop. To apply you must have submitted an application to the Fund in the past five years that was positively assessed. Round 2 (4 May to 22 June 2026) has now closed. Round 3 is the current round: it runs 8 September 2026 15:00 CEST to 27 October 2026 16:00 CEST, with a budget of €33,000.

  7. Cultuur+Ondernemen: Cultuurlening (rolling loan, Netherlands)

    Cultuur+Ondernemen · Netherlands. Creative and cultural professionals and organisations residing and working in NL. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Loan (must be repaid), up to EUR 500,000. Interest 2 percent up to EUR 50,000, then 2-5 percent (risk-based) above EUR 50,000; term up to 10 years. This is a LOAN, not a grant. No application fee.

    The Cultuurlening from Cultuur+Ondernemen is a low-interest loan (not a grant) for creative and cultural professionals and organisations in the Netherlands, to invest in their practice or business. Loans run up to EUR 500,000, with interest of 2 percent up to EUR 50,000 and 2-5 percent (risk-based) above EUR 50,000, and a term of up to 10 years. The loan must be repaid. Eligible applicants reside and work in the Netherlands and are either art-school graduates or have at least three years of professional practice (others can apply with a motivation and reference). Applications are accepted on a rolling basis with no fixed deadline. No application fee. Note: separate regional products exist (for example the Rotterdam and Amsterdam Cultuurlening) with different rates and caps.
