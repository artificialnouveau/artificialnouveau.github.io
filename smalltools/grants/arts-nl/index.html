Visual and Media Arts Grants in the Netherlands

Currently 24 active visual and media arts grants, fellowships and residencies open to applicants in the Netherlands. Hand-curated and updated weekly. Almost every entry is funded; a few notable unpaid open calls and festival submissions are included as clearly flagged exceptions.     Mondriaan Fund (Mondriaan Fonds) · Netherlands. Featured artist(s) must be professional visual artists based in the Netherlands or the Caribbean part of the Kingdom; publishers may co-apply. · Deadline: 30 Jul 2026 · Award: Contribution of up to 35% of eligible expenses.     The Mondriaan Fund's Publication grant supports the production of special publications about living visual artists from the Netherlands or the Caribbean part of the Kingdom. It funds books and other publications that give substantial, high-quality attention to an artist's work and practice. WHO CAN APPLY: visual artists and publishers, individually or jointly; the featured artist(s) must be professional and have been active for at least one year, based in the Netherlands or the Caribbean part of the Kingdom. AMOUNT: the Mondriaan Fund contributes a maximum of 35% of the eligible expenses; the available budget for this round is EUR 205,000. APPLYING: submitted through the Mondriaan Fund's online application portal, which requires a pre-created account (allow several working days to set up). DEADLINE: 30 July 2026, 16:00 Dutch time (10:00 Caribbean time).

  2. Stichting Stokroos: Maker Initiatives Grant 2026

    Stichting Stokroos · Kingdom of the Netherlands (including its Caribbean parts) or Suriname; must have lived and worked there at least one year. Applicants outside the Randstad, or plans taking place there, are particularly encouraged. · Deadline: 31 Jul 2026 · Award: Fixed grant of EUR 25,000 or EUR 40,000 (chosen depending on need). Maximum participant fee EUR 2,200/month (40-hour week); third-party hire capped at EUR 85/hour excl. VAT. Co-funding not required. No application fee.

    Stichting Stokroos Maker Initiatives supports established visual artists, designers and craft practitioners (overlap possible), including duos and collectives, in developing and realising a multivocal artistic project or programme centred on a common concern, in collaboration with other makers or communities. There is one application round per year and Stokroos can support 4 to 6 proposals; a maximum of 40 eligible applications are considered per period. Eligibility: you completed your first subject-specific (vocational or higher) programme in 2020 or earlier, or have an equivalent established track record, or started without formal training (first presentation marks the start of your practice); at least 21 years old; living and working in the Kingdom of the Netherlands (including its Caribbean parts) or Suriname for at least one year; not currently enrolled in a study or post-academic programme. You must apply together with an artistic or creative partner (from professional makers to communities); duos and collectives must also have an external partner. You may submit only one application per year across all Stokroos grants, and must not have received a Seed Grant rejection in the previous calendar year. The grant is a fixed contribution of EUR 25,000 or EUR 40,000 depending on need. Plans can take place anywhere, start in the next calendar year and last a maximum of two years. Applications are submitted via the online form in Dutch or English (portfolio PDF up to 20 pages/person, optional video up to 10 minutes total, CV up to 2 pages/person, budget up to 3 pages, work/plan/collaboration descriptions up to 2,500 characters each). Stokroos aims to respond within 10 weeks; no appeal is possible. Application window: 20 to 31 July 2026. Questions: mail@stokroos.nl.

  3. Open Call: Pee Workshop (Matter Out of Place)

    Nica Roses & Eris Kyriakides, at The Dirty Art Foundation · The Dirty Art Foundation (Amsterdam, Netherlands) · Deadline: 10 Aug 2026 · Award: Performer fee of EUR 200. Participation in the optional public showing is not required to receive the fee.

    Pee Workshop is looking for 16 performers to take part in a two-day research practice led by Nica Roses and Eris Kyriakides as part of the dance and performance event Matter Out of Place. The workshop uses drinking, holding, and releasing liquid as a starting point for choreographic research, paying attention to the body's internal timing: what we consume, how liquid moves through the body, what the bladder signals, and how the organs produce movements that often go unnoticed. This cycle becomes the structure of the research, opening into dance, somatic exercises, choreographic tasks, group compositions, and experiments with collective energy. The material develops through urination but also through the shared experience of moving, waiting, responding, and performing together. Performers, dancers, movement practitioners, and artists interested in somatic research, experimental choreography, and collective performance-making are welcome; professional dance training is not required, but applicants should feel comfortable working physically and collaboratively. All exercises are approached through clear consent, respect for personal boundaries, and collective care. The workshop takes place Monday 17 and Tuesday 18 August 2026 from 10:00 to 17:00 at The Dirty Art Foundation, culminating in an optional 60-minute public showing on Wednesday 19 August. Each performer receives a fee of EUR 200. To apply, send a short introduction about yourself, your movement or performance experience, and why you are interested in the workshop to nicaxroses@gmail.com by 10 August 2026.

  4. Mondriaan Fund: Delfina Foundation Residency 2027 (London)

    Mondriaan Fund / Delfina Foundation · London, UK. For visual artists and curators based in the Netherlands or the Caribbean part of the Kingdom, funded by the Mondriaan Fund. · Deadline: 27 Aug 2026 · Award: Subsidy of EUR 9,188 for an artist or EUR 7,232 for a curator/art observer toward travel, material and living costs (a 10 percent personal contribution is automatically deducted by the Mondriaan Fund); studio, living space and Delfina Foundation support are paid directly by the Mondriaan Fund. Contribution for Caribbean-based artists can be increased for higher travel costs. No application fee.

    Mondriaan Fund-supported working period at London's Delfina Foundation, one of the UK's best known artist-in-residence programmes, housed at 29-31 Catherine Place near Buckingham Palace with space for 6 to 8 international residents (own room, shared kitchen and facilities). Delfina promotes artistic exchange and experiment, offering visual artists and curators a platform to carry out research, develop ideas and position their practice within the global discourse, with introductions to the London art scene and Delfina's international network. The programme includes visits to museums, galleries and non-profit platforms, discussions with London-based artists, portfolio reviews with curators and regular networking lunches; residents are expected to take part. Applicants are not asked for a concrete project proposal but must submit a clear motivation: why they wish to take part, what research they want to conduct, and how it can contribute to Delfina's ongoing programme. WHO: visual artists and curators based in the Netherlands or the Caribbean part of the Kingdom; duo applications not possible; application must be in English. One work period available: 27 September to 17 December 2027. Selection by an advisory committee with a Delfina representative; processing may exceed three months. DEADLINE: Thursday 27 August 2026, before 16:00 Dutch time / 11:00 Caribbean time.

  5. Mondriaan Fund: Residency

    Mondriaan Fund (Mondriaan Fonds) · Netherlands. Applicants must be based in the Netherlands or the Caribbean part of the Kingdom; residencies may take place in the Netherlands or abroad. · Deadline: 27 Aug 2026 · Award: Grant amount varies depending on the location and duration of the residency. Applicants must contribute at least 10% themselves (personal investment, third-party contributions, an interest-free loan, or crowdfunding).

    The Mondriaan Fund's Residency grant supports professional visual artists, curators, and art observers who want to deepen their practice through a residency in the Netherlands or abroad. It contributes to the costs of a self-arranged residency that offers meaningful development for the applicant's work. WHO CAN APPLY: visual artists, curators, and art observers who have been professionally active for at least one year and are based in the Netherlands or the Caribbean part of the Kingdom. AMOUNT: the grant amount varies depending on the residency's location and duration; applicants must contribute at least 10% of the costs themselves, through personal investment, third-party contributions, an interest-free loan, or crowdfunding. APPLYING: submitted through the Mondriaan Fund's online application portal, which requires a pre-created account (allow several working days). DEADLINE: 27 August 2026, 16:00 Dutch time (11:00 Caribbean time).

  6. Stichting Stokroos: Residency at Fundaziun Nairs 2026

    Stichting Stokroos · Residency in Scuol, Switzerland (Fundaziun Nairs). Applicants must live and work in the Kingdom of the Netherlands (including its Caribbean parts) or Suriname for at least one year. Applicants outside the Randstad particularly encouraged. · Deadline: 11 Sep 2026 · Award: Fully supported stay (accommodation and meals paid directly to Fundaziun Nairs) plus an unrestricted living, travel and production budget of EUR 7,000. 80% paid before the residency, the remainder on receipt of a brief final report. No application fee.

    Stichting Stokroos funds a three-month reflective residency at Fundaziun Nairs in Scuol, Switzerland, for one selected visual artist, designer or craft practitioner (overlap possible). Both emerging and established makers may apply. Stokroos covers full accommodation and meals directly with Fundaziun Nairs and provides a discretionary EUR 7,000 budget for living, travel and production. The application should include a proposal that engages specifically with the residency, its Alpine surroundings and/or its historical and cultural context. Eligibility: you completed your first subject-specific (vocational or higher) programme in 2022 or earlier, or have an equivalent established practice, or started without formal training (first presentation marks the start of your practice); at least 21 years old; living and working in the Kingdom of the Netherlands (including its Caribbean parts) or Suriname for at least one year; not currently enrolled in a study or post-academic programme. Applications are individual only; duos and collectives cannot apply. Makers already selected by Fundaziun Nairs may still apply if they meet the criteria. You may submit only one application per year across all Stokroos grants, and must not have received a Seed Grant rejection in the previous calendar year. Apply via the online form (Dutch or English) with a portfolio PDF up to 20 pages, optional video up to 10 minutes total, CV up to 2 pages, and work/plan descriptions up to 2,500 characters each. Selection includes an introductory interview with up to three candidates; Stokroos aims to respond within 8 weeks. No appeal is possible. Application window: 1 to 11 September 2026. More information at nairs.ch.

  9. Stichting Stokroos: Seed Grant 2026

    Stichting Stokroos · Kingdom of the Netherlands (including its Caribbean parts) or Suriname; must have lived and worked there at least one year. Applicants outside the Randstad, or plans taking place there, particularly encouraged. · Deadline: 31 Oct 2026 · Award: Fixed grant (no project budget required): EUR 5,000 for an individual, EUR 7,500 for a duo, EUR 10,000 for a collective. Paid in full within 2 weeks of award. No application fee.

    The Stichting Stokroos Seed Grant gives individual makers, duos and collectives time and space to focus on their artistic development, for example a focused working period for research and experiment, or the development of a prototype, new work or methodology. There are two application rounds in 2026: 15 May to 30 June 2026 (now full) and 1 September to 31 October 2026; Stokroos considers a maximum of 60 eligible applications per period (or closes once the budget is reached) and supports around 50 applications per year. Eligibility: active as a visual artist, designer and/or craft practitioner (overlap possible); living and working in the Kingdom of the Netherlands (including its Caribbean parts) or Suriname for at least one year; at least 21 years old; you completed your first subject-specific (vocational or higher) programme between 2018 and 2022, or started without formal training (first presentation marks the start of your practice); not currently enrolled in a study or post-academic programme. Duos and collectives may apply provided all participants meet the guidelines. The grant is a fixed amount with no budget required: EUR 5,000 (individual), EUR 7,500 (duo) or EUR 10,000 (collective). Plans can take place anywhere, start at least 10 weeks after submission and last a maximum of one year. You may apply for the Seed Grant once every two calendar years and receive it up to twice, and may submit only one application per year across all Stokroos grants. Apply via the online form (Dutch or English) with a portfolio PDF up to 20 pages/person, optional video up to 10 minutes total, CV up to 2 pages/person, and work/plan/collaboration descriptions up to 2,500 characters each. Stokroos aims to respond within 8 weeks; no appeal is possible. Next deadline: 31 October 2026.

  10. Het Cultuurfonds: Ontwikkelbijdrage for Individual Makers and Collectives (rolling, Netherlands)

    Het Cultuurfonds (formerly Prins Bernhard Cultuurfonds), via voordekunst · Netherlands. Open to individual makers and collectives based in the Netherlands, across all disciplines. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Ontwikkelbijdrage (development contribution) up to EUR 5,000, awarded once every three years. A separate matchfundingbijdrage of EUR 250 to EUR 1,500 (max 50 percent of the crowdfunding target) is available once a year for public-facing projects. No application fee.

    Since 2025 Het Cultuurfonds runs a dedicated application route for individual makers and collectives, separate from its project grants for foundations and associations. The ontwikkelbijdrage awards up to EUR 5,000 for research, workshops, masterclasses, courses and training, experiments and equipment, and for the pre-phase of a project, giving makers room to test and develop an idea before it has to be fully executed. It is open to all disciplines: visual artists, theatre makers, spoken word artists, photographers, musicians, ceramicists, restorers and more. A separate matchfundingbijdrage of EUR 250 to EUR 1,500 supports crowdfunding campaigns for public-facing projects such as exhibitions or performances. The route is run with voordekunst. Applying is a two-step process: a short vooraanvraag (pre-application) answered within one week, then a full application answered within four weeks, with development funds paid out within two weeks of approval. Rolling intake, no application fee.

  11. Amarte Fonds: Development and Project Contributions (rolling)

    Amarte Fonds · Netherlands and the Caribbean part of the Kingdom (the ABC and SSS islands). Applicants must be based there. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Development contribution up to EUR 2,750; project contribution up to EUR 5,500. Up to 80 percent paid in advance, remainder after the report is approved. No application fee.

    Amarte Fonds is a private Dutch foundation that supports the arts, with rolling (continuous) contributions for visual artists and independent curators. There are two contribution types: a development contribution of up to EUR 2,750 and a project contribution of up to EUR 5,500. Up to 80 percent is paid in advance, with the remainder after the final report is approved. Applicants must be based in the Netherlands or the Caribbean part of the Kingdom (the ABC and SSS islands). The fund also supports work across theatre, film, music, literature and interdisciplinary projects through specific programmes. There is no fixed deadline and no application fee; decisions are relatively fast. Apply year-round via the online portal.

  20. Mondriaan Fund: Voucher International (rolling)

    Mondriaan Fund (Mondriaan Fonds) · Activity takes place outside the Netherlands. Applicants must be based in the Netherlands or the Caribbean part of the Kingdom. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Up to EUR 2,500, covering travel and accommodation costs only. No application fee.

    Quick-response voucher for visual artists, curators, art critics and art observers based in the Netherlands or the Caribbean part of the Kingdom who have been invited to take part in a public programme abroad. Eligible activities include giving a lecture, performance, book presentation or taking part in a panel at a contemporary visual art institution, venue, festival or symposium outside the Netherlands. Participation in an exhibition and commercial gallery or art fair contexts are not eligible. Visual artists need at least one year of professional practice; curators, critics and art observers need at least two years. Applicants cannot be enrolled full-time in a Bachelor's, Master's or postdoctoral programme. The voucher covers travel and accommodation up to EUR 2,500. Rolling intake, but the timing window is strict: applications are accepted a maximum of 4 months and a minimum of 5 weeks before the activity starts. No application fee.

  21. Fonds 21: Kunst & Cultuur Project Grants (rolling, Netherlands)

    Fonds 21 · Netherlands. Dutch foundations, associations and non-profit BVs/NVs; projects must reach a broad and/or new audience in the Netherlands. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Minimum request EUR 10,000, no stated maximum; around EUR 11.5 million awarded per year across all pillars. No application fee.

    Fonds 21 supports professional, high-quality art and culture projects by Dutch non-profit organizations (foundations, associations and non-profit BVs/NVs), across performing arts, visual arts, film, literature and interdisciplinary work, with additional pillars for arts education and youth & society. The minimum request is EUR 10,000 with no stated maximum, and roughly EUR 11.5 million is available annually. Applications are accepted year-round via the online portal (after a short eligibility snelcheck) and must be submitted at least four months before the project start date. Free-of-charge events are generally not supported; the fund expects most programming to be paid-access. No application fee.

  22. Het Cultuurfonds: Project Grants for Foundations and Associations (rolling, Netherlands)

    Het Cultuurfonds (formerly Prins Bernhard Cultuurfonds) · Netherlands. National office plus provincial chapters; projects in culture, nature and science. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Typically EUR 15,000 to EUR 50,000 for national project applications by foundations and associations; smaller amounts via provincial chapters and themed funds (EUR 1,500 to EUR 25,000). Individual makers and collectives can request up to EUR 5,000. No application fee.

    Het Cultuurfonds (formerly Prins Bernhard Cultuurfonds) invests over EUR 40 million per year in culture and nature in the Netherlands. Foundations and associations can apply year-round for project grants (typically EUR 15,000 to EUR 50,000 at national level), and since 2025 individual makers and collectives can apply for up to EUR 5,000. Provincial chapters run their own quarterly rounds for regional projects, and numerous themed funds (EUR 5,000 to EUR 25,000) cover specific disciplines. Rolling intake via the online application portal. No application fee.

  23. VSBfonds: Kunst & Cultuur Donations (rolling, Netherlands)

    VSBfonds · European Netherlands. Private legal entities (foundations, associations); applicants without a registered legal form are also eligible. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: No fixed minimum or maximum; the requested amount must be in proportion to other funding sources. No application fee.

    VSBfonds supports cultural initiatives that make it possible for everyone in the Netherlands to take part in art and culture, spanning ten programme lines from professional performances to community arts. Applications are accepted year-round with no deadlines from foundations, associations and applicants without a formal legal form, and must be submitted at least four months before the initiative starts (two months for amateur arts). A two-minute online quickscan checks eligibility before the formal application. No fixed minimum or maximum amount; the request should be proportionate to other funding. No application fee.

  24. Fonds voor Cultuurparticipatie: Open Schemes for Cultural Institutions 2025-2028 (rolling, Netherlands)

    Fonds voor Cultuurparticipatie · Netherlands and the Caribbean parts of the Kingdom. Cultural institutions, vocational education institutions and interdisciplinary makers. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: EUR 5,000 to EUR 75,000 depending on the scheme (e.g. cultural education in mbo EUR 5,000-55,000, in vmbo/vso/pro EUR 10,000-65,000, design-based learning platforms up to EUR 75,000, interdisciplinary makers EUR 12,500-50,000). No application fee.

    The Fonds voor Cultuurparticipatie, one of the Dutch national cultural funds (annual budget around EUR 30 million), currently has several schemes open for applications: cultural education in vocational education (mbo, EUR 5,000-55,000), cultural education in vmbo/vso/pro (EUR 10,000-65,000), cultural education for the Caribbean parts of the Kingdom (EUR 5,000-50,000), an open call for design-based learning platforms (up to EUR 75,000) and an interdisciplinary scheme for professional makers (EUR 12,500-50,000). Most schemes target cultural institutions, in some cases in partnership with schools. See the subsidies overview for per-scheme conditions. No application fee.
