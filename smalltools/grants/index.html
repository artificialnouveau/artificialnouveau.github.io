How to subscribe (RSS reader or email)
  1. Optionally tick one or more Categories, pick a Region, and/or pick a Timeline to narrow the feed. If you tick multiple categories, you'll get one feed URL per category, which you can subscribe to separately.
  2. Copy the feed URL above (or click Open feed to preview it).
  3. Paste the URL into either:
    • An RSS reader — Feedly, NetNewsWire, Inoreader, Reeder, Thunderbird. New entries appear in the reader as they're added.
    • A free RSS-to-email service for an inbox digest, no account needed here:
      • Kill the Newsletter — dead simple, no signup; generates a unique address that turns each new feed item into a plain email.
      • Blogtrottr — pick realtime, daily or weekly digest.
      • Feedrabbit — similar digest controls, free up to a few feeds.
  4. Deadline-filtered feeds (Next 30 / 90 days) only include grants with a fixed deadline in that window. Rolling/undated grants are excluded from those.
  5. "Added in last 30 days" includes everything added recently regardless of deadline (great for a "what's new" feed and the closest match to a newsletter when paired with a weekly email digest).

How to subscribe
  1. Optionally tick one or more Categories and/or pick a Region to narrow the calendar. If you tick multiple categories, you'll get one .ics URL per category, which you can subscribe to separately.
  2. Copy the calendar URL.
  3. Google Calendar: "Other calendars" → "+" → "From URL", paste, Add.
  4. Apple Calendar: "File" → "New Calendar Subscription", paste, OK.
  5. Outlook: "Add calendar" → "Subscribe from web", paste.
  6. Your calendar refreshes every few hours, so new grants and changes flow in automatically.

Frequently asked questions

What kinds of grants are listed on The Grant Desk?

Paid open calls, fellowships and residencies for artists, researchers and technologists. Categories cover AI and AI safety, digital and mixed-media arts, film and video, research and journalism, tech and infrastructure, and cross-disciplinary or social-impact work. Every entry is funded; "exposure only" calls and pay-to-play submission fees are filtered out (or clearly flagged when relevant).

How often is the list updated?

A scanning bot surfaces fresh candidates from various Slack and Discord channels, Bluesky, RSS sources and grant-maker pages each week, then I curate the strong fits by hand. Closed calls are auto-filed away after their deadline, so the visible list stays current.

Can I subscribe by RSS or add deadlines to my calendar?

Yes. There is an RSS feed and an ICS calendar feed for every region and category combination. Subscribe in your reader (Feedly, NetNewsWire, etc.) or add the calendar to Google Calendar, Apple Calendar or Outlook so deadlines reach you wherever you already read.

Who runs The Grant Desk, and how can I suggest a grant?

The Grant Desk is maintained by Artificial Nouveau Studio. To suggest a grant, use the "Suggest a grant" form in the top strip; if it is paid and a strong fit, it lands on the desk.
