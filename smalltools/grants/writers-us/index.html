Writing and Translation Grants in the US

Currently 5 active writing and translation grants, fellowships and residencies open to applicants in the US. Hand-curated and updated weekly. Almost every entry is funded; a few notable unpaid open calls and festival submissions are included as clearly flagged exceptions. Open calls

  1. The Center for Fiction / Susan Kamil Emerging Writer Fellowship

    The Center for Fiction · New York City, USA · Deadline: 31 Jul 2026 · Award: USD 5,000 grant plus writers studio space, mentorship, agent introductions, and community programming

    The Center for Fiction / Susan Kamil Emerging Writer Fellowship annually supports nine early-career, New York City-based fiction writers at a pivotal point in their careers. Each fellow receives a 5,000 USD grant, dedicated space and time to write in the Writers Studio, personalized editorial mentorship, opportunities to meet literary agents, and community programming. Applicants must be current residents of one of the five boroughs and must remain in New York City for the entire fellowship year. An emerging writer is defined as someone who has not yet had a novel or short story collection published by a major or independent publisher and who is not currently under contract for a work of fiction; students enrolled in a degree-granting program during the fellowship year are not eligible. The 2027 fellowship application window opens July 1, 2026 and closes July 31, 2026.

  2. Woodward/Newman Award 2027-28 (Constellation Stage & Screen)

    Constellation Stage & Screen · Bloomington, Indiana, USA. Open submissions of unpublished plays; production takes place in Bloomington. · Deadline: 31 Aug 2026 · Award: USD 3,000 cash prize (in lieu of royalties) plus a full production in Constellation's Mainstage season; housing and transportation provided. No submission fee.

    The Woodward/Newman Award, started with the support of Joanne Woodward, Newman's Own Foundation and the Newman family, is awarded annually by Constellation Stage & Screen to the best unpublished new play. The winner receives a USD 3,000 cash prize (in lieu of royalties) and a full production in the Mainstage season, with housing and transportation provided. The contest format and submission fees have been eliminated: submissions are reviewed on an ongoing basis and are also considered for all seven production slots in the season, including TYA (theatre for young audiences) shows. Full-length plays should run 75 to 135 minutes; TYA shows over 40 minutes. Plays must be unpublished at submission (independently published is acceptable); limit of two submissions per playwright per year; musicals may include demos. Plays submitted by midnight 31 August 2026 are considered for the 2027-28 season; later submissions roll to 2028-29. All submissions are kept on file for two years.

  3. Hodder Fellowship 2027-28 (Princeton University)

    Lewis Center for the Arts, Princeton University · Princeton, New Jersey, United States. Open internationally; fellows spend the academic year at Princeton. · Deadline: 08 Sep 2026 · Award: USD 93,000 stipend for the academic year. No application fee.

    The Hodder Fellowship at Princeton's Lewis Center for the Arts supports early-career artists of exceptional promise (writers, composers, choreographers, visual artists, performance artists, and other kinds of artists) to pursue independent projects during an academic year at Princeton, with a USD 93,000 stipend. Candidates typically have published or produced work and are not yet established. Open internationally. Deadline 8 September 2026, 11:59pm EDT; apply via Interfolio.

  4. Cullman Center Fellowships 2027-28 (New York Public Library)

    Dorothy and Lewis B. Cullman Center for Scholars and Writers, New York Public Library · New York City, United States. Open internationally; fellows work in residence at NYPL's Stephen A. Schwarzman Building. · Deadline: 25 Sep 2026 · Award: USD 90,000 stipend, plus an office at the library, full access to collections, and a September-May term. No application fee.

    The Cullman Center for Scholars and Writers at the New York Public Library awards fifteen fellowships a year to writers and scholars (fiction, nonfiction, poetry, playwriting, translation, and academic research) whose projects draw on the Library's collections. Fellows receive USD 90,000, an office, and full research access for a September-May term in residence. Open internationally. Deadline 25 September 2026, 5pm EDT; apply via the NYPL online application portal.

  5. Leon Levy Center for Biography Fellowships 2027-28 (incl. Levy/Sloan Science Biography)

    Leon Levy Center for Biography, CUNY Graduate Center · New York City, USA (in residence at the CUNY Graduate Center, September to May) · Deadline: 04 Jan 2027 · Award: $72,000 stipend plus writing space, full research facilities and research assistance

    Four resident biography fellowships plus the Levy/Sloan Fellowship, funded by the Alfred P. Sloan Foundation, for a biography of a figure in science or technology; separate Sherwin fellowship for nuclear-age history. Open to writers of all nationalities, with explicit preference for first-time biographers who have not yet held a biography fellowship, though mid-career and senior writers are welcome; independent writers eligible. Fellows join monthly seminars and the center's public events. Apply with CV, career narrative, project description, writing sample and two references (agents, editors and publishers are discouraged as referees). No application fee.
