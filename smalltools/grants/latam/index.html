Grants, Fellowships and Residencies in Latin America

Currently 10 active paid grants, fellowships and residencies open to applicants in Latin America, across AI, arts, film, research, tech and cross-disciplinary practice. Hand-curated and updated weekly. Almost every entry is funded; a few notable unpaid open calls and festival submissions are included as clearly flagged exceptions. Browse the list below, or use the interactive desk for filtering and shortlisting.

Open calls

  1. 41st Mar del Plata International Film Festival: Call for Entries

    Festival Internacional de Cine de Mar del Plata / INCAA · Mar del Plata, Argentina (festival 5-15 November 2026). Open to international entries; no prior public screening in Argentina. · Deadline: 15 Jul 2026 · Award: Selection in Latin America's only FIAPF A-accredited competitive festival (International and Latin American competitions, In Transit/WIP, Panorama). No submission fee stated on the registration page.

    The Mar del Plata International Film Festival is the only FIAPF A-list competitive festival in Latin America. The 41st edition accepts international entries across all genres for its International and Latin American competitions, the In Transit work-in-progress section, and Panorama. Films must be completed between November 2025 and November 2026 and not previously publicly screened in Argentina. Extended deadline 15 July 2026; submissions via the INCAA portal.

  2. Beca Plataforma Bogota: Arte, Ciencia y Tecnologia 2026 (Colombia residents only)

    Idartes / Plataforma Bogota · Bogota, Colombia. Colombian citizens or foreigners legally resident in Bogota only. · Deadline: 24 Aug 2026 · Award: 4 grants of COP 12,000,000 each (approx. EUR 2,700) to run interactive art + science + technology labs at Plataforma Bogota. No application fee.

    Plataforma Bogota, Idartes' art, science and technology media lab, awards four grants of COP 12,000,000 each to run interactive labs combining art, science, and technology. ELIGIBILITY: Colombian citizens or foreigners legally resident in Bogota only. Deadline 24 August 2026.

  3. Fundacion Ama Amoedo Grants 2026 (4th edition)

    Fundacion Ama Amoedo · Open to applicants with a significant connection to Latin America, through nationality, cultural heritage, and/or the location where the project takes place. · Deadline: 09 Sep 2026 · Award: Ten grants of USD 10,000 each, awarded annually across four categories: Artists, Art and Social Engagement, Organizations, and Publications. No application fee mentioned.

    The Fundacion Ama Amoedo Grants provide resources and opportunities to strengthen the visual arts ecosystem and its institutions. Through an open call, ten grants of USD 10,000 are awarded annually across four categories: Artists, Art and Social Engagement, Organizations, and Publications. The program supports the realization of projects by artists, organizations, and institutions with a meaningful connection to Latin America. This is the 4th edition of the grants; the Foundation receives applications via its online platform from 7 July to 9 September 2026. ELIGIBILITY: artists, collectives, art professionals, non-profit organizations, institutions, and foundations with a significant connection to Latin America, whether through nationality, cultural heritage, and/or the location where the project will take place. Refer to the Terms and Conditions document on the website for full details. Inquiries: info@fundacionamaamoedo.org. Application deadline: Wednesday 9 September 2026, 11:59 pm.

  4. FILE 2027: Electronic Language International Festival (watch, call expected Dec 2026 - Feb 2027)

    FILE Festival · Sao Paulo, Brazil. Open internationally. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Exhibition at Latin America's leading electronic/media art festival; the 2026 call was free to enter with an explicit AI-art axis.

    FILE (Electronic Language International Festival) in Sao Paulo is Latin America's leading electronic and media art festival, with recent editions explicitly foregrounding AI art. Entry is free and international. CALL NOT YET OPEN: the FILE 2026 call closed 19 February 2026; the 2027 call is expected around December 2026 to February 2027.

  5. CIFO x Ars Electronica Awards 2027 (watch; Latin American artists only)

    Cisneros Fontanals Art Foundation (CIFO) with Ars Electronica · For Latin American media artists; exhibition at Ars Electronica, Linz, Austria. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: USD 45,000 total across awardees, plus exhibition at the Ars Electronica Festival in Linz. No application fee known.

    The CIFO x Ars Electronica Awards support Latin American artists working in media art, with USD 45,000 distributed across awardees and an exhibition at the Ars Electronica Festival in Linz. ELIGIBILITY: Latin American media artists only. CALL NOT YET OPEN: 2026 winners were announced December 2025; the next call is expected mid to late 2026.

  6. Ambulante Gira de Documentales 2027 (watch, call expected ~August 2026)

    Ambulante A.C. · Mexico (touring documentary festival, March-May 2027). · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Selection in Mexico's touring documentary festival. Free entry. Mexican documentaries prioritized; the Pulsos section accepts non-Mexican productions shot in or about Mexico.

    Ambulante's Gira de Documentales tours documentary cinema across Mexico each spring. Entry is free; Mexican documentaries are prioritized, and the Pulsos section accepts non-Mexican productions shot in or about Mexico. CALL NOT YET OPEN: the 2026 call closed 22 September 2025, so the 2027 call should open around August 2026 with a September 2026 deadline.

  7. FICG 42: Guadalajara International Film Festival (watch, submissions expected Sept-Dec 2026)

    Festival Internacional de Cine en Guadalajara / Universidad de Guadalajara · Guadalajara, Mexico (festival April 2027). Ibero-American focus; check category eligibility. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Screening at one of Latin America's most important film festivals.

    The Guadalajara International Film Festival (FICG), run by the University of Guadalajara, is one of Latin America's most important festivals, with a primarily Ibero-American competition focus (some sections accept wider entries; check category eligibility). CALL NOT YET OPEN: FICG 42 submissions are expected September to December 2026.

  8. FICCI 66: Cartagena International Film Festival (watch, call expected H2 2026)

    Festival Internacional de Cine de Cartagena de Indias (FICCI) · Cartagena, Colombia (festival 6-11 April 2027). · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Screening at the oldest film festival in Latin America; the previous cycle had a USD 100,000 prize pool.

    The Cartagena International Film Festival (FICCI) is the oldest film festival in Latin America. CALL NOT YET OPEN: the FICCI 65 call closed 8 December 2025; the FICCI 66 call should open in the second half of 2026 with a deadline around December 2026. The previous cycle offered a USD 100,000 prize pool.

  9. Tsonami Sound Art Festival / Radio Tsonami (watch, broadcast call expected ~October 2026)

    Tsonami Arte Sonoro · Valparaiso, Chile. Open internationally. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Broadcast and festival presentation of sound works; residency calls for the XX-anniversary festival (November 2027) ran mid-2026.

    Tsonami is Latin America's leading sound art festival, based in Valparaiso, Chile. Its XX-anniversary residency calls closed in June 2026, but the Radio Tsonami broadcast open call (international, for radio/sound works) ran October to November 2025 for the 2026 festival and should recur around October 2026. CALL NOT YET OPEN: watch the convocatorias page.

  10. Pivo Research Residency 2027 (watch, call expected ~March 2027)

    Pivo · Sao Paulo, Brazil (Copan building); Pivo Salvador call recurs around October-November (Brazil-prioritized). · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Free, internationally open annual research residency with curatorial and institutional support. No fee.

    Pivo, the independent art space in Sao Paulo's Copan building, runs a free research residency open to international artists, offering studio access and curatorial exchange. CALL NOT YET OPEN: the 2026 call ran March to April 2026; the next is expected around March 2027. Pivo Salvador's separate call recurs around October-November and prioritizes Brazil-based artists.
