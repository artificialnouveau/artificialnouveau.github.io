Grants, Fellowships and Residencies in Latin America

Currently 5 active paid grants, fellowships and residencies open to applicants in Latin America, across AI, arts, film, research, tech and cross-disciplinary practice. Hand-curated and updated weekly. Almost every entry is funded; a few notable unpaid open calls and festival submissions are included as clearly flagged exceptions. Open calls

  1. Red Quincho: Beca Constelaciones 2026 (4th Edition)

    Red Quincho · Rotating urban and rural Red Quincho spaces across Argentina; residency runs September to December 2026. · Deadline: 09 Aug 2026 · Award: ARS 1,000,000 (around USD 690) per grant toward mobility, living costs and production, plus one month of lodging. 15 grants available. No application fee.

    Beca Constelaciones is Red Quincho's mobility and production grant for artists, curators and researchers doing territory-based work, now in its fourth edition. Each recipient spends one month in residence, split into two 15-day placements at different member spaces across Argentina, pairing an urban and a rural context. The grant covers mobility, living costs and production, with lodging provided. Backed by Fundacion Williams, CCEBA and Fundacion Santander through the CABA Mecenazgo scheme. ELIGIBILITY IS RESTRICTED: of the 15 grants, 13 are for people resident in Argentina and 2 are for people currently living in Spain. Artists, curators and researchers apply through separate forms; Spanish-based candidates face an additional jury evaluation. Deadline 9 August 2026 at 23:59 Argentina time.

  2. Beca Plataforma Bogota: Arte, Ciencia y Tecnologia 2026 (Colombia residents only)

    Idartes / Plataforma Bogota · Bogota, Colombia. Colombian citizens or foreigners legally resident in Bogota only. · Deadline: 24 Aug 2026 · Award: 4 grants of COP 12,000,000 each (approx. EUR 2,700) to run interactive art + science + technology labs at Plataforma Bogota. No application fee.

    Plataforma Bogota, Idartes' art, science and technology media lab, awards four grants of COP 12,000,000 each to run interactive labs combining art, science, and technology. ELIGIBILITY: Colombian citizens or foreigners legally resident in Bogota only. Deadline 24 August 2026.

  3. Fundacion Klemm: 29th Premio Klemm a las Artes Visuales 2026

    Fundacion Federico Jorge Klemm · Buenos Aires, Argentina. Open to Argentine nationals and to foreigners with at least two years of legal residence in Argentina. · Deadline: 04 Sep 2026 · Award: First acquisition prize ARS 7,000,000 (around USD 4,800), second prize ARS 4,000,000 (around USD 2,750). No entry fee; the foundation pays transport of selected works.

    The Premio Klemm is one of Argentina's longest-running visual arts prizes, now in its 29th edition, awarded by the Fundacion Federico Jorge Klemm in Buenos Aires. It is notable for a genuinely open definition of medium: the entry categories explicitly include arte digital, robotica, video, videoinstalacion, audio and fotografia alongside painting, drawing and sculpture, so technology-based practice is squarely in scope. Entrants submit one work dated 2021 or later, which must be durable rather than ephemeral. The top prizes are acquisition prizes, so winning work enters the foundation's collection. ELIGIBILITY IS RESTRICTED: Argentine nationals (native or naturalised) or foreigners holding at least two years of legal residence in Argentina, aged 18 or over. Submissions close 4 September 2026 at 23:59. Prize amounts and the closing date are stated in the bases y condiciones PDF linked from the entry site.

  4. Fundacion Ama Amoedo Grants 2026 (4th edition)

    Fundacion Ama Amoedo · Open to applicants with a significant connection to Latin America, through nationality, cultural heritage, and/or the location where the project takes place. · Deadline: 09 Sep 2026 · Award: Ten grants of USD 10,000 each, awarded annually across four categories: Artists, Art and Social Engagement, Organizations, and Publications. No application fee mentioned.

    The Fundacion Ama Amoedo Grants provide resources and opportunities to strengthen the visual arts ecosystem and its institutions. Through an open call, ten grants of USD 10,000 are awarded annually across four categories: Artists, Art and Social Engagement, Organizations, and Publications. The program supports the realization of projects by artists, organizations, and institutions with a meaningful connection to Latin America. This is the 4th edition of the grants; the Foundation receives applications via its online platform from 7 July to 9 September 2026. ELIGIBILITY: artists, collectives, art professionals, non-profit organizations, institutions, and foundations with a significant connection to Latin America, whether through nationality, cultural heritage, and/or the location where the project will take place. Refer to the Terms and Conditions document on the website for full details. Inquiries: info@fundacionamaamoedo.org. Application deadline: Wednesday 9 September 2026, 11:59 pm.

  5. Proimagenes Colombia: FDC 2026 Estimulos Automaticos

    Proimagenes Colombia (Fondo para el Desarrollo Cinematografico) · Colombia. Open to Colombian natural persons and Colombian legal entities only. · Deadline: 11 Dec 2026 · Award: Six automatic-stimulus lines drawn from the FDC's 2026 pool of roughly COP 21,900 million (around USD 5.5 million). The feature film promotion line alone carries COP 3,620 million. Amounts per award are set per line. No application fee.

    The Fondo para el Desarrollo Cinematografico, created by Colombia's Law 814 of 2003 and administered by Proimagenes Colombia, channels at least 70 percent of its resources into film production. The automatic stimuli are the non-competitive strand: rather than pitching against other projects, applicants qualify by meeting defined criteria. Six lines are open for 2026, all closing 11 December 2026: film distribution in theatres, Colombian shorts and features selected for approved festivals, films nominated for approved awards, projects selected for approved industry encounters, individuals selected for approved training workshops and advisories, and promotion of national features or coproductions of at least 70 minutes premiering in 2026. A separate competitive strand (estimulos por concurso) runs 17 modalities on its own calendar. ELIGIBILITY IS RESTRICTED: Colombian natural persons and Colombian legal entities, though a Colombian company may have a foreign legal representative. Contact convocatoriafdc@proimagenescolombia.com.
