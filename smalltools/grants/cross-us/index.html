Cross-Disciplinary and Social Impact Grants in the US

Currently 18 active cross-disciplinary and social impact grants, fellowships and residencies open to applicants in the US. Hand-curated and updated weekly. Almost every entry is funded; a few notable unpaid open calls and festival submissions are included as clearly flagged exceptions. Open calls

  1. PLAYA Art/Sci Awarded Residency 2027

    PLAYA · Summer Lake, Lake County, Oregon, USA · Deadline: 30 Jun 2026 · Award: Awarded residency providing free lodging in a private cabin with kitchen and bathroom plus one weekly group meal. Residents cover their own travel, groceries, and materials. No cash stipend. USD 40 application fee (waivers granted on request by June 28, 2026)

    PLAYA's Art/Sci Awarded Residency brings together artists and scientists for immersive, interdisciplinary inquiry at a rural retreat near Summer Lake in Lake County, Oregon. The 2027 cycle offers four session options ranging from 11 to 25 nights between January and November 2027. Awarded residents receive free lodging in a private cabin with kitchen and bathroom plus one weekly group meal, while covering their own travel, food, and materials. Both established and emerging practitioners in any media may apply, including naturalists, biologists, sound artists, new media artists, writers, performers, and interdisciplinary creators, and all applicants must be at least 18 years old. Applications are submitted through SlideRoom and require work samples and two references. There is a USD 40 application fee, though PLAYA states all fee waiver requests will be granted if submitted by June 28, 2026.

  3. United We Om: Karma Project Grants (Fall 2026)

    United We Om · United States. Applicants and their service work must be based in the USA · Deadline: 30 Jun 2026 · Award: Micro-grants of USD 500 to 2,500 (line-item budget required). Service compensation capped at USD 50 per hour.

    United We Om's Karma Projects micro-grants support and amplify the good works of people dedicated to karma yoga, acts of selfless service without attachment to outcomes, that create lasting change for individuals, communities, and humanity. The Fund seeks to support individuals and small nonprofits creating change in their communities, using their own life experiences to serve others facing similar struggles, where USD 2,500 would significantly benefit the work and represents 1% or more of an annual budget (nonprofits with annual budgets of USD 250,000 or less). Preference is given to those performing service work without financial compensation. The Fund supports fully formed ideas and projects with specific goals and outcomes: expansions of existing programs, innovative pilots, and projects providing necessary resources to people without access, that can be sustained beyond the grant period without further United We Om funding. NOT FUNDED: projects that are not karma yoga; wellness services to communities with traditional access; inflated or above-market budgets; art, film, music, or theatre performances that do not directly serve an underserved community; organizations with annual budgets over USD 250,000; programs requiring membership or ongoing participant fees; one-day events; partially funded projects (additional funds must already be in place); applicants or service work outside the USA; for-profit businesses; projects led by people not from the community they serve; projects with any participation fee including by-donation; religious activities (mantra and prayer); therapy or medical/herbal advice without an active license; political or societal activism; and projects still in development. Grant funds may not be used for staff salaries, travel, administrative or grant-prep work, legal or accounting services, volunteer stipends, tuition or fee subsidies, software/website/tech, or attendance incentives. Grantees may pay themselves up to USD 50 per hour for active service time. APPLICATIONS: two windows annually. The Fall 2026 window is open and closes 30 June 2026 at midnight; grants awarded September 2026. Separate applications exist for individuals and for nonprofits. Do not use AI to write the application. Questions: Executive Director Matt Jared at matt@unitedweom.org.

  4. Centrum: 2027 Residency Application (Self-Directed, Emerging Artist, In the Making)

    Centrum · Fort Worden, Port Townsend, Washington, United States · Deadline: 30 Jun 2026 · Award: Residency offering dedicated time, workspace and accommodation at Fort Worden. Financial terms (any stipend or cost to the artist) are not specified at this application stage. No application fee.

    Centrum's single application covers three 2027 residency programs at Fort Worden in Port Townsend, Washington: Self-Directed Residencies (approx. 55-60 spots), In the Making Residencies (3-5 spots), and the Emerging Artist Residency (6 spots). The program gives artists, writers and creatives across all disciplines time and space to relax, focus and reinvent their practice, alongside a community of other residents. Open to practices including but not limited to visual arts, writing, curatorial, performance, dance, music and social practice. Applicants submit work samples/portfolio, the workspace/accommodation they need, and a short paragraph on what a residency would mean to them, and indicate which program(s) they want to be considered for. Selection is by a panel of alumni jurors; no feedback is offered. ELIGIBILITY: Self-Directed and In the Making are open broadly; the Emerging Artist Residency is restricted to Pacific Northwest-based artists residing in Washington, Oregon, Idaho, Montana or British Columbia. Application window 15 April - 30 June 2026; applications absolutely cannot be accepted after 30 June 2026, so apply early.

  5. Art Omi: Artists Residencies

    Art Omi · Ghent, New York, USA (Hudson Valley) · Deadline: 01 Jul 2026 · Award: No cost to attend (lodging, dedicated workspace, and prepared meals provided); residents cover their own travel. No stipend.

    Art Omi runs five residency programs on its 120-acre campus in Ghent, New York, in the Hudson Valley, about two and a half hours north of New York City. The programs cover visual art (Artists), architecture, dance, music, and writers and translation, selecting participants through a competitive jury process. Selected residents attend at no cost except travel, receiving comfortable lodgings, dedicated workspaces, and fresh prepared meals. Programs are open to international applicants and there is no fee to apply. The visual art, architecture, music, and dance cycles share one annual deadline (most recently October 15), while the Writers and Translation Lab has its own deadline; the next open cycle is the Writers and Translation Lab 2027, due July 1, 2026.

  6. Yaddo Residency

    Yaddo · Saratoga Springs, New York, USA · Deadline: 01 Jul 2026 · Award: Room, board, and studio space provided at no cost; no stipend USD 35 application fee

    Yaddo is a retreat for artists on a 400-acre estate in Saratoga Springs, New York, where residents can work without interruption in a supportive environment. Residencies last from two weeks to two months and include a private bedroom, board with communal dinners and packed lunches, and an assigned studio or workspace. Applications are accepted from individual artists working in choreography, film, literature, musical composition, painting, performance, photography, printmaking, sculpture, and video. Applicants must be working at a professional level or be emerging artists whose work shows great professional promise, and they may not be enrolled in degree programs. International artists are welcome. There are two cycles each year, with the summer deadline on July 1 for residencies running November 2026 through June 2027.

  7. Grand Canyon Conservancy Residency 2027 (Artist / Astronomer / Environmental Educator)

    Grand Canyon Conservancy (Grand Canyon National Park) · Grand Canyon National Park, Arizona, United States · Deadline: 15 Jul 2026 · Award: Free on-site accommodation in the park, financial support (honorarium/stipend; amount varies by track and session - confirm on the track-specific page), plus marketing/exposure through the Conservancy and the National Park. Alumni network access. No application fee. Three tracks (Artist / Astronomer / Environmental Educator) - apply to one track per cycle.

    Grand Canyon Conservancy's award-winning residency program invites artists, scientists, historians and educators to live on site at Grand Canyon National Park, pursue place-based research, and engage the public through meaningful programs that deepen understanding of the park's environmental, spiritual and cultural impact. Founded in 2020; competitive application + peer-panel review. THREE TRACKS: (1) Artist in Residence - for contemporary artists making interactive (immersive and/or participatory) work that shapes how people experience place; (2) Astronomer in Residence - for astronomers from any discipline who wish to share their knowledge and enthusiasm for dark skies; (3) Environmental Educator in Residence - for educators who use unique and inspiring methods to pique curiosity, prompt exploration, and build knowledge of the world. Residents receive free accommodation, financial support, and marketing/exposure; alumni return as part of a nationwide network. Read the individual track pages (linked from the main residency page) for track-specific eligibility, session lengths, stipend amounts, public-engagement expectations and any region/citizenship requirements before applying. APPLICATIONS OPEN: 15 May - 15 July 2026 for the 2027 cycle.

  8. Harvard Radcliffe Institute: Fellowship Program 2027-2028

    Harvard Radcliffe Institute · Cambridge, Massachusetts, USA; fellows must relocate to and reside in the Cambridge/Boston area for the full fellowship (September through May). · Deadline: 10 Sep 2026 · Award: USD 78,000 stipend plus an additional USD 5,000 for project expenses. Relocation, housing, and childcare funds may also be provided. No application fee.

    The Harvard Radcliffe Institute Fellowship offers scientists, writers, scholars, public intellectuals, and artists a year to pursue ambitious projects in a vibrant interdisciplinary setting amid the resources of Harvard University. The Institute welcomes innovative work that confronts pressing social, scientific, and policy issues and seeks to engage audiences beyond academia. Reflecting Radcliffe's history, it welcomes (but does not limit eligibility to) proposals focused on women, gender, and society, or that draw on the Schlesinger Library's collections. It also invites proposals relevant to the Institute's 2024-2029 focus area, academic freedom and connecting across difference (intellectual virtues, free and open inquiry, diversity of thought, political polarization, peace and conflict, inequality, religious pluralism, and related higher-education policy issues), including work that constructively challenges disciplinary orthodoxies or advances transformative perspectives. SUPPORT: USD 78,000 stipend plus USD 5,000 for project expenses, with relocation, housing, and childcare funds available; fellows must reside in the Cambridge/Boston area from September through May. ELIGIBILITY: open to individuals across career stages; tenure is not required and applicants need not be academics. This is NOT a postdoctoral fellowship; those currently enrolled in a degree program are ineligible, as are former Harvard Radcliffe fellows (1999-present). Applicants must meet discipline-specific criteria. APPLY: register on the online portal and select an area (Humanities and Social Sciences; Creative Arts; Nonfiction and Journalism; or Science, Engineering, and Mathematics). Materials: application form, CV, 1,400-word project proposal (with bibliography when appropriate), a writing or work sample, and three references. DEADLINES: humanities, social sciences, creative arts, and nonfiction and journalism by 10 September 2026, 5pm ET; science, engineering, and mathematics by 1 October 2026, 5pm ET.

  9. Jentel Artist Residency

    Jentel Foundation · Banner, Wyoming, USA · Deadline: 15 Sep 2026 · Award: USD 100 weekly stipend (about USD 300 total over three weeks), plus private bedroom, separate studio space, and shared living and kitchen facilities provided at no cost USD 30 application fee

    The Jentel Artist Residency offers approximately three-week residencies in the foothills of the Bighorn Mountains near Banner, Wyoming, giving artists, writers, and composers dedicated time for creative work. Sessions run from mid-January through early December, hosting six residents at a time. It is open to visual artists in any medium, writers of fiction, creative nonfiction, or poetry, and composers across musical genres. Residents receive a private bedroom, a separate studio, shared living and kitchen space, and a USD 100 weekly stipend to help cover food and personal expenses. Applicants must be at least 25 years old and be U.S. citizens or foreign nationals whose primary residence is in the U.S. during the application year, and may not be enrolled full-time in a degree program. There is a USD 30 non-refundable application fee, with deadlines of September 15 for Winter and Spring sessions and January 15 for Summer and Fall sessions.

    Iméra – Aix Marseille Université, in partnership with the Fulbright Commission · Aix-Marseille, France (campus residency) · Deadline: 15 Sep 2026 · Award: €2,400 (~USD 2,600) monthly stipend; fully covered accommodation (valued USD 788–1,966/month); €1,200 (~USD 1,300) travel allowance.   11. Open Interval: An Artist-Scientist Exploration of Turbulence

    Simons Foundation · United States (trio must be regionally co-located) · Deadline: 21 Oct 2026 · Award: USD 30,000 per awarded trio

    Open Interval is a Simons Foundation program that supports trios consisting of an artist, a scientist, and a representative from a 501(c)(3) arts organization to collaborate around a shared scientific theme. The current cycle centers on the theme of turbulence, a phenomenon at the edge of chaos and beauty. Awarded trios receive USD 30,000 to support a sustained period of thought partnership and exploration rather than the production of finished works or exhibitions. The program runs from April to December 2027 with a touchstone meeting in May 2027 and possible cohort convenings. Trio members must be regionally co-located anywhere in the United States so they can collaborate in person. The scientist should hold a Ph.D. and be conducting basic research, with priority given to fields such as astrophysics, neuroscience, quantum physics, biology, or mathematics. The application portal opens September 9, 2026, and all three trio members must submit for the application to be complete.

  12. Helene Wurlitzer Foundation Residency

    The Helene Wurlitzer Foundation of New Mexico · Taos, New Mexico, USA · Deadline: 18 Jan 2027 · Award: Rent-free, utility-paid furnished housing (casita); no stipend (rare stipend support via the Rhonda Artist Stipend Fund); artists cover food and travel USD 30 application fee

    The Helene Wurlitzer Foundation of New Mexico runs one of the oldest artist residency programs in the United States, established in 1954, on a 15-acre campus in Taos, New Mexico. The residency is open to visual artists, literary artists, and music composers, including painters, sculptors, photographers, filmmakers, writers, poets, playwrights, screenwriters, and songwriters. Selected grantees receive rent-free and utility-paid housing in a furnished casita for a session of 10 to 12 weeks, with access to a Commons House offering WiFi, a library, and bicycles. No stipend is provided in most cases, and residents cover their own food and transportation. Applications are open to creative artists worldwide regardless of age, experience, or nationality, and the annual deadline is January 18 for residencies the following calendar year. There is a non-waivable USD 30 application fee.

  13. ACA Artist Response Fund (ARF) Emergency Relief Grant

    Artist Communities Alliance · United States (ACA member residencies) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Up to USD 1,500

    The Artist Response Fund (ARF) is an emergency relief program from the Artist Communities Alliance (ACA) for artists who experience threats to their safety or mental health during a residency and must leave before completing their term. Qualifying situations include assault, harassment, or systemic harm where the artist has attempted to address the issue without success. Grants of up to USD 1,500 help cover relocation costs such as transportation home, temporary lodging, supplies or basic needs, and rent upon returning. Eligible applicants are individual artists currently at, or having attended within the past 12 months, an ACA Organizational Member residency. There is no application fee, and the fund operates on a rolling basis with applications typically processed within one week. Artists apply directly through an online form and can also contact ACA staff with questions.

  14. Musicians Foundation Grants

    Musicians Foundation · United States (remote application) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: One-time emergency financial assistance; grant amount varies by need. Funds are issued as vendorized checks paid directly to billers (medical providers, landlords, utilities).

    The Musicians Foundation provides one-time financial assistance to professional musicians facing a specific time of need caused by an unexpected hardship such as a medical or dental situation, a natural disaster, or a housing crisis. Eligible applicants must be professional performers, composers/arrangers, or educators who have worked as professional musicians in the United States for five or more years, currently reside in the U.S., and have filed taxes as a musician for at least five years. The application has a rolling, always-open structure with a Pre-Application followed by a more detailed second part for those who qualify. Grants are issued as vendorized checks made payable directly to the biller and mailed to the applicant's home address. Processing typically takes between one and twelve weeks depending on volume.

  15. Kaatsbaan Subsidized Residencies

    Kaatsbaan Cultural Park · Tivoli, New York, USA · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Generously subsidized residency; includes hotel-style on-campus rooms, locally sourced meals, and access to studios, a 160-seat black box theater, and a 153-acre Hudson Valley campus. No artist stipend.

    Kaatsbaan Cultural Park offers subsidized residencies for artists of all genres to create, live, and collaborate on its 153-acre campus in the Hudson Valley near Tivoli, New York. Residencies are individually designed between the artist, Kaatsbaan, and partners to fit the needs of the artist or company, welcoming disciplines from dance, theater, music, and visual arts to writing, composition, design, and film. The program is generously subsidized and includes individual hotel-style rooms, healthy locally sourced meals, and access to professional studios with sprung floors and pianos, a 160-seat black box theater, and exhibition space. Applications are accepted on a rolling basis through an inquiry form, with early inquiries encouraged to secure preferred dates. Artists at any career stage worldwide may apply, individually or as collaborative teams. There is no application fee and no artist stipend.

  16. Guggenheim Fellowship

    John Simon Guggenheim Memorial Foundation · United States and Canada · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Variable cash grant; awards are allocated based on the applicant's other resources and the scope of the proposed project, typically tens of thousands of US dollars per fellow.

    The Guggenheim Fellowship is an annual award from the John Simon Guggenheim Memorial Foundation that supports exceptional individuals pursuing scholarship in any field of knowledge and creation in any art form. Fellowships are awarded on the basis of prior achievement and exceptional promise to mid-career professionals across more than 50 fields spanning the arts, humanities, natural sciences, and social sciences. Applicants must be citizens or permanent residents of the United States or Canada, and may apply individually or as a pair working jointly. Grant amounts vary and are adjusted to the fellow's other resources and the scope of their proposed work, with around 175 to 200 fellows selected each year. There is no application fee. The competition runs annually, with applications typically opening in late summer and closing in mid September for fellowships announced the following spring.

  17. Indigo Arts Alliance Mentorship Residency Program 2026

    Indigo Arts Alliance · Portland, Maine, USA · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: USD 2,000 stipend, plus furnished private apartment, round-trip travel to Portland, and 24-hour access to a 3,000 square foot community studio

    The Indigo Arts Alliance Mentorship Residency Program in Portland, Maine pairs artists of the African Diaspora and other Black and Brown communities for co-mentorship, collaboration, and creative exchange. Each pairing brings a National or International visiting artist together with a Local or Regional artist for a residency rooted in mentorship. The program welcomes all disciplines including painting, sculpture, writing, dance, music, theater, photography, and textiles. Indigo Arts Alliance provides a USD 2,000 stipend, a furnished private apartment, round-trip travel to Portland, and 24-hour access to a 3,000 square foot community studio. Applications are currently open on a rolling, year-round basis for artists residing in the North Eastern region (Maine, Vermont, New Hampshire, Massachusetts, Connecticut, and Rhode Island). Applicants must be 21 or older, have at least two years of studio or exhibition experience, and not be enrolled full-time in an academic program.

  18. HHMI Presidential Fellows Program (Chevy Chase, MD)

    Howard Hughes Medical Institute (HHMI) · HHMI Headquarters, Chevy Chase, MD, USA (primarily on-site) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: $150,000–$200,000/year salary, plus exceptional health benefits, retirement plans, time off, and recognition/wellness programs. This is a paid employment position, not a grant.

    HHMI places 2–3 Presidential Fellows per year directly in the Office of the President, working on the institute's highest-priority strategic initiatives. Fellows report to the President and the VP/COO/Senior Advisor. This is a high-trust, high-access, high-output role — not a rotation or observation post. WHAT FELLOWS DO: lead cross-functional strategic projects spanning science programs, AI, finance, communications, HR, technology, science education, and film/television; take initiatives from concept through implementation; manage AI agents and LLM-based workflows; handle sensitive and confidential matters; dedicate 10% of time to self-directed projects (subject to President/COO approval). CONTEXT: HHMI is one of the largest private funders of biomedical research in the world; it also runs national programs in science education, film and television, and AI-enabled research. This fellowship offers rare access to the intersection of science, philanthropy, education, media, and technology. ELIGIBILITY: bachelor's degree required; 0–5 years of professional experience in consulting, policy, research, strategy, law, operations, or comparable fields; welcomes recent graduates and those with advanced degrees (MBA, MPP, PhD, JD); must be fluent with AI tools and LLMs including experience orchestrating AI agents; exceptional written/verbal communication skills; comfort with ambiguity and executive pace. US-based (Chevy Chase, MD, primarily on-site). COMPENSATION: $150,000–$200,000/year. No application fee. APPLY via the ScienceCareers job posting.
