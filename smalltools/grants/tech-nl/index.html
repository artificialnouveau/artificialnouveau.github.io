Open calls

  1. MIT R&D Collaboration Projects 2026 (MKB Innovatiestimulering Topsectoren)

    Rijksdienst voor Ondernemend Nederland (RVO) · Netherlands. SME consortia (minimum two collaborating SMEs). · Deadline: 15 Sep 2026 · Award: Grant covering 35 percent of eligible costs. Small projects EUR 50,000-200,000; large projects EUR 200,000-350,000 per project (EUR 25,000-100,000 or EUR 25,000-175,000 per participant). No application fee.

    The MIT R&D Collaboration Projects scheme (MIT R&D-samenwerkingsprojecten, part of MKB Innovatiestimulering Topsectoren) funds joint R&D projects by Dutch SMEs within the national Top Sectors and Mission-driven Innovation policy. The grant covers 35 percent of eligible costs: small projects of EUR 50,000-200,000 and large projects of EUR 200,000-350,000 (with per-participant ranges of EUR 25,000-100,000 for small and EUR 25,000-175,000 for large). Eligible applicants are consortia of at least two collaborating SMEs, with no single participant carrying more than 70 percent of costs. The national RVO round runs 9 June to 15 September 2026 (closes 17:00) and is a ranked tender, not first-come-first-served; some provinces run separate regional MIT windows. No application fee. Apply via RVO.

  2. UNIIQ: Proof-of-Concept Fund (rolling, Zuid-Holland)

    UNIIQ · Zuid-Holland (South Holland), Netherlands only. Early-stage research-driven startups. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Around EUR 300,000 per company as a convertible loan (investment, not a grant). Total fund EUR 50 million. No application fee.

    UNIIQ is a proof-of-concept investment fund for the earliest, riskiest phase of company building in Zuid-Holland (South Holland). It invests around EUR 300,000 per company as a convertible loan (for example redeemed over about three years or converted at a follow-on valuation), drawn from a EUR 50 million fund. It is an investment, not a grant. Eligible companies are early-stage, research-driven startups based in Zuid-Holland, typically in life sciences, deep tech and high tech. Intake is continuous via an orientation meeting followed by a funding application. No application fee.

  3. ROM InWest: Startup and Scale-up Financing (rolling, Noord-Holland)

    ROM InWest (Regional Development Company for Noord-Holland) · Noord-Holland (North Holland), Netherlands. Startups, scale-ups and innovative SMEs. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Financing of EUR 300,000 to EUR 5,000,000 as loans, equity participations or a combination (not a gift); cannot exceed 49.9 percent of a financing round. This is investment, not a grant. No application fee.

    ROM InWest is the regional development company for Noord-Holland (North Holland). It finances startups, scale-ups and innovative SMEs working on food, energy, healthcare and circular-economy transitions, providing EUR 300,000 to EUR 5,000,000 as loans, equity participations or a combination (explicitly not a gift), and not exceeding 49.9 percent of a financing round. It is an investment, not a grant. Companies must be based in Noord-Holland. Intake is continuous, starting with a quick-scan tool. No application fee.

  4. WBSO: R&D Tax Credit (rolling, Netherlands)

    Rijksdienst voor Ondernemend Nederland (RVO) · Netherlands. Dutch companies and self-employed paying payroll or income tax that conduct qualifying R&D. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Payroll/income tax reduction for R&D, not a cash grant. 2026: 36 percent of the first EUR 380,000 of R&D wage costs (50 percent for startups), 16 percent above; fixed deduction for self-employed. National budget EUR 1.817 billion for 2026. No application fee.

    The WBSO (Wet Bevordering Speur- en Ontwikkelingswerk) is the Dutch R&D tax credit administered by RVO. It reduces payroll/wage tax for companies with R&D staff and provides an income-tax deduction for self-employed people doing R&D; it is a tax credit, not a cash grant. For 2026 the benefit is 36 percent of the first EUR 380,000 of R&D wage and other costs (50 percent for qualifying startups) and 16 percent above that; self-employed receive a fixed deduction (and need at least 500 R&D hours per year). Eligible applicants are Dutch companies and self-employed people paying payroll or income tax who carry out qualifying R&D in the EU. Applications are made in advance via Mijn RVO and cannot be retroactive; for coverage of the final three months of a year the deadline is 30 September. Time-keeping and project administration are required. No application fee.
