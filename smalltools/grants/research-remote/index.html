  1. RYSE Innovation Hub: Youth Innovation Advisor

    RYSE Innovation Hub · 100% remote. · Deadline: 20 Jul 2026 · Award: USD 480 monthly compensation. No application fee.

    RYSE Innovation Hub seeks Youth Innovation Advisors: young people ages 18 to 24 who are passionate about adolescent health and wellness. Youth Innovation Advisors support projects that strengthen youth engagement practices and help shape health solutions created with young people in mind. The role is 100% remote, running from September 2026 to July 2027, with a time commitment of up to 4 hours per week. WHO IT'S FOR: young people ages 18 to 24 passionate about adolescent health and wellness. COMPENSATION: USD 480 monthly. DEADLINE: 20 July 2026, 11:59 PM ET. TO APPLY: submit the application form.

  2. Paid Research Assistant: AI and the War in Ukraine

    Bogna Konior (NYU Shanghai) · Remote-friendly. · Deadline: 23 Jul 2026 · Award: USD 18 per hour; 10-15 hours per week, flexible schedule.

    Bogna Konior, Assistant Professor of Media Theory at NYU Shanghai, is hiring a paid research assistant for a project on Ukraine and artificial intelligence. The work supports a forthcoming book with MIT Press, plus an independent scenario she is writing on the future of AI with the war as a lens. WHAT THE WORK LOOKS LIKE: going month-by-month through news archives (New York Times, Reuters, Financial Times, and specialist outlets), annotating and cataloguing coverage of AI, drones, and autonomous systems in the war, plus other developments she will direct you to; building and maintaining a structured Notion database covering chronology, companies, industries, and key actors; and organizing and editing her existing notes. WHO SHE IS LOOKING FOR: interest in and general knowledge of AI; prior research and annotation experience, careful and detail-oriented, no sloppy citations; comfortable with Notion and with AI tools; Ukrainian language skill a strong plus (not required); familiarity with AI, war, tech, and geopolitics news sources is great. Remote-friendly, start ASAP. She will also consider people with no prior experience, as well as current and previous students. TERMS: USD 18 per hour, 10-15 hours per week, flexible schedule. TO APPLY: email bogna@nyu.edu with a short introduction and background (link to website, socials, CV, LinkedIn, or portfolio if you have one); where you are based and your weekly availability; and one recent AI-related story, company, or development you find interesting and why (a vibe check). Bonus: a sample of past research or writing if you have it.

  3. Digital Impact Alliance (DIAL) Research Fellowship

    Digital Impact Alliance (DIAL) · Remote; international organisation. Experience working in or with global majority countries encouraged. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: USD 5,000 stipend for a 6-8 month fellowship. No application fee.

    The Digital Impact Alliance (DIAL) Research Fellowship supports curious, passionate mid-career researchers and practitioners to explore frontier questions on digital public infrastructure (DPI) and data governance. Fellows bring their own knowledge and research agenda while contributing to DIAL's core thematic priorities: creating more value for more people from data; understanding how digital systems and services impact people, including and especially the most marginalised; and accelerating the adoption of data-sharing and governance practices that fuel innovation and empower people. SUPPORT: a USD 5,000 stipend for research completed over 6-8 months, plus mentorship from DIAL staff and industry experts, access to DIAL's network and resources, collaboration with partner organisations, and support amplifying the work. Each fellow produces two preliminary pieces and one final, more extensive output (research papers or reports, policy briefs, expert-comment series, etc.). ELIGIBILITY: professionals from academia, the private sector, and civil society, typically with 8-15 years of experience in digital development, policy, or research; demonstrated work at the intersection of technology and society (including DPI and data governance); a track record of high-quality writing, research, or policy briefs; experience working in or with global majority countries; and strong analytical and communication skills. TO APPLY (rolling basis): submit a CV or resume, a writing sample or portfolio, and a research proposal. Questions: fellows@dial.global. Apply via https://dial.global/apply-to-our-fellows-program/.
