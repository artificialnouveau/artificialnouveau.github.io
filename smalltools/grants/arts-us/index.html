  1. MTA Arts & Design Digital Art Program 2026 Open Call

    MTA Arts & Design · New York City, USA (Fulton Transit Center, Grand Central Madison, Moynihan Train Hall) · Deadline: 28 May 2026 · Award: $1,000 honorarium for finalist proposals + project budget for selected artist(s) (TBD by project nature)

    Open call for digital and new-media artists to submit existing works for consideration on transit-center screens (video, film, animation, augmented reality, net art, game engines, generative art). Multi-channel installation experience encouraged. Finalists develop site-specific proposals with a $1,000 honorarium; selected artists oversee production and installation with project management from MTA Arts & Design. Sites have no audio. Valid U.S. Taxpayer ID required. Submit up to 5 works (.mov/.mp4) and 10 stills (.jpg/.png) plus bio, CV and artist statement. Deadline 23:59 EST Thursday 28 May 2026.

  2. Eliza Moore Fellowship for Artistic Excellence 2026 (Oak Spring)

    Oak Spring Garden Foundation · Upperville, Virginia, USA (2-5 week on-site residency) · Deadline: 31 May 2026 · Award: $10,000 grant plus a two- to five-week residency with accommodation provided. No application fee.

    Eliza Moore Fellowship for Artistic Excellence from the Oak Spring Garden Foundation, supporting early-career artists whose work engages plants, gardens and landscapes.   3. The Lab: Living Wage Residencies 2026-27 (San Francisco)

    The Lab · San Francisco Bay Area, California, USA (eligibility restricted to artists based in the Bay Area; significant in-person access to The Lab's space in the Mission District is required) · Deadline: 31 May 2026 · Award: USD 17,530 artist fee per resident (commensurate with three months of work at a San Francisco Bay Area living wage per MIT's Living Wage Calculator) plus USD 5,000 for collaborators and materials. Three months of rehearsal/workshop access to The Lab's space, plus two weeks of continuous use of the exhibition and performance spaces for the residency outcome. Curatorial and marketing support, outside studio visits, documentation and publicity. Stage-2 finalists receive an additional USD 250 stipend to develop their full proposal. No application fee.

    The Lab's flagship Living Wage Residency, a substantial in-person residency in San Francisco's Mission District for artists working in any media and discipline. Since 2014 the programme has directed over USD 1 million to artists, with artist fees representing nearly 30% of The Lab's average operating budget. The Lab's history is as a site for experimental and interdisciplinary work spanning performance, exhibition, installation, sound and experimental music, writing, institutional critique, social practice, parades, feasts and further disciplines yet to be defined. The Lab's mission centres traditionally underrepresented artists, particularly those identifying as BIPOC, LGBTQ+, women, disabled and/or from economically marginalised backgrounds. ELIGIBILITY: artists based in the San Francisco Bay Area (tight geographic restriction; in-person access to The Lab's space is central to the residency). SUPPORT per residency: USD 17,530 artist fee (3 months at a Bay Area living wage per MIT's Living Wage Calculator); USD 5,000 for collaborators and materials; three months of rehearsal/workshop access to The Lab's space (as available around other programming); two weeks of continuous use of the exhibition and performance spaces; curatorial and marketing support, outside studio visits, documentation and publicity. PROCESS: Stage 1 application (CV, 3 work links, brief project description and resource plan) due 31 May 2026; Stage 2 invites up to 12 selected artists to develop a full proposal with a USD 250 stipend (Stage 2 deadline TBD). Reviewed by a diverse panel of professional artists and curators plus The Lab's Board. Outside funding additionally secured for a proposal is not factored into the decision, but The Lab may help identify further sources. Supported by the San Francisco Arts Commission.

  4. Headlands Center for the Arts: Artist in Residence 2027 (AIR)

    Headlands Center for the Arts · Sausalito, California, United States (Marin Headlands, Golden Gate National Recreation Area) · Deadline: 01 Jun 2026 · Award: Fully sponsored residency: studio space, chef-prepared meals, on-site housing, travel costs, and living-expense stipend. No application fee.

    Open call for the 2027 cohort of the Headlands Artist in Residence (AIR) program, one of the longer-running fully-sponsored residencies on the US West Coast. Approximately 50 local, national and international artists are selected each year for a residency of 4 to 10 weeks at the Headlands' Marin Headlands campus (former military buildings inside the Golden Gate National Recreation Area, just north of San Francisco). Residents receive a private studio, chef-prepared meals, on-site housing, travel costs, and a living-expense stipend, fully sponsored by the program. Open to artists at all career stages working in any medium: drawing, painting, sculpture, photography, film, video, new media, installation, fiction and nonfiction writing, poetry, dance, music, interdisciplinary practice, social practice, and architecture. Strong fit for new-media / interdisciplinary / research-driven practices. Application opens 1 April 2026 and closes 1 June 2026 via SlideRoom; applicants are notified of decisions by 11 December 2026. Free to apply.

  6. Light Work Artist-in-Residence 2027 (Syracuse, NY)

    Light Work · Syracuse, New York, USA (in-person, one-month residency on site) · Deadline: 01 Jul 2026 · Award: $7,500 stipend plus a furnished apartment and access to state-of-the-art photography facilities. The residency concludes with a feature in a special edition of 'Contact Sheet: The Light Work Annual' alongside a commissioned essay. No application fee.

    Light Work, an independent non-profit founded in 1973, offers a long-running artist-in-residence programme dedicated to the development of experimental and contemporary photographic practices. Over 400 artists have participated in the residency, with many going on to gain international recognition. ELIGIBILITY: artists working in photography or image-based media, from any country and at any career stage. Apply via the Light Work SlideRoom portal.

  8. NEA: Grants for Arts Projects (July 2026 cycle)

    National Endowment for the Arts (NEA) · United States (eligibility for US non-profits with 501(c)(3) status, units of state or local government, or federally-recognised tribal communities) · Deadline: 09 Jul 2026 · Award: Project grants typically USD 10,000 to USD 100,000+, with most awards in the USD 10,000-50,000 range; minimum 1:1 cost-share required.

    NEA's flagship project grant for arts organisations across all artistic disciplines. The Grants for Arts Projects (GAP) programme supports projects in dance, design, folk and traditional arts, literary arts, local arts agencies, media arts, museums, music, musical theater, opera, presenting and multidisciplinary works, theater, and visual arts. July 2026 cycle: 'Submit to Grants.gov' deadline 9 July 2026; portal application window 14-21 July 2026. ELIGIBILITY: US non-profits with 501(c)(3) status, units of state or local government, or federally-recognised tribal communities; organisations must have a three-year history of programming and meet a minimum operating budget. Project grants typically USD 10,000 to USD 100,000+, with most awards in the USD 10,000-50,000 range; a 1:1 cost-share is required. Natural sibling to the NEH Public Humanities Projects programme on the desk.

  9. The Andy Warhol Foundation for the Visual Arts: General Program Grant (September 2026 round)

    The Andy Warhol Foundation for the Visual Arts · United States (applicants must be US-incorporated 501(c)(3) non-profits curating, exhibiting or presenting contemporary visual art) · Deadline: 01 Sep 2026 · Award: Multi-year programme / exhibition support, typical grants USD 50,000 to USD 300,000+

    Twice-yearly grants from The Andy Warhol Foundation for the Visual Arts to US non-profit organisations supporting innovative contemporary-visual-arts programming. Awards announced 1 July and 1 January following the March and September deadlines respectively. Funding supports exhibitions, residencies, public programming, publications and other programme activities that present challenging contemporary visual art to a wider public. ELIGIBILITY: US-incorporated 501(c)(3) non-profits engaged in curating, exhibiting or presenting contemporary visual art; programmes must serve a wider public, not solely the organisation's members. Typical grants USD 50,000 to USD 300,000+ for multi-year programming or exhibition support. Not open to individual artists or to programmes that primarily serve commercial or fundraising purposes.

  11. The Bennett Prize 2026

    The Bennett Prize / Muskegon Museum of Art · Muskegon, Michigan, US (national reach) · Deadline: 19 Sep 2026 · Award: $75,000 ($37,500/year over 2 years) + traveling solo exhibition; additional $10,000 for one finalist

    $75,000 prize for women figurative realist painters, awarded by a five-member jury.
