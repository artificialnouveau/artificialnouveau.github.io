Open calls

  1. RYSE Innovation Hub: Youth Innovation Advisor

    RYSE Innovation Hub · 100% remote. · Deadline: 20 Jul 2026 · Award: USD 480 monthly compensation. No application fee.

    RYSE Innovation Hub seeks Youth Innovation Advisors: young people ages 18 to 24 who are passionate about adolescent health and wellness. Youth Innovation Advisors support projects that strengthen youth engagement practices and help shape health solutions created with young people in mind. The role is 100% remote, running from September 2026 to July 2027, with a time commitment of up to 4 hours per week. WHO IT'S FOR: young people ages 18 to 24 passionate about adolescent health and wellness. COMPENSATION: USD 480 monthly. DEADLINE: 20 July 2026, 11:59 PM ET. TO APPLY: submit the application form.

  2. Paid Research Assistant: AI and the War in Ukraine

    Bogna Konior (NYU Shanghai) · Remote-friendly. · Deadline: 23 Jul 2026 · Award: USD 18 per hour; 10-15 hours per week, flexible schedule.

    Bogna Konior, Assistant Professor of Media Theory at NYU Shanghai, is hiring a paid research assistant for a project on Ukraine and artificial intelligence. The work supports a forthcoming book with MIT Press, plus an independent scenario she is writing on the future of AI with the war as a lens. WHAT THE WORK LOOKS LIKE: going month-by-month through news archives (New York Times, Reuters, Financial Times, and specialist outlets), annotating and cataloguing coverage of AI, drones, and autonomous systems in the war, plus other developments she will direct you to; building and maintaining a structured Notion database covering chronology, companies, industries, and key actors; and organizing and editing her existing notes. WHO SHE IS LOOKING FOR: interest in and general knowledge of AI; prior research and annotation experience, careful and detail-oriented, no sloppy citations; comfortable with Notion and with AI tools; Ukrainian language skill a strong plus (not required); familiarity with AI, war, tech, and geopolitics news sources is great. Remote-friendly, start ASAP. She will also consider people with no prior experience, as well as current and previous students. TERMS: USD 18 per hour, 10-15 hours per week, flexible schedule. TO APPLY: email bogna@nyu.edu with a short introduction and background (link to website, socials, CV, LinkedIn, or portfolio if you have one); where you are based and your weekly availability; and one recent AI-related story, company, or development you find interesting and why (a vibe check). Bonus: a sample of past research or writing if you have it.

  3. GIFCT: Internship, Memberships & Programs

    Global Internet Forum to Counter Terrorism (GIFCT) · Remote (preference for US Eastern timezone) · Deadline: 01 Aug 2026 · Award: Paid role: $25/hour, part-time at a maximum of 24 hours per week (3 days a week). No application fee.

    GIFCT is hiring a part-time intern to support its Memberships and Programs team. Work spans three areas: open-source research and early human rights assessments on companies applying to the Membership Advisory Program, reviewing them against GIFCT's Membership Criteria; assisting the annual member review process and mapping companies that could benefit from GIFCT tools; and helping update the Definitions and Legislation Project with new case studies and resources. Generalist exposure to an organisation working at the intersection of technology, trust and safety, and counterterrorism policy, including drafting communications, records, event logistics and reporting. Strong written and organisational skills required; interest in tech policy, trust and safety, human rights or international security is a plus. Reports to Dr. Nagham El Karhili. Apply by emailing a resume plus answers to three short questions (in lieu of a cover letter) to jobs@gifct.org with 'M&P Internship' in the subject line. NOTE: this is a paid internship, not a grant or fellowship. Deadline 1 August 2026.

  4. Padimai: OOPS Onchain Artist Residency 2026

    Padimai Art and Tech Studio · Remote residency (Padimai is based in Singapore); open internationally. · Deadline: 15 Aug 2026 · Award: SGD 15,000 honorarium per artist plus an additional SGD 5,000 per artist for documentation (audio, video, photography). Five practitioners selected. No application fee.

    OOPS (Origin Of Primary Source) is the first edition of Padimai Art and Tech Studio's Onchain Artist Residency, a three-month remote residency inviting art practitioners to critically and creatively engage with NFTs as artistic registers and as systems of knowledge, documentation, and access. Five practitioners are selected. SUPPORT: SGD 15,000 honorarium per artist, plus SGD 5,000 per artist for documentation. The residency is remote, with community engagement designed through online meetings; at the end, participants deliver three NFTs developed during the residency to Padimai. ELIGIBILITY: individual creative practitioners working at the intersection of art, technology, and blockchain; open internationally. RESIDENCY: 20 September to 20 December 2026. DEADLINE: 15 August 2026. Apply via the residency page.

  5. Digital Impact Alliance (DIAL) Research Fellowship

    Digital Impact Alliance (DIAL) · Remote; international organisation. Experience working in or with global majority countries encouraged. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: USD 5,000 stipend for a 6-8 month fellowship. No application fee.

    The Digital Impact Alliance (DIAL) Research Fellowship supports curious, passionate mid-career researchers and practitioners to explore frontier questions on digital public infrastructure (DPI) and data governance. Fellows bring their own knowledge and research agenda while contributing to DIAL's core thematic priorities: creating more value for more people from data; understanding how digital systems and services impact people, including and especially the most marginalised; and accelerating the adoption of data-sharing and governance practices that fuel innovation and empower people. SUPPORT: a USD 5,000 stipend for research completed over 6-8 months, plus mentorship from DIAL staff and industry experts, access to DIAL's network and resources, collaboration with partner organisations, and support amplifying the work. Each fellow produces two preliminary pieces and one final, more extensive output (research papers or reports, policy briefs, expert-comment series, etc.). ELIGIBILITY: professionals from academia, the private sector, and civil society, typically with 8-15 years of experience in digital development, policy, or research; demonstrated work at the intersection of technology and society (including DPI and data governance); a track record of high-quality writing, research, or policy briefs; experience working in or with global majority countries; and strong analytical and communication skills. TO APPLY (rolling basis): submit a CV or resume, a writing sample or portfolio, and a research proposal. Questions: fellows@dial.global. Apply via https://dial.global/apply-to-our-fellows-program/.

  6. Manifund: AI Safety Regranting (Rolling)

    Manifund · Remote; US-based platform, open worldwide. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Grants typically USD 5,000 to 50,000, recommended by independent regrantors from their own budgets. No application fee. No legal entity required.

    Manifund's regranting program funds AI safety and global-catastrophic-risk work through a network of expert regrantors, each given an independent budget to recommend grants to people and projects they find promising. Applicants submit a project proposal on the Manifund platform; regrantors can then offer funding. GRANTS: typically USD 5,000 to 50,000, though amounts vary. ELIGIBILITY: individuals (no legal entity or institutional affiliation required) and organizations working on AI safety or reducing catastrophic risk from AI. Applications are accepted on a rolling basis; per-regrantor budget availability varies over time. Learn more at the regranting page and apply on the Manifund platform (manifund.org).

  7. Humans in Control: Distributed Organizing Manager (Remote)

    Humans in Control · Remote (United States; US work authorization required) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: USD 65,000 to 80,000 per year, plus benefits (medical, dental, vision, PTO, 401(k)). No application fee.

    Humans in Control empowers the public to shape AI policy through grassroots organizing and bipartisan advocacy for commonsense safeguards that protect children and families. The Distributed Organizing Manager builds and supports distributed/volunteer organizing to advance the organization's advocacy goals. This is a full-time, fully remote role open to candidates anywhere in the United States (US work authorization required). Compensation is USD 65,000-80,000 plus benefits. Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis (no fixed deadline). See the listing for full responsibilities and to apply.

  8. Kapor Foundation Research Fellowship

    Kapor Foundation · Remote (US-focused tech policy/research) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Cash award (amount per project; first awards announced by 30 June 2026)

    Supports journalists producing long-form investigative reports and tech-policy researchers conducting research, analysis or evaluation that informs policy related to the Kapor Foundation's three priority areas, with an emphasis on responsible AI and tech ethics. Priority areas: CS/AI Education, Innovation, Governance. Aims to dismantle systemic inequities in the tech sector by funding researchers and investigative journalists exploring barriers and driving actionable solutions. Applications accepted on a rolling basis; first awards to be announced by 30 June 2026.

  9. Critical Playground: Freelance Writer Commissions

    Critical Playground · Remote · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Paid commissions

    Commissioned long-form writing at the intersection of design, technology, art and culture. Editorial themes: designing with AI as cultural and infrastructural system; responsive and adaptive materials; politics of platforms and creative-infrastructure governance; post-digital hybrid making; designing for collapse and continuity; creative research as practice. In-depth pieces only, no press releases or promotional copy.

  10. Working-Class Creative Grant (Karim Boumjimar)

    Karim Boumjimar (independent artist initiative) · Online / Remote · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: €500 (no strings attached)

    Independent artist-run grant redistributing a portion of Karim Boumjimar's artwork sales as a no-strings-attached 500 EUR award for working-class creatives anywhere in the world. Funds may be used for artistic production, research, materials, travel or basic living needs. Simple application: short intro and a sample of work or interests. No fee, no reporting, no obligation to produce. Rolling review based on available funds; non-selected applicants stay in the pool for future rounds.
