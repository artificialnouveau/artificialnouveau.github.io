Visual and Media Arts Grants (Worldwide)

Currently 15 active visual and media arts grants, fellowships and residencies open to applicants in Worldwide. Hand-curated and updated weekly. Almost every entry is funded; a few notable unpaid open calls and festival submissions are included as clearly flagged exceptions.   1. Future Generation Art Prize 2027 (PinchukArtCentre)

    Victor Pinchuk Foundation (PinchukArtCentre) · Kyiv, Ukraine (exhibition of shortlisted artists at PinchukArtCentre in Spring 2027) · Deadline: 28 Jun 2026 · Award: Main prize US$100,000 ($60,000 cash + $40,000 investment in the winner's practice). Up to 5 Special Prizes share a total amount of US$20,000 for supporting projects that develop their artistic practice. No application fee.

    Biannual global contemporary art prize from the Victor Pinchuk Foundation (PinchukArtCentre, Kyiv), recognising and supporting a future generation of artists. The main winner receives US$100,000 ($60,000 cash plus $40,000 invested in their practice); up to five special prizes share a total of US$20,000 for projects developing the artist's practice. Shortlisted artists exhibit at PinchukArtCentre in Spring 2027, with the award ceremony following. ELIGIBILITY: open to all artists aged 35 or younger worldwide, with no restrictions on gender, nationality, race or artistic medium; former Prize winners are not eligible to enter again but other previous applicants may re-apply. APPLY through the open call online. A selection committee reviews applications and selects up to twenty artists for the exhibition; in addition, 300 correspondent art experts worldwide nominate two to five candidates each. Applications accepted 11 May - 28 June 2026; shortlist announced 14 September 2026.

    Zigutamve – Association for Contemporary Photography · Academy of Fine Arts, Vienna, Austria · Deadline: 30 Jun 2026 · Award: EUR 250 cash award per selected winner + EUR 150 travel allowance. Grand Prix winner receives an additional EUR 250, a solo exhibition, and a free hotel stay. Submission fee: EUR 20 per project.

    ROTLICHT Festival for Analog Photography 2026, organised by Zigutamve – Association for Contemporary Photography, selects 20 projects for exhibition at the Academy of Fine Arts Vienna. Theme: 'Anthropocene: Hybrid Realities' – exploring the tension between human ecological dominance and environmental fragility. Each selected winner receives: exhibition in a 1,300+ m² venue, publication in the official festival catalogue (2 copies), EUR 250 cash award, EUR 150 travel allowance, festival pass, and free registration for Photodays Rovinj 2027. Grand Prix winner additionally receives a solo exhibition, an extra EUR 250, a free hotel stay, and a catalogue cover feature. ELIGIBILITY: open to any individual aged 16 or older, at any career stage, internationally; submissions must include at least one analog photographic process; works must have been taken within the last 5 years (2021-2026); no AI-generated or AI-enhanced work (unless deliberately thematic). Files must be JPEG, max 5 MB, minimum 2000px on longest side, 300 dpi. Jury selects winners by late July 2026. Deadline: 30 June 2026. NOTE: EUR 20 submission fee per project applies. Apply at https://www.rotlicht-festival.at/open-call-submission/.

  4. Prix Ibn Khaldoun – Léopold Sédar Senghor Translation Prize 2026 (OIF / ALECSO)

    Organisation internationale de la Francophonie (OIF) and Arab League Educational, Cultural and Scientific Organization (ALECSO) · International (Francophone zone and Arab world) · Deadline: 30 Jun 2026 · Award: EUR 10,000 (funded equally by OIF and ALECSO). No application fee.

    The Ibn Khaldoun – Léopold Sédar Senghor Translation Prize (19th edition, 2026) is awarded jointly by the Organisation internationale de la Francophonie (OIF) and ALECSO to recognise excellence in literary and humanities/social sciences translation between French and Arabic in either direction. Cash award: EUR 10,000. ELIGIBILITY: open to individual translators, universities and higher education institutions, research centres, national associations and unions, and publishing houses from the Arab world and Francophone space. Works must be literary or humanities/social sciences texts translated between French and Arabic (either direction), published within 3 years prior to the application year, and must be a first translation of the original. Self-published or photocopied works excluded. One work maximum per candidate. Applications submitted exclusively electronically; required materials: CV, presentation of translation work, copies of both translated and original texts. Deadline: 30 June 2026. Apply at https://appelsaprojets.francophonie.org/fr/applications/soutien-a-la-circulation-des-biens-et-mobilite-des-artistes.

  6. Artivism & Amplifiers Fellowship 2026

    Human Impacts Institute · Global; pairs young climate leaders from the Global North and Global South. Open to speakers of English, Spanish, French, and Portuguese. · Deadline: 01 Jul 2026 · Award: USD 1,000 stipend upon completion. Supported by expert workshops and creative mentors.

    The Artivism & Amplifiers (A&A) Fellowship pairs young climate leaders from the Global North and Global South to co-create multimedia campaigns, exhibits, and installations for the Creative Climate Awards 2027. Over six months, fellows build skills in intercultural collaboration, climate storytelling, and community engagement, supported by expert workshops and creative mentors. The program is open to speakers of English, Spanish, French, and Portuguese. Fellows receive a USD 1,000 stipend upon completion. The organizers strongly encourage applications from people most impacted by the climate crisis, including those from the global majority, Indigenous, LGBTQIA+, immigrant, and diaspora communities, and people with disabilities.   9. OIF: Support for Artist Mobility and Circulation of Cultural Goods 2026 (Francophonie)

    Organisation internationale de la Francophonie (OIF) · Francophone zone (OIF member states globally) · Deadline: 31 Aug 2026 · Award: Up to EUR 5,000 for artist mobility (travel, accommodation, visas, logistics).     The OIF Support for Artist Mobility and Circulation of Cultural Goods programme provides grants of up to EUR 5,000 for mobility (travel, accommodation, visa fees, logistics) and up to EUR 2,500 for circulation of cultural goods (excess baggage, freight, customs) within the Francophone zone; both can be combined. ELIGIBILITY: professional artists and established artistic collectives with a demonstrated connection to the Francophone space (by nationality, working language, or artistic collaborations), residing in an OIF member state, with minimum 2 years of experience in the cultural or artistic sector; must directly manage the project (not act as intermediaries); one application per individual per call. ELIGIBLE DISCIPLINES: live performance (music, theatre, dance, comedy, storytelling), cinema/audiovisual, literature/publishing, and visual arts. PRIORITY given to projects promoting gender equality, women artists, creators under 35, and South-South mobility. Projects must take place between 25 March and 30 November 2026. Multiple selection sessions throughout the year; call open until 31 August 2026. Information webinars: 11 March, 13 May, and 8 July 2026 at 3 PM Paris time. Contact: culture.dlc@francophonie.org. Apply at https://appelsaprojets.francophonie.org/fr/applications/soutien-a-la-circulation-des-biens-et-mobilite-des-artistes.

  12. eidolon Grant 2026 (Vernacular Photography)

    eidolon · International; open worldwide. · Deadline: 30 Sep 2026 · Award: A total of EUR 25,000 to be distributed across two application categories. No application fee.

    The eidolon Grant is an international programme, presented annually, for projects that explore, promote and conserve vernacular photography. It is open to artists, academics, professionals, researchers, collectors and vernacular photography enthusiasts whose past work and proposed project is centred around the image heritage of everyday photography. The grant aims to identify phenomena, collections, histories, practices and trends within vernacular photography, offering new interpretations and analyses, and thematising both photographic heritages and contemporary photographic practices. Applications can be made under two categories, with a total of EUR 25,000 to be distributed. Each chosen project will contribute to the enrichment of eidolon's program in the coming year. The deadline for submission is 30 September 2026 at 11:59 p.m. Apply via https://everydayphotography.org/centre/grant-2026.
