Currently 17 active visual and media arts grants, fellowships and residencies open to applicants in Worldwide. Hand-curated and updated weekly.   2. Textile Exchange x Atmos: Fashion Regenerated 2026

Check the call site for full eligibility and submission details. Deadline: 20 July 2026.

  3. TED & POSCA Global Artist Residency 2026: Visible Mending

    TED & POSCA (curated by Fine Acts) · Globally distributed; no single location or central studio. Selected artists work within their own communities, with projects unfolding in public space. · Deadline: 31 Jul 2026 · Award: Each selected artist receives a total of USD 15,000 (covering artist fees, production costs/community activation, and documentation), plus a production budget and tools from POSCA, creative direction and storytelling support, and visibility through TED and Fine Acts' global platforms.

    TED (the global platform for ideas and storytelling) and POSCA (a creative brand by uni Brands Corporation) are launching a new kind of artist residency: a globally distributed cohort of artists working across different geographies, cultures, and communities, united by a shared drive to shape public life through art. Fine Acts, the external curatorial partner selected by TED & POSCA, leads the curation, creative direction, and coordination. There is no single location and no central studio: selected artists work within their own communities, following a shared creative constraint (a unifying conceptual framework around repair, hope, and beauty), with projects unfolding in public space, community participation as a key part of the work, and process mattering as much as outcome. 2026 THEME, VISIBLE MENDING: inspired in part by Kintsugi (the Japanese practice of repairing broken ceramics with gold), the theme explores repair as a creative and civic act, inviting artists to consider how wounds, ruptures, and imperfections can become sites of transformation, and to make processes of repair visible through public interventions that spark reflection, dialogue, and collective imagination. In 2026, four artists will be selected to develop community-rooted public interventions to be implemented in 2027. AWARD: each selected artist receives a total of USD 15,000 to cover artist fees, production costs/community activation, and documentation, plus a production budget and tools from POSCA, creative direction and storytelling support, and visibility through TED and Fine Acts' global platforms. While the residency term is one year, the initiative will run for multiple years (2027, 2028, 2029), expanding the cohort year by year and building an evolving global archive of civic artistic interventions. Apply by 31 July 2026.

  5. Eamonn McCabe Bursary 2026 (Royal Photographic Society)

    The Royal Photographic Society (RPS), in partnership with Eamonn McCabe's family and The Guardian · International bursary. Applicants must be aged 30 years or under on the closing date. Interviews held via Zoom. · Deadline: 03 Aug 2026 · Award: GBP 3,000 to deliver a photography project on the theme of 'sporting endeavour'. The completed project will be published by The Guardian and the RPS, who will work with the recipient to bring the project to a wider audience.

    The Eamonn McCabe Bursary, launched in 2024, commemorates the life of photojournalist Eamonn McCabe (1948-2022), who made his name as a sports photographer before moving to wider editorial work, mainly for The Observer and The Guardian, and who supported a generation of photographers from the 1980s to the 2020s. Run by the RPS in partnership with McCabe's family and The Guardian, this international bursary continues that legacy. AWARD: GBP 3,000 to a photographer aged 30 years or under to deliver a photography project responding to the theme of 'sporting endeavour', which can be interpreted as widely as the applicant wishes, though the general approach should be suitable for publication. The project should normally be new, not previously exhibited or published. As well as professional sports, the call particularly encourages projects covering grassroots and community activities (for example, addressing issues such as mental health or supporting displaced communities), and welcomes creative interpretations of the theme. The completed project will be published by The Guardian and the RPS. ELIGIBILITY: applicants must be aged 30 years or under on the closing date. PROCESS: interviews via Zoom in September; recipient announced by the end of September; project completed within one year of award. Apply via the RPS application portal using RPS Account credentials. Closing date 3 August 2026.

    Organisation internationale de la Francophonie (OIF) · Francophone zone (OIF member states globally) · Deadline: 31 Aug 2026 · Award: Up to EUR 5,000 for artist mobility (travel, accommodation, visas, logistics). Up to EUR 2,500 for circulation of cultural goods (excess baggage, freight, customs). Both can be combined. No application fee.

    The OIF Support for Artist Mobility and Circulation of Cultural Goods programme provides grants of up to EUR 5,000 for mobility (travel, accommodation, visa fees, logistics) and up to EUR 2,500 for circulation of cultural goods (excess baggage, freight, customs) within the Francophone zone; both can be combined. ELIGIBLE DISCIPLINES: live performance (music, theatre, dance, comedy, storytelling), cinema/audiovisual, literature/publishing, and visual arts. PRIORITY given to projects promoting gender equality, women artists, creators under 35, and South-South mobility. Projects must take place between 25 March and 30 November 2026. Multiple selection sessions throughout the year; call open until 31 August 2026. Information webinars: 11 March, 13 May, and 8 July 2026 at 3 PM Paris time. Contact: culture.dlc@francophonie.org. Apply at https://appelsaprojets.francophonie.org/fr/applications/soutien-a-la-circulation-des-biens-et-mobilite-des-artistes.

  8. Game Design Emerging Talents: She Got Game

    Pro Helvetia (Swiss Arts Council) · Remote/international mentoring programme; applicants must be based in one of Pro Helvetia's liaison-office regions (Arab region, East Asia, South America, South Asia and Vietnam, and West, Central, East and Southern Africa). · Deadline: 01 Sep 2026 · Award: CHF 3,000 toward the production of a prototype or demo version, plus individual coaching and community access; potential access to international trade fairs. When attending trade fairs, applicants with childcare responsibilities can request up to CHF 250 per week for childcare or children's travel expenses. No application fee.

    She Got Game is a Pro Helvetia mentoring programme promoting equal opportunities in the Swiss and international game industry, supporting the representation of women in management positions through dialogue with experienced international experts from the game industry. The call is aimed at professional game designers who identify as women from the regions of Pro Helvetia's liaison offices: the Arab region, East Asia, South America, South Asia and Vietnam, and West, Central, East and Southern Africa. ELIGIBILITY: Emerging Talents are game developers or game studios whose relevant training or registration (self-employed, sole proprietorship, or company) dates back less than seven years. Selection criteria include digital content for devices such as computers, consoles, mobile devices, or mixed-reality devices requiring a high level of interaction; clearly defined objectives in terms of participation and career; originality, feasibility, degree of innovation, and marketability of the project; and project leaders holding the copyright and rights of use. Excluded: commissioned work and projects that are part of an advertising or promotional campaign, and purely technological projects without obvious narrative and/or aesthetic content. WHAT IS OFFERED: individual coaching and input talks on project development, business strategy and finances, marketing, PR, and pitch training; involvement in a growing community of female game developers; potential access to international trade fairs; CHF 3,000 toward the production of a prototype or demo version; and up to CHF 250 per week toward childcare or children's travel expenses when attending international trade fairs (a budget must be presented in advance). All modules are conducted in English; the programme is a time-consuming commitment of roughly 30 hours over six months, plus potential additional time attending events or trade fairs. APPLICATION: submit a short CV (including education/training, nationality, and residency status in Switzerland), a short description of the current project (type and genre, gameplay, platform, development status, target audience, with relevant visual material, max. 10 A4 pages), a project portfolio, and expectations regarding the programme's objectives. All documents should be submitted in English; if not possible, applications may be submitted in German, French, or Italian. KEY DATES: applications open 1 July 2026; submission deadline 1 September 2026, 23:59 CEST; notification of decision in November 2026; earliest project start 1 January 2027. Apply via the Pro Helvetia application portal.

  11. eidolon Grant 2026 (Vernacular Photography)

    eidolon · International; open worldwide. · Deadline: 30 Sep 2026 · Award: A total of EUR 25,000 to be distributed across two application categories. No application fee.

    The eidolon Grant is an international programme, presented annually, for projects that explore, promote and conserve vernacular photography. It is open to artists, academics, professionals, researchers, collectors and vernacular photography enthusiasts whose past work and proposed project is centred around the image heritage of everyday photography. The grant aims to identify phenomena, collections, histories, practices and trends within vernacular photography, offering new interpretations and analyses, and thematising both photographic heritages and contemporary photographic practices. Applications can be made under two categories, with a total of EUR 25,000 to be distributed. Each chosen project will contribute to the enrichment of eidolon's program in the coming year. The deadline for submission is 30 September 2026 at 11:59 p.m. Apply via https://everydayphotography.org/centre/grant-2026.

  14. Adolph and Esther Gottlieb Foundation Emergency Grant

    Adolph and Esther Gottlieb Foundation · New York, USA (applicants worldwide) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Up to USD 15,000 (USD 5,000 typical), one-time grant

    The Adolph and Esther Gottlieb Emergency Grant provides interim financial assistance to painters, sculptors, and printmakers facing an unforeseen, catastrophic event such as fire, flood, or a medical emergency. Applicants must have been engaged in a mature phase of their art for at least ten years and must have a current, urgent need that is beyond their means. The maximum award is USD 15,000, with USD 5,000 being typical, given as one-time assistance for a specific emergency. The program does not cover dental work, chronic situations, capital improvements, projects, general indebtedness, or lack of employment. There is no deadline; applications are reviewed on a rolling basis, usually within about four weeks, and within 24 to 48 hours in the most urgent cases.

  15. Pollock-Krasner Foundation Grant for Artists

    Pollock-Krasner Foundation · New York, USA (grants awarded internationally) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Up to USD 50,000 (unrestricted)

    The Pollock-Krasner Foundation awards unrestricted grants to professional visual artists who are painters, sculptors, and artists working on paper, including printmakers. Grants range up to USD 50,000 over a one-year period and can be used for both professional and personal expenses such as art supplies, studio rent, and living costs. Applicants must demonstrate recognizable artistic merit and financial need, and must be actively exhibiting their current work in professional venues such as galleries and museums. The Foundation does not fund students, commercial or graphic art, video, performance, film, or generative and AI-generated work. Applications are accepted on a rolling, year-round basis with no deadline, and the review process typically takes nine months to a year. There is no application fee, and all applications must be submitted online.

  16. Innovate Grant

    Innovate Artist Grants · Online (applications accepted worldwide) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: USD 1,800 unrestricted grant (two awarded per cycle: one to a visual artist, one to a photographer) USD 35 application fee

    Innovate Grant awards two unrestricted USD 1,800 grants every quarter, one to a visual artist and one to a photographer, along with up to 18 honorable mentions who are featured and promoted on the organization's website. The award is unrestricted, so recipients can apply the funds toward new projects, materials, studio rent, travel, or other needs. Grant cycles run four times a year (Winter, Spring, Summer, and Fall). Visual artists and photographers aged 18 and older from anywhere in the world, both US and international, are eligible to apply. Applications require contact details, a grant category, and 3 to 10 images of work, plus a USD 35 application fee. The Spring 2026 cycle closed on June 18, 2026, and the Summer 2026 cycle follows on the regular quarterly schedule.
