Open calls

  1. GIFCT: Internship, Memberships & Programs

    Global Internet Forum to Counter Terrorism (GIFCT) · Remote (preference for US Eastern timezone) · Deadline: 01 Aug 2026 · Award: Paid role: $25/hour, part-time at a maximum of 24 hours per week (3 days a week). No application fee.

    GIFCT is hiring a part-time intern to support its Memberships and Programs team. Work spans three areas: open-source research and early human rights assessments on companies applying to the Membership Advisory Program, reviewing them against GIFCT's Membership Criteria; assisting the annual member review process and mapping companies that could benefit from GIFCT tools; and helping update the Definitions and Legislation Project with new case studies and resources. Generalist exposure to an organisation working at the intersection of technology, trust and safety, and counterterrorism policy, including drafting communications, records, event logistics and reporting. Strong written and organisational skills required; interest in tech policy, trust and safety, human rights or international security is a plus. Reports to Dr. Nagham El Karhili. Apply by emailing a resume plus answers to three short questions (in lieu of a cover letter) to jobs@gifct.org with 'M&P Internship' in the subject line. NOTE: this is a paid internship, not a grant or fellowship. Deadline 1 August 2026.

  2. Center on AI, Security, and Technology (CAST) Fellowship

    RAND Corporation, Center on AI, Security, and Technology (CAST) · Washington DC, San Francisco or Pittsburgh, USA, or remote within the United States; fellows may also be based in the United Kingdom · Deadline: 01 Aug 2026 · Award: US fellows: annual stipend from USD 40,000 to 200,000 for full-time fellows depending on experience, plus a monthly payment usable for health and dental insurance. UK fellows: compensation based on experience. No application fee.

    RAND's Center on AI, Security, and Technology (CAST) runs a rolling fellowship for researchers working on AI security and technology policy, with mentorship from RAND policy experts. Fellows can be based in Washington DC, San Francisco or Pittsburgh, work remotely within the United States, or be based in the United Kingdom. Terms are flexible: engagements typically start at six months and can be extended up to three years, full-time or part-time. US fellows receive an annual stipend ranging from USD 40,000 to 200,000 for full-time work depending on experience, plus a monthly payment that can be put toward health and dental insurance; UK fellows are compensated based on experience. The programme spans career stages from undergraduate through mid-career. ELIGIBILITY: applicants must be based in the US or UK; US applicants need to be eligible for a government security clearance, and UK applicants must have a current and ongoing right to work in the UK. Current RAND staff are not eligible. ROLLING DEADLINE: applications are submitted through an online form and reviewed quarterly, with published cutoffs of 1 February, 1 May, 1 August and 1 November, each answered by the first of the following month. The date recorded here is the next cutoff (1 August 2026); the call does not close after it. RAND notes that if one of its listed staff openings matches your interest you should apply to that instead, and suggests not applying to the fellowship if you have submitted another CAST application within the last six months.

  3. Kennan Institute Digital Fellowship 2026-2027

    Kennan Institute, Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars (supported by the Lounsbery Foundation and the U.S. Russia Foundation) · Fully virtual. No travel is funded and no travel is required. · Deadline: 14 Aug 2026 · Award: USD 2,000 monthly stipend for the six-month programme, plus mentorship from the Kennan community of experts, six virtual webinars tailored to the cohort, and inclusion in the Kennan alumni network of over 4,500 people. Travel and visa costs are explicitly not covered. No application fee.

    The inaugural Kennan Institute Digital Fellowship promotes original scholarship and quality non-fiction writing about Russia and the former Soviet Union, with an emphasis on work that has policy relevance. Ten fellowships are awarded per year, and AT LEAST FOUR POSITIONS ARE RESERVED FOR RESEARCHERS PURSUING TOPICS IN THE NATURAL SCIENCES, MEDICINE, TECHNOLOGY OR RELATED FIELDS, which is the route by which a technology-focused project fits this call. The programme aims to give participants time, professional resources and support while bringing their work to a wider public and to the Washington-based policy community, and to build a cohort network that continues into the alumni body. Each fellow receives a monthly stipend of USD 2,000, mentorship and consultation from the Kennan community of experts where applicable, six virtual webinars tailored to the cohort's needs, and membership of the alumni network. ELIGIBILITY: intended for individuals with several years of experience working on, studying or otherwise examining Russia and/or broader Eurasia; applicants must have a good working knowledge of English, as the fellowship is conducted only in English and any publications produced for the Kennan Institute appear first in English with possible later translation into regional languages. The programme runs entirely virtually from 1 October 2026 to 31 March 2027; the Institute does not cover travel-related expenses including visas. Supported by the Lounsbery Foundation and the U.S. Russia Foundation. Deadline 14 August 2026, 17:00 EST. NOTE: 'digital' here describes the virtual format, not the subject matter, so this fits the desk only if your project is itself about technology, data or science.

  4. Padimai: OOPS Onchain Artist Residency 2026

    Padimai Art and Tech Studio · Remote residency (Padimai is based in Singapore); open internationally. · Deadline: 15 Aug 2026 · Award: SGD 15,000 honorarium per artist plus an additional SGD 5,000 per artist for documentation (audio, video, photography). Five practitioners selected. No application fee.

    OOPS (Origin Of Primary Source) is the first edition of Padimai Art and Tech Studio's Onchain Artist Residency, a three-month remote residency inviting art practitioners to critically and creatively engage with NFTs as artistic registers and as systems of knowledge, documentation, and access. Five practitioners are selected. SUPPORT: SGD 15,000 honorarium per artist, plus SGD 5,000 per artist for documentation. The residency is remote, with community engagement designed through online meetings; at the end, participants deliver three NFTs developed during the residency to Padimai. ELIGIBILITY: individual creative practitioners working at the intersection of art, technology, and blockchain; open internationally. RESIDENCY: 20 September to 20 December 2026. DEADLINE: 15 August 2026. Apply via the residency page.

  5. Wilson Center Research Fellowship, 2027-2028 Class

    Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars · Remote for the duration of the fellowship, with two required trips to Washington DC (at the start and the end) for which travel costs are covered. · Deadline: 30 Sep 2026 · Award: USD 10,000 per month for a twelve-month fellowship, plus covered travel for the two required trips to Washington DC. No application fee.

    The Wilson Center, the congressionally chartered nonpartisan policy research institution in Washington DC, awards 10 to 20 research fellowships for its 2027-2028 class. Fellows conduct research and write in their areas of interest while interacting with policymakers in Washington and with other fellows. The Center accepts policy-relevant, nonpartisan proposals that address key foreign policy challenges confronting the United States and the world, and projects must align with at least one of the Center's four scholarship pillars: strategic competition, economic statecraft, TECHNOLOGY AND INNOVATION, and regional scholarship. The technology and innovation pillar is the relevant route for AI governance, emerging technology and digital policy projects, which the Center also supports through its Science and Technology Innovation Program. Fellows work remotely for the duration of the fellowship; the Center covers travel costs for two required trips to Washington DC at the beginning and the conclusion of the term. The stipend is USD 10,000 per month for the twelve-month term, which runs 1 June 2027 to 30 May 2028. ELIGIBILITY: academic candidates need a PhD in hand and either a published book or monograph beyond the dissertation, or a substantial body of research articles (five or more) in reputable peer-reviewed journals. Non-academic applicants (practitioners, journalists, public intellectuals) need at least ten years of relevant experience and a record of substantial policy impact. There is no citizenship restriction, but English proficiency is required. Applications, including letters of recommendation, must be submitted online via Interfolio by 11:59pm ET on 30 September 2026. Questions: fellowships@wilsoncenter.org. NOTE: this is an open-topic foreign policy fellowship, not an AI-specific one; the technology angle depends on how you scope the proposal.
