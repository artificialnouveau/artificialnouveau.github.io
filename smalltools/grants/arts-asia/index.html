Open calls

  1. A.R.M. Holding / Art Dubai Projects: H Residence Sculpture Park Commission (Dubai)

    A.R.M. Holding / HUNA / Art Dubai Projects · H Residence Sculpture Park, Dubai, UAE. Open to UAE-based, regional and international artists. · Deadline: 25 Jul 2026 · Award: AED 1,000,000 commission envelope, inclusive of artist fee, design development, fabrication, materials, production, transport, installation, lighting and finishing. Proposal fees are available for shortlisted artists. No submission fee.

    A.R.M. Holding and HUNA, with the commission supported and managed by Art Dubai Projects, invite artists to propose a permanent public artwork for the H Residence Sculpture Park in Dubai, the anchor piece of the park's programme. Artists respond to the theme 'We Emerge Stronger than Before' through a permanent, site-specific outdoor sculpture; the strongest proposals will find what the phrase opens up (continuity, endurance, renewal, collective presence, the long view) through material, form, scale and the conditions of the site. Where language or text is incorporated, Arabic should lead. TECHNICAL: footprint minimum around 15 sqm, up to 3 m high; weight restriction 5.5 kN/m2; must be suitable for long-term outdoor display in Dubai (summer over 40 C, humidity to 90 percent, about six hours direct sun daily); presence by day and night. ELIGIBILITY: open to artists with or without gallery representation; UAE-based, regional and international artists welcome; prior experience with permanent or large-scale outdoor/public installation required. BUDGET: AED 1,000,000 all-inclusive; shortlisted artists receive full budget parameters and proposal fees at the proposal development stage. SUBMISSION: CV and portfolio (attention to outdoor, large-scale or permanent commissions), concept statement (max 300 words), a render or visualisation in context plus at least one sketch, technical specifications, and a site response. TIMELINE: submissions close 25 July 2026; shortlist notified from 10 August; commission awarded after September proposal development; work unveiled at H Residence on 2 December 2026. Local fabrication strongly preferred. Questions: Shahad@artdubai.ae.

  2. Inlaks Shivdasani Foundation: Inlaks Fine Art Award 2027

    Inlaks Shivdasani Foundation · India. Open to Indian citizens currently living in India. · Deadline: 15 Aug 2026 · Award: INR 300,000 (around USD 3,400 / EUR 2,950) for one year, plus a fully covered four-week art residency at an Indian art institution. No application fee.

    A one-year award enabling an emerging Indian visual artist to pursue independent practice, with a stipulation to attend a recognised art residency programme in India for four weeks during the year. ELIGIBILITY IS RESTRICTED: Indian citizens currently living in India, born on or after 1 January 1996, who are either recent graduates, postgraduates or final-year students of a leading Indian visual arts institute, or artists without those formal credentials but with a record of exhibition participation. First-year MA students are not eligible. Awardees submit progress reports at six months and on completion, plus documentation of the residency. Applications close 15 August 2026 at 23:59 IST via Submittable. Shortlisting is expected in late October 2026 with interviews in early November.

  3. Inlaks Shivdasani Foundation: Inlaks Theatre Art Award 2027

    Inlaks Shivdasani Foundation · India. Open to Indian citizens. · Deadline: 15 Aug 2026 · Award: INR 15,000 per month for up to 12 months, around INR 180,000 total (roughly USD 2,050 / EUR 1,780). No application fee.

    A skill-enhancement award for emerging Indian theatre practitioners, supporting participation in recognised training workshops, residencies and similar programmes rather than the production of a specific work. ELIGIBILITY IS RESTRICTED: Indian citizens born after 1 January 1991, with preference given to holders of a theatre degree from a leading Indian institution. Recipients report at the six-month mark and on completion. Applications close 15 August 2026 at 23:59 IST via Submittable. The foundation runs several other awards on adjacent deadlines, including the Inlaks-Attakkalari Dance Studentship (30 August 2026) and a two-month Inlaks Residency at the International Center of Photography in the USA for emerging Indian photographic practitioners (31 August 2026).

  4. Hayama Artist Residency

    Hayama Artist Residency · Hayama, Kanagawa Prefecture, Japan · Deadline: 30 Sep 2026 · Award: Fully funded: roundtrip airfare to Tokyo (Narita or Haneda), shared accommodation for 4 weeks, and USD 800 for meals and local transportation. Group exhibition at KOKI ARTS gallery in Tokyo. USD 95 application fee for first-time applicants

    The Hayama Artist Residency introduces visual artists from around the world to Japanese culture and offers them their first gallery exhibition in Japan. The four-week residency takes place in Hayama, a coastal town in Kanagawa Prefecture about 1.5 hours from Tokyo. Selected artists receive roundtrip airfare to Tokyo, shared accommodation with one other resident, and USD 800 for meals and local transportation. A core element is a group exhibition during the stay, hosted in partnership with KOKI ARTS, a contemporary gallery in the Bakurocho art district of Tokyo. The program is open to visual artists over 21 working in any medium, based anywhere in the world, and applications are reviewed by the director and an international selection committee. The June 2027 cycle has an application deadline of September 30, 2026, with notification by December 15, 2026.

  5. Sixi International Artist-in-Residence Project 2027 (Nanjing, China)

    Sixi Museum · Nanjing, China · Deadline: 15 Nov 2026 · Award: Financial support plus living and working space: a city-centre apartment and a loft studio in the outskirts, hardware facilities, staff assistance, and help connecting with the local art scene. No application fee.

    Launched in 2024 to support young emerging artists, the Sixi International Artist-in-Residence Project (run by Sixi Museum in Nanjing, China) facilitates exchanges between artists from different backgrounds and helps them achieve creative breakthroughs and personal growth. The project calls for artists across all disciplines and cultural backgrounds who are ready to engage in dialogue with the city. TWO THEMES FOR 2027: 'Flux and Imprints: Contemporary Echoes of Migration History' and 'Folds of Text: Cross-Media Intertextuality and Intervention'. WHAT IS PROVIDED: living and working space (a city-centre apartment plus a loft studio in the outskirts to suit the research and production demands of different disciplines), financial support, hardware facilities and staff assistance, and help establishing contacts with the local art scene; by the end of the residency artists have the opportunity to showcase their outcomes in exhibitions. Nanjing combines traditional and contemporary Chinese culture, and the project positions itself as one of the few local art residency programmes contributing to international cultural exchange in the city and to the diversified development of contemporary Chinese art. ELIGIBILITY: open to artists across all disciplines and cultural backgrounds. Deadline: 15 November 2026. Apply via siximuseum.com.
