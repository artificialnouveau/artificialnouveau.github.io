Open calls

  1. Corridor: DAISY CHAIN Artist Commission (Adur, West Sussex)

    Corridor Projects · Adur and Worthing, West Sussex, UK. Open to a socially-engaged artist based in the South East of England working with moving image / lens-based media. · Deadline: 03 Aug 2026 · Award: GBP 4,200 artist fee (inclusive of VAT) paid in instalments, plus travel, and an additional budget for materials for creative production, workshops and exhibition outputs (e.g. print). No application fee.

    Corridor seeks a socially-engaged artist based in the South East of England working with moving image / lens-based media for DAISY CHAIN, marking the 60th anniversary of Vera Chytilova's experimental film Daisies (1966). The commissioned artist will work with a group of LGBTQIA+ young people aged 15-26 in Adur (West Sussex) to co-create new artworks: a 2-3 minute moving image work and a series of 2D collage or mixed media artworks for public exhibition and screenings. DAISY CHAIN commissions six new artist moving image works overall (three England-based artists working with older women, young women and LGBTQIA+ youth; three artists in Czechia), developed in partnership with the National Film Museum in Prague (NaFilM) and Brighton-based videoclub, with results toured across England and Czechia in 2027. EXPECTATIONS: devise about eight hands-on workshops incorporating film, collage and mixed media (participants won't necessarily have an arts background); participate in 4-6 online peer sessions on collaborative practice led by artist Annis Joslin; complete exhibition-ready artworks on time and to budget; maintain a transparent dialogue on authorship with participants. Artists must have a participatory practice and experience of working creatively with young people. Note: to create a safe space, workshops will not be publicly labelled as LGBTQIA+. FEE: GBP 4,200 incl. VAT plus travel and a materials budget. TIMELINE: shortlist notified 7 August, online interviews 12 August 2026; launch event in Adur/Worthing March 2027. DEADLINE: 10am, 3 August 2026.

  2. BFI National Lottery Discovery Feature Funding (Debut Fiction Features)

    British Film Institute (BFI Filmmaking Fund, National Lottery) · United Kingdom (applications must come from a limited company registered and centrally managed in the UK) · Deadline: 13 Aug 2026 · Award: Up to GBP 1,000,000 of National Lottery funding towards a fiction feature with a production budget between GBP 1,000,000 and GBP 3,500,000. No application fee.

    Discovery is the debut-director production strand of the BFI Filmmaking Fund, awarding up to GBP 1 million towards fiction features budgeted between GBP 1 million and GBP 3.5 million. It backs first-time feature directors working alongside experienced producers, and is structured so creative risk sits with a team rather than a lone filmmaker. ELIGIBILITY: applications must be made through a limited company registered and centrally managed in the UK, so unincorporated individuals cannot apply. A dedicated lead producer with an established track record must submit, and that producer must be someone other than the writer or director. The director must be making their first fiction feature, meaning no prior feature at or above a GBP 1 million budget and no prior commercial UK or Ireland theatrical distribution of a fiction feature. At least two people must be credited across the writer, producer and director roles, all aged 18 or over and not in full-time education. Projects need a fully developed script and a runtime of at least 70 minutes. LIMITS: one application per production company, producer and director per round, and applications must arrive at least 20 weeks before the proposed first day of principal photography. ROUNDS: Round 2 closes at 5pm on Thursday 13 August 2026. Round 3 opens Thursday 12 November 2026 and closes Wednesday 16 December 2026. Successful applicants must publicly credit BFI National Lottery support.

  3. BFI National Lottery Creative Challenge Fund (Round Two)

    British Film Institute (BFI National Lottery) · United Kingdom (UK-based organisations running development programmes for UK creatives) · Deadline: 21 Sep 2026 · Award: Award amounts are not published on the fund page. No application fee.

    The Creative Challenge Fund does not fund films directly. It funds the organisations that run project-development programmes for UK creatives working on narrative, documentary or animation features, or on narrative immersive media projects. The BFI's stated aim is to decentralise development away from a small number of London-centric gatekeepers and build a wider, more resilient ecosystem of talent-development providers across the UK, with a recently added emphasis on independent producers. ELIGIBILITY: only legally constituted UK-based organisations may apply. Individual filmmakers seeking development money should look to the BFI Filmmaking Fund's Development strand instead. TIMING: Round One closed in March 2026 and has made its first awards. Round Two closes Monday 21 September 2026, with funded programme activity required to begin between 1 January and 30 June 2027. NOTE: award amounts are not published on the fund page. Successful applicants must prominently credit BFI National Lottery support.

  4. BFI National Lottery Impact Feature Funding (Higher-Budget Features)

    British Film Institute (BFI Filmmaking Fund, National Lottery) · United Kingdom (applications must come from a limited company registered and centrally managed in the UK) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Up to GBP 1,250,000, the largest single award in the current BFI portfolio, though the BFI notes it does not expect to support many projects at the maximum level. Production budgets from GBP 1M to GBP 15M for experienced directors, GBP 3.5M to GBP 15M for debut directors. No application fee.

    Impact is the higher-budget production strand of the BFI Filmmaking Fund and carries the largest award available in the current BFI portfolio at up to GBP 1.25 million. It sits above the Discovery strand in scale, serving experienced directors with production budgets from GBP 1 million to GBP 15 million, and debut directors whose budgets exceed GBP 3.5 million. UNLIKE DISCOVERY, Impact accepts applications on a continuous rolling basis with no round structure and no published closing date, though applicants must submit at least 20 weeks before principal photography begins. SELECTION is explicitly criteria-driven: projects must address at least three of the BFI's stated aims, covering equity and inclusion, audience or cultural impact, talent development, creative risk, production activity across the UK nations and regions, and environmental sustainability. ELIGIBILITY: applications must come from a limited company registered and centrally managed in the UK, so individuals cannot apply. A dedicated producer is always required, though unlike Discovery the writer and director may be the same person. Directing credits must be evidenced. No explicit nationality or residency test for the director or writer is published. NOTE: the BFI cautions that maximum-level awards are rare, so realistic requests will typically fall well below the GBP 1.25 million ceiling.

  5. BFI National Lottery Development Funding (Feature Script and Package Development)

    British Film Institute (BFI Filmmaking Fund, National Lottery) · United Kingdom (producers must apply through a limited company registered at Companies House) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Award amounts are not published on the fund page. No application fee.

    Development Funding is the earliest-stage strand of the BFI Filmmaking Fund, supporting original live action and animated feature films through script and package development to the point where they are viable candidates for production finance. It is aimed at distinctive fiction projects from what the BFI calls 'adventurous storytellers', across a wide range of genres rather than a single house style. UNLIKE DISCOVERY, it does not operate in rounds: applications are accepted year-round, with the only fixed interruption being an annual closure through the month of April, plus occasional short pauses during periods of high demand or holidays. ELIGIBILITY: the application must be made by a producer through a limited company registered at Companies House, so unincorporated individuals are ineligible. Nationality and residency requirements are not published on the fund page. NOTE: award amounts are not published, so the ceiling should be treated as unknown rather than assumed. Successful applicants are required to publicly acknowledge National Lottery support in project credits and materials.

  6. BFI National Lottery Audience Projects Fund

    British Film Institute (BFI National Lottery) · United Kingdom (legally constituted organisations centrally managed in the UK, not more than 50% foreign-owned) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Pilot projects GBP 10,000 to GBP 20,000. Standard projects GBP 20,000 to GBP 500,000 per year. Immersive projects may include up to GBP 150,000 of production costs within the GBP 500,000 cap. Total fund GBP 19.7M over three years. No application fee.

    The Audience Projects Fund distributes GBP 19.7 million of National Lottery money over three years to widen UK audiences for film and immersive work, covering exhibition, distribution, festivals, touring programmes and sector-support initiatives. It reopened in November 2025 and runs continuously with no closing date, which makes it one of the more accessible large BFI pots for organisations working on the audience-facing rather than production side. AWARDS are tiered: small pilot projects receive GBP 10,000 to GBP 20,000, while full projects can receive GBP 20,000 to GBP 500,000 per year, with immersive projects permitted to include up to GBP 150,000 of production cost inside that envelope. ELIGIBILITY is strictly institutional. Applicants must be legally constituted organisations centrally managed in the UK and not more than 50% foreign-owned. Eligible types include limited companies (not classed as 'large' under the Companies Act 2006), community interest companies, LLPs, UK local or statutory authorities, and registered UK charities or trusts. Individual practitioners cannot apply. Applicants must evidence experience in marketing, festivals, distribution, immersive installations, cultural programming, touring or sector support. TIMING: there are no deadlines but lead times are enforced. Single-film distribution projects must apply at least 10 weeks before release; all other projects at least 16 weeks before the project starts.

  7. BFI National Lottery Screen Heritage Fund: Individual Skills Funding

    British Film Institute (BFI National Lottery Screen Heritage Fund) · United Kingdom (applicants must hold a UK bank account and be eligible to work in the UK) · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Award amounts are not published on the fund page. No application fee.

    The rare BFI stream that individuals can apply to directly. Individual Skills funding supports training and professional development for people working, or intending to work, in the UK screen heritage sector, the field concerned with preserving, digitising and widening public access to screen archive collections. ELIGIBILITY: applicants must be 18 or over, working or intending to work in the UK screen heritage sector, hold a UK bank account, and be eligible to work in the UK. TIMING: applications are rolling with no deadline, but must be submitted at least eight weeks before the training starts. All funded activity must be completed by 31 March 2029, the end of the current National Lottery funding plan period. CONTEXT: the wider Screen Heritage Fund has three strands. Project funding, open to legally constituted not-for-profit organisations operating a screen archive or collection network, runs year-round with a 16-week lead time and prioritises collections that better reflect the diversity of the UK. Resilience funding for the 2026-29 cycle closed on 31 March 2026 and is not currently accepting applications. NOTE: award ranges are not published on the fund overview page.
