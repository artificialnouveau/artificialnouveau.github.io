  5. Open Call: Pee Workshop (Matter Out of Place)

    Nica Roses & Eris Kyriakides, at The Dirty Art Foundation · The Dirty Art Foundation (Amsterdam, Netherlands) · Deadline: 10 Aug 2026 · Award: Performer fee of EUR 200. Participation in the optional public showing is not required to receive the fee.

    Pee Workshop is looking for 16 performers to take part in a two-day research practice led by Nica Roses and Eris Kyriakides as part of the dance and performance event Matter Out of Place. The workshop uses drinking, holding, and releasing liquid as a starting point for choreographic research, paying attention to the body's internal timing: what we consume, how liquid moves through the body, what the bladder signals, and how the organs produce movements that often go unnoticed. This cycle becomes the structure of the research, opening into dance, somatic exercises, choreographic tasks, group compositions, and experiments with collective energy. The material develops through urination but also through the shared experience of moving, waiting, responding, and performing together. Performers, dancers, movement practitioners, and artists interested in somatic research, experimental choreography, and collective performance-making are welcome; professional dance training is not required, but applicants should feel comfortable working physically and collaboratively. All exercises are approached through clear consent, respect for personal boundaries, and collective care. The workshop takes place Monday 17 and Tuesday 18 August 2026 from 10:00 to 17:00 at The Dirty Art Foundation, culminating in an optional 60-minute public showing on Wednesday 19 August. Each performer receives a fee of EUR 200. To apply, send a short introduction about yourself, your movement or performance experience, and why you are interested in the workshop to nicaxroses@gmail.com by 10 August 2026.

National budget EUR 1.817 billion for 2026. No application fee.

    The WBSO (Wet Bevordering Speur- en Ontwikkelingswerk) is the Dutch R&D tax credit administered by RVO. It reduces payroll/wage tax for companies with R&D staff and provides an income-tax deduction for self-employed people doing R&D; it is a tax credit, not a cash grant. For 2026 the benefit is 36 percent of the first EUR 380,000 of R&D wage and other costs (50 percent for qualifying startups) and 16 percent above that; self-employed receive a fixed deduction (and need at least 500 R&D hours per year). Eligible applicants are Dutch companies and self-employed people paying payroll or income tax who carry out qualifying R&D in the EU. Applications are made in advance via Mijn RVO and cannot be retroactive; for coverage of the final three months of a year the deadline is 30 September. Time-keeping and project administration are required. No application fee.

  24. Cultuur+Ondernemen: Cultuurlening (rolling loan, Netherlands)

    Cultuur+Ondernemen · Netherlands. Creative and cultural professionals and organisations residing and working in NL. · Deadline: Rolling / undated · Award: Loan (must be repaid), up to EUR 500,000. Interest 2 percent up to EUR 50,000, then 2-5 percent (risk-based) above EUR 50,000; term up to 10 years. This is a LOAN, not a grant. No application fee.

    The Cultuurlening from Cultuur+Ondernemen is a low-interest loan (not a grant) for creative and cultural professionals and organisations in the Netherlands, to invest in their practice or business. Loans run up to EUR 500,000, with interest of 2 percent up to EUR 50,000 and 2-5 percent (risk-based) above EUR 50,000, and a term of up to 10 years. The loan must be repaid. Eligible applicants reside and work in the Netherlands and are either art-school graduates or have at least three years of professional practice (others can apply with a motivation and reference). Applications are accepted on a rolling basis with no fixed deadline. No application fee. Note: separate regional products exist (for example the Rotterdam and Amsterdam Cultuurlening) with different rates and caps.
