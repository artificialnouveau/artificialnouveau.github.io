Grants, Fellowships and Residencies in Canada

Open calls

  1. Fluxus Experimental Film & Media Arts Festival 2026: Call for Submissions

    Hamilton Artists Inc., in partnership with McMaster University School of the Arts and Factory Media Centre · Hamilton, Ontario, Canada; submitting artists must be currently based and/or working in Canada. · Deadline: 15 Jul 2026 · Award: Free to submit (NO entry fee). IMAA-scale artist remuneration (a screening/exhibition fee following the Independent Media Arts Alliance fee schedule) is paid to successful applicants within 30 days of the screenings.

    Fluxus is a contemporary short film and media arts festival hosted in partnership between Factory Media Centre, Hamilton Artists Inc and McMaster University School of the Arts. Its mission is to provide an accessible forum for the exhibition of creative, experimental, moving-image art forms, and to develop connections between Canadian media artists, arts institutions and the public. Fluxus showcases experimental and boundary-pushing media works from artists of all levels working in Canada; submissions are free and unrestricted by theme, open to a spectrum of artforms including but not limited to animation, documentary, video art, collage film, music video and experimental film. The festival takes place over two screenings at Hamilton Artists Inc on Friday 18 and Saturday 19 September 2026 (the Friday screening is dedicated to emerging media artists and filmmakers); all screenings are free to attend. REMUNERATION: there is no submission fee, and IMAA-scale artist remuneration is paid to successful applicants within 30 days of the screenings. GUIDELINES: artists must be currently based and/or working in Canada; work must be short format (15 minutes or less) and no more than 5 years old (2021 or later); submissions are digital Vimeo or YouTube links (private/password-protected accepted if the password is provided); solo artists, groups, collectives and collaborative works are welcome, with a limit of one submission per artist/group/collective; films must be independent projects over which the artist retains full creative and artistic control. AI POLICY: Fluxus discourages the use of generative AI in submitted works; if AI factors into the creative process, its use must be declared and described (see Factory Media Centre's Policy on the Use of Generative AI). APPLY via the online submission form with an artist bio (75 words max), artist CV (PDF, 3 pages max), a brief description of the work (100 words max), entry information, the video link, and two image stills. Notification is expected in August; due to the high quantity of submissions, only selected artists will be contacted. Fluxus is committed to equity and strongly encourages applications from Black and Indigenous artists, persons with disabilities, ethnic or racial minorities, immigrants and refugees, Francophones, LGBTQI+ persons, women, and persons of all socio-economic backgrounds. Deadline: Wednesday 15 July 2026 at 11:59pm EST. Questions: programming@theinc.ca.

  2. Inside Out RE:Focus Fund Post-Production Grants 2026

    Inside Out (Toronto 2SLGBTQ+ Film Festival) · Toronto, Canada (Inside Out is a Canadian organisation; international projects may apply, see FAQ) · Deadline: 22 Jul 2026 · Award: Post-production grant funding to support the completion and promotion of short and feature films; the requested amount is set by the applicant (see the FAQ 'How much funding can I request?'). Since 2018 the RE:Focus Fund has distributed more than CAD 300,000 in grants and programs. No application fee.

    Established in 2018 through a lead gift from Martha McCain, Inside Out's RE:Focus Fund addresses industry inequities by providing direct financial support to women, non-binary, and/or trans filmmakers telling 2SLGBTQ+ stories. Launched in 2019, the RE:Focus Fund Post-Production Grants support the post-production and promotion of short and feature films. Applications for the 2026 round open 29 June 2026. ELIGIBILITY: women, non-binary, and/or trans filmmakers whose projects tell 2SLGBTQ+ stories; per the FAQ, international projects may be eligible to apply. The requested funding amount is determined by the applicant, and unsuccessful applicants can request feedback and reapply the following year. Inside Out also runs other RE:Focus initiatives, including a new Residency program for first-time feature filmmakers facing intersectional barriers. KEY DATES: submissions open 29 June 2026, close 22 July 2026, with notifications by 15 August 2026. Apply and read the FAQ via the Eventival page linked from the RE:Focus Fund site; questions to industry@insideout.ca.

  3. Call to Artists: Walden Fire Station and Library (Calgary Arts Development)

    Calgary Arts Development (CADA) · Walden community, Calgary, Alberta, Canada. Open to local and national (Canadian) professional artists/artist teams. · Deadline: 28 Jul 2026 · Award: Total project budget of CAD 275,000 (exclusive of all applicable taxes) for one selected artist/artist team. The artist fee for the total project is CAD 68,750 + GST. Budget breakdown can be adapted to the artist's approach, following CARFAC's recommendation that a minimum 20% of the budget go to the artist's fee; the total allocated budget will not be increased.

    Calgary Arts Development (CADA) is seeking an artist or artist team to join The City of Calgary's Walden Fire Station and Library project team to create permanent public artwork(s) for a combined fire station and library planned for the community of Walden. One artist/artist team will be selected with a total project budget of CAD 275,000 (exclusive of all applicable taxes); the opportunity is open to local and national professional artists/artist teams working in various media. ELIGIBILITY: local and national mid-career to late-career professional public artists and artist teams with a minimum of five years' previous experience creating artwork in the public realm; they must have at least three outdoor permanent public art installations, each with a lifespan of over 10 years and a minimum project budget of CAD 150,000 (see the Canada Council for the Arts definition of a professional artist). Walden does not currently have an active community association, so the project requires experience in artist-led community engagement and a strong understanding of alternative outreach. Artists must be available to meet the project timelines from September 2026 to the end of concept and design by July 2027; fabrication and installation take place in Phase 2, starting in the second half of 2027 and continuing into 2028. REWARD: total project budget CAD 275,000 (artist fee CAD 68,750 + GST); CADA is open to adapting the budget breakdown to the artist's approach, following CARFAC's recommendation of a minimum 20% to the artist's fee, though the total budget will not be increased. TIMELINE: applications open 16 June 2026 (9am MT); pre-recorded information session posted early July; application deadline 28 July 2026 (no applications accepted after 1pm MT); shortlisted interviews mid-September; results late September 2026. Eligibility questions: walden@calgaryartsdevelopment.com.

  4. Michele Larose - Osler Library Artist-in-Residence 2026-2027

    Osler Library of the History of Medicine, McGill University (funded by Dr. Michele Larose) · Montreal, Quebec, Canada (open to all; preference typically given to artists who reside in or can easily travel to Montreal) · Deadline: 31 Jul 2026 · Award: $6,000 CAD (taxes may be deducted); paid in two parts (half at the beginning of the residency, half on completion). Covers travel, materials, and related expenses. No application fee.

    Annual artist-in-residence award funded by Dr. Michele Larose, hosted by the Osler Library of the History of Medicine at McGill University, supporting visual artists who use McGill collections to create works addressing contemporary and/or historical subjects in medicine and the health sciences. Possible projects include painting, photography, performance, sculpture, and digital, video or installation art. Most work is anticipated to be inspired by Osler Library collections; sources may also include other McGill Libraries, the Maude Abbott Medical Museum, and McGill faculties and hospitals. NO STUDIO SPACE provided; the library offers research support, research space, and exhibition space. RESIDENCY DUTIES: work on the project; give a public presentation; exhibit or perform the work; submit a report suitable for the Osler Library Newsletter; complete the work by 30 April 2027. The artist retains ownership of the work but must credit Dr. Larose and the Osler Library. Applicants are encouraged to propose a project engaging one of the Faculty of Medicine and Health Sciences Strategic Research Plan themes: Infection (as a threat); Cancer (as a complex global challenge); The Brain; or Personalized/ing medicine. ELIGIBILITY: degree in Studio Arts or a related field and/or a history of exhibiting work in professional venues; open to non-Canadians, but preference typically given to Montreal-based or easily-traveling artists. APPLY by emailing ONE PDF (filename: lastname.LaroseOsler2026.pdf) to awards.oslerlibrary@mcgill.ca containing a 1-page project description, proposed timeline, CV, one letter of recommendation (addressed to Head Librarian Dr. Mary Hague-Yearl), and work samples (typically 5-15 images). Reference letters may also be sent separately by the referee before the deadline. Results announced in August or September. Deadline: 31 July 2026.

  5. Black Arts Centre: Call for Exhibition and Programming Proposals

    Black Arts Centre · Surrey, British Columbia, Canada. Priority to Black artists, curators, and art organizers, and to projects aligned with the gallery's mandate (BIPOC, Disabled, Queer, Immigrant/Refugee/Newcomer perspectives). · Deadline: 08 Aug 2026 · Award: Successful proposals receive financial, budgeting, and curatorial support from the Black Arts Centre, including project execution, production, logistics, and marketing. No submission fee.

    The Black Arts Centre is calling for proposals from artists for exhibitions and public programming. Exhibition proposals are considered for Spring 2027 and beyond, while programming proposals are considered for projects taking place from September 2026 onward. The submission portal is open from 1 June to 8 August 2026. Proposals are reviewed by the Black Arts Centre team and a revolving committee of external community members, evaluated using an internal rubric; applicants begin to hear back in September. SUPPORT: successful proposals receive financial, budgeting, and curatorial support from the Black Arts Centre, including project execution, production, logistics, and marketing. ELIGIBILITY AND PRIORITY: applicants do not need to identify as Black to submit, but priority is given to Black artists, curators, and art organizers, and to artists and projects that align with the gallery's mandate (BIPOC, Disabled, Queer, Immigrant/Refugee/Newcomer perspectives). Submission does not guarantee approval for this cycle, but all proposals are kept on file for future consideration, and updated or alternate proposals are accepted. Questions and PDF proposals/portfolios: info@theblackartscentre.ca. Deadline 8 August 2026.

  6. SAT x LAB7: Performing Arts and Technology Research-Creation Residency 2026 (Montreal)

    Societe des arts technologiques (SAT) in partnership with LAB7 / 7 Fingers (Les 7 Doigts) · Montreal, Canada. Open internationally; no nationality restriction stated. · Deadline: 31 Aug 2026 · Award: CAD 10,000 grant per selected project, plus roughly two weeks (about 10 days) of studio workspace at the 7 Fingers Creation Centre and personalised coaching from the SAT and LAB7 teams. No application fee.

    The Societe des arts technologiques (SAT) and LAB7 (the research-creation lab of 7 Fingers / Les 7 Doigts) offer research-creation residencies at the intersection of performing arts and technology, on the theme of human movement. Each selected project receives a CAD 10,000 grant, roughly two weeks (about 10 days) of studio workspace at the 7 Fingers Creation Centre in Montreal, and personalised coaching from the SAT and LAB7 teams. Open to non-profit organisations, collectives and independent artists working between performing arts and technological arts; the call is effectively open internationally, with no nationality restriction stated. No application fee. Results are announced in early October 2026. Application deadline: 31 August 2026, 17:00. Apply via the official open-call page.

  7. Al Purdy A-frame Association: Al and Eurithe Purdy Residency 2027

    The Al Purdy A-frame Association · Ameliasburgh, Prince Edward County, Ontario, Canada. Canadian citizens or permanent residents only. · Deadline: 18 Sep 2026 · Award: CAD 750 per week stipend plus paid return travel to Ameliasburgh (funding subject to the Association's fundraising and Canada Council matching). Terms of 3 to 12 weeks. No application fee.

    The Al Purdy A-frame Residency offers Canadian writers time and space to work at the historic A-frame home built by poet Al Purdy and his wife Eurithe on Roblin Lake in Ameliasburgh, Prince Edward County, Ontario. Poetry is prioritized, but all writers are welcome. SUPPORT: CAD 750 per week stipend and paid return travel (subject to the Association's fundraising and Canada Council matching); residencies run 3 to 12 weeks. ELIGIBILITY: Canadian citizens or permanent residents only. DEADLINE: 18 September 2026 (postmark, or 4:00pm PT for electronic submissions). Apply via the residency page.

  8. Toronto Arts Council: Media Artists Program - Creation 2026

    Toronto Arts Council · Toronto, Canada · Deadline: 15 Oct 2026 · Award: Up to $15,000 (CAD) per artist for media-arts creation. No application fee.

    Project funding from the Toronto Arts Council for professional media artists to create or complete independent film, video, audio, digital, VR/AR and multimedia works, with grants up to $15,000. ELIGIBILITY: Canadian citizens, permanent residents or Protected Persons who have lived in Toronto for at least one year, are professional media artists retaining full creative control, and are not students.
