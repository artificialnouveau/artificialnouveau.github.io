Audio, Sound and Music Grants in the EU

Currently 17 active audio, sound and music grants, fellowships and residencies open to applicants in the EU. Hand-curated and updated weekly. Almost every entry is funded; a few notable unpaid open calls and festival submissions are included as clearly flagged exceptions.   1. ZAMEK Artist Residency 2027 (ZAMEK Culture Centre, Poznan)

    ZAMEK Culture Centre (Centrum Kultury ZAMEK) · Poznan, Poland · Deadline: 27 Jul 2026 · Award: Accommodation in an apartment in the former imperial residence, studio access, a fee of PLN 6,000 gross per month (contract for specific work), a production budget of PLN 5,000 to 15,000 gross depending on length of stay and project, plus curatorial support and publicity. No application fee listed.

    Open call for ZAMEK Culture Centre's 2027 Artist-in-Residence programme in Poznan. Supports artists working across visual arts, performance, choreography, dance, music, sound art, architecture and filmmaking (literature is excluded). Two formats: a Conceptual Residency (creative stay, no requirement to complete or present a final work, with public meetings at least every two weeks) and an Operational Residency (concluding with an artistic event such as an exhibition, concert or screening, plus regular public meetings). Selected artists receive accommodation in the former imperial residence, a studio, a fee of PLN 6,000 gross per month, a production budget of PLN 5,000 to 15,000 gross, curatorial support and publicity. Residencies run 4 to 8 weeks in 2027. ZAMEK is particularly interested in projects that engage changing realities, the current moment and the future (climate and migration crises, the war in Ukraine, social and political issues) or the context, history and architecture of the Castle; project scale must fit the programme budget, and projects should not rely on external co-financing. Download the Terms & Conditions on the ZAMEK site to apply. Deadline: 27 July 2026.

  2. CMC/DMARC Sonic Arts Residency 2026/2027

    Contemporary Music Centre (CMC) & Digital Media and Arts Research Centre (DMARC), University of Limerick · University of Limerick, Limerick, Ireland (in person). · Deadline: 05 Aug 2026 · Award: EUR 1,000 honorarium (from CMC), plus a travel bursary up to EUR 350 and an accommodation bursary up to EUR 1,400 (ten nights). No application fee.

    The Digital Media and Arts Research Centre (DMARC) and CMC Ireland announce the second year of the Sonic Arts Residency Programme at the University of Limerick Electronic Music Studio for 2026/27. The residency offers Irish and international composers and sound artists an opportunity to develop their creative practice and experiment using UL's facilities. FACILITIES: the Electronic Music Studio (EMS) featuring both digital and analogue workflows, including a bespoke Buchla 200e; a 32-channel immersive sound lab (SpADE); a makerspace supporting electronics and fabrication; and a range of well-equipped recording studios with a wide selection of microphones and production hardware and software. DMARC is based in the Computer Science and Information Systems Department at UL and has a long history supporting digital arts, electronic music, and computer-aided composition (hosting the 2022 International Computer Music Conference and 2020 International Conference on Live Coding, the Diffusion Prize with RTE Lyric FM, and partnering with Light Moves Festival). PROGRAMME: each residency lasts 10 working days within one of two windows, September-December 2026 or January-March 2027. Support includes a EUR 1,000 honorarium from CMC, a travel bursary up to EUR 350, and an accommodation bursary up to EUR 1,400 (ten nights), with DMARC helping identify accommodation. There is an outreach/presentation element: a one-hour talk/performance for the DMARC community of students and researchers, and required documentation of the process (photo, video, recordings, and/or written reflections) that may be published by CMC on social media; artists may also be invited to contribute to CMC's amplify podcast series or online features at cmc.ie/features. The programme runs 2025-27 with support for six artists (4 Irish, 2 international). ASSESSMENT: candidates apply via an online form submitting a CV, work samples, an outline of the residency project (including technical requirements), how they propose to engage with the facilities, and how the residency would benefit their practice. Criteria: artistic quality of the proposal including development of work using either the SpADE system or Buchla 200e; how the opportunity supports the candidate's current career stage and artistic goals; demonstrated technical capacity to engage with the facilities; and balanced representation of genres, gender, and geographical regions. TO APPLY: complete the online application form linked from the opportunity page. Closing date: 11am (IST), 5 August 2026; late applications will not be accepted. Questions: jurgen.simpson@ul.ie or kfennell@cmc.ie. Applications are jointly assessed by DMARC and the Contemporary Music Centre.

  4. ICST: Artists in Residence 2027 Open Call (ZHdK, Zurich)

    Institute for Computer Music and Sound Technology (ICST), Zurich University of the Arts (ZHdK) · Zurich, Switzerland (in-person residency at ICST) · Deadline: 01 Sep 2026 · Award: Use of ICST facilities and technical infrastructure plus technical support by research staff; per diems for meals and accommodation during the residency; travel contribution toward travel to Zurich (up to CHF 300 for persons living in Europe, up to CHF 500 for those outside Europe). Participants organise their own travel and accommodation (ICST assists if required). No application fee.

    The Institute for Computer Music and Sound Technology (ICST) at the Zurich University of the Arts (ZHdK) invites applications for its 2027 Artists in Residence program, fostering artistic exchange in key areas of current research. Residencies are available in five themes: Spatial Audio; Klavierautomat (algorithmic composition); Moving Loudspeakers; Dance and Generative AI; and Object Performance. The program offers access to ICST's facilities and technical infrastructure, professional support from researchers, and per diems for meals and accommodation. Artists and musicians working with sound and sound media, whose practice reflects a strong interest in one of the listed research areas, are encouraged to apply. One selected project from the Spatial Audio Residency will be featured at the SONIC MATTER Festival 2028 as part of an ongoing partnership. PRACTICAL: residencies include use of facilities and technical infrastructure as specified in the thematic descriptions, plus technical support by ICST research staff. Participants receive a contribution toward travel to Zurich (up to CHF 300 for persons living in Europe, up to CHF 500 for persons living outside Europe) and a per diem to cover meals and accommodation in Zurich; participants organise their own travel and accommodation, with ICST assistance if required. Residency dates are defined individually. APPLICATION: submitted via the online form, uploading a concept sketch, composition idea or project draft (PDF, 2 pages, 10 MB max); a portfolio of three selected works relevant to the residency topic including links to external audio and video (PDF, 2 pages, 10 MB max); and a tabular CV covering education and career (PDF, 1 page, 10 MB max). The residencies are awarded by a jury of ICST researchers and project leaders. TIMELINE: deadline 1 September 2026 at 24:00 CET; candidates notified mid-October 2026. Selected candidates receive forms required for an invitation to Switzerland and must confirm participation and return completed forms within 10 days or the invitation may be withdrawn.

  9. National Gallery of Iceland: CUSP Artist Residency (Reykjavik 2027)

    National Gallery of Iceland (Listasafn Islands), CUSP project · Reykjavik, Iceland. Residency hosted by the National Gallery of Iceland. · Deadline: 03 Sep 2026 · Award: Accommodation and working space for the residency; travel and subsistence stipend; an artist fee for collaborating in the open-lab/masterclass; and, depending on the artist's request and museum means, hardware and software for the working space. No application fee.

    The National Gallery of Iceland announces an artist residency open call under its CUSP program, a European cooperation project funded through Creative Europe's Culture grant. Artists working in complex media or emerging technologies (e.g. generative AI, custom sensors, sound, software-based, blockchain, physical and digital hybrid) are invited to apply. This is the first of four residency stays offered to different artists through the program, one in each of the participating countries (Iceland, Norway, Czechia, and Ukraine), followed by two micro residencies in the Netherlands and Iceland. The programme brings together artists working in time-based media with museum professionals, conservators, and technology specialists developing preservation and conservation methodologies to ensure the long-term endurance of artworks. The selected artist will meet with a preservation specialist or conservator specializing in time-based media art for an in-depth artist interview addressing the preservation needs of their medium, and will present their body of work and working methods publicly during an open-lab masterclass designed in collaboration with the museum. RESIDENCY: up to four weeks in early 2027 in Reykjavik, exact dates arranged with the selected artist. WHAT IS PROVIDED: accommodation and working space; a travel and subsistence stipend; an artist fee for the open lab/masterclass; and possibly hardware and software depending on the artist's request and museum means. ELIGIBILITY: international artists based outside Iceland; applicants must be residents of any of the 27 EU member states or of non-EU countries with association agreements (EFTA/EEA members, acceding countries, candidate countries, and European Neighbourhood Policy countries). APPLICATION: complete the application form with full name, country of residence, gender identity, contact information, an artistic statement (max 2500 characters), a motivation letter (max 2500 characters), a draft idea of the public workshop or masterclass (max 1000 characters), links to social media/professional websites, and a CV and portfolio upload (max 10MB each). DEADLINE: 3 September 2026 at 23:59 GMT. ABOUT CUSP: Creative Understanding, Saving and Preserving Time-Based Media Art is a transnational cooperation project co-funded by the EU's Creative Europe Culture grant, led by the National Gallery of Iceland with Nasjonalmuseet (Norway), LI-MA (Netherlands), Vasulka Kitchen (Czechia), and Mystetskyi Art Arsenal (Ukraine), focused on preserving and presenting time-based media art. Inquiries: cusp.residency.is [at] gmail.com.

    Q-O2 (workspace for experimental music and sound art) · Brussels, Belgium; 10 residencies for international artists and 10 for artists living in Belgium. · Deadline: 15 Sep 2026 · Award: NO artist fee. International residents: shared accommodation in the Q-O2 apartment plus a travel contribution up to EUR 200. A small subsistence budget is reserved for artists who do not come with other funding.     The residencies at Q-O2 offer time and space for artistic research, reflection and creation in the field of experimental music and sound art. In 2027, Q-O2 will host 20 residencies, 10 for international artists and 10 for local artists living in Belgium, working with, on or around sound.   15. Ernst von Siemens Musikstiftung: Project Funding (October 2026 round)

    Ernst von Siemens Musikstiftung · International, no stated country restriction on the organising institution, though the Foundation is Swiss and German in orientation and administration. · Deadline: 01 Oct 2026 · Award: No published minimum or maximum. Primarily covers artistic costs, not overheads. The Foundation explicitly will not fully fund a project, and requires at least one other institution to participate financially at a level similar to the amount requested, functioning as a de facto 1:1 match. No application fee.

    One of the largest private funders of contemporary and new music in Europe, and it funds the organising side of the field rather than individual artists, which makes it directly usable by festivals. Eligible activity includes festivals, world premieres and revivals, composition commissions, events, academies and workshops, education projects, symposia and musicological publications. Experimental and contemporary sound programming is the core remit; this is not a classical-repertoire fund. There are two annual deadlines: 1 October at 14:00 CEST for projects starting the following year with decisions by year-end, and 1 March for projects starting in July or later with decisions by end of June. Grant sizes are not published, and the Foundation aims funding at artistic rather than overhead costs. Only the organising institution may apply and only legal entities qualify: cultural institutions, orchestra managers, festival organisers and ensembles. Self-applications and applications from individuals are explicitly refused. VOLUME LIMITS are strict and worth planning around: each institution may submit one application per deadline, may be supported for only one project per year, and may be supported for a maximum of three projects across three consecutive years.

  16. Musikfonds: Outer Ear Special Programme (Round 2026/2)

    musikfonds e.V. · Germany. Projects taking place abroad are excluded. · Deadline: 30 Oct 2026 · Award: FLAG, UNVERIFIED: per-project amounts are not published on the programme overview page and must be confirmed via the application portal at musikfonds-antrag.de. No application fee indicated.

    Outer Ear is Musikfonds' dedicated special programme for participatory projects in experimental music, and the strand that most cleanly matches an experimental and sound-art public-programme brief rather than concert-series funding. It emphasises the creative process and audience participation over finished works, explicitly targeting audiences for experimental music, which makes it suitable for workshop-plus-presentation formats, listening programmes and process-led sound art events. Application windows run twice yearly: 1 to 30 April 2026 (closed) and 1 to 30 October 2026, the next live deadline. THE FIRST OUTER EAR ROUND WAS HIGHLY COMPETITIVE, with 14 projects approved at an 8.6 percent success rate, so treat it as a long shot. As with all Musikfonds strands, the applicant must be based in Germany, projects abroad are ineligible, and double-funding with BKM or with projects run by permanently institutionally supported bodies is excluded. Note that Musikfonds' Small and Large Project Funding strands are listed separately in the Grant Desk; Outer Ear is a distinct programme with its own window.

  17. Stichting NORMA: NORMA Fonds Project Grant (round 4, 2026)

    Stichting NORMA (collecting society for performing artists) · Netherlands-based arts-sector activity. No city or region restriction stated. · Deadline: 30 Oct 2026 · Award: Maximum EUR 10,000 per project. Total project budget may not exceed EUR 150,000 and the applicant organisation's annual budget may not exceed EUR 500,000. At least 25 percent of the project budget must come from audience or box-office revenue. Performer fees must meet the 2026 wage norms of EUR 139 per rehearsal and EUR 340 per performance. No application fee.

    The NORMA Fonds redistributes copyright remuneration collected by Stichting NORMA into cultural, social and professional projects involving performing artists, and is one of the few Dutch schemes with four intakes a year and a genuinely short cycle. Both arts-sector organisations and individual arts professionals may apply, though the applicant needs a bank account and legal footing and cannot be a BIS-funded institution. The project must centre on performing artists in the copyright sense, meaning people who perform a copyright-protected work: musicians, dancers, actors, voice actors. This fits live experimental music, sound performance and performative media-art work, but not object-based exhibitions or non-performative installation programmes. The maximum grant is EUR 10,000, the total project budget must stay under EUR 150,000, the applicant's annual budget under EUR 500,000, and at least a quarter of the project budget must be earned from audience income, so this is a top-up rather than a primary funding source. Budgets must honour NORMA's 2026 performer wage norms. Applications are submitted online during a one-month window each quarter; the next round runs 1 to 30 October 2026, closing at 23:59. TIMING RULE: decisions arrive no later than eight weeks after the deadline, and the premiere date must fall at least eight weeks after the application deadline. Organisations that already received a NORMA Fonds grant in 2024 or 2025 are excluded, and supporting staff may not exceed 2 FTE.
