Converges into

Necromemex

Since the dawn of civilization, how we depict people after death shapes everything we understand about them and their society. Governments use the executed and the lying-in-state as symbols of legitimacy. Movements turn people into martyrs. We mythologize the person, appropriating their likeness into an idea that's less about the human being and more about the story we choose to tell. Generative AI has upended this process entirely — giving us more control than ever over what dead people can look like, say, and mean. Necromemex maps this transformation across history, making the machinery of necroimetics visible and interactive.
