1970 — The Precedent

They said it couldn't be done

In the late 1960s, European aerospace was fragmented. France, Germany, the UK, Spain — each had their own companies, each too small to challenge Boeing's dominance.

So they did something radical: they merged everything. Not a loose consortium. A real company with shared R&D, shared manufacturing, one mission.

The result? Airbus went from 0% to 50% of the global commercial aircraft market. 130,000 employees. Revenue exceeding Boeing's. A $100B+ enterprise.
